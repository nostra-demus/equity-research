# GOLD — Commodity Dossier

> **Test fixture — illustrative figures, not live research.** Committed so chat-over-dossier and the commodity tests are demonstrable without a live run.

## 1. Snapshot
- Benchmark: LBMA / COMEX gold, ~$2,320/oz (illustrative, 2026-06-30).
- Curve: mild contango (~ −1.5% annual roll) — normal carry, not tightness.
- Balance: demand-led; inelastic supply, central-bank buying historically high, ETF holdings rising.
- Net macro: mixed (positive real yields + firm USD headwind vs safe-haven + CB-buying support).
- Positioning: crowded-ish long (COMEX managed money upper third); ETF flows confirming.

## 2. Thesis Summary
Gold's structural story is intact — inelastic supply, historically high central-bank buying, and a safe-haven bid. But most of that is already in a price sitting in the upper half of its 52-week range, the macro backdrop is only mixed (real yields are still positive and the dollar is firm), and speculative positioning is already a crowded long with flows confirming. So the risk/reward from HERE is balanced, not compelling: the reasons to own gold are real, but the reasons to ADD at this level are weak. There is no proven variant perception versus consensus — the market already knows the central-bank story.

## 3. Risk Summary
- **Strongest bear case:** real yields rise / the dollar strengthens, and the crowded long unwinds — a positioning-driven drawdown even with the structural story intact.
- **Single killer risk:** a hawkish rate surprise (higher-for-longer) lifting real yields.
- **What would flip the view / force a downgrade:** a clear, sustained fall in 10y real yields (macro turns decisively supportive) → upgrade toward Buy; a hawkish shock + positioning unwind → downgrade toward Trim.

## 4. Relative — are we in the right commodity?
Versus the other tracked commodity (sugar), gold is a macro/monetary bet (real yields, USD, official demand) with a comfortable inventory buffer, while sugar is a weather/policy-driven agricultural balance. Gold's setup is a "hold what you have" — the crowded long caps the case for adding. If the portfolio wants a fresh, less-crowded, catalyst-rich exposure, sugar's weather/policy swing factors may offer more asymmetry; gold is the steadier, already-owned macro hedge. On today's evidence, gold rates a Hold, not an add.

## 5. Action Discipline
- **Action:** Hold
- Why this and not the neighbours: the structural bull case is real but largely priced, the macro is mixed, and positioning is crowded — enough to KEEP a core gold hedge (Hold) but not to add (not Buy). §24 favours not chasing a crowded long; there is no evidence to justify Avoid/Trim while central-bank demand persists.
- Data sufficiency + conviction: Sufficient inputs; conviction capped as Commodity-conditional (the view depends on the rate path we do not control).
