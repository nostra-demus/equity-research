> **Test fixture — illustrative figures, not live research.**

## Abstract
Gold's balance is demand-led: mine supply (~3,650 t) is inelastic and broadly flat, so the price is set by investment and official-sector demand. Central-bank buying has stayed historically high (~1,040 t) and ETF holdings are rising — a comfortable above-ground buffer, not a tight stocks-to-use. Weather is not a driver; only mild India/Q1 seasonality applies.

## Balance (surplus / deficit)
Not a consumption balance like an ag — the swing is investment + official demand vs a large above-ground stock. Currently demand-supported (illustrative).

## Inventory buffer vs history
ETF holdings ~3,150 t and rising; above-ground metal is large — the buffer is comfortable, so scarcity is not the bull case.

## Direction & biggest swing factor
Biggest swing factor: whether central-bank buying stays elevated and ETF inflows continue. Weather: n/a.
