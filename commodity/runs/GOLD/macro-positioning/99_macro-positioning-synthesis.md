> **Test fixture — illustrative figures, not live research.**

## Abstract
The macro tilt is MIXED: a rates/USD headwind (positive real yields, firm dollar) is offset by a supportive safe-haven and central-bank bid. Speculative positioning is a crowded-ish long (COMEX managed money in the upper third of its range) and ETF flows confirm the move — which makes the trade vulnerable to a shakeout if real yields back up.

## Net Macro Tilt & dominant driver
Mixed. Dominant driver: real yields — a clear fall would flip macro decisively supportive; a rise is the main headwind.

## Positioning & Flows (crowded? confirming?)
Crowded-ish long, flows confirming. Little dry powder from spec longs; asymmetric downside if the macro wobbles.

## Interaction / timing risk
Supportive structural demand + a crowded long = an asymmetric setup: limited fresh spec buying, real downside on a rates shock.

## Note to the Commodity Thesis
- Macro mixed; real yields the swing.
- Positioning crowded long, flows confirming → timing risk (shakeout on a rates back-up).
- This argues against ADDING at current levels even if the structural story is intact.
