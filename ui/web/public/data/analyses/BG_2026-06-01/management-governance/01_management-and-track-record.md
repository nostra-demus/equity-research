## 3. Stated Strategy & Targets

Management's stated strategy is to be the connective layer between where crops are grown and where they are processed/consumed, and to compound earnings power from (a) the Viterra integration, (b) organic growth projects, and (c) a biofuel/renewable-feedstock tailwind [`FY25 10-K, CEO letter, p.1`; `Q1 FY26 transcript, prepared remarks & closing`]. The explicit, checkable targets management has put on the record:

- **FY2026 adjusted EPS of $9.00–$9.50** (raised from $7.50–$8.00 at the Q4 call), issued 2026-04-29 [`Q1 FY26 transcript`; `Capital IQ Guidance export, guidance date 2026-04-29`]. *In progress — see §4.*
- **Viterra cost synergies:** ">$70M realized by year-end 2025," described as "running ahead of plan," with "greater gains in 2026 and beyond" [`FY25 10-K, CEO letter, p.1`; `Q1 FY26 transcript, closing remarks`]. The full Investor-Day synergy run-rate target is referenced ("themes laid out at Investor Day have not changed") but the **dollar target itself is not in the pool** — Investor Day (March 2026) materials were not provided.
- **Deleveraging:** adjusted net-debt/adjusted-EBITDA stated as a near-term priority; 1.9x at YE2025 improving to 1.6x by Q1 FY26, with management acknowledging leverage is "a little elevated with Moody's right now versus where we want to be by the end of the year" [`Q1 FY26 transcript, Q&A`; `business-model/11_capital-allocation-governance`].
- **Capital-return framework:** a "new framework" from Investor Day to return "closer to 50% of discretionary cash flow" to shareholders, plus a commitment to finish the remaining **$250M Viterra-portion buyback** in 2026 [`Q1 FY26 transcript, Q&A — Neppl to Strelzik`].
- **FY2026 below-the-line guides:** capex $1.5–1.7bn, adjusted tax rate 22–26%, net interest $620–660m, D&A ~$975m [`earnings/04_guidance-consensus`].

Several of these (the Investor-Day synergy run-rate, the multi-year earnings-power and leverage targets) are **forward and not yet checkable** because Investor Day was March 2026, ~2 months before this analysis. They are logged, not graded.

## 5. Management Read

CEO Heckman (~7.4 yrs) and CFO Neppl (~7.0 yrs) are a stable, long-tenured, professionally-credentialed pair — brought in together from Gavilon in 2019 — who have run the company through its largest-ever deal with **zero top-two turnover and no flagged senior departures**, which on the stability axis is a clear positive. On delivery, the checkable record is good-not-spectacular: the metric they guide (adjusted EPS) was hit or beaten in three of the last four years and missed only −0.6% once, with five straight quarterly beats — but these are short-horizon, late-year guides, so this reads as a reliable guider rather than a proven long-range forecaster. **The single biggest management signal is the Viterra bet:** the team's credibility now rides on converting a deal that diluted shareholders ~44% and tripled debt into per-share value, and that promise is real, dated, and not yet proven — synergies are tracking early (">$70M, ahead of plan"), but the full result is the open question that should drive the next read.

*Note: officer ownership/skin-in-the-game (agent `04`) and compensation metrics (agent `03`) are deferred to the 2026 DEF 14A, which is not in the pool, so this read is on competence and delivery only — alignment is assessed by those agents.*
