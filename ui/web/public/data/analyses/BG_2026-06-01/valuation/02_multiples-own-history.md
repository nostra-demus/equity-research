## 3. Re-Rating / De-Rating Read

**The stock has re-rated UP over the past ~12 months, and now sits near the top of its own one-year range — but the move is dominated by an earnings-outlook recovery, not by multiple expansion on a stable earnings base.** Three evidenced points:

1. **Price level:** at $123.35 the shares sit at the ~83rd percentile of the trailing 52-week range ($71.60–$133.93) and have roughly doubled off the mid-2025 low — a level corroborated by recent buyback execution at ~$79.77 in Oct-2025 and the $81.39 Jul-2025 grant-date close [FY25 10-K, lines 2017, 5519]. On the rough adjusted-P/E proxy the implied multiple moved from ~8–10x at the lows to ~13x forward today.
2. **The forward multiple itself is mid-cycle, not stretched, on the company's own curve:** forward EV/EBITDA is ~9–10x (NTM 9.2x, FY26 9.8x, FY27 9.0x) and forward adjusted P/E ~12–13x. Against BG's own long fundamental history (this is a thin-margin processor whose adjusted EPS has ranged from ~$1.7 in 2009 to ~$13.9 in 2022 [Consensus.xlsx]), a low-double-digit forward P/E is mid-range for the franchise — it is neither a trough multiple nor a peak multiple.
3. **Most likely reason for the re-rating — outlook recovery + cycle, not structural premium:** the price move tracks the FY2026 adjusted-EPS guidance raise to $9.00–$9.50 (from $7.50–$8.00) and a +16% lift in the FY2026 consensus to $9.43, with every analyst revising up (6 up / 0 down) [earnings/04 §4–5]. The market re-rated the *earnings*, not the *multiple*: forward EV/EBITDA barely changed because EBITDA estimates rose ~9% alongside the price. This is a **margin/biofuel-led** recovery in a cyclical processor, not a quality re-rating [earnings/04 §4].

**Net:** BG has re-rated up off a cyclical trough and trades near its 1-year high, but on forward EV/EBITDA (~9–10x) and forward adjusted P/E (~12–13x) it sits **mid-range versus its own franchise history**, with the caveat that a clean multi-year band cannot be sourced.

Combined with the indicative-only (web-sourced) price, the own-history lens here is **directional, low-confidence**, and should be cross-checked against peers (`03`) and cash flows (`04`/`05`).
