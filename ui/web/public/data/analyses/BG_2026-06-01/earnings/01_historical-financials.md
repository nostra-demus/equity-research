# Historical Financials — BG

**Company:** Bunge Global SA (NYSE: BG). **Reporting currency:** US$ millions unless noted. **Fiscal year:** ends December 31. In this report **FY0 = FY2025** (10-K, year ended Dec 31, 2025), and the **latest quarter Q0 = Q1 2026** (10-Q, quarter ended Mar 31, 2026).

**Three structural breaks dominate every table below — flag once, applies throughout:**
1. **Viterra acquisition closed mid-2025** (registered shares issued July 2, 2025; $4,201M cash + 65.6M new shares) [FY25 10-K, Note 2; Consolidated Statement of Changes in Equity, line 4902]. It roughly doubled quarterly revenue from ~$12–13bn to ~$22–24bn starting Q3 2025 and lifted diluted share count ~44%. Year-over-year and quarter-over-quarter comparisons across the mid-2025 line are not like-for-like.
2. **Segment redefinition (Q3 2025) and Sugar & Bioenergy exit** — the discontinued segment's results were moved into Corporate and Other, enlarging the corporate drag [FY25 10-K, MD&A, lines 2153–2169].
3. **Reported earnings are heavily distorted by mark-to-market timing.** Bunge's GAAP results swing on non-cash mark-to-market of commodity/freight/FX contracts and readily marketable inventory. The gap between GAAP and adjusted is large and recurring (Q1 2026: GAAP diluted EPS $0.35 vs adjusted $1.83) [Q1 2026 earnings release, p.1]. Treat GAAP single-period swings with caution; see Section 4.

## 6. Key Trend Summary

- **Revenue direction: Volatile / acquisition-inflected.** Reported revenue fell 10.8% in FY2024 ($59,540M → $53,108M) on lower commodity prices, then rose 32.4% in FY2025 to $70,329M almost entirely because of the mid-2025 Viterra consolidation — quarterly revenue stepped from ~$12–13bn to ~$22–24bn in Q3 2025. Organic, like-for-like growth is not separable from the acquisition in the supplied data, so the headline acceleration is not an organic-demand signal.
- **Margins direction: Compressing.** GAAP gross margin fell from 8.14% (FY2023) to 6.39% (FY2024) to 4.85% (FY2025) — down 329 bps over two years. Company-defined Total EBIT margin fell from 5.60% to 2.18% over the same span, and Total EBIT itself dropped from $3,333M to $1,533M even as revenue grew, partly from a Corporate & Other drag that widened to −$796M (pension settlement, impairment, Viterra integration costs). This is a thin-margin commodity processor whose spreads normalized down from the elevated FY2022–FY2023 cycle.
- **Seasonality: not cleanly measurable at the revenue level; present at the cash level.** The one clean pre-acquisition year (FY2024) showed near-zero revenue seasonality; FY2025's Q3/Q4 skew is a Viterra artifact, not a season. Separately, Q1 is reliably an operating-cash-outflow quarter (inventory build): CFO −$285M (Q1 2025) and −$541M (Q1 2026).
- **Inflection points:** (1) **Mid-2025 Viterra acquisition** — the single largest break in the series, roughly doubling revenue, lifting diluted shares ~44% (FY24 142.2M → FY25 166.5M weighted; Q1'26 195.7M vs Q1'25 135.4M), driving net debt from $2,927M (FY24) to $12,916M (FY25) and Net Debt/EBITDA from ~1.3x to ~5.8x, and turning FCF negative (−$879M in FY2025) as CFO collapsed to $844M against rising capex of $1,723M. (2) **GAAP vs adjusted divergence widened** — in FY2025 adjusted EPS ($7.57) sat 54% above GAAP EPS ($4.91), and in Q1 2026 adjusted EPS ($1.83) was over 5x GAAP ($0.35), almost entirely mark-to-market timing. The reliable read of the operating trend is the adjusted series; on that basis FY2025 was down moderately versus FY2024 (adjusted EPS $7.57 vs $9.19) rather than the −39% the GAAP line shows.
