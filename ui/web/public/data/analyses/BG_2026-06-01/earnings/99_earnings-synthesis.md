# Earnings Module — BG (Synthesis)

## Abstract

Bunge's reported earnings are best described as volatile and inflecting on a margin call, not as a clean acceleration: a two-year GAAP gross-margin slide (8.14% to 4.85%) collides with a Q1-2026-and-guidance "improving crush spread" story. The consensus bar is fair, not low: the Street's $9.43 already sits above the $9.25 midpoint and back-half breadth is net negative. The biggest risk is that a policy or spread reversal unwinds the upgrade as fast as it appeared, against net debt of $13,714M. Verdict: mixed earnings setup, policy-conditional.

## 5. Earnings Setup Summary

### Revenue Setup
The trailing-year revenue trajectory is **not sustainable as a trend** because it is overwhelmingly a one-time consolidation effect: the mid-2025 Viterra acquisition roughly doubled the quarterly base, and that step-up laps from Q3 2026, after which the reported YoY tailwind vanishes. The single factor that would flip the *underlying* revenue/spread direction is biofuel mandate policy — the same EPA RVO mechanism that lifted FY26 guidance ~20% can reverse and pull soy/canola-oil demand and the flat price down with it. The gap between reported and organic revenue is the central distortion: FY2025 reported +32.4% and Q1'26 +87.8% are "almost entirely Viterra," and organic like-for-like growth is **not separable from the data pool** — so the first clean read of demand is Q3 2026, not now.

### Margin Setup
Current margins are **mid-cycle and externally hostage, not at a defensible peak or trough**: the soybean crush spread has already round-tripped (segment EBIT $2,222M → $872M → $1,225M across FY23–25), and the FY26 "improving" call is a Q1-and-guidance read on top of a two-year GAAP compression. The driver that takes the largest bite if it moves adversely is that same crush spread — a 15% adverse move is worth roughly ±$1.06 of FY26 adjusted EPS (`07` §2) — concentrated in Soybean, which is ~80% of total EBIT net of the corporate drag. Bunge has essentially **no structural margin protection**: the business is rated "Squeezed — price-taker on inputs AND outputs," with no pass-through escalator, and management "cannot lock the margin until all three legs are priced" by the exchanges (`03` §3, §8). It is a pure spread-taker.

### Quality Check
The single largest gap between reported and economic earnings is **mark-to-market timing**, and it is so large that the adjusted figure effectively *is* the earnings number — Q1'26 adjusted EPS was 5.2x GAAP, with a +$958M non-cash derivative add-back inside CFO; the gap is timing-driven and two-sided, not steadily widening, but it dwarfs GAAP every quarter. The recurring adjustment to watch is **Viterra integration cost** ($114M/$244M/$223M, three years running) — that piece is not genuinely one-time and should be only partially added back. To model normalized FY2026 earnings, start from **adjusted (non-GAAP) Total EBIT and adjusted EPS**, because GAAP single-period figures are uninformative for this business — but label them management-defined and weight cash conversion over reported profit, since CFO/EBITDA at 25–38% and negative FCF are the real, if Viterra-distorted, quality drag.

### Consensus Bar
For the company to beat the current bar by a material margin, the **H2 crush/oil-leg spread must hold or widen** — the biofuel-driven soy-oil stock draw-down management framed for "Q3 and especially Q4" has to actually arrive and lift the spread above the elevated Q1 run-rate. The bar is most likely **mis-set in the back half**: full-year consensus ($9.43, above the $9.25 midpoint) rose because H1/Q2 marks went up enough to offset cautious FQ3/FQ4 marks that are net negative — so the front half looks correctly-to-conservatively priced while the H2 implied in the full-year number looks **too high** if the spread fades. A large share of the upgrade is anchored to one recent macro/policy event — the EPA RVO decision and an Iran-conflict energy-price bump — both of which can reverse, which is precisely why the bar is "fair" rather than low.

## 5b. Leverage & Capital Structure

Both leverage triggers fire (TRIGGER A: ND/EBITDA > 3.0x; TRIGGER B: ND/EBITDA up > 1.0x YoY and net debt up > 2x YoY), so this section is required.

Current leverage is **net debt of $13,714M at Mar-31-2026** (total debt $14,553M less cash $839M) and **$12,916M at FY25 year-end**, giving Net Debt / GAAP-built EBITDA of **~5.78x at FY25-end** (`01` §1, §2; FY25 10-K balance sheet + MD&A line 2906; Q1'26 10-Q lines 204–212). Year-over-year this is a step-change: the ratio went **~1.30x → ~5.78x** and net debt went **$2,927M → $12,916M** (roughly 4.4x) across FY24 to FY25 (`01` §1). The single driver is the **mid-2025 Viterra acquisition** — $4,201M cash plus 65.6M new shares, which added the acquired debt and inflated working-capital financing (FY25 10-K, Note 2) — not operating losses. The reported 5.78x uses a **GAAP-built EBITDA** (Bunge reports no GAAP EBITDA line; it is Total EBIT + D&A, per `01` note [a]); on an adjusted-EBITDA proxy (adjusted Total EBIT $2,034M + D&A $703M = $2,737M) the ratio is **~4.72x**, and on forward FY26 consensus EBITDA ($3,997M) it falls to **~3.4x** as the first full post-Viterra year annualizes (inference from `04` §3 consensus). The **debt maturity profile and weighted-average coupon are not disclosed in the supplied data pool** — short-term debt is $3,245M and current portion of long-term debt $1,361M at Q1'26 (so ~$4.6bn is current), but the full maturity ladder and rate detail are not in the extracted filings; stated explicitly as missing. On capital-allocation constraint, the data pool shows interest expense rising (FY24 $471M → FY25 $628M → FY26 guided $620–660M, already raised once mid-year) and FCF negative (−$879M FY25), but **no disclosed buyback pause, dividend cut, capex deferral, or negative rating action** is in the pool — `08` notes the company is investment-grade with an unqualified Deloitte opinion; capex is still guided up to $1.5–1.7bn, so leverage is elevated but not visibly forcing retrenchment in the supplied evidence.
