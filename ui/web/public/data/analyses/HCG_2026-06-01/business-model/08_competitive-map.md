# Competitive Map — HCG

*HealthCare Global Enterprises Limited (NSE: HCG, BSE: 539787). Competitor scale figures are from a Capital IQ comparable-analysis export (USD, as-of 2026-06-01) unless stated; USD figures are restated to INR at the rate implied by HCG's own line in that export (HCG LTM revenue USD 268.7m ≈ FY26 INR 25,454m → ≈ ₹94.7/USD). Upstream inputs (02_business-identity, 03_segment-map) both present.*

---

## 1. Dominant Segment

**India comprehensive cancer care (oncology hospital network)** — ~97.6% of FY26 revenue and ~98.5% of Adjusted EBITDA; a single-segment business, with value driven by centre maturity rather than a product mix [03_segment-map, §2; Q4/FY26 Investor Deck, May 2026, "Financial Highlights: FY26"]. HCG calls itself "the largest provider of cancer care in India" and "India's foremost pure-play oncology powerhouse" [FY24-25 Annual Report, MD&A p.146 ("Our business / Oncology") and p.20]. Competitors below must compete in **this** segment, not in hospitals generally.

---

## 2. Named Competitors

A structural note on sourcing: HCG's filings do **not** name corporate competitors in a competition section — they speak generically of "our competitors" and contrast HCG against "multi-speciality hospitals" that "have one department for oncology but lack comprehensive cancer care centres," naming only Tata Memorial as an R&D peer [FY24-25 Annual Report, MD&A p.147 ("HCG key differentiators"), p.146, p.149 ("Risks and concerns")]. The named set below is therefore built from (i) the Capital IQ comparable-analysis export in the data pool and (ii) dated industry/trade sources, filtered to the oncology segment. There are two distinct rival types: **multi-specialty majors with large oncology departments** and **standalone single-specialty cancer chains** (HCG's own category).

### Competitor A — Apollo Hospitals (Apollo Cancer Centres / Apollo Proton Cancer Centre)

- **Ticker / listing:** NSE/BSE: APOLLOHOSP (Apollo Hospitals Enterprise Ltd). Apollo Cancer Centres is its oncology vertical; not separately listed.
- **Where they compete:** India comprehensive oncology — the largest dedicated cancer footprint among the multi-specialty majors. 14 dedicated cancer centres, 240+ oncologists, 1,000+ beds, plus a proton-therapy facility (Apollo Proton Cancer Centre, Chennai). Direct catchment overlap with HCG in Bengaluru, Hyderabad, Chennai [Web: apollohospitals.com "Apollo Cancer Centers Overview," 2026-06-01 (indicative, unverified)].
- **Scale:** Apollo Hospitals group revenue is far larger than HCG (group is the largest listed hospital chain in India by revenue; ~74 hospitals, 10,400+ beds), so the oncology vertical alone is plausibly of comparable-or-larger scale than HCG — but Apollo does not disclose a clean stand-alone oncology revenue line [Web: apollohospitals.com, 2026-06-01 (indicative, unverified)]. *Inference, not from filings:* oncology is a minority of Apollo's group revenue, so segment-level scale is not precisely comparable from disclosure.
- **Source named in:** HCG's own filing names Apollo as a prior employer of two HCG executives (CEO Meghraj Gore; SVP-Sales) — an industry reference, not a competition statement [FY24-25 Annual Report, p.37, p.87-88]. Named as a direct HCG competitor in trade/industry profiles [Web: tracxn.com HCG competitor list, 2026 (indicative, unverified)].
- **One-line read:** The deepest-pocketed oncology rival; competes on brand, proton therapy and metro density, but oncology sits inside a multi-specialty group rather than being the whole company as it is for HCG.

### Competitor B — American Oncology Institute (AOI / Cancer Treatment Services International)

- **Ticker / listing:** Private. Backed by CTSI / Asia Healthcare Holdings; CTSI was acquired by Varian (now Siemens Healthineers) [Web: medtechdive.com "Varian ups presence in India with $283M acquisition"; tracxn.com AOI profile, 2026 (indicative, unverified)].
- **Where they compete:** This is HCG's **closest like-for-like rival** — a standalone, single-specialty comprehensive-oncology chain (medical, radiation, surgical oncology under one roof), the same model as HCG. ~18 oncology hospitals across South Asia, weighted to South and Central India / Tier-2 cities — overlapping HCG's non-metro expansion thesis [Web: americanoncology.com "About," 2026; healthcareradius.in profile (indicative, unverified)].
- **Scale:** Revenue not publicly disclosed (private). Network of ~18 oncology hospitals vs HCG's 22-24 comprehensive cancer centres → comparable in centre count, though HCG is materially larger by revenue (HCG FY26 revenue ₹25,454m) [Q4/FY26 Investor Deck, May 2026; Web sources above (indicative, unverified)]. Qualifies as a credible competitor on the "clear segment-specific rival" criterion (same single-specialty model, overlapping geography) even though its revenue is not disclosed.
- **Source named in:** Named as a top HCG competitor in trade/industry profiles [Web: tracxn.com HCG competitor list, 2026 (indicative, unverified)].
- **One-line read:** The only rival running HCG's exact playbook — dedicated cancer chain expanding into Tier-2 cities — and the one most likely to contest HCG's emerging-centre catchments directly.

### Competitor C — Max Healthcare Institute

- **Ticker / listing:** BSE: 543220 / NSE: MAXHEALTH.
- **Where they compete:** Listed multi-specialty chain in which oncology is a named top specialty; competes with HCG in North India and metros [Capital IQ comparable export, Business Description, 2026-06-01: "specialties, including … oncology"]. Catchment overlap is lower than Apollo/AOI (Max is North-India weighted vs HCG's South/West tilt).
- **Scale:** LTM revenue ≈ USD 884m (≈ ₹83.7bn); LTM EBITDA ≈ USD 237m; EBITDA margin 26.8%; market cap ≈ USD 9,641m — the largest comp in the set, roughly 3.3x HCG's revenue and ~6.5x its EBITDA margin-dollars [Capital IQ comparable export, Financial Data + Operating Statistics, 2026-06-01]. At ~3.3x HCG revenue it sits just outside the ~5x scale band on the small side, i.e., comparable enough to be a credible peer.
- **Source named in:** Capital IQ relevancy-ranked comparable set for HCG; also named in HCG trade-press competitor lists [Capital IQ comparable export, 2026-06-01; Web: tracxn.com, 2026 (indicative, unverified)].
- **One-line read:** A higher-margin, much larger multi-specialty operator whose oncology line competes at the premium/metro end — useful as the downstream margin benchmark, less so as a head-to-head catchment rival.

**Broader listed comp bloc (named, for market-structure context, not profiled):** Fortis Healthcare (LTM rev ≈ ₹91bn), Narayana Hrudayalaya (≈ ₹79bn, runs an oncology line), Aster DM Healthcare (≈ ₹46bn), Global Health/Medanta (≈ ₹44bn, "cardiac and cancer care"), KIMS (≈ ₹39bn). All carry oncology departments but none is a pure-play cancer chain [Capital IQ comparable export, Financial Data + Business Description, 2026-06-01].

## 3. Competitive Position

**Position vs peers: Holding-to-gaining in oncology, but on a proxy, not on disclosed segment share.** No competitor or HCG discloses an audited India-oncology market-share series, so a clean share read is not available. The supporting proxies: HCG states an explicit ambition to reach "45%+ market share" in its home Bengaluru market and describes itself as "a natural industry consolidator," with FY26 group revenue +15% YoY (₹22,228m → ₹25,454m) and emerging centres growing far faster than the network (FY25 emerging-centre growth +32%) [FY24-25 Annual Report, MD&A p.29; 02_business-identity §2; 03_segment-map §2]. On scale within the *listed* peer set, HCG is the smallest by revenue (≈ ₹25bn vs Max ≈ ₹84bn, Fortis ≈ ₹91bn) but the only pure-play oncology operator, so it is not losing oncology share to those names so much as occupying a different niche [Capital IQ comparable export, 2026-06-01]. Against its true like-for-like rival (AOI, ~18 centres), HCG is larger by revenue and centre count. The honest read: HCG is the share leader in standalone comprehensive cancer care and is adding capacity, but the absence of a disclosed oncology-share dataset caps confidence in any "gaining" claim.

---

## 4. Competitive Shape

**Fragmented overall, with a small consolidated cluster of dedicated cancer chains.** Indian cancer care is structurally fragmented: HCG's own filing notes comprehensive cancer-care centres are concentrated in metros (≈30% in the top-8 metros, ≈10% in other state capitals, ≈60% in the rest of India), the industry is projected to grow ~12% CAGR, and the sector is "witnessing increased consolidation even among the larger players," with HCG positioning as the consolidator via bolt-on acquisitions [FY24-25 Annual Report, MD&A p.28, p.146, p.149, p.29]. No single player approaches majority national share, and oncology demand sits across thousands of multi-specialty hospitals plus government institutions (Tata Memorial, regional cancer centres). The narrower segment that actually matters — **organised, multi-modality dedicated cancer chains** — is far more concentrated: effectively HCG (leader, 22-24 centres), Apollo Cancer Centres (14 centres), and AOI (~18 centres), i.e., a 3-player lead group, with the listed multi-specialty majors (Max, Fortis, Narayana, Aster, Medanta) contesting the premium/metro slice [FY24-25 Annual Report p.146-147; Capital IQ comparable export, 2026-06-01; Web sources, 2026-06-01 (indicative, unverified)]. HHI / hard top-N share data: not disclosed in the data pool.

## 5. Caveat

Three limitations to flag for downstream `moat`:
1. **No company-named competitor list.** HCG's filings name no corporate competitor in a competition section (only Tata Memorial as an R&D peer). The named set rests on a Capital IQ comparable export (Tier-5 vendor data) plus dated, unverified web/trade sources — not on the company's own disclosure.
2. **Segment-level scale is partly unavailable.** The closest like-for-like rival (AOI) is private and does not disclose revenue; the largest oncology rival (Apollo) does not break out a stand-alone oncology revenue line. So head-to-head oncology economics cannot be fully sized from disclosure — the listed comp set (Max, Fortis, Narayana, Aster, Medanta, KIMS) is group-level multi-specialty, only partially oncology.
3. **No disclosed market-share dataset.** The position read in §3 is proxy-based (HCG's stated Bengaluru ambition, revenue growth, centre count), not a measured share series. What would resolve it: an audited or third-party India-oncology revenue/share dataset (e.g., a Capital IQ/industry oncology-specific market-size report), or segment-level oncology revenue from Apollo and a revenue figure for AOI.
