# Board & Shareholder Rights — HCG

*HealthCare Global Enterprises Limited (NSE: HCG / BSE: 539787). Indian SEBI-LODR issuer, Ind AS, FY ends 31 March. Reporting currency INR (₹). Report date: 2026-06-01. The proxy-equivalent is the AGM Notice + Corporate Governance Report (CG Report); takeover/voting features come from the Companies Act 2013 + SEBI LODR + the Articles of Association as summarised in the AR. The only audited board/CG disclosure in the pool is the FY2024-25 Integrated Annual Report (year ended 31-Mar-2025, filed Sep-2025; auditor signed 24-May-2025). The post-KKR-changeover board (from 30-May / 30-Jun-2025) is disclosed in that AR's "subsequent events" sections and the 20-May-2026 Q4/FY26 transcript; a FY26 audited CG Report is NOT in the pool. Where I rely on post-31-Mar-2025 facts I say so.*

Scope: this agent answers whether the board is a real check on management and whether shareholder rights protect minority holders. It does not assess ownership/insider behaviour (agent 04), compensation magnitude/metrics (agent 03), value the company, or rate the stock. Upstream inputs read and used: in-module `01_management-and-track-record.md` and `00_governance-data-triage.md`; cross-module `business-model/01_disqualifier-scan.md` (RPT disqualifier — owned there, referenced here) and `business-model/11_capital-allocation-governance.md`. All were available.

**Governing fact for this read:** HCG is private-equity-controlled, not founder-controlled. KKR's Hector Asia Holdings II Pte Ltd acquired sole control (~51.59%) on 30-May-2025 under an SPA dated 23-Feb-2025, with a parallel **Promoter Agreement** that gives the controlling owner the right to nominate directors and reconstitute every board committee [FY24-25 AR, Directors' Report §15, §19.4, p.76-79; CG Report, p.120-127]. The board and all committees were reconstituted on 30-May-2025 around KKR nominees. So the board's independence has to be judged against a control bloc that holds ~51-71% of the votes and explicit nomination rights — the classic minority-shareholder exposure for a PE-controlled listed company.

## 4. Takeover Defenses & Voting Rights

*India's framework is statutory (Companies Act 2013 + SEBI LODR + SEBI SAST/Takeover Code); HCG's Articles were amended in FY25 (postal ballot 27-Apr-2025) and adopted. US-style poison pills/staggered boards are generally not used; the relevant minority protections are one-share-one-vote, the SAST mandatory open offer, and Companies-Act meeting/consent rights.*

| Feature | Present? | Detail | Source |

| Classified / staggered board | No | Directors are appointed/re-appointed by shareholder vote; ⅓ of non-independent directors retire by rotation each AGM (Anjali Rossi retiring by rotation at the 27th AGM). | Majority vs plurality director voting | Majority (statutory) | Director appointments are ordinary resolutions (>50% of votes cast); ID re-appointments are special resolutions (≥75%). Read: **voting rights are intact and clean** — one-share-one-vote, no dual-class, no poison pill, no staggered board, and the SAST 26% open offer gave dissenting minorities an exit at ₹504.41. The genuine limit on minority power is arithmetic, not structural: with the promoter bloc holding ~51-71% of votes, the controlling owner can carry any ordinary resolution and (with founder-group support) most special resolutions, so minority votes are protected in form but cannot outvote control. That is the standard PE-controlled-listco condition, not an abusive takeover-defence structure.

## 5. Minority-Shareholder Protection Read

The board is a **real but constrained check on management**: the audit and nomination/remuneration committees are independent-chaired and independent-majority, the chair/CEO roles are split, board independence meets the 50% LODR floor, voting is clean one-share-one-vote with a SAST open-offer exit, and RPTs are immaterial with **no royalty/brand/management-fee leakage to the promoter or founder** — so the core risk that a controlled listco siphons value is not present in the data. What it is *not* is an independent-controlled board with a margin of safety: HCG is ~51-71% controlled, the chair is a non-independent founder, directors are nominated by the controlling owner under a Promoter Agreement that lets it reconstitute every committee, independence sits exactly at the regulatory minimum, and two of the four independents are at the SEBI overboarding ceiling. The single biggest entrenchment/exposure risk for minority holders is **control concentration itself** — with a KKR/founder bloc that can carry any ordinary resolution, minority votes are protected in form but cannot prevail, and the 25.99%-against KMP-bonus vote shows minorities will register dissent that they cannot translate into outcomes (a PE sponsor running the company to its own exit timeline, not a self-dealing promoter). Conclusion: **adequate** minority-shareholder protection — clean rights and committees, no value leakage, but independence only at the floor and real power held by the controlling bloc.
