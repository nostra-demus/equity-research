## 2. Performance Metrics

The actual metrics are disclosed in the CG Report's "Variable Remuneration/Compensation" paragraph. They are stated in plain text **without numeric weights** — the weights are set per individual by the Nomination & Remuneration Committee (NRC) and are not published. The full NRC policy lives on the company website (not in the pool) [FY24-25 AR, CG Report §I, p.125].

| Plan | Metric (as disclosed) | Weight | Per-Share/Returns or Size-Based? | Source |
|---|---|---:|---|---|
| Annual cash variable (Exec Directors incl. CEO) | **Revenue** | Not disclosed | **Size-based** | FY24-25 AR, Annexure 3 "Variable Remuneration", p.105 (L6498) |
| Annual cash variable | **Profit achievement** (absolute) | Not disclosed | Size/absolute-profit (not per-share) | FY24-25 AR, p.105 |
| Annual cash variable | **Operating-margin achievement** | Not disclosed | Margin (profitability ratio) — not per-share/returns | FY24-25 AR, p.105 |
| Annual cash variable | **"Other strategic goals as decided by the Board"** + individual KRAs/KPIs | Not disclosed | Discretionary / unclassifiable | FY24-25 AR, p.105; CG Report §I(d), p.125 |
| Long-term incentive (ESOP 2014 / ESOS 2021) | Vesting = continued employment (time) **+** "achievement of performance criteria as specified by the NRC at grant" | Not disclosed (per-grant) | Equity instrument (ties pay to share price); underlying performance metric **not disclosed** | FY24-25 AR, ESOP note (i)/(ii), p.97-98 (L5992, L6021) |

**Classification verdict.** Every *named* financial parameter in the variable-pay policy is **absolute / size- or margin-based — revenue, absolute profit, operating margin.** There is **no per-share or returns-based metric (no ROCE, ROIC, EPS, FCF/share, or TSR)** named anywhere in the disclosed criteria, even though management itself has set a public 5-year "20%+ pre-tax ROCE" target [Dec-04-2025 Investor Meet; mgmt-gov/01 §3]. ROCE is a stated *strategic* target but is **not shown to be a pay metric.** The one structural pull toward per-share value is the ESOP: equity grants tie a slice of pay to the share price (≈25% of the prior CEO's package, ≈47% of the prior CFO's). But options are struck at exercise prices of ₹130-200 (ESOS 2021) — at or below market, i.e. **not premium-struck**, so they reward the share price reaching modest levels rather than out-performance, and the performance condition attached to vesting is not disclosed. Net: the *named* incentive design rewards growth and absolute profit/margin, with equity providing partial — but undisclosed-strength — per-share alignment.

**One off-balance-sheet incentive of note.** During FY25 the prior promoter, Aceso (via parent AIHPL), proposed paying **bonuses directly to identified KMPs/employees** "as consideration for... enhancement of shareholder value," with **no financial obligation on the company**; approved by the Board (21-Feb-2025) and by shareholders via postal ballot (results 28-Apr-2025) [FY24-25 AR, Standalone Note 44D, p.293]. This is a sponsor-funded management-incentive/value-creation payout (PE-style), tied to the owner's realized value rather than to a disclosed per-share company metric. Its amount and metrics are **not disclosed**, and the minority vote on it was contested (see §3).

**Reported vs adjusted note (hygiene).** The pay disclosure uses cash + ESOP fair-value charge; "% increase" lines explicitly exclude share-based payment [Annexure 3(b) footnote, p.103]. The PAT figures used for the performance comparison are reported (not adjusted) — HCG's adjusted-vs-reported PAT gap was +305% in FY26 [earnings/06], which is why a revenue/EBITDA-keyed bonus can pay out while reported per-share earnings deteriorate.

## 4. Incentive Alignment Read

The named variable-pay metrics reward **revenue, absolute profit, and operating margin** — size and growth, not per-share value — and the disclosed framework contains **no ROCE, EPS, FCF/share, or TSR pay metric**, even though management publicly touts a 20%+ ROCE *target*; the only per-share pull is ESOP equity (≈25-47% of the prior CEO/CFO packages), but those options are struck at-or-below market and their vesting performance condition is undisclosed, so their alignment strength can't be verified. The single biggest signal is twofold and points the same way: pay rose (Chairman +22.5%, 200:1 ratio) on the metrics the company hits while reported PAT collapsed and EPS kept missing, and minority shareholders threw **~26% opposition** at the promoter-funded KMP bonus tied to "shareholder-value enhancement" — the clearest evidence that the pay-design's claim of value alignment is contested by the people it is meant to serve. **Conclusion: mixed, tilted to size-based** — the disclosed cash-bonus metrics are size/absolute-profit-based (no per-share/returns metric), partly offset by equity in the mix; and, decisively, the *current* CEO's and CFO's pay and metrics are **undisclosed**, so true alignment for the team in charge today is **not assessable** (partial-data cap applied).

Out-of-scope note: this report does not value HCG, assign probabilities, or rate the stock — those belong to the valuation module and the master synthesizer.
