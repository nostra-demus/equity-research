# Revenue Drivers — HCG

*HealthCare Global Enterprises Limited (NSE: HCG, BSE: 539787) — pan-India oncology-focused hospital chain. All figures INR million (₹ Mn), consolidated, unless stated. Fiscal year ends 31 March (FY26 = year ended 31 Mar 2026). FY26 figures are sourced to the board-approved audited results presentation dated 19 May 2026 and the 20 May 2026 earnings call; the full FY26 statutory statement and FY26 Integrated Annual Report are not yet in the pool. Reported metrics are used unless an adjusted/company-defined basis is labelled.*

Upstream `01_historical-financials.md` is present and used for the revenue baseline. Business-model `03_segment-map.md` and `05_customer-geography.md` are present and used for the segment, payor, and geography decomposition.

This agent answers one question: **if revenue changes next quarter or next year, what is the most likely cause?** It does not value the stock, rate it, model scenarios, or assess margins/earnings quality (those are other agents).

## 1. Segment Decomposition Status

**Single-segment business (>85% from one segment) — consolidated analysis, with management/deck sub-splits.**

HCG reports **one** operating segment under Ind AS 108: "Medical and Healthcare Services" (cancer care) — 100% of revenue and profit on the audited basis [FY24-25 Annual Report, Note 36 (Consolidated)]. An independent vendor source corroborates this: Capital IQ tabulates a single business segment ("Setting up and Managing healthcare…") equal to total revenue (₹25,454.1 Mn FY26) across FY21–FY26 [CIQ Financials.xls, Segments sheet, rows 16–17, data as of 2026-06-01]. By the audited revenue-by-type split, ~93% of FY25 revenue is income from medical (hospital) services [FY24-25 Annual Report, Note 22 (Consolidated)].

There is **no audited segment-level P&L**. The economically meaningful splits HCG drives revenue with — geographic cluster, centre-maturity cohort, modality mix, payor mix, volume vs ARPP — exist only in the investor deck and transcript (Level-3 management sources), often presented "excluding fertility" and not reconciled to the audited statements. These are used below and flagged as deck/management figures.

One structural change is imminent: the **Milann fertility business (≈2.4% of FY26 revenue) is being divested** (signed May 2026, close expected Q1 FY27), leaving an almost-pure oncology platform [Q4/FY26 deck, May 2026, "Milann Divestment Update"; business-model/03_segment-map.md].

## 2. Revenue Driver Tree

| Business Type | Revenue Formula |
|---|---|
| Hospital / care provider (HCG's type) | In-patient volume × average revenue per patient (ARPP) + out-patient / diagnostics / pharmacy + government grant |

**HCG-specific revenue formula (FY26 basis):**

`Revenue ≈ IP patient volume × ARPP per IP patient + OP-onco/diagnostics/pharmacy revenue + government-grant income`

- FY26 (ex-fertility): IP volume **294,912 patients (+12% YoY)** × ARPP **₹84,261 (+3% YoY)** → revenue ex-fertility **₹24,850 Mn (+15% YoY)** [Q4/FY26 deck, "Financial Highlights: FY26", "Largest Pan-India Oncology Focused Hospital Chain" (p.3)].
- Reported total revenue (incl. fertility, incl. ₹69.8 Mn govt grant) **₹25,454.1 Mn (+14.5% YoY)** [Q4/FY26 deck, "Q4 & FY26 Consolidated P&L"; upstream 01, §1].
- Capacity backdrop: 25 hospitals, ~2,605 beds, 38 LINACs, blended centre utilisation **58%** (FY26) — i.e. the volume engine still runs below the ~80%+ ceiling management implies for mature assets [Q4/FY26 deck, p.3, "Hospital Utilization…" (p.17)].

Within the IP-volume × ARPP frame, **volume is the dominant lever** (≈12 of the ≈15 points of ex-fertility growth) and ARPP is a minor, deliberately-restrained contributor (see §6, §7).

## 6. Revenue Growth Decomposition

FY26 reported total revenue grew **+14.5%** (₹22,228.5 → ₹25,454.1 Mn). HCG discloses an explicit volume/price split, but only on an **excluding-fertility** basis (+15%), so the decomposition below is built on that basis and reconciled to the reported total.

**(a) Ex-fertility basis (management-disclosed split), FY26:**

| Component | Contribution to Growth (pp) | Evidence |
|---|---:|---|
| Volume (IP patients +12%) | +12.0 | `Q4/FY26 deck, p.3 & "Financial Highlights: FY26"` |
| ARPP / price-mix (+3%) | +3.0 | `Q4/FY26 deck, p.3` |
| Combined (volume × ARPP cross-term) | ~0.0 | derived (12% × 3% ≈ 0.4pp; immaterial, absorbed) |
| **Total ex-fertility revenue growth** | **+15.0** | `Q4/FY26 deck, "Financial Highlights: FY26" (revenue ex-fertility ₹21,651 → ₹24,850 Mn)` |

**(b) Reconciliation to reported +14.5%:**

| Component | Contribution to Growth (pp) | Evidence |
|---|---:|---|
| Oncology + Kenya (ex-fertility) growth | +15.0 (on ₹21,651 Mn base) | `Q4/FY26 deck, "Financial Highlights: FY26"` |
| Milann fertility (slower, +5% on small base) | drag of ~−0.5 pp on the blended rate | `Q4/FY26 deck, p.13 (Milann ₹578 → ₹605 Mn)`; derived |
| Government grant income (₹47.1 → ₹69.8 Mn) | +0.1 | `Q4/FY26 deck, "Q4 & FY26 Consolidated P&L"` |
| **Total reported revenue growth** | **+14.5** | `upstream 01, §1`; `Q4/FY26 deck, P&L` |

**Spot-check:** ex-fertility revenue ₹24,850 Mn × (1 ÷ 1.15) = ₹21,609 ≈ disclosed FY25 ex-fertility ₹21,651 Mn ✓. Volume 294,912 ÷ 264,278 = +11.6% ≈ +12% ✓. ARPP ₹84,261 ÷ ₹81,924 = +2.85% ≈ +3% ✓ [`Q4/FY26 deck, "Financial Highlights: FY26"`].

**What this says:** roughly **four-fifths of HCG's revenue growth is volume; one-fifth is price/mix (ARPP)**. There is no FX component on revenue (₹98% India), and M&A is not additive in FY26 (Vizag already in base; Milann is a pending subtraction). The growth is organic and volume-driven. The one missing piece: HCG does not split ARPP growth into pure-price vs case-mix, and does not quantify the revenue it deliberately forewent by paring low-margin business ("Right now we may not be able to answer that question" — `transcript, Q&A (p.10)`), so the ARPP line blends rate, modality mix, and payor mix.

## 7. The Single Biggest Revenue Driver

**In-patient volume — driven by utilisation of the existing 25-hospital network — is the single biggest revenue driver.** A 10–20% move in IP volume would move total revenue more than any other single variable: volume already contributes ~12 of the ~15 points of ex-fertility growth, and management states that for the next 2–3 years **"at least 75% to 80% of our growth"** will come from existing hospitals via better utilisation, with the rest from new centres [`Q4/FY26 transcript, Q&A (p.8)`]. The headroom is concrete and quantified: blended centre utilisation is **58%**, with the largest cluster (West, 45% of revenue) running at just **50%** — i.e. the volume engine is running well below capacity, and incremental volume drops onto an already-built cost base [`Q4/FY26 deck, p.17`; `transcript, Q&A (p.6)`]. **Current direction: improving** — volume +12% in FY26, supported by 23 oncologists added in Q4, North Bangalore now live, the ₹50–100 Mn ramping cohort (half of revenue) growing +17% like-for-like, and underlying cancer-incidence demand that management calls structurally rising [`Q4/FY26 deck, p.3, p.18`; `transcript, Q&A (p.6, p.8)`].

The near-term risk to this driver is **self-inflicted plus geopolitical**: management is deliberately paring low-margin volume and absorbing softer Middle-East medical-value-travel, which together pulled Q4 FY26 reported growth down to +11% from the ~15% full-year pace — both factors management expects to "normalize over the next couple of quarters" [`transcript, Q&A (p.4)`]. The single variable most able to break the revenue setup over the next 12 months is therefore **the pace of utilisation/volume ramp at the West and ramping-cohort centres**, with government-scheme tariff/payment behaviour (one-third of revenue, outside HCG's control) the largest *exogenous* swing factor.
