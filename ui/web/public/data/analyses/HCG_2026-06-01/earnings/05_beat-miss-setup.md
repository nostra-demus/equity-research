# Beat / Miss Setup — HCG

*HealthCare Global Enterprises Limited (NSE: HCG, BSE: 539787) — pan-India oncology-focused hospital chain. INR (₹), consolidated. FY ends 31 March; FQ1 2027 = quarter ending 30 Jun 2026, results release scheduled Jul-30-2026 [04, §1, CIQ Estimates header]. This agent looks forward and decides the most likely path to a beat or a miss; it does not value the stock, rate it, build forecasts, or run sensitivities (those are other agents). All 4 upstream outputs (01 historical-financials, 02 revenue-drivers, 03 margin-drivers, 04 guidance-consensus) were read and inform this report.*

> **Data note:** Consensus IS available (CIQ Estimates, 9 analysts, data as of 2026-06-01) [04, §1], so the partial-data cap to "Unclear" does NOT apply. The binding limitation is that HCG's surprise history is fiscal-year only — there is no quarter-by-quarter consensus-surprise series, and FQ1 2027 quarter-level consensus exists only for revenue / EBITDA / GAAP EPS, not normalized EPS [04, §4, §6]. So the quarterly beat/miss read leans on FY-level pattern plus quarter-level revenue/EBITDA/GAAP-EPS estimates.

## 1. Next Quarter Context

The next print is **FQ1 2027 (quarter ending 30 Jun 2026), released Jul-30-2026** [04, §1]. Seasonality is immaterial for HCG — every quarter is ~24–26% of annual revenue, with Q1 the *smallest* at ~24% and a mild back-half tilt; no quarter breaches the 30%/20% thresholds [01, §5]. The FQ1 2027 quarter-level bar is **revenue ₹7,340.9 Mn (≈ +18% YoY on Q1FY26's ~₹6,131 Mn derived base; see note below), EBITDA ₹1,345.7 Mn, GAAP EPS ₹2.68** [04, §4]; the full-FY27 bar is revenue ₹29,105.9 Mn (+14.3% YoY), reported EBITDA ₹5,737.3 Mn (~19.7% margin, +141 bps), normalized EPS ₹8.72 [04, §3, §4]. On the lines management guides, the bar is "fair" — revenue sits at/just below the bottom of the guided 15–20% range and the margin bar (+141 bps) is marginally *above* the "~100 bps" management called achievable; the bottom line (EPS) has been cut hard and recently (FY27 normalized EPS −19% over six months) [04, §4, §7].

> **Reconciliation flag on the FQ1 revenue bar:** The CIQ FQ1 2027 revenue estimate of ₹7,340.9 Mn implies ~+20% YoY on the derived Q1FY26 revenue of ₹6,131 Mn [01, §3], well above HCG's ~14–15% group run-rate and the +11.5% Q4FY26 exit rate [01, §3]. Two readings are possible: (a) the quarter-level estimate is stale/optimistic relative to the FY27 total (FY27 revenue was cut −1.7% over 90 days while the FQ1 line stayed flat) [04, §4], or (b) it reflects a sharp re-acceleration. The conservative read, consistent with the FY-level cut and the Q4 exit rate, is that **the FQ1 revenue bar looks demanding** unless re-acceleration is genuine. This tension is the single most important feature of the near-term setup and is carried into Sections 2, 3, and 8.

## 6. Seasonality Read

Seasonality neither helps nor hurts the FQ1 2027 setup in any material way. Per the seasonality table in 01, each quarter is ~24–26% of annual revenue with no quarter breaching the 30%/20% thresholds; Q1 is the *smallest* quarter at ~24% (FY25 23.6%, FY26 24.1%) and Q3 is the softest on margin [01, §5]. The mild back-half tilt reflects intra-year bed/centre ramp rather than a true demand season, and Q1's Adjusted EBITDA margin has actually been mid-pack (17.7% FY25, 18.2% FY26) [01, §5]. The one seasonal nuance that could matter is that FQ1 has historically been a sequentially softer revenue quarter than the Q4 it follows (Q1FY26 ~₹6,131 Mn derived vs Q4FY25 ₹5,852 Mn — actually up YoY but the year's lowest share) [01, §3, §5], which makes the ~+20%-YoY-implied FQ1 consensus bar (Section 1) look even more demanding against a seasonally smaller base. Net: seasonality is **not a driver of the verdict**; it marginally amplifies the revenue-bar tension rather than creating it.

## 7. Historical Pattern

HCG has a clear, asymmetric pattern that the synthesizer should weight heavily: it **lands revenue and EBITDA within ~1.5% of consensus but misses normalized-EPS consensus in three of the last four fiscal years** (FY23 −19.2%, FY25 −24.5%, FY26 −8.9%; only FY24 beat, +16.9%) [04, §6]. The misses sit consistently *below* the operating line — the Street repeatedly over-models the bottom line (D&A, finance cost, tax, exceptionals), which is exactly where FY27 EPS estimates are again being cut (normalized EPS −19% over six months, net revision breadth −1 worsening to −2) [04, §4, §5, §6]. The important caveat is that this is a **fiscal-year** pattern; there is no quarter-by-quarter surprise series in the pool [04, §6], so it transfers to the FQ1 read directionally, not mechanically. The synthesizer should treat "operating line in-line, bottom line at risk" as the **base-rate prior** for HCG and require specific evidence to override it. The partial offset: the EPS bar has already been cut hard and recently, so some of the historical over-modeling has been worked out of the current number [04, §7].

## 8. Setup Verdict

**Setup is balanced — with the beat/miss skew differing by line: revenue/EBITDA modestly miss-tilted, bottom-line EPS miss-tilted, but the EPS bar is freshly reset lower.**

The **single most important factor** is that on the lines that matter, the bar is full, not soft: the FQ1 revenue estimate (₹7,340.9 Mn) implies ~+20% YoY against a +11.5% Q4 exit rate and the FY27 EBITDA-margin bar (+141 bps) sits above management's own "~100 bps achievable" framing [04, §3, §4; 01, §3] — so HCG has to *re-accelerate and out-margin its own guide* simply to meet consensus, which is a miss-tilted starting point rather than a beat-me setup. The **single biggest risk that could flip the verdict toward "favors beat"** is the normalization of Q4's two transient drags (deliberate low-margin paring + soft Middle East MVT) arriving on management's "next couple of quarters" timetable [02, §3, §7]: if volume snaps back toward 15% while the freshly-lowered EPS bar is cleared on rights-issue-driven lower interest and a clean (no new exceptional) quarter, the setup tips to a beat. Conversely, the biggest risk toward "favors miss" is the recurring below-EBITDA EPS pattern (M2) combined with a stale-high FQ1 revenue bar (M1).

## 9. Second-Quarter Look-Ahead

The setup for FQ2 2027 (quarter ending 30 Sep 2026, the quarter *after* next) looks **modestly better than FQ1** on three counts: (1) by FQ2 the Q4FY26 paring/MVT drags should be fully lapped on management's own "couple of quarters" timetable, removing the re-acceleration uncertainty that makes the FQ1 bar demanding [02, §3, §7]; (2) FQ2 is seasonally HCG's strongest *margin* quarter (Q2 Adjusted EBITDA margin 18.8% FY25, 19.1% FY26 — the year's high) [01, §5]; and (3) the Kolkata clinician onboarding (expected Q1 FY27) and Milann close should both be in the base by FQ2, lifting East case-mix/ARPP and cleaning group mix [02, §5; 03, §5]. The offsetting unknown is that FQ2 quarter-level consensus is not separately tabulated in the pool the way FQ1 is, so the *bar* for FQ2 cannot yet be assessed [04, §4] — the operating tailwind is visible, the consensus hurdle is not.

## 10. Pre-Mortem

If this setup read fails, the most likely reason is a **base-rate / quarter-transfer error**: treating HCG's fiscal-year "operating-line-fine, EPS-miss" pattern and the FY-level "fair bar" as if they map cleanly onto a single quarter when the actual FQ1 swing factor was something the FY frame masks — most plausibly (a) the FQ1 revenue estimate being a stale artifact that either snapped down (handing an easy "miss vs bar" that means nothing) or that consensus quietly re-based before the print, or (b) a government-scheme payment/tariff event in a scheme-heavy cluster (the AP/Odisha-type disruption that already hit Q3FY26) landing inside the quarter and moving one-third of revenue in a way no operating driver predicted [04, §4, §6; 02, §4; 01, §5]. The secondary failure mode is a guidance-driven reaction (Section 5) overwhelming an otherwise in-line print, which a numbers-focused beat/miss frame would under-weight.
