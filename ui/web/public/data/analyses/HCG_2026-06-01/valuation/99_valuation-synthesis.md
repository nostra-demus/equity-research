# Valuation Module — HCG (Synthesis)

## Abstract

HealthCare Global Enterprises (NSE: HCG) is modestly overvalued: at ₹646.15 the price sits ~11% above a base-case fair value of ~₹575 and ~32% above a defensible bear. The fair-value range is ≈₹400 (bear) / ~₹575 (base, ₹520–₹620) / ~₹735 (bull), driven by the relative/peer method, which shows HCG's −32% EV/EBITDA "discount" evaporating into a +9% EV/EBIT and +64% NTM P/E premium once weak profit conversion is priced. At ₹646.15 the market prices ~33% free-cash-flow growth for a decade — a rate HCG has never produced (FCF fell from ₹1,184M to ₹586M over FY23–FY26). There is no margin of safety (−12.4%); ~32% of downside exists to the bear. Verdict: modestly overvalued with explicit value-trap risk.

## 3. Reconciliation

The methods **agree on the conclusion and disagree on the magnitude**. Three of four value-producing methods (peers ~₹603, SOTP ~₹530, DCF cash-flow ~₹142) and the reverse-DCF all sit below the ₹646.15 price; only own-history's 6-quarter EV-multiple reversion (₹624–667) sits at the price.

The high-to-low spread across the *defensible* method outputs is large — ₹142 (DCF financeable-Gordon) to ₹714 (peer warranted high), a >5x range that trips the >40% cross-method flag. This disagreement is **methodological, not informational**, and `07` reconciled rather than averaged it: the DCF cash-flow low and the multiple-based highs answer two different questions — "what cash can this business distribute if it must fund its own growth at a ~5% ROIC" (answer: little, because ROIC < WACC) versus "what will a buyer pay for hospital EBITDA at 15–25x" (answer: a lot, regardless of ROIC). Both are legitimate; they measure different things.

The reconciled view: the **multiple-based cluster (₹530–₹645) is the base case**, the **DCF cash-flow low (~₹142–₹190) is the deep-stress floor / margin-of-safety anchor**, and nothing supports a value comfortably above the quote without assuming a multiple HCG's returns do not warrant. I trust the **peer/relative method most** for HCG because it is the only one that exposes the profit-conversion gap a naive EBITDA-only read would miss. The DCF's >40% internal divergence and terminal dominance are why valuation confidence is capped (Section 4), not why the verdict changes — the directional signal is consistent across every method.

## 5. Fair-Value Summary

Fair value is **≈₹400 (bear) / ~₹575 (base, ₹520–₹620) / ~₹735 (bull)**, and the single method that drives it is the **peer/relative read**: it shows the −32% EV/EBITDA discount to listed multi-specialty peers is warranted and flips to a +9% EV/EBIT and +64% NTM P/E premium the moment you reach profit, because HCG converts a rupee of EBITDA into EBIT and PAT far worse than peers (D&A ₹2,442M, finance cost ₹1,766M, net margin ~0.5% vs ~12% peer median). The current price of ₹646.15 implies ~33% free-cash-flow growth for a decade on a normalized base (~42% on literal FY26 FCF) — and the evidence says that is not achievable: HCG's FCF has *fallen* from ₹1,184M to ₹586M over FY23–FY26, ROIC has been 4.5–5.0% against a ~10.7–12.4% cost of capital in every one of the last six years, and the moat is narrow and economically unproven. The margin of safety is negative (−12.4%): the price sits above base fair value, with ~32% of downside to the central bear (~₹440) and a deep-stress DCF cash-flow floor near ₹142 if growth is judged permanently value-dilutive. **This is an explicit value-trap risk** — the cheap-looking EBITDA multiple is one the business does not deserve to re-rate from until ramping centres push returns above the cost of capital, which `business-model/09` flags as unproven and `earnings/04` shows the Street still cutting (FY27 EPS-N down −19% over six months).
