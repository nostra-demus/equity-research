## 2. Segment Multiples & Comparables

One segment, so this is a single-line sanity check: what is the consolidated oncology platform worth on a defensible **EV/EBITDA** multiple anchored to a **named comparable**? EBIT is available but the EV/EBIT comp is distorted by HCG's heavy depreciation versus peers (HCG EV/EBIT already sits at a *premium* to peers, +9% — see `03` §3), so EBITDA is the cleaner segment metric. The multiple is anchored to the single closest *single-specialty listed* hospital comp and cross-checked against the broader Indian multi-specialty median.

| Segment | Metric Used | Multiple Applied (range) | Named Comparable (primary) | Comparable's Multiple | Source |
|---|---|---:|---|---:|---|
| Oncology hospital network (= the whole company) | LTM Reported EV/EBITDA | **18x–24x** (base ~20x) | **Rainbow Children's Medicare** (NSE:RAINBOW) — listed *single-specialty* (paediatrics) hospital chain; the same focused single-specialty operating model as HCG, different specialty | **EV/EBITDA 26.4x** (LTM) | [03_relative-valuation-peers §2; Capital IQ comparable export, 2026-06-01 (USD; HCG embeds ₹637.25 — see note)] |

**Why this comparable fits, and why a discount to it.** Rainbow is the only *single-specialty* focused chain in HCG's listed comp set (the truest like-for-like oncology rivals — Apollo's oncology vertical and the private American Oncology Institute — have **no usable public multiple**, so they cannot anchor a number; flagged, not guessed) [08_competitive-map §2; 03 §1]. HCG warrants a **discount to Rainbow's 26.4x**, not parity, because HCG converts EBITDA to profit far worse: Rainbow's LTM EBITDA margin is 32.0% vs HCG 18.3%, and HCG's heavy depreciation/interest crushes the descent to EBIT and PAT (HCG EV/EBIT is already +9% *above* the peer median while EV/EBITDA is −32% below it) [03 §2–3]. The applied band **18x–24x** therefore brackets: the lower end ≈ HCG's own **NTM EV/EBITDA 19.0x** [03 §2, `multiples.xls`] and trailing 23.4x; the upper end stays *below* Rainbow's 26.4x and below the 8-India-peer median **34.9x** [03 §2]. Cross-check anchors (all web/vendor, labelled unverified, Capital IQ export 2026-06-01): India multi-specialty peer median EV/EBITDA **34.6x** (Max 42.0x, Medanta 34.3x, Fortis 34.9x, Narayana 26.0x) — HCG warrants a discount to these on inferior profit conversion. **Note:** every peer multiple is **web/vendor-sourced (Capital IQ), unverified**, and is on the export's ₹637.25 HCG basis (≈1.4% below the ₹646.15 anchor); this does not change the read (gaps are far larger than 1.4%).

## 4. Equity Bridge

Bridging the gross EV (the single-segment sanity check) to equity, on the anchor share count and net debt from `01`. Because this is one segment, there is **no separately-capitalized unallocated-corporate bucket** to subtract (corporate cost is already inside the consolidated EBITDA that produced the EV — there is no segment-sum that excluded it).

| Step | Low (₹ Mn) | Base (₹ Mn) | High (₹ Mn) |
|---|---:|---:|---:|
| Gross enterprise value (§3) | 83,844 | 93,160 | 111,792 |
| − Capitalized unallocated corporate costs | 0 | 0 | 0 |
| − Net debt (incl. leases, `01`) | (11,944) | (11,944) | (11,944) |
| − Minority / non-controlling interest (`01`) | (782) | (782) | (782) |
| + Equity-method investments (`01`) | +68 | +68 | +68 |
| − Conglomerate / holdco discount | 0 | 0 | 0 |
| **= Equity value (₹ Mn)** | **71,186** | **80,502** | **99,134** |
| ÷ Diluted shares (151.79M, `01`) | 151.79M | 151.79M | 151.79M |
| **= SOTP value per share (₹)** | **₹468.99** | **₹530.41** | **₹653.10** |
| vs current price ₹646.15 | −27.4% | −17.9% | +1.1% |

Bridge formula: Equity = Gross EV − net debt − minority + equity-method investments (no corporate-cost add-back, no holdco discount). Base: 93,160 − 11,944 − 782 + 68 = ₹80,502M ÷ 151.79M = **₹530.41/share**. Margin of safety vs price (sign convention: positive = price below fair value) = (530.41 − 646.15)/530.41 = **−21.8%** at base — i.e., the single-segment sanity check sits **below** the current price; price implies ~21.8% *above* this read.

**Conglomerate / holding-company discount: NONE applied — and none is warranted.** HCG is the opposite of a conglomerate: a single-segment, single-specialty operator with one reported segment and ~98.5% of EBITDA from one business line [03_segment-map §2]. A conglomerate discount exists to penalize value-destroying cross-subsidy and capital misallocation across *unrelated* businesses; there are no unrelated businesses here, so the discount premise does not apply. (If anything, the relevant *internal* drag is centre-maturity ROCE dispersion within the one segment — see §5 — which is captured in the consolidated EBITDA, not a holdco discount.)

**Net-debt sensitivity (flagged, not changed).** The bridge uses the CLAUDE.md-default net debt of ₹11,944M (leases in debt), consistent with the lease-in-debt EV. On the **company ex-lease net debt of ₹3,387M**, the equity values would be ₹8,557M higher at every scenario (e.g., base ₹89,059M ÷ 151.79M = ₹586.71/share) — but that would require also stripping leases from the EV and using a pre-Ind AS EBITDAR multiple, which the §2 comp band is not built on. The internally consistent read is the lease-in-debt one above; the ex-lease figure is shown only so the ~₹8.6bn lease-treatment swing (~₹56/share) is not hidden [`01` §5 net-debt definitions].

## 5. SOTP Read

**SOTP does not run as a breakup — HCG is a single-segment oncology operator (one reported Ind AS 108 segment; ~97.6% of revenue and ~98.5% of Adjusted EBITDA from the India cancer-care network), so SOTP collapses to the consolidated read; the dominant-segment EV/EBITDA sanity check yields a fair-value band of ₹469–₹653/share (base ~₹530), which sits *below* the ₹646.15 price at base and only reaches the price at the top (24x EBITDA) of the range.** The oncology hospital network *is* the value — there is no second segment being masked by a blended multiple, and the only non-oncology line (Milann fertility, ~2.4% of revenue, ~1.5% of EBITDA) is being divested at an EV of ₹632M (~0.6% of total EV), so even a clean carve-out adds nothing material; the genuine value-and-leakage story is **internal to the one segment** — 4 mature centres (>₹100M/month) at ~27% ROCE generate 44% of revenue, while 6 emerging centres (~6% of revenue) run at **−22% ROCE** [03_segment-map §1(ii)C, §2], a maturity dispersion that depresses *consolidated* EBITDA and is therefore already inside the multiple, not something a sum-of-the-parts can unlock. The blunt SOTP-specific finding: the EV/EBITDA "discount" to multi-specialty peers (−32%, `03` §3) is **not a hidden segment** waiting to be surfaced by a breakup — it is the market correctly paying less per rupee of HCG EBITDA because HCG converts EBITDA into EBIT and PAT far worse (heavy depreciation/interest), and no segmentation changes that; the decisive valuation levers live in the consolidated DCF (`04`), the own-history/peer multiple work (`02`/`03`), and the centre-maturity ramp, not in a sum-of-the-parts.
