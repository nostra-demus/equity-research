**Notes on this section.**
1. **EPCG is the single largest litigation/tax contingency (₹517.58 Mn = 75% of the quantified claims).** It is a recurring, structural exposure for a hospital that imports LINACs and PET-CT under duty exemption: if export obligations (typically earning foreign exchange via medical tourism) are not met, the avoided customs duty becomes payable. ## 5. Contingency Read

The largest off-balance-sheet exposures are the **Vizag (VHCRPL) put + forward acquisition obligations** — ₹2,035 Mn recognised in other financial liabilities and up to ~₹3,318 Mn at the commitment-note gross figures — and they sit entirely outside `01`'s borrowings line, so HCG's "1.4x" headline net leverage understates the cash claims on the group; ₹1,543 Mn of this has already crystallised (paid Apr-2026) and is live, not contingent. Outside the Vizag M&A obligations, the only material *litigation* contingency is the **₹517.58 Mn EPCG export-obligation duty** (75% of the ₹689 Mn claims-not-acknowledged), which is active but for which the Group expects to meet its obligations and has provided only ₹3.19 Mn — a low-probability-of-loss but unhedged tail tied to its imported oncology equipment. If the full maximum exposure (₹7,128 Mn, ≈50% of equity, before three unquantified items) crystallised simultaneously it would be a material but not solvency-ending event — roughly 1.5x FY26 EBITDA — and the realistic near-term cash drain (the ₹1,543 Mn Vizag tranche plus ₹1,975 Mn of committed capex) is the part that actually bites liquidity rather than the dormant tax tail.

**Partial data:** the FY26 statutory commitments-and-contingencies note is not in the pool — the quantified contingencies (₹689.31 Mn) and the put/forward/guarantee figures are at 31 Mar 2025 values, one year stale. Two parent obligations (the corporate guarantee for subsidiary bank-guarantee/cash-credit facilities, and an open-ended letter of support to 13 subsidiaries) and the residual 15% Vizag acquisition price are **disclosed but unquantified**. For a serial hospital acquirer with overseas subsidiaries and an EPCG/import-duty tail, undisclosed FY26 contingent movements cannot be ruled out. Per `MODULE_RULES.md` (off-balance-sheet exposures partly undisclosed for a levered, acquisitive name), solvency strength should be capped at 75 at the synthesis layer on this account.
