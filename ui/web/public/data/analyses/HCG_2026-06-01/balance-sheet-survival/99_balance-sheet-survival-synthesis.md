# Balance-Sheet-Survival Module — HCG (Synthesis)

## Abstract

HealthCare Global (HCG) carries moderate leverage that fell sharply in FY26 — net debt 2.57x EBITDA including capitalised leases (0.80x ex-lease), down from 4.13x, but the deleveraging was funded by a ₹4,250 Mn rights issue, not free cash flow. There is no bullet maturity: the debt is a flat ~₹1.0–1.3 Bn/yr secured term-loan ladder plus a long, self-amortising lease tail, and the dated term maturity inside 12 months (~₹1.3 Bn) is covered ~4x by ₹5,360.5 Mn of cash. Runway is ~15 months on maintenance capex; no lender covenant is disclosed, so headroom is Not assessable. Liquidity survives a 60% EBITDA fall if the on-demand overdraft rolls; an assumed coverage covenant would break first, near −24%. Verdict: Adequate.

## 1. Solvency Verdict

- **Verdict:** **Adequate**
- **Net leverage (net debt / EBITDA):** **2.57x incl. leases** (cash only), **0.80x ex-lease** (cash only), on FY26 Reported EBITDA ₹4,657.9 Mn; company-headline ~0.73x (ex-lease, margin-money-netted) and 1.43x (Vizag-adjusted). Gross debt incl. leases / EBITDA = **3.73x** [01 §5, §7].
- **Liquidity runway:** **~15 months** on a maintenance-capex basis (cash ₹5,360.5 Mn vs net burn ₹4,250.9 Mn/yr); ~31 months on cash and FCF-covered once the ~₹2.8 Bn on-demand overdraft is treated as rolled; ~10.8 months as the conservative outer bound (full overdraft repaid + all growth capex spent) [03 §3].
- **Maturity wall (% within 24 months):** **46.7%** of borrowings (FY25 undiscounted); within 12 months **34.2%** ex-lease — but **~57% of that is the rollable on-demand overdraft**, so the dated term maturity inside 12 months is **~14.6% (~₹1.3 Bn)**; 21.7% within 12 months including leases [02 §1–2].
- **Tightest covenant + headroom:** **Not assessable** — no lender financial maintenance covenant is disclosed in the pool. The only quantified anchor is management's self-imposed 2.5x ex-lease net-leverage ceiling, against which leverage sits ~68% below [04 §2–3].
- **Stress break point (EBITDA decline that breaks it):** **~−24%** — an *assumed* 2.0x lease-inclusive interest-coverage covenant would breach first (shallower than a normal recession), but it is a labeled assumption, not a disclosed covenant. Liquidity barely breaks on earnings alone (~−85% with the overdraft rolled); the next real cliff is the ~₹2.8 Bn on-demand overdraft being *called* (a counterparty action), which opens a ~₹0.6–1.3 Bn 12-month gap in a −40% to −60% downside [06 §3–4].
- Solvency strength /100: **62** (capped at 70 — no clean maturity schedule)
- Liquidity runway /100: **58** (capped at 60 — no undrawn-facility / revolver-availability disclosure)
- Refinancing risk /100 *(higher = WORSE — inverted)*: **40** (confidence Low — no year-by-year schedule at the FY26 date)
- Covenant headroom /100: **Not assessable** (no covenant disclosed)
- Downside resilience /100: **60**
- Data quality /100: **52** (from 00 — Partial)
- Overall usefulness /100: **72** (capped at 75 — no covenant disclosure)
- Biggest solvency risk (one line): **The combination of an undisclosed lender coverage covenant that could trip near a −24% EBITDA fall and an undisclosed-availability ~₹2.8 Bn on-demand overdraft that, if pulled, opens a near-term cash gap — both sit outside what the EBITDA haircut alone controls.**

## 3. Reconciliation

**Disagreement 1 — Credit rating (material).** The triage (`00`) lists credit ratings as **absent** ("No rating-agency report or rating reference in pool"). Agents `01`, `02`, and `04` independently cite **ICRA A+ (Stable) long-term / A1+ short-term**, sourced to the FY24-25 Annual Report, Corporate Governance Report, "O. Credit Ratings", p.~155, and MD&A p.174, with no revision during FY25. **Reconciled view:** a rating *reference* IS in the pool (the AR discloses the ICRA ratings); what is absent is a full rating-agency *report*. The investment-grade-equivalent domestic rating is accepted as the more-evidenced finding (three agents, specific page citations). It supports domestic refinancing access but does not lift the partial-data caps, because the triage's substantive point stands — no rating-agency report means rating *detail*, triggers, and outlook drivers cannot be examined. The "no credit ratings" cap (do not infer a rating) is therefore moot: a real, cited rating exists; it is noted, not inferred.

**Disagreement 2 — Headline leverage basis (presentation, not a number conflict).** Management and the decks lead with ex-lease, margin-money-netted leverage of ~0.73–1.43x; `01` computes 2.57x on a like-for-like lease-inclusive basis. These are not contradictory — they are different bases. Per the module's conservative default, the **lease-inclusive 2.57x is the anchor** used throughout this synthesis and by the stress agent; the ex-lease figure is shown alongside, not buried.

**Disagreement 3 — 12-month maturity figure (resolved by labeling).** `03` (which ran in parallel before `02` landed) used the full ₹3,112.2 Mn current-borrowings line as the conservative 12-month figure; `02` clarified that ~57% (~₹1.78 Bn) is the rollable on-demand overdraft, leaving ~₹1.3 Bn of genuinely dated term maturity. Both readings are carried explicitly: the ~₹1.3 Bn dated figure for the refinancing read, the full ₹3.1 Bn as the conservative liquidity-uses bound. No conflict — the conservative reading is the outer bound, not the central case.

No other material disagreements between specialists.

## 5. Survival Summary

HCG is moderately levered and the trend is improving in headline terms but for a one-time reason: net debt including leases fell from 4.13x to 2.57x EBITDA in FY26, but the fall was driven by a ₹4,250 Mn rights issue and some term-loan repayment, not free cash flow (FY26 FCF was only ~₹586 Mn against ₹2,885 Mn of capex) — it is balance-sheet repair, not organic deleveraging. The near-term maturity wall is **self-funded**: there is no bullet, the dated term maturity inside 12 months is ~₹1.3 Bn against ₹5,360.5 Mn of cash (~4x cover), refinancing cost actually steps *down* at the current 5.25% repo, and the ICRA A+/A1+ rating supports domestic access — the only refinancing-dependent item is the roll of the ~₹2.8 Bn on-demand overdraft, whose committed availability is undisclosed. The liquidity runway is ~15 months on maintenance capex and effectively open-ended (FCF-covered, ~31 months on cash) if that overdraft rolls; the tightest covenant cannot be measured because none is disclosed, so the assessment leans on management's self-imposed 2.5x ex-lease ceiling (~68% headroom) and on labeled-assumption market covenants. On the stress test, the structure first fails — *if* a typical bilateral 2.0x lease-inclusive coverage covenant exists — at only ~−24% EBITDA, shallower than a normal recession, requiring a waiver but no equity or asset sale to survive; on liquidity alone, a normal −30%/−40% recession is comfortably survivable (a ₹1.5–1.9 Bn 12-month surplus) and even a −60% year leaves ~14 months of cash, provided the secured overdraft is not called at the same time.

## 6A. Survival Playbook (non-speculative levers)

Levers supported by evidence:
- **Equity issuance — demonstrated.** A ₹4,250 Mn rights issue was completed in Q4 FY26 (oversubscribed 1.3x); KKR holds sole control (since 30 May 2025) and runs an internal 2.5x ex-lease leverage ceiling, so a controlling sponsor with a deleveraging discipline is in place [01 §6; 06 §4].
- **Asset-sale program — signed.** The Milann fertility-clinic divestment is signed: EV ₹632 Mn, ~₹282 Mn upfront (75% of ₹376 Mn equity consideration), close expected Q1 FY27, balance ₹94 Mn over 18 months. **Related-party flag:** the buyer (Inviga Healthcare Fund I) is controlled by promoter/Non-Exec Chairman Dr. B.S. Ajaikumar [02 §4].
- **Capex flexibility — large and demonstrated.** ~₹1,696 Mn/yr of the ₹2,885 Mn FY26 capex is discretionary growth capex that can be paused; maintenance capex is only ₹1,189 Mn. Pausing growth capex lifts the runway from ~10.8 to ~15+ months and is the single largest internal lever [03 §2–3; 06 §4].
- **Dividend/buyback suspension — not applicable as a lever.** No dividend has ever been paid (negative consolidated retained earnings of ₹(4,092) Mn legally preclude it), so there is no distribution to cut — but equally no distribution leakage to manage [01 §6A; 03 §2].
- **Covenant-amendment likelihood — not evidenced.** No lender covenant is disclosed, so prior waiver/amendment history cannot be assessed; this lever is unproven from the pool [04 §2].

## 8. Simple Summary

- **Debt:** ₹9,104 Mn of borrowings (₹17,354 Mn including leases); net of ₹5,360 Mn cash, net debt is ₹3,744 Mn ex-lease / ₹11,993 Mn incl. leases — leverage 0.80x ex-lease, **2.57x including leases**. ~96% floating, largely unhedged, ~8% cost.

- **Liquidity runway:** ~15 months on maintenance capex; ~31 months / cash-flow-covered if the overdraft rolls.
- **Tightest covenant:** **unknown — no lender covenant is disclosed.** The only yardstick is management's self-imposed 2.5x leverage limit, which it sits ~68% below.

- **Rating / key data:** **ICRA A+ (Stable) / A1+** disclosed in the FY24-25 AR (investment-grade-equivalent, domestic). Key gaps: lender covenants, year-by-year maturity table, committed-facility availability, full rating report, FY26 contingencies note.
