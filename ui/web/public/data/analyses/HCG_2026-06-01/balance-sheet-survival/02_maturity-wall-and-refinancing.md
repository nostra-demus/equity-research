**Web-sourced benchmarks (labelled).** RBI repo rate held at **5.25%** (neutral stance, FY2026-27 first MPC) [Web: tradingeconomics.com / RBI, 2026 (indicative, unverified)]. India **364-day T-bill cut-off ~5.769%** on 13 May 2026 [Web: CEIC, 2026-05-13 (indicative, unverified)]. **1Y–2Y G-Sec ~6.30% YTM** [Web: CCIL Tenorwise Indicative Yields, 2026-06-01 (indicative, unverified)]. 10-yr G-Sec ~7.0% late May 2026 [Web: worldgovernmentbonds.com / tradingeconomics.com, 2026-05-29 (indicative, unverified)].

**Why the step-up is negative.** HCG's debt is ~96% floating and **already reprices automatically** with these benchmarks — it is not a stack of fixed-rate bullets that must be refinanced at a new, higher coupon. Much of the term-loan book was struck when the repo rate was ~6.5% (FY24-25); the repo has since fallen to **5.25%**, so the floating cost mechanically declines, and a *new* term loan refinancing a maturing one would price at roughly the same spread on a **lower** benchmark. Management corroborates: after the rights issue "and… we have repaid some long-term debts, we do expect some… reduction in the interest cost" against the current ~8% [Q4/FY26 transcript, Q&A, 20 May 2026]. The risk direction is therefore **a rate increase, not refinancing at a higher coupon** — see §4.

**Rate sensitivity.** With ~96% floating, a rate move flows almost fully through to interest. The FY25 AR's own +1% sensitivity on the term-loan portion (₹6,791.84 Mn variable long-term borrowings) is **−₹67.92 Mn pre-tax** [FY24-25 AR, Note 40(b)(ii), p.369]. **Caveat:** that disclosed sensitivity covers only the ₹6,791.84 Mn term loans and **excludes the ~₹1,783–2,798 Mn floating overdraft**, so the true full-book +100 bps impact is larger — roughly **−₹85 to −₹90 Mn pre-tax** on ~₹8.6–8.9 Bn of floating borrowings (Inference, not from filings; the AR sensitivity understates by leaving the overdraft out). A +200 bps shock on the full floating book is therefore on the order of **−₹170 to −₹180 Mn** pre-tax, ~3.6–3.9% of FY26 reported EBITDA (handed to agents 04/06). No interest-rate-swap/hedge disclosure was located in the pool — floating exposure appears largely **unhedged** [00_solvency-data-triage, §3].

## 5. Refinancing Read

The wall is **not a bullet** — it is a flat ~₹1.0–1.3 Bn/yr secured term-loan ladder over 4–10 years plus a long-dated, self-amortising Ind AS 116 lease tail (₹9.5 Bn undiscounted beyond year 4), and the headline "34% due within 12 months" shrinks to a **~₹1.3 Bn dated term maturity** once the ~₹1.78 Bn rollable on-demand overdraft is stripped out; that ₹1.3 Bn is covered ~4x by the ₹5.36 Bn cash pile, so timing risk is low. On cost, because ~96% of the debt is floating and the RBI repo rate has fallen to 5.25% (from ~6.5% when most loans were struck), refinancing/repricing today runs at roughly **7.1–8.1%** versus the current ~8% all-in — an **indicative ≈ −50 bps step-DOWN**, consistent with management's guidance for "some reduction in interest cost." The single biggest refinancing risk is therefore **not the term wall or the cost** but **(a) a rate reversal hitting the ~96% unhedged floating book (a +200 bps shock ≈ −₹170–180 Mn pre-tax, ~3.6–3.9% of EBITDA) and (b) a bank withdrawing the ~₹1.78 Bn on-demand overdraft whose committed availability is undisclosed.**

**Survival under "market closure" (no new unsecured issuance) over the next 12 months:** **Yes, on the best available evidence.** HCG carries ₹5,360.5 Mn cash against ~₹1.3 Bn of dated term maturities and generates ~₹2.3 Bn/yr of maintenance-capex-adjusted operating cash; it has no bonds, no ECB, and no near-term unsecured bullet to refinance, so it does not depend on new unsecured issuance to clear the 12-month wall. The one assumption required: the **on-demand bank overdraft (~₹1.78 Bn) is not called** during the closure — committed availability is undisclosed, so this is labelled an assumption, not a proven fact [00_solvency-data-triage, §3]. The deferred Vizag consideration (₹1,543 Mn) was already paid in Apr-2026 and is outside this window; residual Vizag put/forward liabilities (~₹0.6–1.3 Bn) and the small Milann inflow (~₹282 Mn) are handed to agents 03/05.

**Inverted-score flag:** refinancing-risk severity is an inverted measure (higher = worse). The qualitative read here is **low** for the term wall and cost, **moderate** for the unhedged floating-rate repricing and the undisclosed overdraft-availability exposure. **Confidence is capped at Low** per the partial-data rule (no year-by-year maturity schedule at the FY26 canonical date; the granular table is FY25, one year stale) [MODULE_RULES partial-data table; 00_solvency-data-triage §5].
