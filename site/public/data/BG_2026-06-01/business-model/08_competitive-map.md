# Competitive Map — BG

Upstream inputs present: business-identity (`02_business-identity.md`) and segment-map (`03_segment-map.md`) both read. No dependency gaps.

## 1. Dominant Segment

Soybean Processing and Refining — 51.6% of FY2025 net sales ($36,313M) and 52.6% of reportable Segment EBIT ($1,225M of $2,329M), the value driver on both measures [FY25 10-K, Note 26, FY2025 table; segment-map upstream §2]. The dominant single swing factor for the whole company is the soybean crush spread.

## 2. Named Competitors

Bunge names its key competitors by segment in the audited 10-K. For the Soybean Processing and Refining segment, the listed key competitors are: "Archer Daniels Midland Co. ('ADM'), Cargill Incorporated ('Cargill'), Louis Dreyfus Company B.V. ('Louis Dreyfus'), Wilmar International Limited ('Wilmar'), and COFCO International, as well as a variety of regional players in each region" [FY25 10-K, Item 1, Soybean Processing and Refining segment, p.3 / lines 307–309]. The same four names (ADM, Cargill, Louis Dreyfus, Wilmar) recur as key competitors across the Softseed and grain segments [FY25 10-K, lines 336, 435].

The three most credible rivals in the dominant soybean-crush/oilseed segment are ADM, Cargill, and Louis Dreyfus — together with Bunge, these are the "ABCD" agribusiness traders that the 10-K names first. Wilmar (Asia-Pacific palm/oilseed weight) and COFCO International (China state-owned) are also named but are profiled below the top three as secondary rivals.

### Competitor A — Archer Daniels Midland Co. (ADM)

- **Ticker / listing:** NYSE: ADM (public).
- **Where they compete:** Global oilseed processing and refining (soybean crush, refined vegetable oils, protein meal) — head-to-head in Bunge's dominant segment; also grain merchandising and specialty/refined oils. Named as a key competitor in all four of Bunge's segments [FY25 10-K, lines 307, 336, 382, 435].
- **Scale:** FY2025 (year-ended Dec 31, 2025) revenue ~$80.3bn [Web: ADM FY2025 10-K via macrotrends.net / stockanalysis.com, retrieved 2026-06-01 (unverified)]. Roughly 1.1x Bunge's $70,329M FY2025 net sales — closest peer in scale.
- **Source named in:** [FY25 10-K, Item 1, Soybean Processing and Refining segment, p.3 / line 307].
- **One-line read:** The most directly comparable public oilseed processor-trader; the natural margin and capital-efficiency benchmark for the dominant segment.

### Competitor B — Cargill Incorporated

- **Ticker / listing:** Private (US, family/employee-owned); not listed.
- **Where they compete:** Global oilseed crush, grain origination and merchandising, oils and ingredients, plus meat/protein — overlaps Bunge's soybean and grain segments. Named as a key competitor in all four of Bunge's segments [FY25 10-K, lines 307, 336, 382, 435].
- **Scale:** FY2025 (year-ended ~May 2025) revenue ~$154bn, the largest of the named peers and a reported four-year low [Web: St. Star Tribune / Bloomberg / Cargill 2025 Annual Report, retrieved 2026-06-01 (unverified)]. About 2.2x Bunge's $70,329M net sales — larger but within the ~5x credibility band, with the caveat that Cargill's revenue includes large meat/protein lines Bunge does not run, so the figure overstates direct oilseed overlap.
- **Source named in:** [FY25 10-K, Item 1, Soybean Processing and Refining segment, p.3 / line 307].
- **One-line read:** The largest private rival; broader than Bunge (meat/protein), so a partial — not pure — comparable for the crush segment, and it discloses no segment economics for clean comparison.

### Competitor C — Louis Dreyfus Company B.V.

- **Ticker / listing:** Private (Netherlands-domiciled; majority owned by the Louis-Dreyfus family); not listed. Files limited public financials.
- **Where they compete:** Global oilseed crush, grains, and merchandising; the fourth "ABCD" trader. Named as a key competitor in Bunge's soybean, softseed and grain segments [FY25 10-K, lines 307, 336, 435].
- **Scale:** FY2025 (year-ended Dec 31, 2025) net sales $53.2bn; EBITDA $1.83bn; volumes +10.6% y/y [Web: ldc.com press release "Resilient 2025 Results," published 2026-03-18, retrieved 2026-06-01 (unverified)]. About 0.76x Bunge's $70,329M net sales — smallest of the top three, comparable scale.
- **Source named in:** [FY25 10-K, Item 1, Soybean Processing and Refining segment, p.3 / line 307].
- **One-line read:** The closest pure-play oilseed/grain-trader comparable by business mix, smaller than Bunge and more weighted to merchandising.

Secondary named rivals (below the top three): Wilmar International (SGX-listed; Asia-Pacific palm/oilseed-weighted) and COFCO International (private subsidiary of China's state-owned COFCO; named in the soybean and grain segments) [FY25 10-K, lines 308, 435].

## 3. Competitive Position

Position vs peers: not disclosed. Bunge discloses no market-share percentage for itself or any competitor in any segment; it describes itself only qualitatively as "a leading global oilseed processor and producer of vegetable oils and protein meal, based on processing capacity and volume" [FY25 10-K, Item 1, p.3 / lines 256–257]. The only available volume proxy is contaminated by M&A: FY2025/Q1 FY2026 processed and merchandised volumes rose, but management attributes the increase to added Viterra capacity in Argentina, Canada and Europe — i.e., acquired capacity, not organic share gain [Q1 FY26 call, prepared remarks, lines 130, 147; segment-map upstream §2]. A separate capacity-utilization proxy exists but is renewable-diesel-specific, not a share metric: management estimated "25% more capacity to run harder" in US crush, ~1.2bn incremental gallons of feedstock supply [Q1 FY26 call, Q&A, lines 290–294]. Net: no clean read on whether Bunge is gaining or losing share organically.

## 4. Competitive Shape

Global oligopoly at the top, with a long tail of regional players. The structure is the long-recognized "ABCD" complex — ADM, Bunge, Cargill, Louis Dreyfus — that Bunge's own 10-K names first as its key competitors across every segment, now a tighter field because Bunge absorbed Viterra (the fifth major trader) in July 2025, removing one independent competitor [FY25 10-K, lines 307, 455–457; segment-map upstream note]. Bunge characterizes the industry as "intensely competitive... with numerous global and regional competitors," and flags that competitors have added oilseed processing/refining capacity and that traditional petroleum companies have entered refining for renewable-diesel feedstock — i.e., new entrants pressuring the oil side [FY25 10-K, Item 1A risk factor "We face intense competition," lines 1027–1038]. Supporting fact for the oligopoly read: the four ABCD traders (with revenues ~$53bn–$154bn each) are the named price-setters, but no HHI and no top-N share percentage is disclosed in the data pool to quantify concentration.

## 5. Caveat

Credible competitors are identified — the three named here (ADM, Cargill, Louis Dreyfus) come directly from the audited 10-K's segment-level "Key Competitors" disclosure (Level 5), so none is invented. The gaps are: (1) no market-share data for Bunge or any peer anywhere in the pool, so the gaining/holding/losing call cannot be made and is stated as "not disclosed"; (2) the only volume growth proxy reflects the Viterra acquisition, not organic competition; and (3) peer scale figures (ADM, Cargill, Louis Dreyfus revenue) are dated web sources labelled unverified — the data pool contains no peer financials or Capital IQ peer export. What would resolve it: a third-party crush-capacity-share dataset (e.g., by region and by processor) or a Capital IQ/analyst peer export with segment revenue and crush volume, to convert the qualitative "leading processor" claim into a quantified share and a directional trend.
