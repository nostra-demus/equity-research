# Segment Map — BG

Bunge Global SA (NYSE: BG). Reporting currency US$. Fiscal year ends December 31. All figures below are FY2025 (year ended December 31, 2025) from the 2025 Form 10-K / Annual Report unless stated otherwise.

Note on structure change: Effective Q3 2025, Bunge changed its reportable segments to align with the value-chain operating structure created by its acquisition of Viterra. It now reports four segments. The former Sugar and Bioenergy segment was discontinued as a separate segment effective January 1, 2025 and its historical results were reclassified into Corporate and Other [FY25 10-K, Note 26 — Segment Information; FY25 10-K, MD&A "Factors Affecting Operating Results", p.36 / lines 2153–2169]. Prior-year segment data in the table below has been recast to the new four-segment structure.

## 2. Dominant Segment

**Soybean Processing and Refining is the dominant segment on both measures.** It is the largest by profit — $1,225M of $2,329M total reportable Segment EBIT (52.6%) — and the largest by revenue — $36,313M of $70,329M net sales (51.6%) [FY25 10-K, Note 26, FY2025 table, lines 8360–8375; reconciliation p.37, lines 2375–2379]. Revenue and profit point to the same segment, so there is no conflict to resolve. The dominance held in FY2024 too (Soybean = $872M of $2,159M reportable Segment EBIT, 40%) and was reinforced in FY2025 by the Viterra acquisition and improved South American results [FY25 10-K, MD&A, lines 2437–2442].

This is not a single-segment business. The top segment is ~52% of EBIT, below the 85% single-segment threshold, and the second and third segments (Grain Merchandising and Milling at 20.0%, Softseed at 22.4% of reportable EBIT) are individually material. But the four segments are economically similar — all are commodity oilseed/grain origination, processing, refining and merchandising businesses with thin EBIT margins (2.5%–4.6%). The company is effectively one low-margin agribusiness commodity-processing model expressed across four crop/value-chain slices, not four distinct economic engines. The single largest swing factor for the whole company is the soybean crush spread.

A structural caveat: the four reportable segments produce $2,329M of EBIT, but Corporate and Other consumed −$796M in FY2025 (up from −$367M in FY2024), so total company EBIT was only $1,533M [FY25 10-K, p.37, lines 2381–2383]. The Soybean segment's 52.6% "profit share" therefore overstates its share of company-wide profit; measured against total EBIT including the corporate drag, Soybean's $1,225M is ~80% of the $1,533M total. Either way, Soybean is unambiguously the value driver.

## 3. Segment Disclosure Quality

Disclosure quality is mixed because of a recent, material redefinition. Segment definitions are NOT consistent year-over-year: Bunge changed its reportable segments in Q3 2025 (from a prior structure that included Agribusiness and a Refined & Specialty Oils orientation, plus a separate Sugar and Bioenergy segment) to the current four value-chain segments, and stopped reporting Sugar and Bioenergy as a segment effective January 1, 2025 [FY25 10-K, lines 2153–2169, 4993–4996]. Prior-year (2024, 2023) figures have been recast to the new four segments, which makes the three-year comparison usable, but any comparison to filings issued before mid-2025 will not line up. The "Other" / Corporate bucket is significant on profit even though it is trivial on revenue: Corporate and Other carries only $3M of net sales (0.0%) but a −$796M EBIT drag (more than half the size of total company EBIT) and $2,625M of assets (5.9%) [FY25 10-K, Note 26, lines 8375, 8380]. That bucket now also absorbs the discontinued Sugar and Bioenergy results and Viterra integration costs, reducing transparency into "clean" corporate overhead. On the positive side, disclosure is granular where it matters: Bunge discloses Segment EBIT, net sales, total assets, capital expenditures, depreciation, income from affiliates, and operating volumes for each of the four segments, plus a full EBIT-to-net-income reconciliation and ASC 815 vs ASC 606 revenue disaggregation [FY25 10-K, Note 26, lines 8360–8536]. So profit IS disclosed at segment level, not revenue only. Net: the segment data is detailed and reconciles cleanly, but the segment boundaries are one year old, the Sugar/Bioenergy exit muddies the Corporate bucket, and inter-segment revenue is large (FY25 eliminations of ~$3.6bn in FY2024; the FY2025 elimination line is shown but inter-segment detail is folded into the consolidated reconciliation) — downstream agents (unit-economics, competitive-map) should treat the four segments as one commodity-processing complex and watch the Corporate drag separately.
