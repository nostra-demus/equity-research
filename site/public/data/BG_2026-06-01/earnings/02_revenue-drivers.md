# Revenue Drivers — BG

**Company:** Bunge Global SA (NYSE: BG). **Reporting currency:** US$ millions unless noted. **Fiscal year:** ends December 31. **FY0 = FY2025** (10-K). **Latest quarter Q0 = Q1 2026** (10-Q, quarter ended Mar 31, 2026). Upstream `01_historical-financials.md` and `04_guidance-consensus.md` present; business-model `03_segment-map.md` and `10_external-dependency.md` present and read.

**Three structural breaks govern every revenue comparison below — flagged once, applied throughout (carried from upstream):**
1. **Viterra closed mid-2025** (shares issued July 2, 2025; $4,201M cash + 65.6M shares) [FY25 10-K, Note 2]. It roughly doubled quarterly revenue from ~$12–13bn to ~$22–24bn from Q3 2025. **Almost every segment's FY2025 and Q1 2026 net-sales increase is "primarily due to Net sales contributions from the Acquisition of Viterra"** — this is inorganic, not organic demand, and is treated as such everywhere below.
2. **Segment redefinition (Q3 2025)** to four value-chain segments; Sugar & Bioenergy exited and folded into Corporate & Other.
3. **Revenue is gross of pass-through commodity cost.** Bunge's net sales = volume × price of physical commodities it moves; >90% of net sales is COGS (FY25 gross margin 4.85%). Revenue therefore moves violently with both volume AND flat commodity price, but **revenue is not the value metric — segment EBIT (the spread) is.** A revenue spike from a higher soybean flat price can coincide with flat or lower EBIT.

**Segment-name reconciliation:** the FY25 10-K calls the specialty segment "Other Oilseeds Processing and Refining"; the Q1 2026 10-Q and earnings release call the same segment "Tropical Oils and Specialty Ingredients" (identical volumes, Loders JV, tropical-oils content). Treated as one segment below.

## 1. Segment Decomposition Status

**Segment decomposition applied — 4 reportable segments from the business-model module** (`03_segment-map.md`), plus a Corporate & Other bucket that carries no external revenue ($3M) but a large EBIT drag. This is **not** a single-segment business: the largest segment (Soybean Processing and Refining) is 51.6% of net sales and 52.6% of reportable Segment EBIT — below the 85% single-segment threshold. However, per the segment-map, the four segments are "economically similar… one low-margin agribusiness commodity-processing model expressed across four crop/value-chain slices," so the consolidated driver tree and the segment trees point at the same underlying variables. Segment-level net sales, volumes (in metric tons), and Segment EBIT are disclosed for all four segments in both the 10-K and the 10-Q, so decomposition is fully supported.

## 2. Revenue Driver Tree

| Business Type | Revenue Formula |
|---|---|
| Commodity producer / processor | Production (or merchandised) volume × realized commodity price |
| Multi-segment | Sum of segment volume × price |

**Company-specific revenue formula:**

> **Net sales ≈ Σ (segment processed/merchandised volume in metric tons × realized commodity price per ton)** across Soybean, Softseed, Tropical Oils/Other Oilseeds, and Grain — where volume is driven by origination/crush footprint (now Viterra-expanded) and crop sizes, and realized price tracks the flat price of soybeans, soy/canola/sun oil and meal, corn, and wheat. Mix (which segment, which region, processing vs lower-priced merchandising) and FX translation are secondary. **Earnings (segment EBIT) are the crush/merchandising spread, not net sales — revenue can rise on price or acquisition while the spread does not.**

## 5. Revenue Drivers By Segment

Revenue shares are FY2025. Soybean and Softseed are the two segments management forecasts higher for FY26; Grain and Tropical Oils are forecast lower.

### Soybean Processing and Refining (51.6% of net sales)
FY25 net sales $36,313M (+14% YoY); Q1'26 $9,552M (+43% YoY). Adjusted Segment EBIT Q1'26 $377M vs $241M (GAAP $209M, distorted by +$168M MtM timing). Drivers: soybeans processed Q1'26 10,757kt (+33%); merchandised 5,133kt (+130%); realized price up YoY short-term; refined oil flat; region mix (SA strong, Europe/Asia weak). *FY2025: processed+merchandised volume +17.8% vs net sales +13.7% ⇒ price/mix ~−4pp drag, Viterra volume the offsetting lift (inference from disclosed volumes + MD&A).* 

### Grain Merchandising and Milling (25.8% of net sales)
FY25 net sales $18,128M (+80% YoY); Q1'26 $7,177M (+201% YoY). But Q1'26 adjusted Segment EBIT only $44M vs $60M (GAAP −$76M, +$114M MtM). **The segment where revenue and earnings diverge most.** Grain volume Q1'26 26,558kt (+212%); realized grain price deteriorating; corn-milling divestiture (Q2 2025) removes recurring sales + $155M one-off gain; ocean-freight revenue down on bunker-fuel spike.

### Softseed Processing and Refining (16.0% of net sales)
FY25 net sales $11,252M (+62% YoY); Q1'26 $3,904M (+158% YoY). Adjusted Segment EBIT Q1'26 $195M vs $82M (GAAP $76M, +$119M MtM). Softseeds processed Q1'26 3,281kt (+50%); merchandised 1,406kt (+1,380% on Viterra); canola/sun oil price up ("limited supply from Black Sea"); European sun crop size a swing factor.

### Tropical Oils and Specialty Ingredients / Other Oilseeds (6.6% of net sales)
FY25 net sales $4,633M (+12% YoY); Q1'26 $1,228M (+13% YoY). Adjusted Segment EBIT Q1'26 $45M vs $23M (GAAP $110M, −$65M MtM). Smallest segment; FY26 guided **lower**. Price-led +13% on Iran-conflict oil + mandates; volume +3%; cocoa-butter-equivalent demand/price deteriorating.

**Corporate & Other:** $3M net sales (0.0%) — immaterial to revenue; relevant to EBIT (−$796M FY25 drag), out of scope here.

## 7. The Single Biggest Revenue Driver

**The single biggest driver of Bunge's revenue is volume × flat commodity price in the Soybean Processing and Refining segment, and the variable most likely to move it next is biofuel mandate demand (the EPA RVO and equivalent mandates in Brazil, Indonesia, and the EU).** Soybean is 51.6% of net sales and ~52.6% of reportable segment EBIT. A 10–20% move in mandated/blended biofuel volumes pulls soy-oil (and canola/sun-oil) demand and the flat price with it — the exact mechanism that lifted FY2026 adjusted-EPS guidance from $7.50–8.00 to $9.00–9.50 in two months after the EPA RVO decision. Current direction is **improving** (soy-oil stocks to "draw down… Q3 and especially Q4"; biofuel the "bright spot"). Caveat: this driver moves *revenue and the crush spread together*, but the larger trailing-year revenue mover was the **Viterra acquisition** (inorganic, lapping from Q3 2026). The biggest *swing risk* is the heavily inverted forward curve and "limited" second-half visibility, plus El Niño and US–China trade as unresolved switches [Q1 2026 transcript, Q&A].
