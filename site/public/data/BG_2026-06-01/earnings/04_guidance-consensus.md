The base rate for an adjusted-EPS beat is high; the base rate for a clean revenue beat is roughly a coin flip.

## 7. Bar Assessment

**Bar is fair.**

Rationale: management raised FY2026 adjusted-EPS guidance to $9.00–$9.50 on 2026-04-29 — a ~+20% lift to the midpoint (from $7.75) — and the Street has already moved up to meet it. Current consensus of $9.43 sits just above the $9.25 midpoint and inside the range, every FY2026 adjusted-EPS revision in the last month was upward (6 up / 0 down), and FY2026 EBITDA/EBIT estimates rose ~9–12%. The easy part of the setup — a guide issued ~10% above the prevailing $8.41 Street number — has been arbitraged away as analysts caught up, so the level is no longer obviously beatable; but estimate momentum is firmly positive, breadth is one-sided up, and the company has beaten adjusted EPS five quarters running, which keeps miss risk contained. The bar is neither set low (consensus is not sitting below the guide) nor stretched above the guide, hence fair rather than low or high.

Two qualifications keep this from being "low": (1) the upgrade is margin/biofuel-led, not volume-led — FY2026 revenue estimates were trimmed (4 down / 1 up) even as EPS rose, so the beat thesis rests on crush and refining margins holding, which management itself called uncertain with "heavily inverted" forward curves; (2) the back-half is where the risk sits — FQ3/FQ4 2026 adjusted-EPS breadth is net negative and management flagged low H2 visibility, grain and tropicals guided down, and rising tax and interest costs from Q2. The near-term quarter (FQ2 2026, $1.93 consensus) is well-supported and broadly beatable on the recent beat cadence; the full-year hinges on a second half the Street is already nudging lower.
