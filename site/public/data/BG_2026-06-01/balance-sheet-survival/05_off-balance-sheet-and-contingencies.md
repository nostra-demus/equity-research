# Off-Balance-Sheet & Contingencies — BG

**Company:** Bunge Global SA (NYSE: BG). **Reporting currency:** US$ millions unless noted. **Latest annual:** FY2025 10-K (year ended Dec 31, 2025); **latest interim:** Q1 2026 10-Q (quarter ended Mar 31, 2026). Probability language below is taken verbatim from Bunge's own disclosures (probable / reasonably possible / remote per the loss-contingency framework it states it applies). Items already counted in Agent 01's debt stack are flagged so they are not double-counted here. No solvency verdict is rendered — that is the synthesizer's job; this agent sizes the exposures and their ratios only.

**One-line frame:** Bunge's biggest off-balance-sheet item is **not** a hidden funding line — the securitization SPE and finance leases are already consolidated/on the debt line. The exposures that sit genuinely outside the debt stack are (1) **$2,151M of standby letters of credit and surety bonds** backing trade/performance, (2) **~$1.6bn of divestiture indemnity ceilings** (BP Bunge Bioenergia + Russia) against ~$125M recorded, and (3) **Brazilian indirect-tax claims** (ICMS + PIS/COFINS) where the **claimed amount is $760M at Q1 2026 but only a fraction is provisioned** because Bunge judges most not-yet-probable. Pension is near-fully funded ($3M net) and is not an overhang.

**Notes.**

If the program were withdrawn or capacity cut, working-capital funding would shift back onto the balance sheet — a liquidity-structure risk Agent 03 owns, flagged here only as a structural dependency, not a contingent payment.
- **The $1,802M of purchase commitments is the largest genuinely off-balance-sheet contractual obligation** and is mostly executory operating spend (freight, power, plant construction, take-or-pay). It is not debt and is partly demand-matched, but the $800M "other take-or-pay / throughput" bucket is a fixed-volume obligation that does not flex down in a demand collapse — relevant to Agent 06's working-capital/throughput stress.

**Notes.**
- **The Brazil indirect-tax claims are the most important live contingency.** At Q1 2026 the authorities have issued **$760M of ICMS + PIS/COFINS claims** ($166M + $594M), of which only the portion Bunge deems probable is reserved (the total non-income-tax provision is $92M). The gap — roughly **$668M of claimed-but-not-provisioned** indirect tax — is the largest single under-reserved exposure, and the PIS/COFINS piece **rose $104M in one quarter** [Q1 10-Q, Note 15, lines 1477–1478]. Brazilian tax disputes are slow (claims reference years "1990 to present") and the company contests them, but this is an active, growing exposure, not a dormant one. Some cash is already tied up: deposits are held in **judicial escrow** pending resolution, earning the Selic rate [FY25 10-K, line 6486].
- **Divestiture indemnities carry a large ceiling vs a small reserve.** BP Bunge Bioenergia alone is **$95M recorded against $1,357M max** (a 14x gap), with **no expiration on existing claims**. The Q1 10-Q aggregates all divestiture indemnities to **$125M recorded vs $1.6bn maximum** (a ~13x gap). Bunge measures these at fair value under ASC 460/450, so the recorded figure reflects its probability-weighted estimate; the $1.6bn is the disclosure-rule "full payment regardless of probability" ceiling [FY25 10-K, lines 6449–6457].
- **General litigation framing is benign in management's words** ("does not expect … material adverse effect, net of established reserves") but the company itself flags the **"remote possibility"** of a materially-exceeding liability [FY25 10-K, lines 7794–7807]. Per module rule, contingencies are live until proven remote; the Brazil tax universe and the indemnity ceilings are the items that keep this from being a clean read.

## 5. Contingency Read

The largest off-balance-sheet contingent line is **$2,151M of standby letters of credit and surety bonds** (up 34% YoY post-Viterra), but these are trade/performance backstops that pay out only on Bunge's own non-performance, so the expected call is far below face; the largest *live and under-reserved* exposure is the **Brazilian indirect-tax universe — $760M of ICMS + PIS/COFINS claims issued at Q1 2026 against only the probable portion of a $92M non-income-tax reserve, with PIS/COFINS up $104M in a single quarter** — plus **~$1.6bn of divestiture indemnity ceilings (BP Bunge Bioenergia + Russia) held at $125M recorded**. None of these is large enough on its own to threaten solvency against $17.4bn of equity: even the full ~$5.1bn of pure contingent calls is ~29% of equity and would crystallize only in a tail scenario, and pension is a non-issue ($3M net unfunded). The realistic survival relevance is concentrated and modest — a few hundred million of Brazilian tax that could turn from "possible" to "probable" over multi-year litigation, plus a securitization-program dependency that, if pulled, pushes working-capital funding back onto the balance sheet (an Agent 03/06 liquidity item, not a contingent payment). Disclosure quality is good (Note 20 gives recorded-vs-maximum for every guarantee and indemnity), so undisclosed exposures are not a material worry — the one uncapped item is the statutory Illinois grain-trust guarantee, which is flagged but not sizeable in context.
