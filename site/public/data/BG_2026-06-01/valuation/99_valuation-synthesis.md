# Valuation Module — BG (Synthesis)

## Abstract

On normalized through-cycle cash flow Bunge looks modestly over-valued: the triangulated base-case fair value of ~$100–$115/share (midpoint ~$108) sits roughly 12% below the indicative ~$123 price, and the intrinsic mid-cycle DCF — the method that both normalizes the crush cycle and subtracts $13.7bn of post-Viterra net debt — drives that answer. The reverse-DCF says the price embeds only ~2.3% FCFF growth and a ~3.3% EBIT margin, achievable if the FY26 biofuel-led recovery merely holds. The biggest downside is a retrace to the FY25 trough margin, which takes the equity toward an ~$85 bear value (~31% below price). Verdict: modestly over-valued, with no margin of safety at today's level and the price itself only indicative — not pool-verified.

## 3. Reconciliation

The high-to-low base-case spread is **~53% ($90 SOTP base → $137.5 peers center)** — well above the 40% gate, so the disagreement is itself a finding and caps valuation confidence at 55. `07` already reconciled it, and I adopt that reconciliation; it is a real economic disagreement, not a data error:

- **The split is forward-normalized vs through-cycle.** The peers/reverse-DCF lens credits the FY26 biofuel-led margin recovery and produces ~$126–$140; the intrinsic DCF (mid-cycle 3.2–3.5% margin) and SOTP (normalized but haircut, minus $13.7bn net debt) hold margins toward the cycle middle and produce ~$90–$115.
- **Most trusted for THIS company: intrinsic DCF (~$105 midpoint).** It is the only method that normalizes the crush cycle and respects the post-Viterra balance sheet, and it ties out economically (reinvestment 25.6% × ROIC 9.7% ≈ 2.5% terminal g). It sits below price.
- **SOTP (~$90) corroborates the DCF's low half** and is trusted for the downside, not the level — the divergence from peers is the net-debt subtraction plus the trough-vs-normalized denominator, not a multiple dispute.
- **Peers (~$137.5 center) is the high outlier, trusted only on its forward-P/E leg (~$126).** Its EV/EBITDA upside to $155 rests on a noisy two-stock peer median (~12–14x) at a cyclical high, and `03` itself judged the EV/EBITDA discount "largely warranted."
- **Reverse-DCF reconciles the apparent contradiction:** its "conservative-to-fair, upside asymmetry" read rests on a forward FY26 FCFF base (~$1,750M) that already credits the recovery, whereas the DCF/SOTP are more conservative on through-cycle margins. The honest statement: **at ~$123 the equity is fair-to-fully-valued IF the FY26 crush/biofuel recovery holds mid-cycle, and modestly over-valued (~$90–$115) IF margins retrace toward the FY25 trough.** The reconciled base is **~$100–$115 (midpoint ~$108)**.

## 5. Fair-Value Summary

The fair-value range is **~$100–$115 (midpoint ~$108)**, and the single method that drives it for this company is the **intrinsic mid-cycle FCFF DCF** — the only lens that both normalizes a volatile crush cycle (margin band 3.2–3.5%, not a peak or trough point) and subtracts the $13.7bn of post-Viterra net debt that absorbs 60–70% of gross enterprise value before equity holders are paid. At the indicative ~$123 price the reverse-DCF says the market is pricing only ~2.3% FCFF growth and a ~3.3% EBIT margin — economics that the FY26 guide ($9.00–$9.50 adjusted EPS) and the matched $9.43 consensus already cover, so the priced-in bar is conservative-to-fair *provided* the FY26 biofuel-led margin recovery holds mid-cycle and does not retrace to the FY25 trough (2.18% reported EBIT margin). The margin of safety is where the concern lies: there is none at today's level — the price sits ~12% above the base midpoint and ~31% above the ~$85 bear value, and the SOTP-A trough cross-check shows a still-lower floor ($30 base) if a full trough recurs on the freshly-doubled debt. On value-trap risk: the EV/EBITDA and EV/Sales discounts to ADM are **not** mispricing — they are warranted by BG's higher leverage (~4.5–5.8x vs ADM ~3.0x), thin 3–4% margins, a narrow moat shared with the same rivals, and sub-cost-of-capital returns (ROIC 6.7% ≤ 7.2% cost of equity); the *bull* case (~$140) requires a multiple above BG's own forward curve on returns the business has not earned through a cycle, so upside much beyond ~$126 leans on a re-rating the quality/moat evidence does not support and carries genuine value-trap risk.
