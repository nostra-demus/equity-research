Bunge is a cyclical inflection: a tightened ABCD oligopoly, an improving crush spread, a ~20% FY2026 EPS guide raise, and a forward P/E in line with peers — a reasonable cyclical long at the start of a positive cycle.

### Antithesis 1
The inflection is one EPA policy decision on a spread Bunge does not control; the business earns below its cost of capital; cash conversion collapsed to 25–38% with negative FCF; leverage tripled to ~5.8x on a dilutive (+44%) deal; the Street already sits above the guide midpoint with net-negative back-half breadth; and the price already pays for the recovery to merely hold.

### Revised Thesis 2
Then value it through-cycle, not on the policy-juiced number. A mid-cycle DCF that subtracts $13.7bn of net debt yields ~$108 — below the indicative ~$123 price — and SOTP confirms the low half (~$90). At ~$123 there is no margin of safety; the bull case (~$140) requires an unearned re-rating (a flagged value trap).

### Antithesis 2
But the price is web-sourced, not pool-verified, and the methods disagree ~53% (forward-normalized vs through-cycle). Maybe the forward lens is right and the policy tailwind persists — in which case ~$123 is fair, not expensive.

### Final Thesis
Even granting the forward lens, the price needs the recovery to *hold at mid-cycle* just to be fair — there is no positive expected return at ~$123 (probability-weighted ≈ −11.5%, risk/reward ≈ −0.37), and the asymmetry is to the downside (~31% to bear). This is a policy/commodity bet with no proven company-specific edge. **Verdict: Watchlist.** Do not own at ~$123. A pool price below ~$100, a clean FY2026 cash print, or a peer comps set proving the discount is unwarranted would move the call — none of which is reasoning-dependent.

**Insight threshold reached: the remaining uncertainty is mostly data-dependent, not reasoning-dependent.**

- **Net debt:** ~$13.7bn at Q1 2026 (gross ~$14.6bn less cash $0.84bn). It went up ~4.4x in a year — almost entirely the Viterra acquisition, **not** operating losses.
- **Cash:** only ~$839M in hand — about 8% of total available liquidity. The rest (~$9.7bn) is undrawn committed revolver.
- **Maturity wall:** looks scary (37% within 12 months) but ~$3.9bn of that is **working-capital debt that rolls**. The real squeeze year is **2028** ($2.76bn term loans + the $3.5bn revolver expiring together).
- **Floating vs fixed:** ~56% (~$7.8bn) floating; +200bp adds ~$156M/yr of interest (~25% of FY25 interest).
- **Interest burden:** FY25 interest $628M; FY26 guided $620–660M; EBITDA/interest ~3.5x.
- **Liquidity risk:** runway ~16 months conservative / ~50 months realistic — but it leans on rolling debt (IG-access-dependent) because FCF is negative.
- **What happens if EBITDA falls 40–60%:** **nothing breaks** — there is no earnings-linked covenant. Coverage optics deteriorate (EBITDA/interest ~2.1x at −40%, ~1.4x at −60%; net leverage 10–15x), but the structure survives without a rescue. The true break is a **balance-sheet tail** — a deep inventory (RMI) write-down + short-term-debt build forcing the current ratio toward ~1.0x.

**Verdict cap check:** "Adequate" (not "Distress risk") → no rating cap from solvency. **Two data gaps** drive the residual uncertainty: a credit-rating-agency report and the numeric covenant thresholds — pull Capital IQ Capital Structure Summary/Details and Fixed Income → Summary (maturities, coupons, floating/fixed) to close them.
