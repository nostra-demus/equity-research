**Classification verdict on the metrics that ARE disclosed.** All three named PBRSU performance metrics — **EPS, ROIC, and relative TSR** — are **per-share or returns-based**. None of them is a size/empire-building metric (no revenue, no absolute EBITDA, no deal count, no headcount, no tonnage/volume target appears in the disclosed LTIP metric set) [FY25 10-K, Note 24, lines 8104–8106]. ROIC in particular is the single most relevant anti-empire-building metric for a serial acquirer that just closed a $10.6bn deal with ~44% dilution: a manager paid on ROIC is penalized for buying growth that does not earn its cost of capital [cross-ref `business-model/11_capital-allocation-governance`, §1]. Relative TSR ties a slice of pay to outperforming a peer group, not to absolute share-price drift. **This is a returns-oriented LTIP metric set on its face** — the unknown is the *weight* on each (proxy), and whether the **annual cash bonus** (metrics deferred) leans size-based; web sources suggest the bonus is also Adj-EPS/AROIC-based, but that is unverified.

**One structural caveat.** The metrics are *adjusted* (Adjusted EPS, Adjusted ROIC). Management defines the adjustments, and FY2025 adjusted EPS ($7.57) sat well above GAAP diluted EPS ($4.91) — a ~54% gap driven by integration/transaction items [cross-ref `business-model/11_capital-allocation-governance`, §1; `earnings/06_earnings-quality`]. Paying on *adjusted* figures during a large acquisition can let integration costs be normalized out of the metric that pays — i.e., the dilution and charges that shareholders bear may be partly excluded from the number management is paid on. Whether the adjustments are aggressive is owned by `earnings/06`; the governance point is that "returns-based" here means *adjusted* returns-based, and the adjustment policy is the thing to watch.

## 4. Incentive Alignment Read

On the evidence the pool actually carries, the **disclosed long-term-incentive metric set — EPS, ROIC, and relative TSR — is per-share/returns-based, with no size, revenue, EBITDA, deal-count, or volume metric visible**, and equity is granted as performance- and time-vesting RSUs (options dropped in 2021) under a shareholder-approved plan with a clawback policy [FY25 10-K, Note 24, l.8094–8110]; a returns metric (ROIC) on an acquisitive company is the right anti-empire-building lever, and CEO pay is heavily incentive-weighted (web, unverified). The conclusion is **mixed — leaning aligned (returns/per-share)** rather than misaligned: the *direction* of the visible metrics is returns-based, but I cannot confirm the **weights**, cannot see the **annual cash-bonus metrics**, the metrics are **management-adjusted** (FY25 adjusted EPS ran ~54% above GAAP), and **say-on-pay support fell 12 points to 84.6% in 2025** — so the verdict is held at "mixed," capped at the partial-data ceiling, and not elevated to a clean "well-aligned." **The single biggest incentive signal: the LTIP pays on ROIC and relative TSR, not on size — which, if the weights confirm it, is exactly the structure you want for a serial acquirer mid-integration; the open question is whether *adjusted* returns and the cash bonus dilute that alignment, answerable only from the 2026 proxy.**

*Out-of-scope items not addressed here (by design): officer ownership %/insider buying-selling (agent `04`), board/comp-committee independence (agent `05`), and the aggressiveness of the non-GAAP adjustments themselves (earnings `06`).*
