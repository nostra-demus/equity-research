# Management-Governance Module — BG (Synthesis)

## Abstract

Bunge's stewards are aligned and competent but not yet proven on the one decision that matters: a stable, long-tenured professional team (CEO Heckman ~7.4 yrs, CFO Neppl ~7.0 yrs, zero CFO churn) that hit or beat its adjusted-EPS guide in three of the last four years now rides on the unproven $10.6bn Viterra deal. Capital allocation is mixed — FY2025 share count rose ~44%, the dividend was uncovered by free cash flow (FCF −$879M), and disclosed ROIC (6.7%) sits below the 7.2% cost of equity. The visible LTIP pays on EPS, ROIC and relative TSR (returns-based, not size), but weights, the cash bonus and pay magnitude live in an absent 2026 proxy. The biggest governance signal is structural: a ~34% Glencore/CPP/BCI bloc with up to four board seats sits near the 2/3 supermajority needed to block fundamental actions. No hard disqualifier. Verdict: aligned and competent, on probation.

## 3. Reconciliation

The specialists are mutually consistent; the genuine tensions are all variants of one theme — **a returns-aware, candid team set against an unproven dilutive deal and a new ownership overhang** — and where they pull apart I take the more conservative reading.

1. **Returns-based incentives (03, aligned) vs value-not-yet-created capital allocation (02, mixed).** Pay is structured on EPS/ROIC/rTSR (the right anti-empire-building levers), yet capital allocation has not produced per-share value: share count up ~44%, ROIC 6.7% < 7.2% CoE, dividend uncovered by FCF. These are not contradictory — incentives point the right way, but the *outcome* is unproven and partly outside the pay window because the metrics are *adjusted* (FY25 adj-EPS ran ~54% above GAAP, and the recurring Viterra-integration addback can normalize out costs shareholders bear). **Reconciled view (conservative):** alignment is in the *design*, not yet in the *result*; I do not let the good metric set lift the capital-allocation read.

2. **Clean structural governance (05, "adequate") vs concentrated control (04, the dominant risk).** One-share-one-vote, no poison pill, no staggered board, an independent chair, and a clean 2026 AGM are real positives. But the ~34% Glencore/CPP/BCI bloc with up to four board seats and 2/3-supermajority swing weight is the live exposure, sharpened by Glencore also being a trading competitor/counterparty. **Reconciled view (conservative):** minority protection is "adequate, not strong" — the clean ballot does not offset the structural overhang, so shareholder friendliness is held at 52 (below the no-data cap of 60), leaning to `04`'s more cautious read.

3. **Candid attribution (06, Green) vs framing softness on cash and a recurring "one-off" (06, Red/Amber).** The MD&A naming its own Viterra dilution is strong anti-spin; against it, the call leads with an adjusted "discretionary cash flow" construct that steps around −$879M consolidated FCF, and integration cost has been adjusted out for three straight years (RF-DISC-002). **Reconciled view:** candor is real on pinned-down items but unproven in genuinely bad times (only a beat-and-raise transcript exists) and softened by the recurring addback — hence 64, at the cap.

No specialist's evidence was overridden; the conservative side was taken on each tension.

## 5. Stewardship Summary

(a) **Delivery and per-share value:** This team has delivered on the metric it commits to — adjusted EPS hit or beaten in three of the last four years, five straight quarterly beats — but the *defining* decision, the $10.6bn Viterra acquisition, has not yet created per-share value: FY2025 share count rose ~44%, the dividend was not covered by free cash flow, and the company's own disclosed ROIC (6.7%) sits below its own cost of equity (7.2%). (b) **Incentives and ownership:** the visible LTIP pays on EPS, ROIC and relative TSR — the right anti-empire-building levers for a serial acquirer mid-integration — but weights, the cash bonus and pay magnitude are in an absent proxy, the metrics are management-adjusted, and insider skin-in-the-game is unquantified with no open-market buying on record, so alignment is in the design, not yet demonstrated in cash. (c) **Minority protection and candor:** structure is clean (one-share-one-vote, no pill, annual elections, independent chair, JV-only RPT well below the disqualifier line) and management owned its misses candidly — most tellingly naming its own dilution in the MD&A — but a ~34% Glencore/CPP/BCI bloc with up to four board seats and 2/3-supermajority swing weight is a real entrenchment overhang, and candor in genuinely bad times is untested. (d) **The single most important reason to trust — or not:** trust rests on a stable, returns-incented, candid professional team with clean governance machinery; the reason to withhold full trust is that they have bet the company on a deal that dilutes shareholders today and remains unproven, while a new concentrated blockholder can shape the board against a dispersed minority.
