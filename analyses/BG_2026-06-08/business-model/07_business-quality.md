## 2. Aggregate Quality Score

**Aggregate score: 27 / 100 — Weak**

This aggregate is not a simple average of the 11 rows. It is weighted toward the factors that most define the business. For a commodity processor, the three factors that dominate the quality read are commodity dependence (10), margin stability (15), and capital intensity (20) — all very weak — because these are the factors that determine whether the business can compound value through a cycle. Pricing power (22) and cyclicality (18) reinforce the same conclusion. The one factor that prevents a lower aggregate is industry rate-of-change (60): the competitive set is stable and known, which at least means the company is not fighting technological obsolescence on top of commodity exposure. Disclosure quality (68) is a secondary positive that does not change the underlying business character. The aggregate of 27 reflects a business that is fundamentally a commodity-throughput intermediary with thin, volatile margins, heavy capital requirements, near-zero pricing power, and earnings that move more with the soybean crush cycle than with anything Bunge does.

## 4. Read

Bunge is a commodity-throughput processor and trader: it buys agricultural raw materials, moves them through its global infrastructure, and sells processed outputs at spreads determined by global supply and demand rather than by any action Bunge takes. The business repeats itself every crop cycle — volumes are structurally growing with global food and fuel demand — but the economic value created is entirely at the mercy of crush margin cycles, biofuel policy, Argentine and Brazilian crop conditions, and freight rates. The single quality factor a buyer should watch over the next 24 months is margin stability: specifically, whether the soybean and softseed crush spreads recover from their post-FY2023 compression, given that the business as currently sized (post-Viterra, ~$44.5B total assets, $14B of debt) generates CFO of only $0.8B when spreads are at mid-cycle. Bunge is not a durable compounder. It is a commodity-cycle and policy-cycle bet: results depend on whether crush margins, biofuel mandates, and South American crop conditions move in Bunge's favor — not on durable competitive advantages compounding per-share value over time.
