## 2. Classification

**Capital allocation concerns** — Bunge's FY25 record is dominated by the Viterra acquisition: a deal worth $10.6B (stock + cash + assumed debt) that exceeded the company's own pre-deal equity market cap, was substantially debt-funded, and left net leverage at roughly 5.8x EBITDA. FCF was negative in FY25 (CFO $844M minus capex $1,723M = -$879M), yet the company continued paying dividends of $459M. Share count jumped 44% from the deal. Integration of Viterra's internal controls over financial reporting was excluded from the FY25 audit. Two additional acquisitions (ViOil, IFF soy) demonstrate an ongoing appetite for deals. On the positive side: no governance red flags (clean audit, stable auditor, no restatements, low related-party exposure), and the C-suite is stable and experienced.

---

## 3. Most Material Signal

The acquisition pattern is the most material signal, and specifically the Viterra deal. At $10.6B total consideration against a pre-deal Bunge equity market cap of roughly $8–9B, Viterra was a bet-the-company acquisition funded with $3.6B of assumed Viterra debt, $1.9B of new cash debt, and ~65.6M new shares (diluting existing holders 44%). Net debt / EBITDA post-close sits at roughly 5.8x — well above the 2–3x range typical for investment-grade agribusiness. CFO collapsed from $1.9B (FY24) to $844M (FY25) while capex rose. The company now has $1.28B of senior notes maturing in 2026 alone. If the Viterra integration stalls, agricultural commodity margins remain compressed, or credit markets tighten, the leverage overhang becomes a liquidity and ratings risk. Further deterioration here — a miss on synergies, an earnings year below FY25's already reduced EBITDA, or a forced asset sale at distressed prices — would push the classification toward "governance red flags" and force a hard score cap in the synthesis.
