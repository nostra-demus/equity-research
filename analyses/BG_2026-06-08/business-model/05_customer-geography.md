## 4. Read

Bunge is not concentrated on the customer side. No single named customer accounts for a material share of revenue; the company sells commodity products — soybeans, meals, oils, grains — to thousands of industrial buyers across animal feed, biofuel, food processing, and milling industries with no disclosed multi-year offtake contracts. The geography picture is more nuanced. By legal entity location, revenue is spread across the US (22.2%), Switzerland (22.2%), Netherlands (12.2%), and a large "Rest of World" bucket (43.3%), which understates real geographic exposure because Switzerland and Netherlands are booking/holding entities rather than end markets. By operational capacity, South America — principally Brazil and Argentina — is the dominant hub for origination and crushing, carrying 47% of soybean processing capacity, the majority of grain milling assets, and $835M of farmer financing credit exposure. This South American dependence is naked: there are no long-term contracts protecting Bunge from Argentine export restrictions, Brazilian FX controls, or a crop-failure event that collapses both origination volumes and farmer credit repayment simultaneously. The single biggest dependency the synthesizer should know about is Bunge's structural reliance on South American (primarily Brazilian) origination — an exposure that is operationally dominant, contractually unsecured, and subject to sovereign policy risk (export taxes, FX controls) and climate risk (crop failure) in the same event.
