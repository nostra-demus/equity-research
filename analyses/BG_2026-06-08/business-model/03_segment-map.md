## 2. Dominant Segment

**Soybean Processing and Refining** is the dominant segment, contributing 51.6% of FY2025 external revenue ($36,313M of $70,329M total) and 52.6% of total reportable segment EBIT ($1,225M of $2,329M). [FY2025 10-K, Note 26, p. F-76]

The segment's dominance holds on both revenue and profit. Its EBIT leadership is reinforced by its asset base — $16.3B of the company's $41.9B in reportable segment assets (39%), and $790M of $1,692M total segment capex (46.7%) in FY2025. The soybean segment is where Bunge commits the most capital and where value is created or destroyed depending on crush margins, South American (primarily Argentine) crop conditions, and global oilseed pricing.

The segment is not a monolithic compounder: EBIT swung from $2,222M in FY2023 to $872M in FY2024 to $1,225M in FY2025 — a near-threefold range in three years — illustrating that this is a high-cyclicality, commodity-margin business, not a durable-margin franchise. [FY2025 10-K, MD&A, Segment Overview and Results of Operations, pp. 37–39]

## 3. Segment Disclosure Quality

**Segment definitions changed materially in 2025.** Effective Q3 2025, Bunge restructured its reportable segments in connection with the Viterra acquisition (closed July 2, 2025), moving from the prior Agribusiness / Edible Oil Products / Milling Products / Sugar and Bioenergy structure to the current four-segment value-chain structure (Soybean / Softseed / Other Oilseeds / Grain). Additionally, effective January 1, 2025, the Sugar and Bioenergy segment was reclassified into Corporate and Other. In Q1 2026, "Other Oilseeds Processing and Refining" was renamed "Tropical Oils and Specialty Ingredients" — a cosmetic change with no impact on composition. The FY2024 and FY2023 financials in the 10-K have been recast on a comparable basis, making three-year trend analysis feasible but limited — comparisons to periods before FY2023 require care. [FY2025 10-K, Note 26, p. F-76; Q1 2026 10-Q, Note 19]

**Segment EBIT is fully disclosed at the individual segment level**, making profit share analysis clean and straightforward. The CODM uses segment EBIT as the primary operating performance measure. Revenue (net sales to external customers) and inter-segment revenues are also disclosed by segment, as are total assets, capex, and depreciation. [FY2025 10-K, Note 26, p. F-76]

**The Corporate and Other bucket is meaningful and growing.** Corporate and Other EBIT was -$796M in FY2025, versus -$367M in FY2024 and -$358M in FY2023. The FY2025 figure includes integration and acquisition costs related to Viterra, and the Sugar and Bioenergy reclassification. This bucket held $2.6B of assets at December 31, 2025. The sharp deterioration is primarily Viterra-related and should normalize partially in FY2026, but the bucket is large enough (dragging consolidated EBIT from $2,329M to $1,533M) that downstream agents must treat total consolidated EBIT as structurally different from reportable segment EBIT. [FY2025 10-K, Note 26, p. F-76]

**No hidden "Other" revenue bucket within the four segments.** The segments collectively account for all external revenue; $3M resides in Corporate and Other (0.004% of total), which is immaterial. [FY2025 10-K, Note 26, p. F-76]

**Margin disclosure limitation**: Bunge reports segment EBIT but does not separately break out segment gross profit or operating income in the standard income-statement format. The note does disclose raw materials cost, industrial expenses (fixed and variable), depreciation, and SG&A by segment, allowing EBIT to be reconstructed — but segment gross margin is not directly given. Downstream agents building unit-economics models should use segment EBIT margins directly from Note 26.
