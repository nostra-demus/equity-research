# Competitive Map — BG

## 1. Dominant Segment

Soybean Processing and Refining — 51.6% of FY2025 external revenue ($36,313M) and 52.6% of total reportable segment EBIT ($1,225M). [FY2025 10-K, Note 26, p. F-76; upstream segment-map]

---

## 2. Named Competitors

### Competitor A — Archer Daniels Midland Co. (ADM)

- **Ticker / listing:** ADM — NYSE
- **Where they compete:** Global oilseed crushing (soybeans, softseeds), grain origination and merchandising, and value-added food/feed ingredients; competes directly with Bunge in North America, South America, and Europe across all four of Bunge's reportable segments
- **Scale:** ~$80.3B total revenues, FY2025 (calendar year ended December 31, 2025). Oilseed crush volume: 36.3 million metric tons of oilseeds processed in FY2025. [ADM FY2025 8-K / earnings release; web source: MacroTrends, FY2025, unverified]
- **Source named in:** BG FY2025 10-K, Item 1 — named as Key Competitor in all four segments (Soybean Processing and Refining, Softseed Processing and Refining, Other Oilseeds Processing and Refining / Tropical Oils, and Grain Merchandising and Milling). [FY2025 10-K, pp. 3–6]
- **One-line read:** ADM is the closest structural peer — publicly listed, US-headquartered, comparable global footprint — but entered 2025–2026 under significant financial and governance stress (accounting investigation, CEO departure), with crush profit down 81% in FY2025 on weak soybean oil demand; Bunge's own crush volumes of 41M mt (soybeans) exceeded ADM's 36.3M mt in FY2025.

---

### Competitor B — Cargill Incorporated

- **Ticker / listing:** Private; not publicly listed
- **Where they compete:** Global grain origination and merchandising, oilseed crushing, food ingredients, animal protein, and ocean freight; competes with Bunge in North America, South America, Europe, and Asia-Pacific across all segments
- **Scale:** ~$154B revenues, fiscal year ended May 2025 (Cargill's fiscal year closes in May). Largest global agribusiness company by revenue. [Web source: Star Tribune, fiscal year ended May 2025, unverified]
- **Source named in:** BG FY2025 10-K, Item 1 — named as Key Competitor in all four segments. [FY2025 10-K, pp. 3–6]
- **One-line read:** Cargill is the largest global agribusiness by revenue — roughly 2x Bunge's FY2025 top line — with deeper diversification into meat, animal protein, and food ingredients; its private ownership means financial details are limited, but revenues fell ~4% in fiscal 2025 on commodity price declines, consistent with the industrywide cycle Bunge navigated. Cargill's scale gives it structural leverage in freight, origination, and working capital that Bunge must offset with geographic positioning and asset density.

---

### Competitor C — Louis Dreyfus Company B.V. (LDC)

- **Ticker / listing:** Private; not publicly listed (majority-owned by the Louis-Dreyfus family)
- **Where they compete:** Global grain origination and merchandising, oilseed processing, and ocean freight; competes with Bunge primarily in South America origination (Argentina and Brazil), global soybean and softseed flows, and grain merchandising
- **Scale:** ~$50.6B net sales, FY2024 (calendar year). Segment Operating Results $2.35B in FY2024, down from $2.61B in FY2023. First-half 2025 net sales were $26.2B. [Web source: LDC press release, FY2024 results, unverified; LDC H1 2025 press release, unverified]
- **Source named in:** BG FY2025 10-K, Item 1 — named as Key Competitor in Soybean Processing and Refining, Softseed Processing and Refining, and Grain Merchandising and Milling segments. [FY2025 10-K, pp. 3, 5, 6]
- **One-line read:** LDC is the third of the classic "ABCD" group (Archer Daniels, Bunge, Cargill, Dreyfus), with roughly 70% of Bunge's pre-Viterra scale; post-Viterra, Bunge's $70B revenue base has materially outgrown LDC ($50.6B FY2024), widening the asset and volume gap; LDC remains a direct rival in South American grain flows and soybean meal merchandising.

## 3. Competitive Position

Bunge does not disclose market share data by segment, and neither do its private competitors (Cargill, LDC). The closest available proxy for share is crush volume. Bunge crushed 41.0 million metric tons of soybeans in FY2025, versus ADM's 36.3 million metric tons — placing Bunge ahead of ADM on volume in the dominant segment. [FY2025 10-K, MD&A, Soybean Processing and Refining, p. 38; ADM FY2025 earnings release, web source unverified] In the US market specifically, Bunge holds approximately 26% of domestic soybean crushing capacity versus ADM at 21% and Cargill at 21%, giving Bunge the largest single share among the top three. [Web source: Farm Action concentration data, 2024, unverified] The Viterra acquisition (closed July 2, 2025) added significant grain merchandising volume — Bunge's Grain Merchandising and Milling segment volumes roughly doubled to 67.2 million metric tons in FY2025 — which should increase Bunge's share of global grain trade flows relative to LDC. [FY2025 10-K, MD&A, Grain Merchandising and Milling, p. 42] No direct share disclosure or third-party verified share data exists for the global oilseed processing market.

**Position vs peers: Holding, with share likely gained post-Viterra in grain merchandising; no confirmed trend change in soybean crushing share.**

---

## 4. Competitive Shape

The global oilseed processing and grain merchandising industry is an **oligopoly**. Four companies — Archer Daniels Midland, Bunge, Cargill, and Louis Dreyfus (the "ABCDs") — have long dominated global grain and oilseed trade flows, with estimates placing their combined share of global commodity trade at roughly 50–60%. [Web source: European Parliament study on commodity traders, 2024, unverified; Oxfam "Cereal Secrets" report] In the US soybean crushing market specifically, the top four players (Bunge, ADM, Cargill, and Ag Processing) hold approximately 80% of domestic crushing capacity, a highly concentrated structure. [Web source: Farm Action Agricultural System Concentration Data, 2024, unverified] Each major player holds a geographically differentiated network of processing plants, grain elevators, and port terminals that represents a substantial barrier to entry — new entrants face capital requirements of several billion dollars to match the scale and logistics integration of the incumbents. Two emerging rivals — COFCO International (the grain-trading arm of Chinese state enterprise COFCO Corporation) and Wilmar International (Singapore-listed, Asia-Pacific-focused) — are expanding in Bunge's markets, particularly in South America origination and Asian oilseed processing, and are named in Bunge's own filings. [FY2025 10-K, pp. 3–6] The oligopoly is stable at the top but experiencing incremental pressure at the margins from state-backed and Asian-listed entrants.

---

## 5. Caveat

Cargill and Louis Dreyfus are privately held and do not publish audited financials in full detail. Revenue figures for both rely on company press releases and trade press reports, which are unverified secondary sources. Crush volume and market share data for the global soybean processing market are not disclosed by any of the major players; the US capacity shares cited are from an advocacy-group dataset (Farm Action, 2024) and have not been cross-checked against a primary source. Bunge itself notes in its filings that it does not disclose market share. Any downstream moat scoring that uses share data should weight these figures with caution and treat the position read as "Holding" rather than "Gaining" in the absence of confirmed share evidence.
