# Business Identity — BG

## 1. What The Company Actually Does

Bunge Global SA is a global grain and oilseed merchant and processor. It buys agricultural commodities — primarily soybeans, canola/rapeseed, sunflower seed, corn, and wheat — directly from farmers or via intermediaries, then either ships them to end-markets as raw commodities or crushes (presses) them into meal and vegetable oil before selling. Its customers are companies that make animal feed, biofuels, and food ingredients: livestock producers, poultry companies, biodiesel refiners, food manufacturers, bakeries, and branded consumer goods companies. The company operates in more than 50 countries, with processing assets concentrated in South America (especially Argentina and Brazil), North America, and Europe, and grain origination now extending across Australia and Canada following its July 2025 acquisition of Viterra. The core problem Bunge solves is logistics and transformation: a farmer in rural Brazil or Argentina cannot efficiently reach a food manufacturer in Southeast Asia or a biodiesel plant in Europe; Bunge builds and finances the infrastructure to make that connection work, captures a margin per ton along the way, and takes on the price-risk management in between. [FY2025 10-K, Item 1, p.3–5]

## 2. How The Company Makes Money

**Processing and refining (oilseeds — soybeans, canola, sunflower, and specialty):**

`Revenue = volume crushed (metric tons) × realized price of output (oil + meal) per ton`
`Margin ≈ crush spread = value of oil + meal produced − cost of oilseed input − processing costs`

Bunge crushed 41.0 million metric tons of soybeans in FY2025 and handled 20.5 million metric tons of merchandised soybeans; softseed crush volumes were approximately 9–10 million metric tons. The Soybean Processing and Refining segment generated $36.3 billion in net sales and $1.225 billion in segment EBIT in FY2025. [FY2025 10-K, MD&A segment table, p.38]

The Softseed Processing and Refining segment generated $11.3 billion in net sales and $521 million in segment EBIT in FY2025. [FY2025 10-K, MD&A segment table, p.40]

The Other Oilseeds and Specialty Ingredients segment (tropical oils including palm, coconut, shea; soy protein concentrate) generated $4.6 billion in net sales and $118 million in segment EBIT in FY2025. [FY2025 10-K, MD&A segment table, p.41]

**Grain merchandising and milling:**

`Revenue = volume merchandised (metric tons) × price per ton`
`Margin = merchandising spread (buy-sell price difference) + logistics/freight margin + milling conversion margin`

The Grain Merchandising and Milling segment (corn, wheat, barley, cotton, sugar) generated $18.1 billion in net sales and $465 million in segment EBIT in FY2025. Volumes doubled to 67.2 million metric tons, driven by the Viterra acquisition. [FY2025 10-K, MD&A segment table, p.42]

**Consolidated:**

Total FY2025 net sales were $70.3 billion (up from $53.1 billion in FY2024, primarily due to Viterra). Total segment EBIT was $2.329 billion. [FY2025 10-K, Note 26, p. referenced in segment summary at line 8536]

What drives volume: oilseed availability in key growing regions (Argentina, Brazil, North America, Ukraine/Black Sea, Canada/Australia), capacity utilization at crush plants, and the size of global grain crops.

What drives price and margin: the crush spread (the difference between what oil and meal sell for versus what the raw bean costs), which is set by global supply/demand for animal feed protein and vegetable oil including biofuel demand; and for grain merchandising, the price differential between origin and destination markets. FX — particularly the Argentine peso, Brazilian real, Canadian dollar, and euro versus the US dollar — directly affects both sourcing costs and revenue conversion.

What drives cost and margin: commodity input costs (the largest element of cost of goods sold), energy and freight rates (which affect processing and logistics cost), and mark-to-market timing differences on hedges that cause short-term reported earnings volatility.

## 3. Business Type Classification

Asset-heavy global commodity merchant and oilseed processor, earning volume-driven transformation and logistics margins on low-value-added throughput in a structurally competitive, price-taking industry.

## 4. What Drives Variance

Revenue and margin move most sharply with the oilseed crush spread: when global protein meal demand (driven by livestock expansion, especially poultry and pork in Asia) exceeds supply from crush plants, spreads widen and EBIT per ton rises; when capacity exceeds demand, spreads compress and margins fall. Commodity price levels drive headline revenue but do not directly drive profitability — Bunge hedges its price exposure, so it is the spread, not the outright price, that matters for earnings. Grain merchandising margin fluctuates with trade-flow disruptions (sanctions, weather, export restrictions) and freight costs, which can move sharply with geopolitical events as seen with the Middle East conflict impact on Q1 2026 bunker fuel costs. [Q1 2026 earnings call, April 29, 2026, prepared remarks; FY2025 10-K, MD&A Overview, p.33–34] FX is a secondary but material driver: a weaker Argentine peso reduces the USD value of South American origination, while US dollar strength generally pressures farmer selling in producing regions.
