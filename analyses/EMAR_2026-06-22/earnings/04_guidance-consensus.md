## 7. Bar Assessment

**Bar is low.**

The consensus bar for FY2026 is set materially below Emaar's recent trajectory, and three factors reinforce this:

First, the FY2026 EPS consensus of AED 1.92 implies a year-on-year *decline* of ~3.5% from FY2025's AED 1.99 actual (which itself beat consensus by 11%). Consensus has been cut ~17% over the last 90 days — from AED 2.32 to AED 1.92 — partly because multiple new initiations entered with conservative models in May–June 2026 (AlphaMena, FAB Securities, Arqaam; see Recent Changes tab). This has compressed the mean estimate, not because existing analysts bearishly revised down, but partly because new entrants with lower starting points joined the pool. The bar is now set below last year's achieved result.

Second, Emaar has beaten EPS consensus by an average of approximately +17% in each of the three prior fiscal years (FY2023, FY2024, FY2025) and beat in all 5 of the last 5 quarters. The company carries a revenue backlog of AED 134.3 Bn (UAE alone), which provides substantial visibility into near-term recognition; the current FY2026 revenue consensus of AED 52.7 Bn is comfortably covered by the backlog at the current recognition pace of roughly AED 43–50 Bn per year in UAE development revenue. [Preliminary Annual Report, Feb-12-2026, Slide 16]

Third, while EPS estimates have been cut, Q1 2026 actuals (announced 2026-05-11) still beat by +9.6%, and the next reporting date (Q2 2026) is set for 2026-08-10. The backlog and Q1 delivery suggest recognition is running, not stalling.

The main risk to this assessment: the Street may have cut estimates for a real reason (delays in unit deliveries, mix shift toward international markets that are lower-margin, or DMTT/UAE corporate tax drag that wasn't in models). The gross margin consensus for FY2026 has shifted from ~55.5% (per estimates from 6 months ago) to ~51.2% currently — a material step-down. If this margin compression reflects a structural rather than timing factor, the "low bar" argument weakens. No transcript is available to assess management's own explanation; guidance extraction is therefore capped at what the investor presentation and filing data disclose.
