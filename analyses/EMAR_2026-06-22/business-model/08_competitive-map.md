# Competitive Map — EMAR

## 1. Dominant Segment

UAE Development: master-planned residential communities sold to buyers on percentage-of-completion terms, generating AED 36,443M in revenue (73% of group) and AED 16,710M in EBITDA (approximately 65% of group) in FY2025. ## 2. Named Competitors

### Competitor A — Aldar Properties PJSC

- **Ticker / listing:** ADX: ALDAR (Abu Dhabi Securities Exchange)
- **Where they compete:** Large-scale master-plan residential and mixed-use development in the UAE, primarily Abu Dhabi; increasingly expanding into Dubai through direct projects and a 2025 collaboration agreement with Emaar on certain Dubai-adjacent ventures. Aldar also competes on the same buyer pool — high-net-worth UAE residents and foreign investors — with products that overlap Emaar's community-living format.
- **Scale:** FY2025 group revenue AED 33,818M (approximately 68% of Emaar's AED 49,557M); group property sales AED 40.6 billion for FY2025, up 21% year-on-year. [Web: The National, "Aldar delivers 36% rise in 2025 profit," 9 Feb 2026, unverified; stockanalysis.com ADX:ALDAR income statement, data as of June 2026, unverified]
- **Profitability / return on capital:** FY2025 net profit margin approximately 22% (net profit AED 7,611M on revenue AED 33,818M); EBITDA margin approximately 28% (EBITDA AED 9,525M); ROIC approximately 14.8%; ROE approximately 19.8%. [Web: stockanalysis.com ADX:ALDAR financials and statistics, data as of June 2026, unverified — not from filed annual report; treat as indicative]. Compare: Emaar's FY2025 net profit margin is approximately 45% (AED 22,326M / AED 49,557M) and EBITDA margin 52% — materially higher than Aldar's on both measures, though Emaar's blended margin benefits from its high-margin malls business.
- **Source named in:** Aldar is named as a UAE market competitor in industry trade press (The National, Provident Estate top-developer rankings 2025). It is cited in Emaar's own FY2025 investor presentation as a peer in the context of MSCI EM ranking, where Emaar claims "1st in revenue and 1st in net profit in Emerging Markets including China" — an implicit acknowledgment that Aldar is the primary comparator for UAE-listed real estate. [FY2025 Investor Presentation, Feb-12-2026, slide 6]
- **One-line read:** Aldar is the dominant Abu Dhabi developer now pushing into Dubai, growing faster in revenue terms (47% YoY vs Emaar's 40%) but with structurally lower margins, meaning Emaar earns roughly twice as much per dirham of revenue.

### Competitor B — DAMAC Properties Dubai Co PSC

- **Ticker / listing:** DFM: DAMAC (Dubai Financial Market). Note: DAMAC went private in 2022 via a buyout by its founder; as of mid-2026, financial disclosures are limited and the company's DFM ticker remains visible on market data screens but formal public filings have been sparse since the privatisation. Full audited margin/ROIC data is not publicly disclosed.
- **Where they compete:** Dubai residential development, with emphasis on luxury branded apartments (partnered with Versace, Bugatti, Fendi) and mega-community projects. Directly overlaps Emaar's core Dubai apartment and luxury townhouse buyer segment.
- **Scale:** FY2024 revenue approximately AED 13.1 billion (web: Wikipedia, citing 2024 annual figure, unverified); group property sales AED 36 billion in 2025 per industry trade press, making DAMAC the largest private (non-government) developer in Dubai by sales value in that year, roughly half of Emaar's AED 80.4 billion in gross property sales. [Web: Provident Estate "Top 10 Developers by Sales 2025," unverified; Wikipedia DAMAC Properties article, unverified]
- **Profitability / return on capital:** Not public / not disclosed in usable form since the 2022 privatisation. Revenue-to-profit ratios are not verifiable from available public sources as of June 2026. Prior to going private, DAMAC reported net profit margins that varied significantly with market cycles (including losses in 2019–2020). No peer ROIC/ROE anchor can be reliably sourced for DAMAC without audited filings.
- **Source named in:** DAMAC is named as a direct Dubai competitor in: (a) industry ranking tables (Provident Estate top-10 developer report, Jan–Aug 2025, showing DAMAC at AED 24B in sales vs Emaar at AED 51B over the same period); (b) trade press (Dubai Livin, "Top 5 Developers Transforming Dubai's Real Estate Market in 2025"); (c) market share analyses citing the Dubai Land Department transaction data. DAMAC is not named by Emaar in its own investor presentations or annual reports, which do not identify competitors by name.
- **One-line read:** DAMAC is Emaar's closest Dubai residential rival by launch volume and brand profile, but operates at smaller recognised revenue scale with opaque financials since its privatisation — limiting a direct margin comparison.

### Competitor C — Nakheel (subsidiary of Dubai Holding)

- **Ticker / listing:** Not publicly listed; Nakheel is a wholly-owned subsidiary of Dubai Holding (a government-owned conglomerate), which merged Nakheel and Meydan under Dubai Holding Real Estate in 2023. No public financial statements are filed.
- **Where they compete:** Large-scale master-planned waterfront development in Dubai — Palm Jumeirah, Palm Jebel Ali, Palm Deira, Jumeirah Village, and related communities. Competes with Emaar for the same plot-and-villa buyer in iconic Dubai locations, and also competes in the community retail leasing space (Nakheel Malls).
- **Scale:** Not disclosed as a standalone entity post-merger. Industry data places Nakheel at approximately AED 13 billion in Dubai property sales for January–August 2025 (third-ranked developer by value in that period, per Provident Estate data, unverified web source). This implies a 2025 annualised rate below half of Emaar's volume.
- **Profitability / return on capital:** Not public / not disclosed. Nakheel does not file public consolidated financials; it was historically restructured under sovereign support following the 2009 debt crisis. No margin or ROIC anchor is available.
- **Source named in:** Nakheel is referenced as a leading Dubai developer in Emaar's own FY2024 annual report (Arabic text, "استعراض العام" section, page 15 of the Arabic annual report, citing major UAE developers including Nakheel, Aldar, and others in market context). It is also consistently ranked 3rd in Dubai developer sales tables in industry reports (Provident Estate, top-10 developer rankings, Jan–Aug 2025, unverified).
- **One-line read:** Nakheel / Dubai Holding Real Estate is a government-backed competitor that controls the most iconic waterfront addresses in Dubai but lacks the listed, audited financials that would allow direct profitability comparison.

## 3. Competitive Position

Emaar is gaining share in the dominant UAE Development segment. Its UAE gross property sales rose from AED 65.4 billion in FY2024 to AED 71.1 billion in FY2025 (+9%), while the total Dubai primary market grew at a slower pace — Emaar's absolute AED 51 billion in sales for January–August 2025 implies approximately 15–16% of the total Dubai primary market by value, consistent with the 15.8% market share cited in industry data for full-year 2024. [Web: Provident Estate developer rankings Jan–Aug 2025, unverified; market share data sourced from Dubai Land Department transaction records as reported in trade press, unverified]. The FY2025 revenue backlog of AED 134.3 billion (UAE only) — equivalent to 3.7x FY2025 UAE development revenue — is itself a proxy for forward share retention: Emaar has already pre-sold a volume of inventory that locks in its recognised revenue pipeline for five to six years. The company itself claims "1st in revenue" among listed real estate companies in Emerging Markets (excluding China) per MSCI/Datastream/Reuters as at 31 December 2025, which is a ranking claim not a market-share disclosure, but is directionally consistent with Emaar holding the leading position. [FY2025 Investor Presentation, Feb-12-2026, slide 6]

## 4. Competitive Shape

The UAE residential development market — specifically the Dubai primary market, which is Emaar's dominant arena — is an oligopoly with 4–5 firms controlling an estimated 45–55% of primary sales value. Emaar leads with approximately 15–16% share; DAMAC follows at approximately 9–11%; Nakheel and Sobha Realty each hold 7–9%; Aldar holds a smaller Dubai-specific share but is growing. [Web: Provident Estate 2025 rankings and LinkedIn/Dubai Land Department market data as reported in trade press, all unverified]. The remainder is fragmented across dozens of smaller Dubai developers (Binghatti, Danube, Meraas, Ellington, and others). The market is not a single-firm monopoly — supply has been increasing as new entrants launched projects — but the top-tier is consolidated around branded, community-scale operators who command land-bank advantages and buyer-trust premiums. The oligopoly structure has proven durable through multiple cycles, with the same 4–5 names holding top rankings since at least 2018, though DAMAC's privatisation and Nakheel's government ownership complicate direct comparison.

## 5. Caveat

Three limitations apply:

1. **Emaar's own filings do not name competitors.** The FY2024 Arabic annual report and the FY2025 preliminary investor presentation contain no named competitor list in risk factors or the business overview — the standard UAE/DFM filing format does not require a named competitive risk disclosure in the same way US 10-K filings do. All three competitors above are sourced from industry trade press and market-share data, not from Emaar's own filings, except for the indirect MSCI ranking claim on slide 6 of the FY2025 presentation.

2. **DAMAC and Nakheel margin/ROIC data are unavailable.** DAMAC went private in 2022; Nakheel is government-owned and never individually listed. Neither entity has publicly audited income statements for 2024 or 2025 in this pool. The moat agent should treat these two as named-but-unquantified peers for margin comparisons and rely solely on the Aldar margin/ROIC anchor for the "earns more than peers" claim.

3. **Aldar margin data is from a web data aggregator (stockanalysis.com), not directly from Aldar's own audited annual filing.** These figures should be treated as indicative and cross-checked against Aldar's ADX-filed FY2025 annual report when available. The directional read (Emaar's margins are materially higher than Aldar's) is robust even with this caveat, as the gap is large: ~52% EBITDA margin for Emaar vs ~28% for Aldar.
