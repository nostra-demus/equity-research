# Segment Map — EMAR

## 1. Segment Table

Emaar Properties PJSC (DFM: EMAAR) reports under IFRS, in AED, with a December 31 fiscal year. The company uses two overlapping segment views in its filings: (a) the statutory Capital IQ / annual-report disclosure with three business segments — Real Estate, Leasing/Retail, and Hospitality — and (b) the FY2025 investor presentation which splits performance into five operating segments: UAE Development, International Development, Emaar Malls, Emaar Hospitality, and Entertainment/Leasing & Others. The investor-presentation cut is used below as the primary view because it is the more operationally meaningful split; the statutory three-segment view is cross-checked and differences noted.

## 2. Dominant Segment

**UAE Development is the dominant segment, both by revenue (73%) and by EBITDA (approximately 65% of the total).**

In FY2025 it generated AED 36,443M in revenue — 2.5x the combined output of all recurring businesses (Malls, Hospitality, Entertainment) — and AED 16,710M in EBITDA at a 46% margin. The revenue backlog stood at AED 134.3 billion (UAE only) as of December 31 2025, roughly 3.7x FY2025 group revenue, providing multi-year earnings visibility. [FY2025 Investor Presentation, slides 13, 16, and 17]

The malls business (Emaar Malls) is the single highest-margin segment at 86% EBITDA and 19% of group EBITDA — it is the quality anchor of the portfolio and a structural support for recurring cash flow — but it is less than a fifth the size of UAE Development by EBITDA in absolute terms. The development engine sets the pace for value creation; the malls and hospitality assets provide ballast.

## 3. Segment Disclosure Quality

**Segment definitions have been broadly consistent over the review period (FY2020–FY2025).** The Capital IQ data export (sourced from DFM annual filings under IFRS 8) shows the same three statutory segments — Real Estate, Leasing/Retail, and Hospitality — across all six annual periods. The FY2025 investor presentation adds a fifth operating sub-segment (Entertainment/Leasing & Others separated from Malls) and reclassifies certain JV Mall assets that had previously been reported in "Others" into "Emaar Malls (incl. JV assets)" — this is a presentation change flagged in footnote 1 of slide 13, not a statutory restatement, and does not affect the audited line items.

**Profit metrics are disclosed at segment level (NPBT) in the statutory data, but capital expenditure disclosure has deteriorated.** The Capital IQ statutory segment file shows capex by segment only for FY2020 and FY2021; capex for FY2022–FY2025 is absent at segment level (all cells blank). This is a material gap for the unit-economics and capital-allocation agents: the "Others" bucket in the statutory three-segment file carries no revenue but does carry AED 447M of other profit items (FY2025), which likely relates to holding-company and inter-company items; at 1.7% of total NPBT it is not material. [Capital IQ Annual Segments export, FY2020–FY2025]

**Segment-level asset disclosure is present** — all three statutory segments show asset bases annually. Real Estate carries AED 149.6 billion out of AED 186.7 billion total (80% of group assets in FY2025), consistent with its role as the capital-absorbing development engine. [Capital IQ Annual Segments export, FY2025]

**The investor presentation provides more granular operating KPIs** (occupancy, RevPAR, units sold, revenue backlog by project) but those figures are management-prepared and unaudited. The statutory annual report for FY2024 is in Arabic as the primary text (IFRS notes are present but not available for direct extraction from the data pool). The FY2025 full statutory annual report had not yet been published as of February 2026 (the pool contains the preliminary investor presentation); once filed, it will be the authoritative source for segment note reconciliation.

**Summary of disclosure gaps:** (a) segment-level capex missing for FY2022–FY2025; (b) full FY2025 audited annual report (with formal IFRS 8 segment note) not yet in pool — only the preliminary FY2025 investor presentation; (c) no segment-level interest or tax allocation beyond FY2022 in the statutory data. Downstream agents (`unit-economics`, `competitive-map`) should note that margin and ROIC calculations at segment level will require the FY2025 audited filing when available.
