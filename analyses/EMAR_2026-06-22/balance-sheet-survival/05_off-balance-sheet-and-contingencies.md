## 5. Contingency Read

The largest off-balance-sheet exposure is the equity-method JV pipeline — AED 60 Bn+ in gross development value across Dubai Hills Estate (50:50 with Meraas), Emaar South (with DWTC), Rashid Yachts & Marina (70:30), and others — none of which sits on Emaar's consolidated balance sheet. If a JV partner faced liquidity pressure or a project required an unplanned equity injection, the obligation would land on Emaar's books from outside the consolidated perimeter; Note 16 already discloses AED 2,675 mn in specific JV contingent obligations, and the broader pipeline funding call risk is structurally unquantified. The second material item is the AED 5,487 mn performance guarantee to Dubai Municipality, which backs development completion obligations across active projects: it is a live, regulatory-required exposure that would crystallize only on project failure, and given the company's project completion history the probability is low, but at 5.1% of equity it is non-trivial. Against both exposures, the counter is Emaar's strong liquidity position (gross cash AED 52.6 Bn, with AED 42.9 Bn earmarked in DLD-regulated project escrow that is operationally matched to the completion obligations these guarantees back) and investment-grade ratings (BBB+/Baa1 as of FY2025); the contingent risks are real but do not threaten solvency under any plausible base-case scenario.
