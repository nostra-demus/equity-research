## What This Module Found

Emaar carries gross debt of AED 10,615 mn against EBITDA of AED 24,132 mn (FY2025) — gross leverage (debt vs annual profits before interest, tax, depreciation, and amortisation) of 0.44x, and on the strict basis (gross debt minus only freely usable cash, no investments netted) net leverage is just 0.04x. At Q1-2026 the company is in outright net cash of AED 2,115 mn on the strict basis. Leverage has fallen from 2.11x in FY2021, driven by debt paydown (from AED 19 Bn to AED 10 Bn) and EBITDA tripling. [Capital IQ Annual Income Statement + Capital Structure Summary, FY2025; Q1-2026 interim]

The single most important driver is that the near-term maturity wall is self-funded by cash already in hand: 52.8% of financial debt (AED 5,182 mn) is due within 12 months and is dominated by Sukuk Series 3 (AED 2,753 mn, September 2026), which the AED 12.2 Bn of unrestricted cash covers 4.4x without touching the AED 7,343 mn committed undrawn revolver or accessing capital markets. [FY2025 Preliminary Annual Report, Feb-12-2026, p.8]

The single most important risk is **disclosure-quality, not solvency**: AED 43 Bn of project-escrow cash sits alongside the freely usable AED 12.2 Bn of unrestricted cash, and no credit agreement, sukuk trust deed, or covenant schedule is in the data pool — so the covenant headroom figures are computed against assumed market-typical thresholds, not filed terms. At 0.04x net leverage and 49x interest coverage (EBITDA divided by interest expense), no plausible covenant design trips under any scenario tested.

## Bottom Line

- **Verdict:** Fortress balance sheet. Net cash strict basis at Q1-2026; gross leverage 0.44x; coverage 49x; survives the FY2020 trough with room.
- **Could be better than it looks:** the broad-basis net cash (which also nets liquid short-term investments) is AED 17.3–24.6 Bn, and the AED 154.8 Bn revenue backlog locks in 3–4 years of recognisable revenue — counter-cyclical optionality that most real-estate developers lack.
- **Could be worse than it looks:** no covenant documents are in the pool, so the indicative 93.9% / 99.0% headroom figures are directional only, not confirmed. Subsidiary debt in India, Egypt, and Pakistan may carry dividend blockers not visible in consolidated data.
- **What evidence is missing:** the sukuk trust deeds and the AED revolving credit facility agreement — together they would provide covenant definitions, change-of-control puts, cross-default provisions, and dividend-blocker language for AED 6,424 mn of sukuk and AED 3,673 mn of revolving credit. This is the single highest-value next request.
- **One thing to watch next:** the Sukuk Series 3 maturity in September 2026 — already self-funded, so the watch item is whether Emaar refinances, lets it run off, or partially redeems; the spread at which any rollover prices will set the cost step-up for the rest of the stack.

## Plain-English Glossary

- **EBITDA:** annual profit before interest, tax, depreciation, and amortisation — a rough proxy for the cash the business throws off each year.
- **Gross leverage / net leverage:** total debt (or debt minus cash) divided by EBITDA; how many years of cash profits it would take to repay the debt.
- **Strict / broad / gross-liquidity basis:** three ways to count "net debt." Strict = gross debt minus only freely usable cash. Broad = also nets in liquid short-term investments. Gross-liquidity = cash plus investments with no debt netted (Emaar's own "Net Cash AED 61.7 Bn" headline figure).
- **Sukuk:** a Sharia-compliant bond — for survival purposes, treated as ordinary debt with a coupon and a maturity.
- **Unrestricted vs restricted (project-escrow) cash:** unrestricted = freely usable for any corporate purpose; restricted = legally ring-fenced for specific real-estate projects under Dubai Land Department rules and not available to service debt.
- **Revolver / committed undrawn revolving credit:** a pre-arranged credit line the company can draw down if needed; "committed undrawn" means the lender has promised the money but it hasn't been borrowed yet.
- **Coverage (EBITDA / interest):** how many times annual cash profits cover the annual interest bill.
- **Covenant / covenant headroom:** lender-imposed financial rules in the loan documents (e.g. "EBITDA must stay above 3x interest"); headroom = how much room before the rule is breached.
- **FCF (free cash flow):** cash from operations minus capex — the recurring cash left after the business has paid to maintain itself.
- **Mid-cycle EBITDA:** an averaged EBITDA across the recent cycle (here, the FY2021–FY2025 five-year average of AED 14,000 mn), used as a more conservative base than today's peak.
- **Bps (basis points):** hundredths of a percent — 157 bps = 1.57 percentage points.
- **JV (joint venture) / equity-accounted:** a business Emaar co-owns with a partner; its results are carried as one line on Emaar's accounts rather than fully consolidated.
- **Change-of-control put / cross-default / springing covenant:** standard debt-document triggers that could accelerate repayment if ownership changes, if another debt defaults, or if a financial test is missed.
