**WAC distortion note.** The all-in WAC of ~6.3% is heavily distorted by subsidiary floating-rate facilities in high-inflation currencies: EGP revolving (19.5%–20.5%), PKR revolving (~21%), and INR term loans (6.55%–9.12%). The economically relevant refinancing rate comparison is the sukuk WAC (~3.73%) against the ~5.3% indicative refi rate, which drives the ~+157 bps step-up on the three parent-level instruments.

**Floating-rate sensitivity.** The floating share (~AED 990 mn; PKR KIBOR, EIBOR-linked AED revolver, CBE-rate EGP line) reprices each time the relevant central bank benchmark moves. A 100-bps parallel shift in all benchmarks would add roughly AED 9.9 mn to annual interest expense — immaterial (0.04% of FY2025 EBITDA of AED 24,132 mn).

**Next-24m wall coverage assessment.** The next-24-month maturity wall (AED 5,866 mn: AED 5,182 mn in 2026 + AED 684 mn in 2027) is covered more than 2× by unrestricted cash alone (AED 12,180 mn at Q1-2026), without touching short-term investments, revolver availability, or any FCF. The largest single item in the 2026 bucket is the Sukuk Series 3 (AED 2,753 mn, September 2026) — a parent-level instrument maturing in approximately three months from the run date. Cash on hand covers the Series 3 redemption ~4.4× unassisted; the balance of the 2026 bucket (AED 2,429 mn in subsidiary working-capital facilities) is described in the investor presentation as rolling annually and is not a term-redemption event in the same sense.

**Rating posture and market access.** S&P BBB+/Moody's Baa1, both Stable Outlook — one-notch upgrades each in 2025 (01_capital-structure-and-leverage.md). Emaar has issued sukuk in all three prior series without disclosed difficulty and retains access to the Gulf and international Islamic finance market. The credit profile (net cash, EBITDA/Interest 49×, AED peg eliminating USD basis risk) makes it among the strongest investment-grade names in the GCC real-estate segment.

**Market-closure scenario.** If unsecured capital markets closed entirely for 12 months (no new sukuk issuance), Emaar would still retire the September 2026 sukuk (~AED 2,753 mn) in full from existing unrestricted cash (AED 12,180 mn), with no reliance on market access. The subsidiary working-capital facilities (~AED 2,429 mn in the same year) are locally sourced revolvers that roll at the subsidiary level and do not require parent-level capital-market access. The conclusion is **self-funded under market closure for the next 12 months** — this assessment relies on the accuracy of the unrestricted-cash figure from Q1-2026 interim results; restricted-project-escrow cash (~AED 43 Bn) is excluded from this calcuation per §15 hygiene rules and module doctrine.

**One-line verdict: Self-funded / low refi risk.**

## 5. Refinancing Read

The 2026 maturity bucket (AED 5,182 mn, 52.8% of financial debt) arrives within 12 months of the run date, with the Sukuk Series 3 (AED 2,753 mn) due September 2026 — the single most material near-term event. Cash on hand as of Q1-2026 (AED 12,180 mn unrestricted) covers the entire 2026 wall 2.4× before any FCF is generated or revolver drawn, making the wall self-funding under even a full market-closure scenario. The cost of replacing the three sukuk has risen materially: the sukuk carry a weighted-average coupon of ~3.73%, and the indicative refinancing rate for a BBB+/Baa1 GCC issuer at matching tenor is approximately ~5.3% (5-yr UST 4.24% + ~100–120 bps spread, web-sourced Jun-18-2026, indicative/unverified), implying a step-up of approximately +157 bps on rollover — which would raise the annual interest cost on the sukuk pool by roughly AED 101 mn when all three are refinanced, an amount Emaar's EBITDA of AED 24,132 mn absorbs in under two business days. The single biggest refinancing risk is not a solvency or liquidity risk but a cost risk: the entire debt stack was locked in pre-2022 at rates that no longer exist, and the next decade of sukuk rollovers will permanently embed a higher interest expense — though at current earnings and cash-generation levels, this transition is financially inconsequential.
