**Trend summary:** Leverage has fallen dramatically — from 2.11x net debt / EBITDA in FY2021 to effectively zero (0.04x) in FY2025, with the company in a net-cash position on the strict basis at Q1-2026 (−0.08x). Three factors drove this: (1) gross debt has been paid down from AED 19.0 Bn (FY2021) to AED 10.6 Bn (FY2025) as maturing facilities were repaid rather than rolled at the same size; (2) EBITDA tripled from AED 7.8 Bn to AED 24.1 Bn as the 2021–2024 off-plan sales wave converted to IFRS 15 revenue; and (3) unrestricted cash nearly quadrupled from AED 2.6 Bn to AED 9.8 Bn. The one interruption was FY2024, where strict-basis net debt briefly rose to AED 2.6 Bn as the Short-Term Investment classification changed (Capital IQ reclassified AED 11.1 Bn of STI separately from cash, making the strict-basis figures more volatile quarter to quarter) — not an underlying worsening of the balance sheet. The trajectory is unambiguously deleveraging. [Capital IQ Annual Balance Sheet, FY2021–FY2025]

## 6A. HoldCo / OpCo & Structural Subordination

| Item | Evidence | Why It Matters |
|---|---|---|
| Where debt sits | The three USD sukuk series (AED 6,423.9 mn combined) and the main AED revolving credit (AED 3.7 mn drawn) and the AED unsecured term loan (AED 635.4 mn) sit at the Emaar Properties PJSC parent/group level. The INR term loans (AED 1,694.3 mn) sit at Emaar India (subsidiary ~77% owned). The EGP revolving facilities (AED 603.3 mn) sit at Emaar Misr / Egypt (subsidiary ~73.79% owned, EGX-listed). The PKR revolving facility (AED 430.8 mn) sits at Emaar Pakistan (subsidiary) | Subsidiary-level creditors (INR, EGP, PKR lenders) are structurally senior to the parent's sukuk holders for claims on subsidiary assets. Parent sukuk holders depend on upstream dividend flows from the subsidiaries |
| Upstreaming constraints (dividend blockers, regulatory) | Not disclosed in the data pool — no credit agreement, sukuk trust deed, or facility letter excerpt is in the pool. No formal dividend-blocker or covenant disclosure available | Potential block to dividends from Emaar Misr (EGX-listed, minority shareholders have rights) or Emaar India to the parent; if EGP or INR facilities contain dividend blockers, parent sukuk coverage could be impaired in a stress. Cannot be confirmed from available data |
| Material restricted / trapped cash | AED 42,878.9 mn (FY2025) of restricted cash sits in DLD-regulated project escrow accounts — legally earmarked for project completion. This is 81% of total reported cash including restricted; only 19% (AED 9,754 mn) is unrestricted. Any leverage analysis using the company's "Net Cash" of AED 61.7 Bn overstates available cash by ~AED 51.9 Bn | The restricted escrow cash cannot be used to service debt; net leverage on the strict basis is 0.04x, not the near-zero apparent from the gross liquidity figure. The distinction matters in a stress scenario |
| Minority interest at subsidiaries | Total minority interest AED 13,398 mn (FY2025) — Emaar Misr is a separately-listed Egyptian company (~26% public float); any upstreaming from Misr to Emaar Properties requires board approval and minority shareholder respect | In a financial stress, Emaar Misr's minority shareholders could contest a large dividend upstream, constraining the parent's access to subsidiary cash |

The parent's sukuk are senior unsecured at the parent level, but access to subsidiary cash flows for debt service is subject to normal dividend mechanics at each subsidiary. This creates structural subordination risk at modest scale relative to the overall balance sheet — given the parent itself holds AED 9.8 Bn unrestricted cash (FY2025) and AED 12.2 Bn (Q1-2026), the risk is low in the current environment but warrants flagging.

**Net cash flag (§15 and MODULE_RULES §8):** At the Q1-2026 period-end, Emaar is **net cash on the strict basis** (AED −2,115 mn, i.e. unrestricted cash exceeds gross debt by AED 2,115 mn). On the broad basis (including STI), the company is deeply net cash at both dates (AED −17,287 mn at FY2025 and AED −24,619 mn at Q1-2026). This near-zero / net-cash position is a **positive strategic-flexibility signal** per MODULE_RULES §8 and CLAUDE.md §24 Filter 3: it means Emaar can fund counter-cyclical actions (accelerate launches, support suppliers, acquire land, hold staffing) if a Dubai property downturn materialises, without depending on the refinancing market. The Third Series sukuk (AED 2,752.6 mn, Sep-2026) is by far the largest near-term obligation and is covered nearly 3.5x by unrestricted cash alone at the Q1-2026 level — even before drawing on the AED 7.3 Bn undrawn revolving credit. This is not characterised here as the company being "under-levered" — low leverage is unambiguously positive for capital safety, which is the only frame this module applies.

6. FY2025 EBITDA is at an elevated cyclical point (off-plan sales boom). Mid-cycle EBITDA of AED 14,000 mn is provided for stress calibration; even at that level, gross leverage of 0.76x is very low.
