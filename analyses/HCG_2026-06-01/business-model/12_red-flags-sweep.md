## 3. Most Severe New Flag

The single most material new flag is the **minority buy-out funding obligation**: ~₹3,318m of written put options (₹1,778.0m) plus forward-purchase liability (₹1,540.0m) committed to acquire non-controlling stakes, with ₹1,401.34m already classified current, against ~₹3.2bn of annual operating cash flow and ~₹9.1bn of gross debt [FY24-25 AR, Note 34, p.356; MD&A, p.~169]. The synthesizer should weight it because it is a contractual, near-dated claim on the very cash flow that business-quality showed is already almost fully consumed by expansion capex and below-EBITDA finance charges — and because management itself states, in its risk factors, that funding these partner puts depends on banks lending "per the planned timelines," which it "cannot assure" [FY24-25 AR, Risk Factors, p.~149]. This sharpens the existing leverage/capital-intensity picture rather than duplicating it: it is not "HCG has debt," it is "HCG has a self-disclosed, bank-dependent obligation to buy out minorities that competes with capex for the same constrained cash, and the company admits the funding is not assured." If MVT-driven export obligations or working-capital strain coincide with a tight credit window, the puts become the pressure point.

---

## 4. Cross-Cutting Patterns

A consistent thread runs through three of the new flags: **HCG's reported headline is cleaner than its underlying cash and control reality.** The audit opinion is unmodified yet the auditor records a revenue/payroll edit-log control gap; reported PAT is positive yet ~4x below the adjusted figure and one "normalisation" is a recurring labour cost; the balance sheet shows "nothing hidden" yet carries ~₹3.3bn of bank-funding-dependent minority buy-out obligations plus an EPCG export tail. None of these is individually disqualifying and none contradicts the disqualifier-scan's clean read — but together they argue that the synthesizer should treat HCG's accounting *presentation* as flattering relative to its *cash-claim* profile, and discount management's adjusted metrics accordingly. This pattern reinforces, rather than offsets, the capital-allocation module's control-and-leadership-instability flag: an unproven new owner and CFO are inheriting a structure with embedded minority put liabilities and thin true earnings.
