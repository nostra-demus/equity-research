# Business Identity — HCG

*HealthCare Global Enterprises Limited (NSE: HCG / BSE: 539787). Reporting currency INR. FY ends 31 March. Latest annual filing: Integrated Annual Report FY2024-25. Latest results: FY2025-26 (year ended 31 March 2026).*

## 1. What The Company Actually Does

HCG runs a chain of cancer hospitals in India. It owns and operates comprehensive cancer centres that put diagnosis and the three treatment modalities — radiation oncology, medical oncology (chemotherapy), and surgical oncology — under one roof at each site, supported by molecular pathology, PET-CT imaging and genomic testing [FY24-25 Annual Report, MD&A "Our business" / Oncology, p.163]. As of 31 March 2025 the network was 22 comprehensive cancer centres (one of them in Nairobi, Kenya) plus 4 multispecialty hospitals, spanning 10 Indian states and 19 cities, with about 2,500 beds and a network of 400+ oncologists [FY24-25 Annual Report, "Geographic presence", p.14-15]. The customers are cancer patients and their families, who pay either out of pocket, through private insurance / third-party administrators, through corporate tie-ups, or through government schemes; a portion of revenue comes from medical-value-travel patients from Africa, the Middle East, SAARC and CIS countries [FY24-25 Annual Report, MD&A, p.166; Q4/FY26 investor presentation, May 2026, payor-mix slide]. The company also runs Triesta Sciences, an in-house oncology diagnostics and genomics lab business (1 reference lab plus 12 diagnostic labs) that serves HCG and third-party hospitals, and Milann, a fertility / IVF chain (6 centres) [FY24-25 Annual Report, "Our specialised branches", p.10-11]. What the patient is paying to solve is a high-stakes, time-sensitive medical problem — getting an accurate cancer diagnosis and the full course of treatment in one place — in a country where roughly 70% of cancers present at an advanced stage and comprehensive oncology capacity is concentrated in a few metros [FY24-25 Annual Report, "HCG: Pioneering the future of cancer care", p.20-21]. Note: in May 2026 HCG agreed to sell 100% of the Milann fertility business to exit what it calls a "non-core" segment, so the fertility arm is on its way out of the company [Q4/FY26 investor presentation, May 2026, "Milann Divestment Update" slide].

## 2. How The Company Makes Money

This is a hospital operator. Revenue is essentially a capacity-utilisation-times-price business at each centre:

- **Oncology hospitals (the core, largest piece):** `Revenue ≈ operational beds × occupancy × realization per patient + outpatient/diagnostic/pharmacy revenue`. Management tracks bed occupancy (58% as of FY26) plus a blended "centre utilisation" metric covering chemo-bed, operating-theatre and radiation-machine utilisation, and average revenue per patient [Q4/FY26 earnings call, May 20 2026, prepared remarks + Q&A; FY24-25 Annual Report, MD&A, p.32]. Within oncology, the FY26 revenue split by modality was medical oncology ~38%, surgical oncology ~21%, outpatient oncology services (OPD, diagnostics, pharmacy) ~16%, and radiation ~14%, with the balance non-oncology [Q4/FY26 investor presentation, May 2026, modality-mix slide].
- **Diagnostics (Triesta):** `Revenue ≈ tests performed × price per test`, across routine pathology, molecular and genomic (next-generation-sequencing) assays, sold both inside HCG and to outside hospitals [FY24-25 Annual Report, MD&A "Precision Diagnostics", p.166].
- **Fertility (Milann — being divested):** `Revenue ≈ IVF/ART cycles × price per cycle`; 1,287 successful IVF cycles in FY25 [FY24-25 Annual Report, "Our specialised branches", p.10-11].

Consolidated revenue from operations was ₹22,228 million in FY25 and ₹25,454 million in FY26, up about 15% year on year [FY24-25 Annual Report, MD&A, p.167; Q4/FY26 investor presentation, May 2026, results slide].

What drives volume: number of operating centres, occupancy/utilisation at each centre, patient registrations, oncologist roster, and acquisitions (e.g., the Vizag/VHCRPL hospital consolidated from October 2024 added ₹501.7 million of revenue) [FY24-25 Annual Report, MD&A, p.167]. What drives price (realization): case mix toward higher-acuity surgical and radiation work, and payor mix — cash and insurance/TPA patients pay more than government-scheme patients, and management is deliberately shifting toward the former (FY26 payor mix: cash + TPA ~56%, government ~33%, medical-value-travel ~2%, corporates/others ~9%) [Q4/FY26 investor presentation, May 2026, payor-mix slide; Q4/FY26 earnings call, May 20 2026, Q&A]. What drives margin: centre maturity. HCG classifies centres into monthly-revenue "buckets," and as a centre moves from a lower bucket to a higher one (after the fixed cost of beds and machines is already built out), its margin expands; FY26 Adjusted EBITDA was ₹4,711 million at an 18.5% margin (up ~68 bps), and pre-tax ROCE improved to 14% [Q4/FY26 investor presentation, May 2026, profitability slide; Q4/FY26 earnings call, May 20 2026, prepared remarks].

## 3. Business Type Classification

Asset-heavy, single-specialty (oncology) hospital chain in India, monetised through bed/equipment utilisation and patient-level realization, with a centre-maturity ramp driving margins.

## 4. What Drives Variance

When revenue moves, the usual causes are volume (occupancy and utilisation across chemo beds, operating theatres and radiation machines), new-centre ramp-up, and acquisitions — newer "emerging" centres grew far faster than mature ones (FY25: emerging centres +32% vs the network's lower-teens growth), so the mix of young vs mature centres swings the growth rate [FY24-25 Annual Report, MD&A, p.152]. When margin moves, the cause is mostly mix — payor mix (cash/insurance vs government, which moved ~100 bps favourably over the prior two quarters) and case mix toward higher-realization surgical and radiation work — layered on the operating leverage of centres climbing into higher revenue buckets [Q4/FY26 earnings call, May 20 2026, Q&A]. Secondary swing factors are input costs (clinician/consultant fees, drug and consumables formulary, pay-per-use radiation-equipment charges) and the loss-making or sub-scale centres (notably some Mumbai assets in the sub-₹5-crore/month bucket) that drag blended margin until they ramp [FY24-25 Annual Report, MD&A, p.167; Q4/FY26 earnings call, May 20 2026, Q&A]. FX is a minor factor, limited to the Kenya operation and international medical-tourism revenue [FY24-25 Annual Report, MD&A, p.166].
