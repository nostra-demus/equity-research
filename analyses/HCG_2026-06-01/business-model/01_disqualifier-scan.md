## 2. Triggered Disqualifiers — Detail

No disqualifier triggered.

Two items were examined closely because they superficially resemble triggers, and both were cleared on primary evidence:

- **The FY24 restatement (Disqualifier 5)** is a pooling-of-interests restatement for a common-control business combination (the Nagpur slump-sale), mandated by Appendix C to Ind AS 103, not a correction of an accounting error or misstatement. The auditor's "Other Matter" paragraph states the opinion is "not modified" in respect of it [FY24-25 AR, print p.218; Note 45.1, print p.294]. A separate FY25 acquisition (Vizag/VHCRPL, 51% acquired Oct 2024) is normal purchase accounting with goodwill of Rs 2,070.14M, also not an error restatement [FY24-25 AR, Note 45.1, print p.373-374].

- **The promoter changeover (Disqualifier 2 context)** — between the FY25 annual report (Sep 2025) and now, control has been moving from CVC/Aceso to KKR, and founder Dr. B.S. Ajaikumar is reclassified as "Promoter and Non-Executive Chairman" [FY24-25 AR, print p.64 ("collaboration with KKR"); FY26 transcript, PDF p.8 ("promoter changeover and management change"); FY26 deck, PDF p.6]. This is a change of PE control plus a founder reclassification, not a pledge, enforcement, or audit event. No promoter-share pledge is disclosed in any document in the pool.
