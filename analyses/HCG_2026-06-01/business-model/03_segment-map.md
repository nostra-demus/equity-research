# Segment Map — HCG

*HealthCare Global Enterprises Limited (NSE: HCG, BSE: 539787). All figures INR million (₹ Mn) unless stated. Audited segment/revenue disclosures from the FY2024-25 Integrated Annual Report (year ended 31 March 2025); FY2025-26 figures (year ended 31 March 2026) are from the company's Q4/FY26 investor deck and earnings call, as the FY26 annual report is not in the data pool.*

---

## Headline

HCG reports **one** operating segment under Ind AS 108: **Medical and Healthcare Services** (cancer care). It is effectively a single-segment business — by the audited revenue-by-type split, ~93% of FY25 revenue is income from medical (hospital) services [FY24-25 Annual Report, Note 22 (Consolidated)]. The "segments" that matter economically are not reported segments; they are business lines and maturity cohorts the company discloses only in its investor deck. The decisive structural fact: the **Milann fertility business is being divested** (signed May 2026), leaving an almost-pure oncology platform.

## 2. Dominant Segment

**The oncology hospital network is the company — there is no second segment of consequence.** On the audited single-segment basis, Medical and Healthcare Services is 100% of revenue and profit [FY24-25 Annual Report, Note 36 (Consolidated)]. Stripping out the only material non-oncology line, the FY26 deck shows total revenue ₹25,454 Mn vs revenue excluding fertility ₹24,850 Mn, implying Milann ≈ ₹604 Mn (~2.4% of revenue); and total Adjusted EBITDA ₹4,711 Mn vs ₹4,642 Mn excluding fertility, implying Milann Adjusted EBITDA ≈ ₹69 Mn (~1.5% of group Adjusted EBITDA) [Q4/FY26 Investor Deck, May 2026, "Financial Highlights: FY26"]. So oncology contributes roughly 97.6% of revenue and 98.5% of Adjusted EBITDA.

This is a **single-segment business (>85% from one segment)** — I am not manufacturing sub-segments. The relevant internal split that drives value is not a product line but **centre maturity**: 4 mature centres (>₹100 Mn/month) generate 44% of revenue at 27% ROCE, while the 6 emerging centres (6% of revenue) run at **−22% ROCE** [Q4/FY26 Investor Deck, May 2026, "Path to Improve Profitability and Return on Capital"]. Where the value sits, and where it leaks, is a maturity story inside one segment — that is the read downstream `unit-economics` and `competitive-map` should carry, not a multi-segment story.

Profit is disclosed only at the consolidated/group level (one segment), so the dominant-segment call rests on revenue and management's "excluding fertility" Adjusted EBITDA bridge, not on audited segment profit — see §3.

## 3. Segment Disclosure Quality

Segment definition is **consistent year-over-year**: both the FY24 and FY25 annual reports state the identical single operating segment, "Medical and Healthcare Services," under Ind AS 108 [FY24-25 Annual Report, Note 36 (Consolidated); FY23-24 Annual Report, Note 36 (Consolidated)]. There is **no audited segment-level profit disclosure** — because the company runs as one segment, the filing gives only (a) revenue by type (medical services vs pharmacy vs other) and (b) revenue/non-current assets by India vs Outside India [FY24-25 Annual Report, Notes 22 and 36 (Consolidated)]. There is **no "Other" or unallocated-Corporate bucket** in the segment note (a single segment makes one moot), so the >10% "Other"-bucket concern does not arise.

The material disclosure gap is the opposite of an "Other" bucket: the economically important splits (business line, geographic cluster, centre-maturity cohort, modality mix, payor mix) exist **only in the investor deck**, not in audited segment accounting, and many are presented "excluding fertility" and on a "pre-Ind AS" basis — i.e., not reconciled to the audited statements. Treat cohort ROCE and centre-level EBITDA margins as **management figures (deck), not audited**. A reconciliation note: the FY26 deck reports FY25 total revenue as ₹22,228.5 Mn, which equals the AR's Note 22 figure of ₹22,181.36 Mn **plus** income from government grant ₹47.14 Mn — consistent, just a presentation difference [FY24-25 Annual Report, Note 22/24; Q4/FY26 Investor Deck, May 2026, P&L].

**Segment definition is about to change.** In May 2026 HCG signed the divestment of 100% of BACC Healthcare Private Limited (the Milann fertility brand) at an enterprise value of ₹632 Mn, expected to close in Q1 FY27; the fertility business's assets (₹667.1 Mn) and liabilities (₹344.4 Mn) are classified as held for sale at 31 March 2026, and FY26 carried a ₹319 Mn goodwill impairment on it [Q4/FY26 Investor Deck, May 2026, "Milann Divestment Update", Consolidated Balance Sheet note (1), and P&L note (1); May 20 2026 earnings call transcript, prepared remarks]. *Inference, not from filings:* post-close, FY27 disclosures should show an even purer oncology business (~97.5%+ → effectively 100% oncology), with International (Kenya) the only non-India, non-oncology-adjacent line remaining. Downstream agents should not expect a new reportable segment to appear; they should expect the existing single segment to get cleaner.
