## 2. Aggregate Quality Score

**Aggregate business quality: 52 / 100 (Mixed/Average).**

This is not a strict average of the rows; it is judgment-weighted. Two factors dominate the read in opposite directions and roughly offset: the demand side is high quality — low cyclicality (72) and low commodity dependence (70) on top of a structurally growing, non-deferrable oncology market with stable ~13–14% EBITDA margins (67) — while the capital and return side is weak — heavy and still-rising capital intensity (28) that has pushed group ROCE to ~5% and FCF margin to ~2%, with a third of revenue dependent on government tariffs (regulatory dependence 40). Secondary factors (pricing power 48, stickiness 56, recurring 52, competition 42, disclosure 57) cluster in the mixed band and pull the aggregate toward the middle rather than the extremes. The single fact that most caps the score is that a genuinely defensive, growing demand profile is being monetised through a capital-hungry roll-out that does not yet earn its cost of capital at the group level.

## 4. Read

HCG is a **structurally-growing, capital-intensive, single-line oncology platform with utility-like demand but sub-utility returns** — closest to a long-duration infrastructure build-out than to a durable compounder today. The demand is defensive and non-cyclical and operating EBITDA margins are stable, but the business consumes almost all of its operating cash flow in expansion capex and below-EBITDA finance/D&A charges, so it has not yet converted a good market into good group returns on capital. It is not a commodity or macro bet in disguise; it is a maturity-curve bet inside one segment. The single quality factor a buyer should watch over the next 24 months is **whether capital intensity converts into rising returns** — specifically whether the 14 ramping centres migrate into the >₹100 Mn/month, ~26%-margin / 27%-ROCE bucket and the 6 emerging centres turn ROCE-positive, because that migration (not pricing, which is roughly capped at ~5% medical inflation plus mix) is what would lift this from mixed quality toward genuinely high quality.
