**Direction and driver (2–3 sentences).** Leverage **rose materially into FY25, then fell in FY26.** The FY24→FY25 build was acquisition- and capex-driven: net debt incl. leases jumped from ~₹10.0 Bn to ₹16.0 Bn as HCG funded the Vizag (VHCRPL) acquisition (₹3,410 Mn consideration, ₹1,972 Mn net cash) and a step-up in growth capex (PP&E additions ₹2,089 Mn FY25), pushing net debt incl. leases to 4.13x reported EBITDA [FY24-25 AR, Note 45.1; business-model/11; earnings/01]. Under new control (KKR took sole control 30 May 2025), management states an **internal net-debt/EBITDA ceiling of 2.5x** (ex-lease basis) and cut headline leverage from "2.2x–2.3x to 1.4x" [Q4/FY26 transcript, Q&A; business-model/11].

## 6A. HoldCo / OpCo & Structural Subordination

| Item | Evidence | Why It Matters |
|---|---|---|
| Where debt sits (HoldCo vs OpCo) | Borrowings are spread across the listed parent (HealthCare Global Enterprises Ltd) **and** operating subsidiaries/LLPs (HCG Medi-Surge, HCG Oncology LLP, Suchirayu, Nagpur/NCHRI, Vizag/VHCRPL, Cancer Care Kenya). Term-loan security is granted at the operating-entity level (charges on those entities' assets), and the parent has given **corporate guarantees** for subsidiaries' term loans (₹631.57 Mn at 31 Mar 2025, plus a separate guarantee for bank-guarantee/cash-credit facilities) [FY24-25 AR, Note 46(c), p.~300; Note 18.1.1]. | HCG is an operating-holding company, not a pure HoldCo with isolated debt-at-top. Debt sits close to the assets that secure it; structural subordination of parent creditors is **limited** because lenders hold OpCo-level charges and parent guarantees rather than relying on dividend upstreaming. |
| Upstreaming constraints (dividend blockers, regulatory) | No dividend has ever been paid; consolidated retained earnings are **negative** (₹(4,092) Mn at 31 Mar 2025), which legally precludes dividends [business-model/11; FY24-25 AR, Note 16]. Subsidiary-to-parent cash movement is via inter-corporate loans/guarantees, several of which are impaired (provision for non-recoverability ₹181.63 Mn) [FY24-25 AR, Note 46(b)]. | Cash is retained in the group for capex/M&A; there is no dividend leakage. The negative retained earnings and impaired intra-group loans show some subsidiaries do not generate upstreamable surplus — relevant to where coverage actually sits (agent 04). |
| Material restricted / trapped cash | ~₹287 Mn margin money restricted (FY26); small Kenya operation (foreign) not separately quantified for cash-trapping in the pool [FY26 deck footnote; 00_triage]. | Restricted cash modestly overstates usable liquidity; quantified and flagged, not netted silently. |

**Conclusion:** Not a classic structurally-subordinated HoldCo/OpCo problem — debt is OpCo-level and secured, with parent guarantees, and there is no HoldCo-level bond stack relying on dividends. Structural subordination risk is therefore low-to-moderate, but the **negative consolidated retained earnings and impaired intra-group loans** are noted for downstream coverage/liquidity work. The triage's "HoldCo material debt but upstreaming unclear" cap (solvency strength max 70) is **not** fully triggered here because debt placement maps cleanly to operating entities; the cap that does bind is the no-maturity-schedule one (see Anchor caveats).

**Caveats downstream agents must propagate:**

2. **FY26 deleveraging is equity-funded, not cash-funded.** The drop from 4.13x (FY25, incl. leases) to 2.57x was driven by a ₹4,250 Mn rights issue, not free cash flow (FY26 FCF ~₹586 Mn). It is a one-time balance-sheet repair, not organic deleveraging [earnings/01].

5. **~96% floating-rate, largely unhedged** at ~8% average cost — a rate shock flows almost fully through to interest (agent 04/06).
6. EBITDA figures are post-Ind AS 116; the small adjusted-vs-reported gap (+₹53 Mn) means using reported EBITDA is conservative and adequate.

No solvency verdict is rendered here — that is the synthesizer's role (agent 99).
