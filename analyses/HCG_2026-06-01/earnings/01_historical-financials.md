# Historical Financials — HCG

**Company:** HealthCare Global Enterprises Limited (NSE: HCG, BSE: 539787) — pan-India oncology-focused hospital chain.
**Reporting currency:** INR million (₹ Mn), consolidated, unless stated. Fiscal year ends 31 March (FY25 = year ended 31 Mar 2025).
**Basis note:** All figures post-Ind AS 116 (leases capitalised). Two EBITDA constructs appear in HCG disclosures and are kept separate throughout: **Reported EBITDA** (audited statutory operating result, pre other-income) and **Adjusted EBITDA** (company-defined; Reported EBITDA + ESOP cost + one-time costs). See Section 4.

> **Data limitation:** The data pool contains audited statutory consolidated statements for FY23, FY24 and FY25 (FY25 and FY24 Annual Reports). FY26 figures are taken from the **board-approved audited results presentation dated 19 May 2026** (the standalone audited statutory results PDF is not in the pool). FY26 numbers are therefore cited to that filing, not to a full statutory statement. FY22 levels are available only from a company multi-year chart (deck), so FY22 detail below the revenue/EBITDA line is marked N/A.

## 6. Key Trend Summary

- **Revenue: Stable-to-decelerating.** Top-line has compounded ~16% over FY23–FY26 (FY26 +14.5%), but the within-year run-rate is cooling — FY26 quarterly YoY growth stepped from +16.6% (Q1) to +11.5% (Q4) [1, p.6; 3; 5]. Growth is volume-led (~12% FY26 volume, ~3% ARPP), broad-based across South/West/East clusters, with the small International (Kenya) business growing fastest (+71% FY26) off a low base [1, p.6, p.9].
- **Margins: Expanding at the EBITDA line, compressing at gross.** Reported EBITDA margin rose to 18.3% in FY26 from 17.2% in FY24 (+88 bps YoY in FY26), driven by operating leverage on employee and other costs as centres mature [1, p.5, p.20]. Gross margin slipped from ~75% (FY23/FY24) to 72.8% (FY26) as consumables/COGS rose as a share of revenue — the EBITDA gain comes from below the gross line, not from pricing.
- **Seasonality: Immaterial.** Each quarter is ~24–26% of annual revenue; Q4 is the largest at ~26% and Q3 the softest on margin. No quarter breaches the 30%/20% thresholds.
- **Inflection point — earnings quality, not revenue.** The clearest break in the last 3–5 years is the divergence between operating performance and bottom line: revenue, EBITDA and CFO all rose steadily, yet PAT to owners fell from ₹482 Mn (FY24) to ₹444 Mn (FY25) to ₹138 Mn reported (FY26), and FCF dropped to ₹586 Mn as capex climbed to ₹2,885 Mn. The cause is the acquisition/expansion phase (Nagpur, Vizag, North Bangalore): depreciation rose to ₹2,442 Mn and finance costs to ₹1,766 Mn in FY26, and FY26 carried two exceptional charges (₹319 Mn goodwill impairment on the fertility business, ₹127 Mn labour-code provision) [1, p.20; 2, p.313-314]. A ₹4,250 Mn rights issue in Q4 FY26 cut company-defined net debt to ₹3,387 Mn (0.73x reported EBITDA) from ₹6,317 Mn, and the non-core Milann fertility business is being divested (close expected Q1 FY27) [1, p.7, p.15].
