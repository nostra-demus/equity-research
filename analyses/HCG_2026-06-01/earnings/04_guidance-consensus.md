## 7. Bar Assessment

**Bar is fair — with a downward revision tilt on the bottom line.**

Rationale: (1) On the lines management actually guides, the bar is not soft. Consensus FY27 revenue (+14.3%) sits at/just below the bottom of the guided 15–20% range, and consensus is pricing a slightly *higher* FY27 EBITDA-margin expansion (~+141 bps) than the "~100 bps" management called "achievable" — so revenue is roughly in line and EBITDA is, if anything, marginally demanding, not lowballed. (2) The bottom-line bar, by contrast, has been cut hard and recently — FY27 EPS Normalized −19% over six months and −7.5% in the last month, net revision breadth −1 (3-month) worsening to −2 (1-month), with the cut concentrated below EBITDA (D&A / FY26 base reset) rather than in operating margin. (3) HCG has missed normalized-EPS consensus in three of the last four fiscal years while landing revenue/EBITDA within ~1.5% — a structural tendency for the Street to over-model the bottom line. The freshly-lowered EPS bar offsets that history somewhat, but the revenue/EBITDA bars are full and revision momentum is still negative, so this is neither a soft "beat-me" setup (not Low) nor an over-extended one (not High). Net: a fair bar where the most likely friction point is again below EBITDA (depreciation, finance cost, tax, exceptionals), not the top line.

Single most important watch-item for the FQ1 2027 print (release Jul-30-2026): whether revenue re-accelerates back toward the ~15% guided pace after the Q4 FY26 dip (low-margin paring + Middle East medical-value-travel softness, which management said normalizes "over the next couple of quarters"), and whether D&A/finance cost land where the cut estimates now sit.
