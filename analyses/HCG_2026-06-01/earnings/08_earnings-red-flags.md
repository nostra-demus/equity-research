## 5. Red-Flag Severity Verdict

**Material concerns.**

There are no Critical flags — no going-concern, fraud, auditor-resignation, or covenant-breach trigger, and the FY25 audit opinion is unmodified — so the earnings setup is not invalidated outright. But ten High-severity flags cluster on one fault line: the earnings setup is genuine and improving at EBITDA, yet weak, distorted, and decelerating below EBITDA, and the market narrative (rising target prices) is running ahead of a bottom line the Street is actively cutting (FY27 EPS −19% in six months). The single most dangerous red flag is the EBITDA-up / PAT-down divergence (reported PAT ₹482M → ₹444M → ₹138M while EBITDA rose 20%): it means "earnings accelerating" is true only at the operating line and false at the bottom line, so any synthesis verdict must specify the metric or it will mislead. What would resolve it: the FY26 audited statutory statements plus an FQ1 2027 print (release 30 Jul 2026) showing revenue re-accelerating toward the ~15% guide AND D&A/finance cost/tax landing where the cut estimates now sit — i.e., a clean, exceptional-free PAT that converts the EBITDA gain into bottom-line earnings.

## 7. Pre-Mortem — If The Earnings Setup Fails

If the earnings setup proves wrong, the most likely reason we missed it is that we let the EBITDA story stand in for the earnings story — the +20% EBITDA growth and +88 bps margin gain are real and cash-backed, but they sit on top of a bottom line that is structurally thin (FY26 PBT pre-exceptional was a fraction of revenue), exceptional-distorted (reported PAT ₹138M vs adjusted ₹557M), and exposed to forces below EBITDA — D&A from the capex build-out, finance cost, a noisy tax line, a recurring labour-code charge dressed as a one-off, and the bank-funding-dependent minority puts — exactly where the Street has missed EPS in three of the last four years and is cutting FY27 estimates now (flags #1, #5, #6, #27). The trap is that a reader anchors on the accelerating EBITDA and the rising sell-side target prices, models the bottom line off them, and is then surprised when FY27 PAT once again lands below consensus because the operating gain never made it through depreciation, interest, tax and exceptionals to EPS — the precise failure mode the four-year surprise record already telegraphs.
