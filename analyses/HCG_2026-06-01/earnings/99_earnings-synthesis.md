# Earnings Module — HCG (Synthesis)

## Abstract

HCG's earnings trend splits by line: revenue and EBITDA are accelerating (FY26 revenue +14.5%, reported EBITDA +20.3%, +88 bps margin), but reported PAT fell to ₹138 Mn from ₹444 Mn and intra-year revenue growth cooled from +16.6% (Q1) to +11.5% (Q4). The dominant driver is operating leverage as the ₹50–100 Mn ramping cohort (50% of revenue) matures on a near-flat 25-hospital, 58%-utilised base. The consensus bar is fair, not soft — FY27 revenue sits at the bottom of the guided 15–20% range and the EBITDA-margin bar (+141 bps) exceeds management's "~100 bps." The biggest risk is the structural below-EBITDA miss pattern: EPS missed consensus in three of the last four fiscal years and FY27 EPS-Normalized was cut 19% in six months. Verdict: mixed earnings setup.

## 5. Earnings Setup Summary

### Revenue Setup
The current ~14.5% reported trajectory is sustainable in direction but not at the FY26 level without re-acceleration: the +11.5% Q4 exit rate, not the +14.5% full-year figure, is the run-rate going into FY27, and consensus already sits at the bottom of management's guided 15–20%. The single factor that would have to change to flip revenue direction is the West-cluster / ramping-cohort utilisation ramp (West is 45% of revenue at only 50% utilisation) — if that ramp stalls while a government-scheme disruption recurs (as Andhra Pradesh did in Q3 FY26), volume growth rolls over. Reported and underlying revenue diverge by roughly 2.4 percentage points in FY27: the Milann fertility divestiture (~₹605 Mn, close Q1 FY27) subtracts from reported revenue while organic oncology growth continues, so reported FY27 growth will understate the organic rate by about that gap.

### Margin Setup
Current EBITDA margins are not at a cyclical peak in the usual sense — they are mid-ramp, with the +88 bps FY26 gain coming from spreading near-fixed costs over a ramping-cohort base, a tailwind that flattens (it does not reverse) once that cohort reaches the ~26% mature-cohort ceiling. The driver most able to take a large bite is COGS / drugs-and-consumables: a 10–20% adverse move in consumable inflation or a further shift toward drug-heavy medical oncology (38% of revenue) compresses gross margin faster than operating leverage can offset, because the regulated pharmacy slice is DPCO price-capped. HCG has no contractual margin-protection mechanism: pass-through is "slow and partial (annual mix/tariff cycle), not contractual," and on ~33% of revenue (government schemes) HCG is a pure price-taker — a structurally weaker margin-defence position than an operator with indexed escalators.

### Quality Check
The single largest gap between reported and economic earnings is at the PAT line, not at EBITDA: reported FY26 PAT (₹138 Mn) understates economic earnings because two genuine, disclosed exceptionals (₹319 Mn goodwill impairment, ₹127 Mn labour-code) crushed it — the adjusted-vs-reported gap is +305%, and this is the largest gap in the file. The adjustments are mostly genuine one-offs, with one exception that recurs: the labour-code provision is a statutory forward cost dressed as a normalisation, so even adjusted PAT (₹557 Mn) is modestly flattered, and the ESOP add-back recurs every year (now tiny). To model normalized FY27 earnings I would start from adjusted PAT (₹557 Mn), not reported (₹138 Mn) — but haircut it for the recurring labour-code cost and watch DSO, because the FY25 receivable build (+36% vs +16% revenue) shows a chunk of revenue converts to cash slowly with real credit-loss leakage.

### Consensus Bar
For HCG to beat the current bar by a material margin, Q4's two transient drags (deliberate low-margin paring + soft Middle East medical-value-travel) would have to normalise on management's "next couple of quarters" timetable AND the EBITDA-margin gain would have to run at or above the +141 bps the Street already prices — i.e. HCG must re-accelerate revenue and out-margin its own "~100 bps achievable" guide simultaneously. The bar is most likely set incorrectly at the FQ1 quarter level, where the ₹7,340.9 Mn revenue estimate implies ~+20% YoY against an +11.5% exit rate — that looks too high, and is the most probable miss-vs-bar even on healthy underlying growth. A meaningful slice of the current consensus is anchored to management's forward "normalisation" claim and to the rights-issue-driven interest-cost relief; the former is an unproven judgement, and a slower-than-promised normalisation would reset the forward bar down.

## 5b. Leverage & Capital Structure

Leverage is within normal range and did not change materially in an adverse direction during the period — no dedicated treatment required. Net debt (company-defined, ex-lease) fell to ₹3,387 Mn = 0.73x reported EBITDA at 31 Mar 2026, from ₹6,317 Mn (1.63x) a year earlier (01 §1–2; FY26 deck p.15) — below the 3.0x absolute trigger and a *decrease*, not the >1.0x adverse increase that would fire Trigger B. The de-leveraging was funded by a ₹4,250 Mn rights issue completed in Q4 FY26, not by free cash flow (FCF fell to ₹586 Mn), and management cites an internal 2.5x ceiling with current leverage "1.4x" on its own broader basis (04 §2). One capital-structure item from the cross-module sweep is carried forward in Section 8 as a master-synthesizer note, not as a leverage trigger: ~₹3,318 Mn of bank-funding-dependent minority put/forward buy-out obligations (BM 12) that compete with FY27 expansion capex for the same cash — this is a fundability/contingency item, not current balance-sheet leverage, and HCG's reported net-debt/EBITDA does not capture it.
