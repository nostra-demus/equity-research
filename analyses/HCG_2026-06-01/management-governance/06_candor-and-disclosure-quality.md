# Candor & Disclosure Quality — HCG

*HealthCare Global Enterprises Limited (NSE: HCG / BSE: 539787). Indian SEBI-LODR issuer, Ind AS, FY ends 31 March. Reporting currency INR (₹; management often uses ₹ crore — 1 crore = 10 Mn). Report date: 2026-06-01. This agent judges how management communicates when results are bad — owning misses vs obscuring them with adjustments and spin. It does not re-profile management (agent 01), re-score earnings quality (earnings/06, cross-referenced), value the company, or rate the stock.*

Cross-module inputs used: `01_management-and-track-record.md` (the promise-vs-delivery record), `earnings/06_earnings-quality.md` (non-GAAP aggressiveness, accruals), `earnings/04_guidance-consensus.md` (guidance reliability / beat-miss), and `earnings/08_earnings-red-flags.md` (narrative-vs-numbers flags, the auditor edit-log finding). All were available — **no partial-data cap applies.** Candor is assessed from two full bad-news transcripts plus the audited filings, so the read is not filings-only.

**Context that frames this read:** HCG is private-equity-controlled (KKR took sole control 30-May-2025), the CEO (Mattoo) has ~11 months in seat, and the third CFO in ~33 months joined end-May-2026 [01 §1–2]. So the communication pattern below is largely the **new KKR-installed team's emerging style** (two earnings calls and one strategy meet), while the multi-year *bottom-line* miss it has to explain spans the prior CVC-era team too. There are no pre-changeover (CVC-era) transcripts or a separate CEO shareholder letter in the pool — the candor lens is the current team's calls plus the FY24-25 / FY23-24 audited Annual Reports.

## 1. Owning the Misses

The misses to assess (from `01` §4 and `earnings/04` §6): the company hits revenue and EBITDA but **misses the bottom line** — normalized EPS missed consensus in 3 of the last 4 fiscal years (FY23 −19.2%, FY25 −24.5%, FY26 −8.9%; only FY24 beat), and FY26 reported PAT collapsed to ₹138 Mn from ₹444 Mn (FY25) and ₹482 Mn (FY24) even as EBITDA grew 20.3%. The worst-news period is therefore the FY26 result (PAT −69% YoY), discussed on the 20-May-2026 Q4/FY26 call; the prior bad-news quarter is Q3 FY26 (the Andhra Pradesh state-scheme strike), discussed on the 09-Feb-2026 call.

**Read:** the pattern is asymmetric. *Operating* bad news (Q4 revenue dip, the AP strike, the Odisha scheme) is owned — disclosed proactively or answered specifically. The *bottom-line* miss — the PAT collapse and the four-year EPS-miss pattern, which is the most important miss in the file — is **not owned**; it is omitted from the narrative and left to the deck's (transparent) reconciliation, while the framing stays on the EBITDA line where the record is good.

## 2. Non-GAAP / Adjustment Aggressiveness

Cross-checked against `earnings/06` §4–7 and `earnings/08` §2.7. HCG discloses Adjusted EBITDA (adds back ESOP + "one-time cost") and Adjusted PAT (adds back two exceptionals). It does **not** publish adjusted EBIT or adjusted EPS.

| Signal | Detail | Source |
|---|---|---|
| Recurring "one-off" addbacks | **Two recur and are flagged.** (1) **ESOP** is added back to Adjusted EBITDA *every year* (₹78.5 / ₹65.4 / ₹14.4 Mn FY24–FY26) — a recurring real cost excluded in principle, though now tiny (0.06% of FY26 revenue). (2) The **labour-code provision (₹127 Mn)** added back to reach Adjusted PAT is a **recurring forward statutory cost (gratuity/PF under the new Labour Codes) presented as a one-off normalisation** — the cash will be paid once the codes are in force. So the "clean" adjusted PAT is itself flattered. | `earnings/06` §4–5; `earnings/08` §2.7 ("labour-code 'one-off' is a recurring forward cost" — Medium) |
| GAAP-to-adjusted gap (magnitude / trend) | **At EBITDA: trivial — +1.15%** (₹4,658 Mn reported → ₹4,711 Mn adjusted). **At PAT: enormous — +304.7%** (₹138 Mn reported → ₹557 Mn adjusted). The PAT gap is driven by ₹319 Mn goodwill impairment (genuine, Milann-disposal-related, non-cash, one-off) + the ₹127 Mn labour-code item (recurring, mislabeled). Both sum exactly to the ₹445.8 Mn audited "Extraordinary Items" line (319.1 + 126.7 = 445.8 ✓). | `earnings/06` §7; `Q4&FY26 deck, p.8, p.20` |
| Does adjusted framing flatter reality? | **At EBITDA, no** — the adjustment is immaterial and the construct is honest (management's "~75% EBITDA-to-CFO" is even slightly conservative vs adjusted EBITDA). **At PAT, partly yes** — management leads on adjusted EBITDA/PAT and never states reported PAT on the call, and one of the two PAT add-backs recurs, so the headline "clean" figure (₹557 Mn) overstates the durable run-rate. Crucially, the *reconciliation itself is fully disclosed and the items are real* — this is **transparent flattery, not deception**: a reader who opens the deck can see exactly what was added back. | `earnings/06` §7, §9–10; Q4/FY26 transcript prepared remarks |

**Non-GAAP verdict: mostly disciplined at EBITDA, mildly aggressive at PAT.** The EBITDA construct is clean; the aggressiveness is (a) leading the entire narrative on adjusted figures while omitting reported PAT, and (b) treating one recurring statutory cost as a one-off. Because every adjustment is disclosed and reconciles to the audited line, this is a framing/emphasis issue, not a fabrication issue. *Net: lean toward "aggressive" on emphasis, "conservative/clean" on substance at EBITDA.*

## 3. Disclosure Transparency

Disclosure **got less transparent over time on the operating KPIs that matter, under the new team — a warning.** HCG dropped occupancy / ARPOB (bed-utilisation) reporting and replaced it with a company-defined, blended **"center utilisation" (58%)** metric; when an analyst explicitly asked them to restore the bed/occupancy metric ("with the change in reporting format, we have done away with bed or occupancy-related metric… help us add that"), the CEO declined and said they would "stick to" volume and ARPP [Q3/9M FY26 transcript, Q&A lines 442–450; Q4/FY26 transcript lines 264, 353–356]. Management also **refused to disclose center-wise economics** ("refraining from giving center-wise details") and **declined to quantify the low-margin revenue it foregone** ("Right now we may not be able to answer that question"), the one number an analyst needed for a like-for-like read [Q4/FY26 transcript lines 268–272, 406–409]. Separately, a disclosure that sits in the filing but is **omitted from management's "clean audit" framing**: the FY25 auditor's Rule 11(g) report states the audit-trail (edit-log) feature "was not enabled in the accounting software used" for certain revenue/consumption and payroll-master books (no tampering found) — a control-design gap on exactly the books where it matters [FY24-25 AR, Independent Auditor's Report, Rule 11(g), print p.219–220; `earnings/08` §2.7]. On the positive side, segment disclosure is structurally simple (single oncology segment, >97% of revenue), the cluster-level revenue/growth/volume split *is* given each quarter, and the Milann-divestiture economics were disclosed in full (EV ₹63 cr, equity ₹37.6 cr, 75% upfront / 25% over 18 months) [Q4/FY26 transcript lines 124–131]. Net: cluster and transaction disclosure is adequate; the **granular operating KPI that lets outsiders check capacity utilisation was removed under the current team and not restored on request** — the single clearest transparency warning here.

## 4. Tone in Bad Times

The tone in the worst recent periods is **promotional, not candid in emphasis** — though with genuine candor on operating specifics. Both bad-news calls (Q3 FY26's AP strike; Q4 FY26's PAT collapse) opened with extended clinical-showcase monologues (a tumour "encircling the optic nerve," a 97-year-old's robotic resection, a centenarian's immuno-radiation) and "profitable broad-based growth" language before reaching the numbers, and **neither call's prepared remarks stated reported PAT**, the year's worst figure [Q4/FY26 transcript p.2–4; Q3/9M FY26 transcript p.1–3]. Q&A is answered directly on most operating questions (the Q4 revenue dip, the AP strike days, receivables, margin-bucket moves) — the receivables answer even gave hard figures (Q3 billing ~₹25 cr, net receivable benefit ~₹5 cr) [Q3 transcript lines 472–484] — but it deflects on the harder governance/capital questions: the rights-issue rationale ("that is all I can divulge"), center-wise detail, and the quantum of foregone low-margin revenue [Q3 lines 318–321; Q4 lines 268–272, 406–409]. So: candid on *what happened operationally*, promotional on *how the year is framed*, and evasive on a few specific items where a straight answer would expose a weaker point.

## 5. Candor Read

**Verdict: mixed.** Management can be trusted to disclose *operating* bad news plainly — the Andhra Pradesh scheme strike was volunteered with a clean ex-AP growth figure, and the Q4 revenue miss was answered specifically — and every adjustment is disclosed and reconciles exactly to the audited "Extraordinary Items" line, so this is not deception. But it cannot yet be trusted to *own the bottom-line miss that matters*: the FY26 reported PAT collapse (−69% YoY) and the four-year normalized-EPS miss pattern are absent from the narrative on both bad-news calls, the entire framing leads on adjusted EBITDA/PAT while never stating reported PAT, one recurring statutory cost (labour-code) is dressed as a one-off inside that adjusted figure, and — the single biggest candor signal — the new team **removed the occupancy/bed-utilisation KPI and refused to restore it on a direct analyst request**, replacing it with a softer company-defined metric, while also declining to quantify the low-margin revenue it foregone. The strongest evidence *for* candor is the proactive AP-strike disclosure and the specific receivables answer; the strongest evidence *against* is the disclosure downgrade on operating KPIs plus the omission of reported PAT from how the worst year was sold. This is a private-equity-controlled company with a ~11-month-old CEO, so the read is a provisional one on an emerging communication pattern, and it lands as **mixed** — better than promotional/low-trust because the substance is disclosed and the audit is unmodified, worse than candid/high-trust because the worst news is obscured by emphasis and disclosure granularity was reduced under the current team.
