# Capital-Allocation Scorecard — HCG

*HealthCare Global Enterprises Limited (NSE: HCG / BSE: 539787). Indian SEBI-LODR issuer, Ind AS, FY ends 31 March. Reporting currency: INR (₹), consolidated. Report date: 2026-06-01. This module is the deep-dive behind the `business-model/11_capital-allocation-governance` quick-read and supersedes it where they overlap. It does not value the company or rate the stock.*

**Window:** A true 3–5 year scorecard is feasible — FY23, FY24 and FY25 are audited (FY23-24 + FY24-25 Annual Reports), and FY26 is from the board-approved audited-results presentation (19-May-2026) and the 20-May-2026 earnings call; FY22 levels are available only above the EBITDA line from a multi-year chart. Per the governance data triage, **no multi-year-history cap binds** [00_governance-data-triage §5]. One freshness caveat: the FY26 audited statutory statements and the FY26 Corporate Governance Report are **not** in the pool, so FY26 capital-allocation figures are cited to the results deck/transcript, not a full statutory filing — confidence on FY26 rows is set at 3 accordingly.

**Definitions used:** FCF = CFO − total capex (PP&E acquisition), per CLAUDE.md §15. Net debt is shown on two bases the company itself reports — company-defined ex-lease and balance-sheet incl. leases — and labelled in each row. ROCE figures quoted are the company's own "pre-Ind AS, post corporate-cost allocation, excluding fertility" basis [FY26 deck p.5]. Incremental ROIC is computed below from audited NOPAT and invested-capital change.

**The one fact that frames every row.** Control of HCG changed hands twice in five years (Founder → CVC/Aceso 2020 → KKR/Hector Asia ~51.59%, completed 30-May-2025) and almost the entire senior team turned over with it (KKR-nominee CEO from 30-Jun-2025; third CFO in ~33 months joining end-May-2026) [01_management-and-track-record §1–2; FY24-25 AR Directors' Report §15, p.76-77]. So the multi-year capital-allocation record below is largely the **prior (CVC-era) team's**, while the FY26 moves (rights issue, deleveraging, the Milann exit) are the **first acts of the new KKR-installed team**. The two must be read as separate regimes, not one continuous track record.

**Reinvestment read.** Capex is **growth-led and runs at/above depreciation** — FY26 capex ₹2,885m (growth ₹1,696m + maintenance ₹1,189m) vs D&A ~₹2,442m, and FY25 PP&E additions ₹2,088.67m vs depreciation ₹2,113.44m [FY26 deck p.14; FY24-25 AR p.314, p.329]. Blended FY26 ROCE is 14.0% against a 20%+ five-year target — a 6 ppt gap. So organic reinvestment is **value-creating at the mature-centre level but value-dilutive while new capacity ramps**, and the consolidated incremental return has not yet cleared a 20% hurdle.

**Forward capital claim the synthesizer must carry.** Beyond capex and M&A, HCG has **~₹3,318m of committed minority buy-out obligations** — written put options to NCI ₹1,778.0m (up from ₹931.58m a year earlier) + a forward-purchase liability ₹1,540.0m — of which **₹1,401.34m already sits in current liabilities**, and management's own risk factor states it relies on banks to fund "put options of the partners" and "cannot assure" the funding [FY24-25 AR Note 34, p.356; MD&A p.169; Risk Factors p.149; cross-ref business-model/12]. This is a near-dated, contractual claim on the same cash that capex consumes — a real future capital-allocation pressure point, not a current return-of-capital item.

## 5. Capital-Allocation Read

Over FY23–FY26 HCG returned **nothing** to shareholders (no dividend — legally constrained by ₹(4,092)m negative retained earnings — and no buyback ever) and put effectively all of its ₹12,004m four-year CFO, plus borrowings, into capex (~68% of CFO) and goodwill-heavy acquisitions (~33% of CFO; Vizag was 60.7% goodwill), then issued ₹4,250m of new equity in FY26 to repair a balance sheet that serial M&A had levered ~33% higher — so on a **per-share** basis this period created little to no value: reported PAT to owners fell from ₹482m (FY24) to ₹444m (FY25) to ₹138m (FY26) and diluted EPS fell from ₹3.43 to ~₹1.0 even as EBITDA grew, because the spending lands below EBITDA as depreciation and finance cost and one acquired asset (Milann) was impaired ₹319m and then sold to the founder. The **best** use was organic reinvestment into mature centres (which earn ~25% ROCE), though the blend is dragged to 14% by ramping new capacity and falls short of the 20%+ target; the **worst** was goodwill-heavy M&A whose only quantified return (Vizag ~2.9% pre-integration) is unverified against any hurdle and whose Milann leg round-tripped at a write-down to a promoter-linked buyer. The single biggest capital-allocation signal is that **growth here has been bought with debt and equity faster than it has converted to per-share earnings — under the prior owner — and the FY26 deleveraging that looks like discipline was funded by a rights issue, not free cash flow.** Conclusion: **mixed** (skewed value-destructive on the prior-regime M&A leg; the new KKR-era moves — delever, sharpen portfolio, target 20% ROCE — point toward improvement but have under one year of evidence and remain unproven).
