# Management & Track Record — HCG

*HealthCare Global Enterprises Limited (NSE: HCG / BSE: 539787). Indian SEBI-LODR issuer, Ind AS, FY ends 31 March. Reporting currency INR (₹). Report date: 2026-06-01. Proxy-equivalent = AGM Notice + Corporate Governance Report; KMP/management changes are disclosed in the Board's Report (§19/§21) and 8-K-equivalent exchange announcements/transcripts. Most recent audited governance disclosure is the FY2024-25 Integrated Annual Report (year ended 31-Mar-2025, filed Sep-2025); FY26 (year ended 31-Mar-2026) is covered by the 20-May-2026 earnings transcript and 19-May-2026 deck only — the FY26 audited Annual Report / CG Report is not in the pool.*

Scope: this agent answers who runs HCG, whether they are stable, and whether they delivered on what they promised. It does not score capital allocation (02), incentives (03), ownership (04), the board (05), or candor (06) in detail, and it does not value the company or rate the stock. Cross-module inputs used: `business-model/02_business-identity.md` (control context), `business-model/11_capital-allocation-governance.md` (turnover/control facts), and `earnings/04_guidance-consensus.md` + `earnings/99_earnings-synthesis.md` (guidance and beat/miss record). All three were available.

**One fact governs this entire read:** the people running HCG today are almost all new, installed by a new controlling owner. KKR (via Hector Asia Holdings II Pte Ltd) acquired sole control (~51.59%) on 30-May-2025; a KKR-nominee CEO took over on 30-Jun-2025; a third CFO in ~33 months joins at end-May 2026; and the founder moved to a Non-Executive role. The current configuration has roughly 0–11 months of tenure together, so its own promise-vs-delivery record is mostly not yet observable. The checkable record below belongs largely to the *prior* (CVC-era) management and to the company across owners.

## 3. Stated Strategy & Targets

Management's stated plan under the new (KKR-installed) CEO is **profitable, return-focused growth on the existing oncology network rather than top-line chasing**: deepen utilisation of the 25-hospital / ~58%-occupancy base, shift case- and payor-mix toward higher-realization work, add brownfield capacity, and exit the non-core Milann fertility business (sale agreed May-2026) [Q4/FY26 transcript, prepared remarks + Q&A; business-model/02]. The explicit, checkable targets it has set: (a) sustained revenue growth of "~15%," with a "15-20%" range cited; (b) EBITDA margin expansion of "~100 bps" in FY27 (off FY26's 18.5% adjusted), and EBITDA growing faster than its historical ~18% CAGR; (c) a 5-year **pre-tax ROCE target of "at least 20%+"** (FY26 actual 14%); (d) a **net-debt/EBITDA internal ceiling of 2.5x** (currently 1.4x); and (e) "200+ beds" of brownfield capacity over the next 24 months plus 8-10 day-care centres [earnings/04_guidance-consensus §2; Dec-04-2025 Investor & Analyst Meet transcript; Q4/FY26 transcript]. HCG does **not** give formal point/range revenue, EBITDA, or EPS guidance — targets are qualitative/directional.

**Read of the record:** the company reliably hits its *revenue and EBITDA* lines (FY26 ~15% / +88 bps; revenue and EBITDA within ~1.5% of consensus every year for four years) but reliably **misses the bottom line** — normalized EPS missed consensus in three of the last four years, and reported FY26 PAT collapsed to ₹138 Mn even as EBITDA grew 20%. The misses sit *below* EBITDA (D&A, finance cost, goodwill-heavy acquisitions, exceptionals), which is exactly where serial M&A and rising debt land. The FY26 deleveraging "promise" was kept, but through a ₹425 cr equity raise, not free cash flow. So: directional operating promises kept; per-share/earnings promises mostly missed; the highest-value target (20%+ ROCE) is unproven and 6 percentage points away.

## 5. Management Read

HCG is a private-equity-controlled (KKR) oncology operator whose entire top team is new: a CEO with ~11 months in seat (ex-Apollo regional CEO), the **third CFO in ~33 months** joining end-May-2026, and the founder moved to a non-executive role — so the *current* team's promise-vs-delivery record barely exists yet and the prior multi-year record belongs to a CVC-era management that has been replaced. On the record that is checkable, the company keeps its revenue and EBITDA promises (FY26 ~15% growth, +88 bps margin, delevered to 1.4x) but has **missed the normalized-EPS bar in three of the last four fiscal years** and saw FY26 reported PAT fall to ₹138 Mn, so promises are best described as **kept at the operating line, missed below it.** The single biggest management signal is the combination of two control changes in five years and near-total C-suite turnover (CFO three times) — a clean controls function (no auditor/CompSec change, unmodified audit) keeps this from being a fraud/integrity flag, but it resets the meaning of any track record and makes execution durability under KKR the open question; the read is **partially capped because there is no checkable delivery record for the team actually in charge today.**
