# Ownership & Insider Behavior — HCG

*HealthCare Global Enterprises Limited (NSE: HCG / BSE: 539787). Indian SEBI-LODR issuer, Ind AS, fiscal year ends 31 March. Reporting currency INR (₹). Report date: 2026-06-01. The proxy-equivalent is the AGM Notice + Corporate Governance Report; ownership is the shareholding-pattern filing under SEBI-LODR Reg-31; insider transactions are captured via SAST/PIT disclosures, ESOP allotments, and the SPA transfer — there is no US Form-4-style granular open-market buy/sell log in the pool. Most recent **audited** shareholding disclosure is the FY24-25 Integrated Annual Report (as-of 31-Mar-2025, filed Sep-2025). The most current ownership read is the Capital IQ Public Ownership export (position dates Dec-31-2025), which captures the post-KKR-changeover picture but is a vendor source (confidence 2). A post-changeover FY26 audited annual report / detailed Reg-31(1) shareholding-and-encumbrance pattern is NOT in the pool.*

Scope: this agent maps who owns HCG, whether insider stakes were bought or granted, whether insiders are buying or selling, whether shares are pledged, and who controls the votes. It does not assess compensation (agent 03) or board independence (agent 05), and it does not value the company or rate the stock. Cross-module inputs used and available: `business-model/01_disqualifier-scan.md` (pledging/related-party verdict), `business-model/11_capital-allocation-governance.md` (control/pledge facts), `business-model/12_red-flags-sweep.md` (minority put obligations, RPT context). Upstream in-module input used: `01_management-and-track-record.md` (control history) and `00_governance-data-triage.md` (source map). All were available.

**One fact governs this read:** HCG is a private-equity-controlled company, not a founder/owner-operator one. KKR (via Hector Asia Holdings II Pte. Ltd.) acquired sole control on 30-May-2025 and held **50.84%** at Dec-31-2025 [CIQ Public Ownership Summary, Top Holders, position date Dec-31-2025]. The founder, Dr. B.S. Ajaikumar, retains a pre-IPO promoter stake of **~9.22%** but is now a Non-Executive Chairman with no executive role [CIQ Public Ownership, Dec-31-2025; FY24-25 AR §15.4]. The "skin in the game" here is overwhelmingly a financial sponsor's control block bought for cash, plus a residual founder holding — not management buying with conviction. No insider open-market buying or selling is recorded in the pool, and **no promoter share pledge is disclosed anywhere.**

## 5. Ownership / Insider Read

Skin in the game is a **financial sponsor's cash-bought control block (KKR/Hector Asia, 50.84%), not management buying with personal conviction** — the CEO and incoming CFO are KKR appointees with no disclosed personal stake, and the founder's residual ~9.22% is a legacy pre-IPO holding that he neither added to nor visibly sold on the changeover, so the insider signal is **neutral, not bullish**. The net insider "activity" of the last year was the negotiated CVC→KKR block transfer (51.59% at a disclosed SPA price plus a 26% open offer at ₹504.41/sh) — a control transaction, not an open-market vote of confidence, and there is no insider open-market buying or selling to read as conviction or as a red flag. The biggest control/pledging risk to minorities is **not pledging (none is disclosed) and not vote-rigging (one ordinary class, no super-voting) but PE-horizon alignment**: a single sponsor controls the votes on an exit clock, the founder shares promoter status under an undisclosed-terms Promoter Agreement, and the Chairman is simultaneously the buyer of the divested Milann business (a promoter-linked RPT to monitor) — with the post-changeover Reg-31 encumbrance line still unverified.
