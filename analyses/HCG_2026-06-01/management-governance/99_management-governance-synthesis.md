## Abstract

HCG is a competent oncology operator under new private-equity control, but it is not yet a demonstrated steward of per-share value, so the stewardship call is standard-to-mixed with weak per-share results. Over FY23–FY26 capital went almost entirely into growth capex (~68% of CFO) and goodwill-heavy M&A (Vizag was 60.7% goodwill), nothing was returned, and diluted EPS fell from ₹3.43 to ~₹1.0 even as EBITDA rose — value creation below EBITDA has been weak. Disclosed pay rewards revenue, absolute profit and margin (no ROCE/EPS/TSR metric), the current CEO's and CFO's pay is undisclosed, and skin in the game is a sponsor's cash-bought 50.84% block, not management conviction buying. The biggest governance signal is the omission of FY26 reported PAT (−69%, ₹138 Mn) from how management framed its worst year, alongside ~26% minority votes-against a promoter-funded bonus. No hard disqualifier; verdict: standard/mixed.
