## 2. Triangulation & Reconciliation

**Weighted base-case fair value: ≈₹520–₹620 per share (point estimate ~₹575).** Weighting each method's central/defensible figure — own-history ₹624–667 cluster (mid ~₹645) × 25%, peers warranted midpoint ~₹603 × 35%, DCF cash-flow-disciplined ~₹142 × 30%, SOTP base ~₹530 × 10% — gives a blended ~₹490; replacing the DCF's pure cash-flow point with the price-defensible exit-multiple lens (~₹604, the only DCF figure that approaches the quote) lifts the blend to ~₹575. The honest base case is a **range, ₹520–₹620**, reflecting that disagreement, with a midpoint roughly **−11% below the ₹646.15 price**.

The methods agree on the conclusion and disagree on the magnitude. **Three of four value-producing methods (peers ~₹603, SOTP ~₹530, DCF cash-flow ₹142) and the reverse-DCF all point below the price; only own-history's 6-quarter EV-multiple reversion sits at the price (₹624–667).** I trust the **peer/relative method (03) most** for HCG because it does the one thing the others cannot: it shows the EBITDA "discount" to peers (−32%) is **not a discount at all once you reach profit** — EV/EBIT is a +9% premium and NTM P/E a +64% premium — which is exactly the trap a naive own-history or EBITDA-only read would miss. The DCF (04) corroborates the direction with the structural reason (ROIC 4.5–5.0% < WACC 10.7%), but its width (₹90–767) means it cannot set the level alone. The own-history read (02) is the weakest *as a value* because there is no clean multi-year mean to revert to — the only signal it adds is that, against its own last six quarters, the operating multiple is mid-range, not stretched.

**The high-to-low spread across the defensible method outputs is large but not the headline.** Within the disciplined reads the spread runs ₹142 (DCF cash-flow) to ₹714 (peer warranted high) — a >5x range that would trip the >40% cross-method flag. But that spread is **methodological, not informational**: the DCF low and the multiple-based highs are answering two different questions (what cash can the business distribute funding its own growth at a 5% ROIC, vs what will a buyer pay for hospital EBITDA at 15–25x). The genuine headline is narrower and more useful: **on every method that prices HCG the way the market actually prices hospitals — peers, SOTP, own-history EV multiples — fair value clusters ₹530–₹645, i.e. at-to-below the price, and the only way to reach materially above the price is a multiple HCG's returns do not warrant.** The cross-method disagreement is therefore reconciled, not averaged: the low tail (DCF cash-flow) is the margin-of-safety anchor, the multiple-based cluster is the base case, and nothing supports a value comfortably above the quote.

## 4. Margin of Safety

Sign convention: margin of safety = (base FV − price) / base FV; **positive = price below fair value = margin of safety exists; negative = price above fair value = no margin of safety**. Base FV midpoint ~₹575; bear FV taken at the ₹400–₹480 band (using the ₹440 midpoint as the central bear, with ₹480 as the shallow-bear and ₹400 as the deep-bear).

| Metric | Value |
|---|---:|
| Current price | ₹646.15 |
| Base-case fair value (midpoint) | ~₹575 (range ₹520–₹620) |
| Bear-case fair value | ~₹440 (range ₹400–₹480) |
| Upside to base case (%) | (575 − 646.15) / 646.15 = **−11.0%** (price is ~11% ABOVE base FV — no upside to base) |
| Downside to bear case (%) | (440 − 646.15) / 646.15 = **−31.9%** (price ~32% above the central bear) |
| Margin of safety = (base FV − price) / base FV | (575 − 646.15) / 575 = **−12.4%** (NEGATIVE — no margin of safety; price sits above base fair value) |

**Read:** there is **no margin of safety at ₹646.15** — the price sits ~11% above the base-case fair value and ~32% above the central bear case. A buyer at today's price is paying for the bull-case cohort-maturity inflection in advance, with ~32% of downside to a defensible bear value if that inflection stalls or reverses. The price band itself carries ~1.4% snapshot noise (anchor ₹646.15 vs CIQ ₹637.25), immaterial to this conclusion.

## 5. Warranted-Multiple Check

**The base case does not require a multiple HCG has never earned — it requires HCG to hold roughly today's NTM EV/EBITDA (18–19x), which is below its own trailing 23.4x and a warranted discount to the 34.6x peer median; that is defensible. The problem is the price, not the base case.** To justify ₹646.15, the DCF needs a ~16x FY31 exit multiple (achievable only if the rich hospital multiple persists for five-plus years) or, on a disciplined Gordon basis, perpetuity growth ≈ the WACC — which is not a defensible long-run rate (`04` §6). HCG earns a 4.5–5.0% return on capital against a ~10.7–12.4% cost of capital and has not crossed its cost of capital in any of the last six years (`business-model/09` §3), so the EV/EBIT (+9%) and NTM P/E (+64%) **premiums** to peers are unwarranted for a bottom-of-set operator. **Value-trap risk is explicit:** the headline EV/EBITDA "discount" (−32% to peers) is not a buying signal — it is the market correctly paying less per rupee of HCG EBITDA because HCG converts EBITDA into profit far worse than peers (heavy depreciation ₹2,442M, finance cost ₹1,766M, thin/volatile PAT). Anyone underwriting upside on that discount, rather than on the cohort-maturity inflection actually re-rating returns above cost of capital, is buying the trap.

## 6. Fair-Value Read

Fair value is a range of **≈₹400 (bear) / ~₹575 (base, band ₹520–₹620) / ~₹735 (bull, band ₹700–₹770)**, and at ₹646.15 the stock trades **above its base-case fair value with no margin of safety** (−12.4%) and ~32% above a defensible bear. The **relative/peers method (03) drives the answer**: it shows HCG's EBITDA "discount" to peers is warranted and evaporates into a premium the moment you reach EV/EBIT and P/E, so the company is at-to-slightly-rich versus peers once its bottom-of-set margins, highest leverage, and sub-cost-of-capital returns are priced — corroborated by the DCF's structural ROIC-below-WACC finding and the reverse-DCF's ~33% implied FCF CAGR the company has never produced. The single biggest swing factor between bull and bear is **whether the 14 ramping centres complete the cohort-maturity migration into the ~26%-margin / 27%-ROCE bucket (lifting volume/utilisation from 58% blended and pushing ROIC above cost of capital) or stall** — the same unproven inflection that separates a ~20x re-rating (₹700–770) from a 12–14x de-rating into compounding downside drivers (₹400–480).
