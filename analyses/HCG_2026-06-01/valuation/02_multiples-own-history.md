## 3. Re-Rating / De-Rating Read

**EV-based multiples: mid-to-low range versus the last six quarters; below the FY25 peak but above the FY22–FY24 norm.** On the cleanest like-for-like window (Source A), current LTM EV/EBITDA (23.4x) sits at the **39th percentile**, **−3.4% below its own 6-quarter mean (24.20x)** and **−4.6% below the median (24.48x)**; LTM EV/EBIT (48.95x) is at the 30th percentile (−5.3% vs mean); EV/Sales (4.29x LTM, 60th percentile) is essentially **at its own mean (+0.6%)**. So on operating multiples the stock has **de-rated modestly off its early-2025 highs and now sits mid-range** — not stretched, not washed out. Against the longer Source B annual history, however, current EV/EBITDA (23.4x reported) is **above** the FY22–FY24 cluster (~18–19x) and only below the FY25 snapshot peak (~29x) — i.e., versus a 4-year lens the operating multiple is **above its own historical centre**, which the 6-quarter window alone hides.

**Equity multiples: at the bottom of the range — but mechanically, not because the market re-priced quality down.** Current P/Book (7.02x) and P/Tangible Book (10.26x) are both at the **0th percentile (series low)**, −18.8% and −36.6% below their 6-quarter means. This is **not** a clean de-rating signal: the ₹4,250M rights issue (completed Q4 FY26, oversubscribed 1.3x; `earnings/01`, `01`) lifted book value per share from ~₹59 (FY25, ~139.4M shares) to ~₹88 (FY26, 149.3M shares) while the price was roughly flat — so P/BV reset to the series low **arithmetically**, because the book denominator jumped, not because the equity was marked down. Likewise the reported LTM P/E spiking to ~673x reflects the FY26 PAT collapse (₹319M goodwill impairment + ₹127M labour-code charge + higher D&A/finance cost), not a multiple the market is actually paying — which is why the forward P/E (74x NTM, 24th percentile) is the honest equity reading and itself sits below its own 6-quarter mean.

**Most likely reason for the spread:** leverage and depreciation, not sentiment. The capex/expansion phase (Nagpur, Vizag, North Bangalore; D&A ₹2,442M, finance cost ₹1,766M in FY26 — `earnings/01`) compressed earnings-based equity multiples while the EV multiples (which sit above the earnings hit) held mid-range. The rights issue then re-set book value. The net effect is a **divergence between near-fair EV multiples and floor-level equity multiples that is mechanical** — a leverage/D&A/share-count story, not a market re-rating of business quality.

The equity multiples imply more upside (P/E NTM ₹714–₹724; P/BV ₹749–₹758), and the longer annual EV/EBITDA implies downside (₹496–₹569). **Full reversion range across all multiples: ≈₹496–₹758.** The tight cluster on the operating multiples (₹624–₹667) is the highest-confidence read; the wider tails depend on which history and which multiple you trust.

**Reversion assumption — and whether evidence supports it (required).** Reverting to mean assumes the **warranted multiple has not structurally changed.** Two of the rows fail that test and should be discounted:
- **P/BV reversion to 8.5–8.6x is NOT warranted.** The mean was set when book value was ~₹59/share pre-rights; the ₹4,250M rights issue permanently raised book to ~₹88/share, so the old P/BV mean is computed on a different (smaller) equity base. Applying it to today's larger book double-counts the equity raise — the ₹749–₹758 implied price is overstated for that reason.
- **P/E NTM reversion to ~82x carries the EPS-estimate risk flagged in `earnings/04`.** FY27 EPS-N (₹8.72) has been cut −19% over six months and HCG has missed normalized-EPS consensus in three of the last four fiscal years; the ~82x mean was struck on higher EPS estimates, so the ₹714–₹724 implied price rests on both a high multiple and a still-falling earnings base.
- **EV/EBITDA reversion is the most defensible** because EBITDA (the operating line) has been stable-to-rising and lands within ~1.5% of consensus, and the multiple has not structurally moved (still 22–26x LTM). But note the **direction depends on the window**: against the 6-quarter mean it implies ~+2–3%; against the longer FY22–FY25 mean/median (18–21x) it implies **−12% to −23%**, because the stock re-rated upward through FY25 and the current multiple sits above its 4-year centre. That window-dependence is itself the finding — there is no single "the mean" to revert to.

## 5. Own-History Read

1. Against its own **last six quarters**, HCG trades **mid-to-low range on operating multiples** — LTM EV/EBITDA 23.4x is −3.4% below its own mean and at the 39th percentile, and EV/Sales is essentially at its own mean — so reverting the EV multiples to their recent centre implies only ≈₹624–₹667 (within ±3.5% of the ₹646.15 price); the stock is **near its own recent EV-multiple fair value, not stretched and not washed out.**

2. The **equity multiples sit at series lows** (P/BV 7.0x and P/Tangible Book 10.3x both at the 0th percentile, reported LTM P/E effectively NM at ~673x), but this is **mechanical, not a quality re-rating**: the ₹4,250M rights issue lifted book per share from ~₹59 to ~₹88, and FY26 PAT was gutted by a ₹319M goodwill impairment, a ₹127M labour-code charge, and rising D&A/finance cost — so the floor-level equity multiples reflect a bigger denominator and a one-off-hit numerator, not the market marking the business down.

3. **Single biggest caveat:** there is **no clean multi-year mean to revert to**, and the windows disagree directionally. The clean like-for-like series is only ~15 months long; the only ~4-year series is a once-a-year Sept snapshot on a narrower EBITDA definition that shows the current EV/EBITDA (23.4x) sitting **above** the FY22–FY24 norm (~18–19x) and below only the FY25 re-rating peak (~29x). Reverting to the 6-quarter mean says +2–3%; reverting to the 4-year mean says −12% to −23%. The P/BV "discount to mean" must be discarded outright because the rights issue structurally reset book value — the old P/BV mean is not a warranted target.
