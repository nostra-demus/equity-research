# Earnings Module — BG (Synthesis)

## Abstract

Bunge's earnings setup is genuinely mixed: management's adjusted framework points to a positive inflection (FY26 EPS guide raised from $7.50–$8.00 to $9.00–$9.50), but GAAP cash earnings are decelerating sharply. The biggest driver in both directions is biofuel-policy-linked crush margin on Soybean + Softseed, which together produce 75% of segment EBIT. Consensus has already priced the upper bound of the just-raised guide, leaving little cushion. The single biggest risk is the cash-conversion collapse — CFO/EBITDA fell from 84% to 38% in FY25 while FCF turned negative $879M and net debt reached $12.9B. The verdict is Mixed earnings setup with the dual narrative held simultaneously.

## 5. Earnings Setup Summary

### Revenue Setup
The FY25 +32.4% headline is essentially all Viterra inorganic step-up — every segment MD&A attributes increases to "the Acquisition of Viterra." This is a one-time scale change that annualizes through Q3 2026, after which the FY27 comp turns brutal because organic ex-Viterra revenue almost certainly declined in FY25. The single factor that would have to flip the revenue direction is **biofuel-policy-driven realized vegoil price** — an RVO walk-back or 45Z weakening compresses soy-oil prices, which compresses crush, which slows farmer selling, which compresses origination volumes — a four-link chain that pulls roughly two-thirds of consolidated revenue in one direction simultaneously. The gap between reported and underlying organic revenue is wide enough that a clean Viterra-pro-forma comparable should be the first ask of management on the next call.

### Margin Setup
Current margins are at a cyclical inflection but not a cyclical peak — gross margin already compressed 329 bps over FY23–FY25 from the FY22/FY23 biofuel-driven peak, and management itself describes refining premiums as "not where we were back in '22 and '23." The single segment that takes the largest bite from a 10–20% adverse move is **Soybean Processing** (52% of FY25 revenue, 53% of segment EBIT). Bunge has effectively no margin-protection mechanism — the business-model value chain analysis confirms there is no formal pass-through on either side of the crush spread; the Q1 FY26 bunker-fuel spike hit Grain Merchandising EBIT with $122M of swing in one quarter and no offset. The company is a pure price-taker on inputs and outputs, with hedging that mitigates timing but not direction.

### Quality Check
The single largest gap between reported and economic earnings is the **CFO/EBITDA collapse** from 84% (FY24) to 38% (FY25) — and it is widening, not narrowing (Q1 FY26 CFO was $(541)M, more negative than Q1 FY25's $(285)M). The "adjustments" management uses are mixed in quality: SBC is appropriately included in adjusted EBIT (a positive sign), but Viterra integration costs have been "one-time" for four consecutive reporting periods totaling $622M, and the Q1 FY26 $336M MTM gap is mechanical but consistently appears. To model normalized FY27 earnings I would start from GAAP and add back only genuinely non-recurring items because the adjusted framework relies on a quarter (Q1 FY26) that was simultaneously aided by negative tax rate, $336M MTM tailwind on reconciliation, and the largest beat magnitude in 9 quarters.

### Consensus Bar
For Bunge to materially beat the FY26 bar, management would need to raise FY26 EPS guidance again above $9.50 — Street already sits $0.18 above the $9.25 midpoint and only $(0.07) below the top end. The bar is most likely set **too high on the full year and approximately right on Q2** — 100% upward EPS breadth for FY26 in the last month, but Q3/Q4 revisions are net-DOWN (2 up / 4 down each) and FY26 revenue revisions are net-down (1 up / 4 down), indicating analysts believe in margin/H1 strength but are skeptical of H2. Roughly $1.20 of the current consensus (the 30-day revision) is anchored to a single biofuel-policy event (RVO finalization) that could partially reverse on regulatory action.
