# Historical Financials ‚Äî BG

Reporting currency: **U.S. dollars (US$ millions, except per-share data).** Fiscal year ends **December 31.** All figures reported under U.S. GAAP. Bunge Global SA (NYSE: BG) reports on a calendar-year basis. The Viterra Acquisition closed **July 2, 2025**, materially distorting all year-over-year and Q1-over-Q1 comparisons in this report; FY25 includes ~6 months of Viterra contribution and Q1 FY26 includes a full quarter of Viterra, while FY24/FY23 and Q1 FY25 do not.

Data availability limitation: The FY25 10-K presents only three years of comparative annual income statement data (FY23, FY24, FY25). FY21 and FY22 figures are not available in the supplied data pool, so the annual table covers three years rather than five. Quarterly comparatives in the data pool are limited to Q1 FY26 and Q1 FY25; no FY24 / FY23 quarterly detail is available. As a result, the 8-quarter trend table is shrunk to 2 quarters, and the multi-year seasonality table is not possible.

## 6. Key Trend Summary

1. **Revenue direction: Volatile.** FY23-FY25 net sales went $59.5B ‚Üí $53.1B ‚Üí $70.3B (-10.8% ‚Üí +32.4%) [1]. The FY25 jump is overwhelmingly **Viterra-driven** (closed Jul 2, 2025); ex-Viterra organic revenue declined in FY25 vs FY24 [Inference, not from filings ‚Äî based on the disclosed +6mo Viterra contribution and 10-K segment commentary attributing increases to "Net sales contributions from the Acquisition of Viterra"] [9].
2. **Margin direction: Compressing.** Gross margin moved 8.14% ‚Üí 6.39% ‚Üí 4.85% across FY23-FY25, a 329 bp decline; Total EBIT margin compressed 342 bps from 5.60% to 2.18% over the same window [calc 2, calc 5]. Soybean Processing segment EBIT margin fell from 6.1% (FY23) to 2.7% (FY24) to 3.4% (FY25), and Softseed segment EBIT margin fell from 13.9% (FY23) to 9.5% (FY24) to 4.6% (FY25), reflecting crush-margin and refining-premium normalization off the FY22/FY23 biofuel-driven peak [10].
3. **Cash flow direction: Decelerating sharply.** CFO collapsed from $3,308M (FY23) ‚Üí $1,900M (FY24) ‚Üí $844M (FY25), while capex rose $1,122M ‚Üí $1,376M ‚Üí $1,723M, driving FCF from +$2,186M to **negative $879M in FY25** ‚Äî the first negative-FCF year in the three-year window [3, calc 6]. Q1 FY26 CFO was -$541M (more negative than Q1 FY25's -$285M) [6].
4. **Balance sheet: Inflecting on Viterra.** Total debt jumped from $6,238M (Dec 2024) to $14,051M (Dec 2025) and $14,553M (Mar 2026); cash dropped from $3,311M to $1,135M to $839M [1, 5, 6]. Net debt / reported FY25 EBITDA stretched to ~5.8x; mgmt-defined "adjusted leverage" is 1.6x at Mar 31, 2026 (mgmt definition includes RMI offset and adjusted EBITDA) [7, calc 8].
5. **Inflection points: Two of them.** First, the FY22/FY23 biofuel-margin peak (referenced by mgmt as "back in '22 and '23" for refining premiums [7]) is rolling off ‚Äî soybean and softseed segment EBIT margins have compressed for two consecutive years. Second, the **Viterra close on July 2, 2025** doubles the asset base ($24.9B ‚Üí $44.5B total assets) [1] and structurally raises both interest expense ($471M ‚Üí $628M annual, projected $620-660M for FY26 [7]) and share count (diluted weighted-average shares went from 135M in Q1 FY25 to 196M in Q1 FY26, a 45% increase [6]) ‚Äî both of which materially compress EPS even before operating results are considered.

On management's adjusted basis, however, Q1 FY26 adjusted EPS of $1.83 was up 1.1% YoY vs $1.81 in Q1 FY25, and FY26 adjusted EPS guidance was **raised from $7.50-$8.00 to $9.00-$9.50** intra-quarter [8], implying mgmt sees the trend inflecting positively on an underlying basis ‚Äî though the gap between GAAP and adjusted EBIT/EPS is large enough that this claim must be evaluated by the earnings-quality agent.
