# Revenue Drivers — BG

## 1. Segment Decomposition Status

**Segment decomposition applied — 4 reportable segments from business-model module** [analyses/BG_2026-05-20/business-model/03_segment-map.md]. The four reportable segments (effective Q3 2025) are: Soybean Processing and Refining (51.6% FY25 revenue), Grain Merchandising and Milling (25.8%), Softseed Processing and Refining (16.0%), and Other Oilseeds Processing and Refining (6.6%) [FY25 10-K, p.34, p.37]. The largest segment is just over 50% of revenue, so the business is **not** single-segment; per-segment driver decomposition is required.

## 2. Revenue Driver Tree

Bunge is a **commodity producer / merchandiser** for which the dominant revenue formula is:

**Revenue = Volume (metric tons processed + merchandised + refined) × Realized commodity price**

This is the appropriate formula because Bunge discloses revenue and volumes both in MT for every reportable segment, and explicitly states: *"the prices of agricultural commodities are subject to global supply and demand…volumes [are] an important operating measure"* [FY25 10-K, p.2143–2145 narrative].

Company-specific revenue formula (one line):
**FY25 net sales ≈ (41.0 MT soy processed + 20.5 MT soy merchandised + 3.6 MT refined oil + 10.8 MT softseed processed + 2.8 MT softseed merchandised + 2.5 MT other oilseed sold + 67.2 MT grain & milling sold) × regional realized prices of soy oil / soy meal / canola / wheat / corn / palm — with biofuel policy (RVO, Brazil mandates, Indonesia, EU) acting as the dominant volatility lever on realized vegoil and crush-margin prices** [FY25 10-K, p.38, p.39, p.41, p.42; Q1 FY26 transcript, prepared remarks p.4–5; FY25 10-K Risk Factors, p.555–560].

**Critical observation:** FY25's +32.4% reported revenue growth is **not organic demand growth**. Every segment's FY25 MD&A explicitly attributes the increase to "Net sales contributions from the Acquisition of Viterra" — ex-Viterra organic revenue likely declined in FY25 vs. FY24 given the directional commentary on prices ("lower prices in almost all other regions") and underlying volumes outside Argentina [FY25 10-K, p.2414–2418, p.2516–2519, p.2645–2649; 01_historical-financials.md, Section 6, point 1]. This must not be described as organic demand.

## 7. The Single Biggest Revenue Driver

The single biggest revenue driver over the next 12 months is **biofuel-policy-driven realized vegoil price and crush-margin trajectory** — specifically, the durability of the finalized U.S. RVO together with evolving Brazil and Indonesia biofuel mandates. A 10–20% move in board crush margins or in the price of soybean oil (driven by biofuel mandate strength) would flow directly into Bunge's largest segment (Soybean Processing, 52% of revenue) and into Softseed Processing (16% of revenue), and would simultaneously affect volumes (through farmer selling decisions tied to flat price levels) and realized prices on roughly two-thirds of consolidated revenue. Management's own +$1.50 raise to FY26 adjusted EPS guidance (from $7.50–$8.00 to $9.00–$9.50, an 18% raise) explicitly attributes the improved outlook to "soybean and soft seed processing and refining segment results forecasted to be higher" driven by "favorable biofuel environment, synergy capture and ramp-up of in-flight projects" [Q1 FY26 transcript, prepared remarks p.171, p.204–211]. **Current direction: Improving on near-term U.S. RVO clarity and meal demand surprise; uncertainty rises in H2 2026 because forward curves are "heavily inverted" reflecting Ukraine-conflict-related lack of liquidity** [Q1 FY26 transcript, Q&A p.476–479]. The Viterra acquisition is a one-time step-change in scale that will annualize through Q3 2026 — it is the largest **reported** revenue driver but is not a recurring growth lever; biofuel-policy-driven price/margin is the recurring lever.
