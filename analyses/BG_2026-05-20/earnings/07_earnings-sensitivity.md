## 4. The Single Highest-Sensitivity Variable

**Combined soybean + softseed crush margin.** A ±150 bps move in combined Soybean + Softseed segment EBIT margin (within the FY23–FY25 historical range of 7.8% → 3.8%) moves EPS by roughly **±$2.73**, more than the entire current FY26 adjusted EPS guide of $9.00–$9.50. The variable is **largely external** — Bunge has no formal pass-through on either side of the crush spread per business-model `06_value-chain.md` cited in 03 §3, and management states the company is a "spread-taker, not a price-setter" [Q1 FY26 transcript p.11]. Current direction is **Tailwind** because RVO clarity, distillate shortages, biofuel mandate momentum in Brazil/Indonesia/EU, and animal-protein-driven meal demand are simultaneously supportive. For the adverse case to materialize, two or more of the following would need to occur: an RVO walk-back or 45Z weakening that compresses vegoil demand; a global recession that compresses meat/meal demand; a return of FY22/23-style crush capacity oversupply absorbing tight spreads; or an Argentine policy reversal (export-tax hike) that disrupts the largest country footprint post-Viterra.

## 5. Interaction Effects

Multiple variables in this table are economically linked and can compound, not offset. (1) **Biofuel policy → crush margins**: an RVO walk-back compresses soy-oil prices, which compresses board crush, which compresses Soybean + Softseed segment EBIT — these two variables are not independent and the bear cases would coincide. (2) **Commodity prices ↔ FX**: agricultural commodity prices in Argentina and Brazil are priced in USD, but costs (and any local-currency export-tax retentions) are in BRL/ARS — a USD strengthening typically coincides with weaker BRL/ARS commodity prices, hitting both lines simultaneously. (3) **Freight ↔ commodity prices**: bunker-fuel spikes (driven by crude oil) often coincide with vegoil price spikes (because vegoil prices co-move with energy in biofuel-linked markets) — partial natural hedge on Soybean/Softseed segments offset by Grain Merchandising freight headwind, but the offset is not 1:1. (4) **Crush margin ↔ farmer selling**: lower flat prices reduce farmer selling, which compresses origination volumes and merchandising margins simultaneously. The compounding implication: a single adverse macro event (recession + biofuel mandate walk-back + USD spike) could simultaneously trigger the bear case on crush margins, RVO, freight (via crude oil drop, ambiguous), FX, and the Softseed/Argentine variable — easily exceeding $5 of cumulative EPS downside on a non-linear basis.

## 6. Non-Linear Or Asymmetric Risks

Several material non-linearities exist:

- **Operating deleverage on Soybean + Softseed**: combined segment fixed industrial expense (Q1 FY26 $615M consolidated fixed industrial) does not scale down with volumes — if crush utilization drops 10–15%, segment EBIT margin compression accelerates non-linearly. The FY23→FY25 Softseed segment EBIT margin collapse from 13.9% to 4.6% on only a partial volume decline shows the magnitude available [03 §6 Softseed table].
- **No pass-through on freight or energy spikes**: per business-model `06_value-chain.md` cited in 03 §3, Bunge has no contractual pass-through on bunker fuel or ocean freight on the Grain Merchandising leg — a spike hits same-quarter EBIT with no offset, as demonstrated by Q1 FY26's $122M YoY swing to -$76M on one quarter. The downside is bigger and faster than the upside because freight contracts run 2 months to ~5 years; cost spikes hit immediately while reversals lag.
- **Leverage covenant proximity**: net debt / reported FY25 EBITDA at 5.78x [01 calc 8] is structurally elevated post-Viterra. While mgmt-defined adjusted leverage is 1.6x at March 31, 2026 [01], the gap between mgmt-defined and reported is wide enough that a sustained EBITDA shortfall (e.g., bear case on crush margins for two consecutive years) could compress headroom against credit facility covenants. Specific covenant thresholds are not in the supplied data pool — qualitative non-linearity flag only.
- **MTM timing**: the Q1 FY26 -$336M MTM hit reversed within the year on management's framing [03 §5 row 3], but the GAAP reported EPS at $0.35 (vs adjusted $1.83) shows that single-quarter GAAP earnings can swing >5x without any change in underlying economics — a non-linearity at the reported-EPS level even if cycle-adjusted economics are flat.
- **Biofuel policy is a step-function, not continuous**: RVO levels are set by regulatory decision events, not market-clearing prices, so the policy variable is binary/jumpy rather than smooth — the $1.50 EPS event from the most recent RVO finalization demonstrates the size of a single discrete event.
