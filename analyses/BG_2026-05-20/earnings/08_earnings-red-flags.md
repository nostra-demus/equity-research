If the earnings setup turns out to be wrong, the most likely reason we missed it is that **the FY25-to-FY26 EPS bridge depended on replacing a stack of non-recurring contributors — the $155M corn-mill divestiture gain, the Argentina FX-positioning gains, the unusually low Q1 FY26 tax rate, the $336M MTM timing tailwind on adjusted EBIT — with organic Viterra synergy capture and crush-margin tailwind that did not materialize at the promised pace, and we underweighted the cash-conversion collapse (CFO/EBITDA from 84% to 38%; FY25 FCF $(879)M) as a Viterra working-capital timing issue when it was actually telling us that the "adjusted" earnings management was guiding to were not converting to cash even before H2 stress. The single empirical test is the Q3/Q4 FY26 CFO trajectory — if seasonal South American harvest cash inflows do not normalize CFO toward the historical 70-80% of EBITDA range, the adjusted-vs-cash gap stops being a timing differential and becomes a permanent earnings overstatement.**
