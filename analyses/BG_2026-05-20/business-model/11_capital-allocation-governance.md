## 2. Classification

**Capital allocation concerns** — primarily driven by a single massive transformational acquisition (Viterra at $10.6B with $2.66B of goodwill), heavy share issuance to fund it (65.6M new shares, ~50% rise in basic weighted-average count, diluted EPS fell 38%), debt more than doubled to $14B, and operating cash flow halved year-on-year while capex stays at 2.4x D&A. Governance has structural complexity (~34% bloc held by three Viterra-seller shareholders with board nomination rights) but no qualitative red flags (clean Deloitte opinion, long auditor tenure, stable CEO/CFO, modest related-party transactions). The classification reflects that the company has bet the firm on Viterra integration succeeding — the Grain Merchandising goodwill cushion is only 9% (a flagged critical audit matter), and synergy delivery is unproven.

## 3. Most Material Signal

The Viterra acquisition itself. If integration synergies underdeliver or the Grain Merchandising reporting unit's fair value falls (the auditor flagged it explicitly: only 9% headroom vs carrying value with $593M of allocated goodwill), Bunge would face a goodwill impairment that would materially change the capital-allocation read on the deal — moving the classification toward "Governance red flags" given that the deal also added $7.8B of debt, brought in a 34% concentrated shareholder bloc with board nomination rights, and diluted existing shareholders by ~33%. Conversely, if synergies run ahead of plan (Q1 FY26 management commentary suggests they are), the same deal flips toward "Owner-operator discipline."
