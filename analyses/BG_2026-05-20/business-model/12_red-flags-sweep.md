## 3. Most Severe New Flag

**The single most severe new flag is the FY25 EBIT being inflated by the $155M one-off corn-milling divestiture gain (Severity 60).** It is severe because (a) it is large — the gain is 10% of consolidated FY25 Total EBIT of $1,533M and ~33% of the Grain Merchandising & Milling segment's $465M EBIT — (b) it cannot recur in FY26 and beyond, so the FY26 guidance of $9.00–$9.50 adjusted EPS has to overcome this absent gain plus deliver the promised Viterra synergies, and (c) the upstream specialists treat segment EBIT trends as if they are clean — they are not. The synthesizer should haircut FY25 segment EBIT by $155M when comparing FY25 to FY24, and should view Grain Merchandising's apparent "14% YoY EBIT improvement" as actually a ~24% decline ex-gain.

## 4. Cross-Cutting Patterns

A coherent quality-of-earnings pattern is visible across several new flags: (i) a $155M divestiture gain inflated FY25 segment EBIT, (ii) Argentina FX-positioning gains contributed to Corporate & Other and other segments in FY25, (iii) the Q1 FY26 tax rate was "unusually low" on discrete timing items, and (iv) the GAAP-to-adjusted EPS gap was 5x in Q1 FY26. Independently each is small; together they suggest that the company's reported and "adjusted" numbers are being supported by a stack of non-recurring or timing-shifted items at exactly the moment management is asking investors to underwrite an $9.00–$9.50 FY26 EPS guide. The synthesizer should treat the FY25-to-FY26 bridge with extra skepticism: the strongest single hurdle for the FY26 number isn't biofuel policy (already flagged upstream), it's the replacement of these one-offs with organic synergy capture.

A second, smaller pattern: maximum-potential contingent claims (standby LCs/surety $2.15B + indemnity max $1.59B + guarantees max $582M) total ~$4.3B against $133M recorded — a 32x leverage on contingent-to-recognized liability. Each piece is individually defensible; in aggregate the off-balance-sheet contingent footprint is large relative to FY25 net income of $816M.
