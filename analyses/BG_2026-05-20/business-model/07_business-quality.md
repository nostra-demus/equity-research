## 2. Aggregate Quality Score

**Aggregate: 26 / 100 (Weak).**

This aggregate is not an arithmetic mean (which would land ~26 too, by coincidence) — it is judgment-weighted. For an asset-heavy oilseed crusher, the four factors that should dominate the read are commodity dependence (10), pricing power (18), regulatory dependence (18), and margin stability (20). All four are in the very-weak band and they are *linked* — together they describe a business whose earnings power resets every year based on factors management cannot control. Capital intensity (22), competitive intensity (25), and cyclicality (28) are secondary but reinforce the same picture. Disclosure quality (55) is the only factor in the mid-band and is doing no work to lift the aggregate.

## 4. Read

This is a globally integrated, asset-heavy oilseed crusher and grain merchant — closer to a cyclical commodity processor than to a durable compounder. The economics are a leveraged bet on crush margins, biofuel policy, and crop-flow arbitrage between hemispheres; management has real scale, hedging discipline, and an oligopoly position (ABCD + Wilmar + COFCO), but no pricing power, no recurring revenue, no customer stickiness, and capex that consumes more than 100% of trailing EBIT. The single quality factor to track over the next 24 months is **margin stability** — specifically whether the Viterra integration delivers the promised cost synergies before another crush-spread compression cycle or a biofuel-policy reversal (RVO cut, 45Z weakening, EU mandate delay) shows up. If FY26 EPS guidance of $9.00–$9.50 holds, the read on margin stability moves from a 20 to perhaps a 35; if biofuel policy softens, the same number collapses through FY25's 3.4% Soybean crush margin.
