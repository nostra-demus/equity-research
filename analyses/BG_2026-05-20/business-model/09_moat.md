## 4. Where The Company Sits

**Company sits at the bottom of named peers on disclosed margin (gross 4.85% vs ADM 6.3%) and roughly at parity on disclosed ROIC (BG 6.9% TTM vs ADM 6.7% TTM).** Adjusted ROIC of 8.1% lifts BG above ADM but the adjustment excludes Viterra integration costs that are real cash outflows. Against Cargill and LDC, no benchmark is possible — they do not disclose. Where data exists, BG does not demonstrate a margin or ROIC premium that would prove a moat.

## 5. Moat Verdict

**No moat proven.** The strongest candidate is "distribution + scale" (the ABCD oligopoly position), and BG does have a hard-to-replicate global asset footprint — >400 plants, 298 storage facilities, $11.7B of property, plant and equipment after Viterra. But this is shared with ADM, Cargill, and LDC, who name BG as a competitor in their own filings; the FY25 10-K itself says competition is "principally based on price" and BG's disclosed FY25 gross margin (~4.85%) sits below ADM's (~6.3%). The durability test over the next five years is whether the $250M+ annual run-rate Viterra cost synergies management has guided to (cited in Q4 2025 / Q1 2026 transcripts) translate into a sustained gross-margin or ROIC premium versus ADM. If BG's adjusted ROIC settles above 10% while ADM's stays in the 6–8% range across a full crush-cycle, that would be evidence of a narrow scale-cost moat. Today, the data does not support that claim.
