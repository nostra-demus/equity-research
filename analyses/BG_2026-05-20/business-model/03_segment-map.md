## 2. Dominant Segment

**Soybean Processing and Refining is the dominant segment on both revenue (51.6%) and segment EBIT (52.6%) in FY25** [FY25 10-K, p.37–38]. It is the only segment that is consistently the largest by both measures across the FY23–FY25 disclosed window (segment EBIT contribution: $2,222M / 60% FY23, $872M / 40% FY24, $1,225M / 53% FY25). Softseed is the swing #2 contributor on profit (22.4% FY25 EBIT share but only 16.0% of revenue), while Grain Merchandising and Milling is #2 on revenue (25.8%) but only #3 on EBIT (20.0%) — and the FY25 EBIT figure includes a one-time $155M gain on the U.S. corn-milling divestiture, without which Grain Merchandising and Milling EBIT would have been ~$310M / ~13% of segment EBIT [FY25 10-K, p.42].

The economic reality: Bunge is a soybean crusher first, an oilseed/grain merchant second, and a specialty oils business a distant third. The "four segments" framing is real but the value engine is concentrated in soy crush margins.

## 3. Segment Disclosure Quality

Disclosure quality is **mixed and recently disrupted**. Bunge **changed its reportable segments in Q3 2025** following the closing of the Viterra Acquisition, moving from a previous Agribusiness / Refined and Specialty Oils / Milling structure to the current four-segment value-chain structure (Soybean, Softseed, Other Oilseeds, Grain Merchandising and Milling) [FY25 10-K, p.30, p.99]. **Effective January 1, 2025, the prior Sugar and Bioenergy segment was reclassified into Corporate and Other** [FY25 10-K, p.30, p.43]. Prior-year comparatives have been recast on the new basis, which is helpful, but the recast means FY23/FY24 figures here are not the same as those originally reported under the old structure. Segment EBIT is disclosed at each reportable segment level (with volumes, net sales, COGS, SG&A, FX, NCI, other income, and affiliate income broken out) — this is reasonably granular [FY25 10-K, p.37]. The material disclosure problem is the **Corporate and Other bucket: it carries a $(796)M EBIT loss in FY25, vs. $(367)M FY24 — a 117% deterioration — and is large enough to swing consolidated Total EBIT from $2,329M of segment EBIT down to $1,533M reported.** This bucket mixes corporate SG&A, Viterra integration costs ($223M FY25), and the residual BP Bunge Bioenergia (sugar/bioenergy) results, making the segments look cleaner than the consolidated entity actually is [FY25 10-K, p.43]. With the post-Viterra structure only one year old and an embedded ~10% "Other" drag on EBIT, year-over-year segment comparability through FY26 will require care.
