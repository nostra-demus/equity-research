# Business Model Reality Check — BG (Synthesis)

## Abstract

Bunge Global SA is a globally integrated, asset-heavy oilseed crusher and grain merchant that buys soybeans, canola, sunflower, wheat, corn, and palm from farmers, processes them into meal, oil, biofuel feedstocks, and milled products, and sells them to feed, food, and fuel customers worldwide. Soybean Processing dominates at 51.6% of FY25 net sales ($36.3B) and 52.6% of segment EBIT, with the rest split across Softseed, Other Oilseeds, and post-Viterra Grain Merchandising. The strongest positive is structural scale — part of the ABCD oligopoly with >400 plants and 298 storage facilities — yet FY25 gross margin of just 4.85% trails ADM's 6.3%. The strongest negative is that roughly 76% of revenue is ASC 815 commodity-marked daily, leaving earnings power at the mercy of crush spreads and biofuel policy. No disqualifier triggered, but this is an average cyclical commodity business worth deeper work only if valuation is cheap.

## 3. Reconciliation

Most material disagreement to reconcile:

**Capital allocation classification.** `11_capital-allocation-governance.md` rates Viterra a "capital allocation concern" (severity 55) but suggests it could "flip toward Owner-operator discipline" if synergies run ahead of plan. `12_red-flags-sweep.md` is more skeptical, identifying a cross-cutting quality-of-earnings pattern ($155M divestiture gain + Argentina FX-positioning gains + unusually low Q1 tax rate + 5x GAAP-to-adjusted EPS gap) that "suggests the company's reported and 'adjusted' numbers are being supported by a stack of non-recurring or timing-shifted items at exactly the moment management is asking investors to underwrite a $9.00–$9.50 FY26 EPS guide." **Reconciled view for this synthesis:** Side with red-flags-sweep — the FY25-to-FY26 bridge depends on replacing one-offs with organic synergy capture that is unproven. The capital-allocation classification remains "concerns" not "discipline."

**Segment EBIT trend interpretation.** `03_segment-map.md` shows Grain Merchandising & Milling FY25 segment EBIT of $465M (up from $408M FY24). `12_red-flags-sweep.md` notes this includes a $155M one-off divestiture gain; ex-gain segment EBIT was ~$310M (~1.7% margin), a ~24% YoY decline, not a 14% improvement. **Reconciled view:** Use the ex-gain figure for any FY24→FY25 trend reading.

**Moat vs scale framing.** `07_business-quality.md` notes BG sits in an oligopoly. `09_moat.md` concludes no moat proven because BG sits BELOW ADM on disclosed gross margin (4.85% vs 6.3%) and at parity on ROIC (6.9% vs 6.7% TTM). **Reconciled view:** Scale is real but is table-stakes for ABCD membership, not a competitive advantage over named peers.

## 4. Note To The Final Synthesizer

- **Strongest business-model positive:** Structural scale in a four-firm oligopoly (ABCD + Wilmar/COFCO) with >400 plants, 298 storage facilities, and $11.7B of long-lived assets that would be effectively impossible for a new entrant to replicate. This is a barrier-to-entry advantage, NOT a barrier-against-named-peers advantage. [FY25 10-K, p.7, Note 26]

- **Strongest business-model negative:** ~76% of FY25 net sales are ASC 815 commodity contracts marked to market — the entire P&L is a wrapper on soy/oil/meal/canola/sunflower/palm/wheat/corn prices with no contractual pricing power on either side. Hypothetical 10% adverse commodity move = $131M fair-value hit (70% larger than FY24 post-Viterra). [FY25 10-K, Item 7A; Note 26]

- **Most important segment:** Soybean Processing and Refining at 51.6% of revenue and 52.6% of segment EBIT FY25 — and the only segment that is consistently the largest by both measures across the FY23–FY25 window. Bunge is a soybean crusher first, a grain merchant second, a specialty oils business a distant third.

- **Cleanest unit-economics read (or why it cannot be derived):** Cannot be derived cleanly — Bunge does not disclose per-plant capex, plant life, maintenance capex per ton, or build-cost-per-ton-of-crush-capacity. What can be said is that per-ton segment EBIT was $29.9/MT FY25 vs $61.8/MT FY23, a ~52% range driven entirely by crush spread (not by costs Bunge controls). This is a thin-spread commodity processor.

- **Where the company sits vs named peers on margin / ROIC:** BG sits at the BOTTOM of named peers on disclosed gross margin (4.85% vs ADM 6.3%) and roughly at parity on disclosed ROIC (6.9% TTM vs ADM 6.7% TTM). Cargill and LDC do not disclose. There is no observable margin or ROIC premium that would prove a moat.

- **Main external dependency:** Biofuel policy — specifically US RVO/RFS volumes, 45Z tax credit rules, and parallel mandates in Brazil, Indonesia, EU. A single EPA RVO decision drove FY26 EPS guidance from $7.50–$8.00 to $9.00–$9.50 (~$1.50/share, ~18%) in three months. A 20% adverse policy shift would hit multiple segments simultaneously with no hedge available.

- **Most important capital allocation or governance signal:** The Viterra acquisition. $10.6B price (stock+cash with 65.6M new shares = ~17% basic-share dilution), debt jumped $6.24B → $14.05B, OCF fell $1,900M → $844M. Three Viterra-seller shareholders now hold ~34% combined (Glencore 17%, CPP 14%, BCI 3%) with board nomination rights. Grain Merchandising goodwill cushion is only 9% above carrying value ($593M at risk) — flagged as a critical audit matter. Global Cotton goodwill cushion is only 15% ($47M at risk). Capital-structure-transaction cap is applicable but the existing 45/100 score already sits below the 50/100 cap.

- **Whether any automatic disqualifier triggered:** No. Verdict is not locked.

- **Biggest missing data point:** Per-ton capital intensity (gross PP&E per ton of installed crush capacity, maintenance capex per ton, greenfield build cost per ton) for the soybean processing footprint. Without it the structural value-creation question — does a ton of crush earn its cost of capital — cannot be answered from filings.

- **Whether the business deserves deeper work, and what would change the answer:**
  - **Deserves deeper work only if valuation is cheap.** Quality is weak (26/100), no moat is proven against named peers, ~76% commodity-marked revenue makes earnings power non-controllable.
  - **What would change the answer toward "high-quality":** Viterra cost synergies running ahead of the $250M+ run-rate guide AND BG's adjusted ROIC settling above 10% across a full crush-cycle while ADM's stays in the 6–8% range. Both conditions would constitute new evidence of a narrow scale-cost moat.
  - **What would change the answer toward "low-quality":** A Grain Merchandising or Global Cotton goodwill impairment (combined $640M at risk), an adverse RVO/45Z reversal, or FY26 adjusted EPS missing the $9.00–$9.50 guide because the one-off stack ($155M corn-mill gain, Argentina FX-positioning gains, low Q1 tax rate) was not replaced by organic synergy capture. The 32x leverage of contingent-to-recognized liability (off-balance-sheet ~$4.3B vs $133M recorded) is a tail-risk surface, particularly the $645M Brazilian tax claims now in active litigation. Any of these materially worsens the read.

## 5. Simple Summary

- **What it does:** Buys oilseeds and grain from farmers, processes them into meal, oil, biofuel feedstock, and milled products, and sells them globally to feed, food, and fuel customers.
- **How it makes money:** Takes a "crush margin" — the spread between raw oilseed cost and the combined sales value of meal + oil — plus a merchandising spread on grain handled.
- **Whether each new unit creates value:** Cannot be answered from filings; Bunge does not disclose per-ton capex or plant economics. Per-ton EBIT in FY25 was $29.9/MT, ~50% below FY23, driven entirely by crush spread (not costs Bunge controls).
- **Which segment matters most:** Soybean Processing — 51.6% of revenue and 52.6% of segment EBIT FY25. The other three segments together explain less than half the value engine.
- **Whether it has a moat, and against whom:** No moat proven against named peers (ADM, Cargill, LDC). Real scale advantage against new entrants but ADM, Cargill, and LDC have equivalent or larger networks; BG's FY25 gross margin sits BELOW ADM's.
- **What external variables it depends on:** Commodity prices (~76% of revenue is ASC 815 marked daily), biofuel policy (single biggest lever), FX (BRL/CAD/EUR/ARS), ocean freight, weather, geopolitics — external dependency risk score 78/100 (worse).
- **Whether capital is allocated well:** Capital allocation concerns. Viterra deal was transformational ($10.6B, 17% dilution, 125% debt increase) and the Grain Merchandising goodwill cushion is only 9% above carrying — a flagged critical audit matter. Quality-of-earnings stack ($155M one-off gain, Argentina FX gains, low Q1 tax rate, 5x GAAP-to-adjusted EPS gap) is supporting current numbers.
- **Whether it deserves deeper work:** Worth deeper work only if valuation is cheap. This is an average cyclical commodity processor, not a durable compounder.
