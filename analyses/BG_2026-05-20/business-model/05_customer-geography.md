## 2. Geography Map

Bunge's geographic disclosure (Note 26, p.F-79) reports net sales by "location of the subsidiary making the sale," not by end-customer geography. Switzerland and the Netherlands are global trading hubs (Bunge incorporated in Switzerland per FY25 10-K, p.1; trading history in Amsterdam per FY25 10-K, p.13). Trading-hub geography overstates "Europe" exposure and understates South America / Asia. A more useful end-operational proxy is the long-lived asset and headcount footprint disclosed in the same filing.

## 4. Read

Bunge is **not concentrated on the customer side as disclosed** — the company runs a B2B commodity-distribution business serving thousands of feed manufacturers, biofuel producers, food processors, grocery chains, and livestock operators, and discloses zero individual customer concentration in the FY25 10-K. On the geography side, the filing's "subsidiary location" revenue split is structurally misleading (Switzerland and Netherlands are trading hubs, not end markets); the operational footprint is more balanced — US ~33%, Canada/Argentina/Brazil each 11–16%, Rest of world ~27% by long-lived assets. There are no disclosed long-term customer contracts, but there are also no disclosed customer dependencies, so the "naked" qualifier applies trivially in a commodity B2B model. **The single biggest dependency the synthesizer should know about is country-of-operations exposure to Brazil and Argentina (combined ~23.5% of long-lived assets, plus $835M in outstanding farmer-financing receivables in Brazil per FY25 10-K p.19) — this is the only specific counterparty/geography concentration that the filing itself flags as a risk factor.**
