## 3. Classification

**Mostly externally driven.**

The mechanics: ~76% of revenue is ASC 815 commodity-contract sales, marked daily; segment profitability is described by management as "affected by the availability and market prices of agricultural commodities, including oilseeds and grains, and the availability and costs of energy, transportation, and logistics services." A single policy decision (EPA's RVO) drove a ~$1.50 per share guidance lift in three months. Q1 FY26 EPS swung from $1.83 adjusted to $0.35 reported almost entirely on a $1.28 mark-to-market timing difference. Management does hedge actively (VaR limits, ocean freight forwards, FX forwards, energy swaps, interest rate swaps) and has scale/network levers, but the underlying P&L is a wrapper around crop crush spreads, biofuel policy, FX (BRL/CAD/EUR/CNY/ARS), and freight rates.

## 4. External Dependency Risk Score

**78 / 100** (higher = worse).

Lands in the 61–80 band: mostly externally driven, limited management levers beyond hedging, mix, and capacity siting. Not at the 81–100 "pure pass-through" extreme because (a) the company has real scale/network economics from the Viterra combination, (b) disciplined VaR/hedging meaningfully damps single-variable shocks, and (c) refining and specialty ingredients segments add some value-add buffer. But the score sits well above mid-band because the company itself enumerates ~10 distinct external variables that materially drive results, and provides quantified 10% sensitivities precisely because management cannot make those exposures disappear.

## 5. The Single Biggest Lever

**Biofuel policy** — specifically, US RVO/RFS volumes, 45Z tax credit rules, and parallel mandates in Brazil, Indonesia, and the EU. Management's own evidence: a single RVO decision drove FY26 guidance from $7.50–$8.00 to $9.00–$9.50 (~$1.50 EPS, ~18%). A 20% adverse policy shift (e.g., RVO cut, 45Z weakened, EU mandate delayed) would compress vegetable-oil demand, soy/canola crush margins, and the Bunge Chevron JV simultaneously — hitting multiple segments at once with no hedge available.
