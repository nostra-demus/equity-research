## 2. Performance Metrics

The metric classification below is therefore drawn from (a) the observable bonus/salary split in the Capital IQ data, (b) management's own stated KPIs on earnings calls, and (c) web-sourced information about the company's remuneration structure. All metric weights are marked "Not disclosed" unless a primary filing supports them.

| ESOP (LTIP) — ESOP 2024 | Time-based vesting only (no disclosed performance conditions) | Not disclosed — time-based | Neither (pure time-based; no ROIC, EPS, or TSR hurdle stated) | Web: Niva Bupa ESOP Scheme 2024 (transactions.nivabupa.com/pages/doc/offers/material/documents/ESOP%20Scheme_2024.pdf) — objective: "attract, retain, motivate, reward"; exercise price: weighted average price; performance conditions: none disclosed |

**Metric classification summary:**
- GWP growth and retail market share are size-based: they reward scale and premium volume, not per-share outcomes.
- Combined ratio and EOM are returns-based in the insurer context: they directly measure underwriting and operating efficiency.
- IFRS PAT is an absolute profit metric — not per-share, but closely correlated with EPS given stable share count.
- There is no disclosed metric tied to ROE, EPS growth, FCF per share, or TSR (total return to shareholders) — the most direct per-share value measures. This is the single most significant compensation design gap.
- The ESOP is time-based only (no performance hurdle disclosed), which means it rewards tenure rather than value creation once granted.

**Regulatory constraint on pay structure:** As an IRDAI-regulated insurer, Niva Bupa's CEO remuneration must comply with IRDAI guidelines on managerial remuneration. IRDAI requires approval for MD/CEO compensation, and the FY26 revised fixed pay of ₹4.13 cr was explicitly IRDAI-approved (November 2025). This creates a regulatory upper-bound on remuneration that limits management's ability to set a very high fixed-pay base, but does not constrain the variable bonus formula independently.

## 4. Incentive Alignment Read

The pay structure at Niva Bupa is **mixed**. On the positive side: the CEO's annual bonus is large (70% of total cash in FY25), the company consistently discloses operational metrics like combined ratio, EOM, and retail market share as management's internal targets — and those metrics have all improved — suggesting bonus outcomes are at least partially tied to operational performance rather than pure scale. The CEO holds meaningful personal equity (est. ₹94 crore at current prices), which creates real skin in the game. Shareholder say-on-pay support is 99.99%, showing no minority-holder opposition.

The biggest weakness is what is absent: no disclosed ROE, EPS, or per-share TSR metric in either the annual bonus or the ESOP. The ESOP is time-based only — it rewards staying, not value creation above a hurdle. GWP growth and retail market share — the most prominent KPIs in management commentary — are size metrics. A company paid partly on premium volume has a structural incentive to grow the book even if the combined ratio and ROE do not support it. The fact that the combined ratio has risen from 101.2% (FY25) to 101.4% (FY26) — still above 100% — while GWP grew 27% argues that scale is being prioritised; management's narrative attributes this to new-business mix, which is credible but unverified from a primary filing.

**Conclusion: mixed.** The most important incentive signal is that GWP growth (size) and EOM reduction (returns) are both cited as targets, creating a partial balance — but there is no hard per-share hurdle in the LTIP, and the bonus formula is entirely opaque. Until the Board's Report remuneration section is reviewed, alignment cannot be confirmed as returns-based.
