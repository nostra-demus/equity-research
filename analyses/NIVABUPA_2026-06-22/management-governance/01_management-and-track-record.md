## 3. Stated Strategy & Targets

Management operates around six publicly stated pillars: (1) building a profitable, granular health insurance franchise; (2) being the health partner of choice (not just insurer); (3) multichannel, digital-weighted distribution; (4) technology and analytics-driven operations; (5) disciplined underwriting and claims management; and (6) talent-led execution.

The specific, checkable targets management has set, as of the most recent reporting period (Q4 FY26, May 8, 2026):

- **Combined ratio target:** ~99% combined operating ratio (under IFRS) by FY2029, from 101.4% in FY2026. The path: loss ratio stable or rising 100–150 basis points (structural mix effect), offset by expense ratio improvement of ~250 basis points through operating leverage and technology.
- **ROE target:** Mid- to high-teens ROE by FY2029, from ~10.7% in FY2026, contingent on the 99% combined ratio being achieved.
- **EOM improvement:** ~2%–2.5% per year of further reduction in the Expense of Management ratio (from 33.7% in FY2026 toward the 31%–32% range by FY2028).
- **GWP growth:** Management has not provided a point-in-time annual GWP target. Directionally, they have not disputed the analyst assumption of ~25% CAGR over 2–3 years and guided the industry retail health market to grow 17%–19% CAGR on a 5-year view. Niva Bupa has historically grown faster than the industry.
- **Retail market share:** No explicit share target stated, but management cited a move from 9.1% (FY24) to 9.4% (FY25) to 10.1% (FY26) and characterised the direction as "continuing."

## 5. Management Read

The four senior executives — Ramachandran (CEO), Mahendra (CFO), Kharbanda (CBO/Deputy CEO), and Mishra (COO) — have each been in post since April 2020, giving the team 6+ years of continuity, with CEO and CFO both extended through 2030. On the targets they actually set — EOM ratio, PAT growth, NPS, retail market share — they have delivered, and in some cases outperformed. The biggest operational miss in the trackable window is the Q1 FY26 claims auto-adjudication suspension for unstated security reasons, which they disclosed proactively and resolved within one quarter; this is a transparency positive but the undisclosed nature of the "information security assurance review" is a mild caution.

The single biggest management signal is the consistent, multi-year profitability trajectory: IFRS PAT has roughly doubled each year for three years (₹106 cr → ₹203 cr → ₹366 cr), the EOM ratio has dropped 550 basis points in a single year, and ROE crossed 10% for the first time in FY26. This is a team that has delivered on the metrics that matter for an insurer scaling toward underwriting breakeven. The gap — and it is real — is that the absence of the audited Annual Report means RPT flows with Bupa Singapore (brand, technology, reinsurance) and the full auditor opinion cannot be verified from primary sources.
