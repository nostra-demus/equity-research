**Note A — Gross Margin discontinuity:** The FY22 and FY23 gross margin figures (≈44–49%) are NOT comparable to FY24–FY26 (≈11–13%). This is an accounting reclassification: prior to FY24, Niva Bupa recognised the full first-year premium as earned in the year written; from FY24, IRDAI mandated the 1/N basis, which spreads the first-year premium over the policy term. This changes both the revenue recognition line and the policy-benefit matching, producing a structural discontinuity in the gross margin series. The FY24–FY26 gross margin figures are comparable with each other but not with FY22/FY23. Gross margin within the post-1/N window (FY24–FY26) shows a mild compression of 90–130 basis points per year, reflecting rising claims mix. [1], [5]

**Note B — CFO/FCF volatility:** FY25 CFO of ₹16,753 mn is elevated by the one-time build-up effect of the 1/N transition (first-year premiums previously recognised immediately now flow through as a reserve liability, temporarily boosting operating cash). FY26 CFO normalises to ₹9,287 mn because this build-up effect does not repeat at the same magnitude. The FY25 CFO/FCF figures should not be treated as a sustainable run-rate. [1], [5]

**Note C — EBITDA construct:** In insurance accounting, EBITDA is not a standard disclosed metric. The Capital IQ vendor derives it as EBIT + D&A (₹221 mn in FY22, ₹93 mn in FY23, ₹234 mn in FY24, ₹271 mn in FY25, ₹308 mn in FY26). The company does not report EBITDA itself. [1]

For intra-year (quarterly) tracking, IFRS is the more meaningful basis because 1/N distorts the IGAAP quarterly pattern severely.

## 6. Key Trend Summary

**Revenue growth direction:** Revenue has grown at 33–48% per year over FY22–FY26, decelerating from approximately 48% in FY22–FY23 to 33% in FY25–FY26. The deceleration is off a larger base and partly reflects the post-1/N accounting normalisation rather than any structural demand slowdown. At the full-year level, double-digit revenue growth remains intact. Management guided 17–19% CAGR for the retail health insurance industry on a 5-year view [Q4 FY26 Earnings Call, May 8, 2026, p.8].

**Margin direction:** Every margin line has improved every year from the loss-making FY22 base. EBITDA margin expanded from (8.3%) in FY22 to 6.1% in FY26, with consistent 86–98 basis point annual expansion in FY25–FY26. EBIT margin expanded 100 basis points in FY26. ROE reached 10.7% in FY26 (first time above 10%), up from 5.3% in FY24 and negative in FY22. Margins are expanding but the company is still in an early-stage profitability curve; absolute EBITDA margin of 6.1% is low for an established insurer.

**Seasonality:** Material and structurally pronounced. Q1 (April–June) captures roughly 39% of annual revenue because Indian retail health insurance renews in April. Q4 (January–March) is the most profitable quarter in both revenue and EPS terms. Q2 and Q3 are structurally loss-making under the 1/N India GAAP basis. The 1/N accounting amplifies this seasonality relative to underlying economics; IFRS accounts show a smoother intra-year pattern.

**Inflection points:** Two clear inflection points in the record. First, FY24 marks the break-even year for EBIT (₹1,781 mn vs ₹393 mn in FY23), driven partly by scale gains and partly by the 1/N accounting change that altered the premium recognition timing. Second, FY25 shows an anomalous CFO spike (₹16,753 mn vs ₹8,299 mn in FY24) attributable to the build-up of first-year premium reserves under the new 1/N basis — a one-time accounting inflow that does not repeat in FY26 (CFO ₹9,287 mn). Any FCF-based valuation or quality assessment must adjust for these two accounting events.
