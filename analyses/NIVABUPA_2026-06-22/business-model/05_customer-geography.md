## 4. Read

On the customer side, Niva Bupa is not concentrated by any single named payer. Revenue comes from millions of retail policyholders (~67% of GWP) and a spread of corporate and bancassurance group accounts (~31%), with no individual customer disclosed at even a fraction of 20%. The business is, however, concentrated on the geography side: 100% of revenue is India, with no international operations, meaning every regulatory action — IRDAI commission caps, GST policy changes, state-level tariff agreements — hits the entire book simultaneously.

The concentration that matters most is not a single customer but a single distribution channel risk. Approximately 20–21% of total premium is channelled through the bancassurance (Banca + NBFC) route, and RBI/IRDAI regulatory changes on how banks sell insurance products are an active policy focus. This channel has no disclosed long-term contractual protection for Niva Bupa; agreements with bank partners are governed by individual tie-ups and can be restructured by regulation.

The single biggest dependency the synthesizer should know about is India-only regulatory concentration: one adverse IRDAI ruling on expense of management limits or commission structures (a live risk as of FY26) would affect 100% of the company's revenue with no geographic hedge available.
