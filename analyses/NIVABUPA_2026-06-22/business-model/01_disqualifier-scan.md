## 2. Triggered Disqualifiers — Detail

No disqualifier triggered.

The data pool contains no primary audited filing (no IRDAI statutory Annual Report, no SEBI LODR annual results, no Notes to Accounts, no Independent Auditor's Report, no cash flow statement from an audited source). As required by this scan's operating rules, the absence of the primary filing is noted as a data gap — it does NOT itself constitute evidence that any disqualifier has been triggered.

The scan applied evidence from the highest-quality sources available: six NIVABUPA earnings call transcripts (Q2 FY25 through Q4 FY26, S&P Global Market Intelligence, Nov 2024 – May 2026) and the data-triage finding that Capital IQ financial exports covering FY2021–FY2026 are in the pool (though unreadable as binary XLS). Across all six transcripts spanning 18 months post-listing, no single fact consistent with any of the eight disqualifiers was found — no auditor qualification, no pledge disclosure, no named RPT at scale, no auditor change, no restated financials (the 1/N change is a prospective regulatory accounting shift, not a correction), no enforcement action, no customer concentration, and no pattern of negative operating cash flow.

The most important limitation is that the primary evidence to conclusively clear checks #1, #3, and #8 (auditor's report, RPT note, and cash flow statement respectively) sits in the audited Annual Report that is absent from the pool. The management-governance module and the red-flags-sweep agent should flag this data gap and recommend that the FY25 or FY26 statutory Annual Report (IRDAI-filed) be obtained before any final investment verdict is reached. The absence of those documents does not trigger a disqualifier here — it caps data sufficiency elsewhere in the pipeline.
