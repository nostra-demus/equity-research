# Competitive Map — NIVABUPA

## 1. Dominant Segment

Retail Health Insurance: approximately 70% of gross written premium (GWP ₹6,582 crores of ₹9,433 crores total in FY26), growing 35% year-on-year, and the segment management tracks most closely. Retail market share rose from 9.1% in FY24 to 10.1% in FY26. [Segment-map upstream; Q4 FY26 Earnings Call transcript, May 8, 2026]

## 2. Named Competitors

### Competitor A — Star Health and Allied Insurance Company Limited

- **Ticker / listing:** NSEI:STARHEALTH (NSE-listed, India)
- **Where they compete:** Retail health insurance in India — the dominant segment. Like Niva Bupa, Star Health is a standalone health insurer (SAHI) regulated by IRDAI. It also writes group health and personal accident, but retail is the overwhelming focus.
- **Scale:** GWP ₹20,369 crores FY26 (N basis), up 16% year-on-year. This is approximately 2.2x Niva Bupa's GWP. [Star Health Q4 FY26 Earnings Call transcript, Apr 29, 2026, prepared remarks — CEO: "GWP increased 16% Y-o-Y on an N basis to INR 20,369 crores"]
- **Profitability / return on capital:**
  - Combined ratio (Ind AS, N basis): 98.8% in FY26, improved from 101.1% in FY25. This means Star Health achieved underwriting profit in FY26 — Niva Bupa (combined ratio 101.4% IFRS FY26) has not yet. [Star Health Q4 FY26 Earnings Call, Apr 29, 2026]
  - Retail loss ratio: 68.2% FY26, improved from ~69% FY25. Niva Bupa's retail loss ratio was 66.8% FY26 — marginally better than Star Health on this line. [Star Health Q4 FY26 call; Niva Bupa Q4 FY26 call]
  - PAT: ₹911 crores FY26 (Ind AS), up 16% year-on-year from ₹787 crores. Normalized PAT ₹1,222 crores FY26 (excludes mark-to-market equity losses of ₹127 crores). Niva Bupa's PAT was ₹366 crores FY26. [Star Health Q4 FY26 call]
  - ROE: 13.1% normalized FY26 (reported ROE 10.1% FY25 restated). Niva Bupa ROE 10.7% FY26. [Star Health Q4 FY26 call; Niva Bupa Q4 FY26 call]
  - ROIC: Not separately disclosed; ROE is the disclosed return metric for both companies.
- **Source named in:** Capital IQ Competitors export for Niva Bupa (source: "Niva Bupa Health Insurance Company Limited (NSEI:NIVABUPA) — Form Doc", data as of Jun-04-2026); Star Health Q3 and Q4 FY26 earnings call transcripts in the data pool; Niva Bupa Q1 FY26 transcript (analyst reference: "your peer has been very aggressive on capturing a higher market share").
- **One-line read:** Star Health is the dominant retail health insurer in India with 31.3% market share in FY26, operating at scale roughly 2x Niva Bupa's, and just turned underwriting-profitable in FY26 — its key advantage is distribution depth (8 lakh+ agents, 15,000+ hospital partners), but its retail loss ratio (68.2%) runs slightly above Niva Bupa's (66.8%), suggesting Niva Bupa has a modest underwriting edge per policy.

### Competitor B — ICICI Lombard General Insurance Company Limited

- **Ticker / listing:** NSEI:ICICIGI (NSE-listed, India)
- **Where they compete:** Retail health insurance in India — growing aggressively into Niva Bupa's dominant segment. ICICI Lombard is a multi-line general insurer (motor, health, commercial), not a SAHI; health is one segment among several. Retail health GWP grew 51.1% in FY26, nearly triple the SAHI industry's ~19.9% growth in the same period.
- **Scale:** Total company GWP: S&P consensus for FY26 ₹258,949 crores (revenue basis), making ICICI Lombard the largest private general insurer in India by premium. Retail health specifically: retail health market share grew from 3.3% (FY25) to 4.1% (FY26) — still well below Niva Bupa's 10.1%, but the growth rate is the fastest among named peers. [ICICI Lombard Q4 FY26 Earnings Call transcript, Apr 15, 2026; S&P Global Market Intelligence consensus estimate, data as of Apr-13-2026]
- **Profitability / return on capital:**
  - Combined ratio (overall company, N basis): 102.4% FY26, broadly flat from 102.6% FY25. No standalone combined ratio for the retail health segment is disclosed. [ICICI Lombard Q4 FY26 call]
  - PAT: ₹2,761 crores (₹27.61 billion) FY26 on N basis, up 14.1% year-on-year. [ICICI Lombard Q4 FY26 call]
  - ROE: 17.8% FY26 (down from 19.1% FY25, partly due to a wage code provision of ₹55 crores). Adjusted for the wage code, ROE was 18.1%. 10-year average ROE cited as 19.1%. [ICICI Lombard Q4 FY26 call]
  - ROIC: Not separately disclosed; ROE is the primary return metric cited.
- **Source named in:** Capital IQ Competitors export for Niva Bupa (Jun-04-2026); ICICI Lombard Q4 FY26 earnings call transcript in the data pool; Niva Bupa management refers to "multi-liners" and SAHI EoM regulatory asymmetry in Q4 FY26 analyst Q&A (implicit competitive reference).
- **One-line read:** ICICI Lombard's retail health book is growing at 51% per year — faster than any SAHI — backed by a 4,500-branch bank distribution network through ICICI Bank, but its overall combined ratio remains above 100%, its health segment profitability is not separately disclosed, and it operates under a lower EoM regulatory cap than SAHIs, which structurally constrains its ability to replicate a high-investment agency model.

### Competitor C — Care Health Insurance Limited

- **Ticker / listing:** Not listed (private company; subsidiary of Religare Enterprises / Mountvista Healthcare; no equity traded publicly as of data pool)
- **Where they compete:** Retail and group health insurance in India — a direct SAHI competitor across individual, family, and senior-citizen product lines.
- **Scale:** LTM revenue approximately USD 792 million (~₹6,650 crores at ~84 INR/USD, indicative conversion) as reported in the Capital IQ Competitors export (data as of Jun-04-2026, LTM date not specified for Care Health). This puts Care Health between Niva Bupa (₹9,433 crores GWP) and ManipalCigna (₹231 mm USD LTM, ~₹1,940 crores) in scale among private-sector SAHIs. [Capital IQ Competitors export, data as of Jun-04-2026 — "Care Health Insurance Limited, LTM Revenue $792.01mm"]
- **Profitability / return on capital:** Not public / not disclosed. Care Health is not listed; no audited public financials, no earnings transcript, and no third-party data in the pool discloses its combined ratio, loss ratio, or return on equity. The Capital IQ record provides revenue only.
- **Source named in:** Capital IQ Competitors export for Niva Bupa (Jun-04-2026), sourced from Niva Bupa's own IRDAI-filed documents ("Form Doc").
- **One-line read:** Care Health is India's third-largest SAHI by estimated premium, competing directly in the retail individual and family health market against Niva Bupa, but remains private and discloses no public financials — its competitive moves are opaque relative to listed peers.

## 3. Competitive Position

Niva Bupa is gaining share. Retail health market share rose from 9.1% in FY24 to 10.1% for full-year FY26 and 10.4% in Q4 FY26 specifically. [Q4 FY26 Earnings Call, May 8, 2026, CEO: "Our market share on retail health closed at 10.1% on a full year basis, up from 9.4%... in Q4 specifically, we moved our market share to 10.4%"]

The share gain is supported by volume proxies: retail GWP grew 35% in FY26 against the SAHI industry retail growth of approximately 19–30% (management cited "industry retail H2 FY26 ~30%"; Star Health cites industry retail growth of 19.9% for the year). Niva Bupa is outgrowing the industry in the dominant segment. The channel that drove the gain is the agency network: net 58,000 agents added in FY26, reaching every pin code through a virtual agency for Tier 3 and below markets. [Q4 FY26 call; Star Health Q4 FY26 call for industry growth benchmark]

The counter-signal is that Star Health, despite running at 2.2x Niva Bupa's GWP, is now underwriting-profitable (combined ratio 98.8% vs Niva Bupa's 101.4%), which means the market leader is compounding from a more stable base. Niva Bupa's share gains have come alongside a retail loss ratio (66.8%) that is marginally better than Star Health's (68.2%), suggesting underwriting quality is not being sacrificed for growth.

**Position vs peers: Gaining** — confirmed by market share data.

## 4. Competitive Shape

The Indian retail health insurance market is an oligopoly with one dominant player and several fast-growing challengers. Star Health holds approximately 31.3% of the retail health segment (FY26), giving the top-two players (Star Health + Niva Bupa) combined retail share of roughly 41–42%. The segment also attracts multi-line general insurers (ICICI Lombard at 4.1% retail health share, growing 51% per year) and other private SAHIs (Care Health, Aditya Birla Health, ManipalCigna). The precise Herfindahl-Hirschman Index is not disclosed by IRDAI in available data, but the structure is clear: one player (Star Health) at approximately 3x the next SAHI (Niva Bupa), with 5–8 credible challengers below and multi-liners entering the segment from above. The market is growing fast enough (~20–30% retail GWP per year) that absolute premium is rising for most players even as shares shift — this reduces the zero-sum intensity of near-term competition but raises the stakes for whoever builds the larger renewal book.

## 5. Caveat

The Capital IQ Competitors export identifies nine companies as named competitors in Niva Bupa's own IRDAI filings, including National General Insurance (UAE-domiciled, irrelevant to the Indian retail health segment) and Bajaj General Insurance (primarily a multi-line non-health insurer). These were excluded from the 2–3 core profiles above because they do not materially compete in the dominant segment (retail health in India) or are outside the 5x revenue scale threshold relative to Niva Bupa. The three profiled competitors — Star Health, ICICI Lombard, and Care Health — are the most direct and best-sourced rivals.

For Care Health specifically, the absence of audited public financials means its combined ratio, loss ratio, and return on equity cannot be stated. The moat agent's peer-economics table will need to note this gap. If Care Health's financials become available (via an IPO filing, IRDAI published data, or CRISIL rating report), the peer anchor for the third competitor should be updated.
