| Long-term incentive (TBRSUs, 40% of LTIP) | **Continued service** (time-based vesting, 1–3 years) | 40% of LTIP (web) | **Retention** — not a performance metric; pure time-vesting | `FY25 10-K, Note 24 (l.8102–8112)` |

**Classification verdict.**

- **LTIP (60% PBRSU component):** All three PBRSU metrics — EPS, AROIC, and rTSR — are per-share or returns-based. None is a size-based metric (no revenue, no absolute EBITDA, no deal count, no tonnage). AROIC in particular is the correct anti-empire-building lever for a serial acquirer: a manager paid on ROIC bears the cost of acquisitions that do not earn their capital cost. EPS aligns with per-share value. rTSR penalizes underperformance relative to peers regardless of commodity tailwinds. **This is a returns-oriented LTIP metric set.**

- **Annual bonus (AIP, ~15% of CEO total comp):** The primary funding metric is **Adjusted PBT(I)** — an **absolute, size-based metric**. A larger business earns more absolute profit even if per-share returns fall. The quality-of-earnings modifier and individual objectives partially offset this, but the core AIP pool-funding mechanism rewards profitable scale, not per-share efficiency. With the AIP at only ~15% of target CEO total comp and the LTIP at ~77%, the absolute weight of the size-based metric in total pay is modest — but it exists.

- **Adjusted metrics caveat.** All performance metrics are management-defined adjustments. FY2025 adjusted EPS ($7.57) ran ~54% above GAAP diluted EPS ($4.91) [cross-ref `business-model/11_capital-allocation-governance`, §1]. Paying on adjusted returns during a large acquisition normalizes out integration costs and charges that shareholders bear — meaning the ROIC and EPS metrics are "adjusted returns-based," not raw-GAAP returns-based. This is a structural softener on alignment.

**Read on pay-for-performance.** The structure that is visible points to high leverage on outcomes. The CEO's pay is ~92% variable/equity, options were replaced by RSUs with EPS/AROIC/rTSR performance gates, a recoupment policy exists, and the CEO holds a personal stake beyond grants. The say-on-pay recovery — from 84.6% in 2025 to ~98% in 2026 — suggests the 2025 Viterra-year pay program was corrected or better explained in the 2026 proxy rather than reflecting a lasting structural problem with pay design. From the minority holder's view, strong adjusted-metric payouts in a year of significant accounting-level deterioration and massive dilution is a signal worth watching even if the adjustments are individually defensible.

## 4. Incentive Alignment Read

On the evidence the pool carries, confirmed by web-sourced proxy material, the **disclosed long-term incentive metric set — EPS, AROIC, and relative TSR — is per-share/returns-based, with no size, revenue, EBITDA, deal-count, or volume metric in the LTIP component**, and equity is granted as performance- and time-vesting RSUs under a shareholder-approved plan with a clawback policy. AROIC on an acquisitive company is the correct structural lever against empire-building, and CEO pay is heavily incentive-weighted (~92% variable per web sources). The **annual cash bonus**, which is the smaller component (~15% of total target comp), does use **Adjusted PBT(I) — an absolute, size-based funding metric** — diluting the alignment from "well-aligned" to "mixed." The metrics are also on management-defined adjusted figures (FY25 adjusted EPS ~54% above GAAP), softening the alignment further. Say-on-pay has recovered to ~98% at the May 2026 AGM after the 84.6% dip in 2025, suggesting shareholders do not view the design as fundamentally broken.

**Conclusion: mixed — leaning aligned (returns/per-share).** The LTIP (77% of target pay) is returns-based and per-share. The AIP (15%) funds off absolute profit — size-based. The adjustment policy adds a further softener. The verdict is held at "mixed" rather than "well-aligned" because (a) one component is explicitly size-based, (b) all metrics are management-adjusted, (c) the proxy is not in the pool and weights are unverified, and (d) the 2025 say-on-pay dip (even though recovered in 2026) signals past investor discomfort. The **single biggest incentive signal: the LTIP pays on ROIC and relative TSR, not on scale** — which, for a serial acquirer mid-integrating a $10.6B deal, is exactly the anti-empire-building structure you want; the open question is whether adjusted-metric definitions and the size-based AIP funding mechanism dilute that structural alignment in practice.

*Items not addressed here by design: officer ownership percentages and insider buying/selling (agent 04), board and compensation-committee independence (agent 05), and the aggressiveness of non-GAAP adjustments (earnings 06).*
