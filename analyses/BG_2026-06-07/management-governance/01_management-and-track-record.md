## 3. Stated Strategy & Targets

Management's stated strategy is to be the premier integrated agribusiness platform — buying and processing oilseeds and grains globally, extracting value on crush spreads, refining, and grain merchandising, and expanding into higher-value food ingredients and renewable feedstocks. The Viterra acquisition (closed July 2, 2025) was the centrepiece of that strategy: it added softseed and grain origination capacity in Canada, Australia, and Europe, and expanded the Argentina soybean platform. Explicit, checkable targets include: (1) $250 million per year in annual gross pre-tax cost synergies from the Viterra combination within three years of close (i.e., by mid-2028), upgraded at the March 2026 Investor Day to $350 million in operational cost synergies plus $250–$300 million in commercial synergies; (2) an interim synergy run-rate of ~$220 million by year-end 2026; (3) FY2026 adjusted EPS guidance of $9.00–$9.50 (midpoint $9.25); (4) FY2026 capex of $1.5–$1.7 billion to support in-flight growth projects (Destrehan, Louisiana port expansion and crush plant; Morristown, Indiana SPC facility). Management does not guide revenue or EBITDA, only adjusted EPS and below-the-line items.

## 5. Management Read

Heckman and Neppl have run Bunge together for ~7 years — unusual longevity for a non-founder professional management team — and the pair has navigated a commodity supercycle, two years of normalization, and a transformational acquisition without losing their seat or changing their key personnel. The operational record (exit from non-core assets, portfolio simplification, Viterra closed and integrating ahead of schedule on costs) matches what management promised in broad terms, and annual guidance has been met in three of the last four fiscal years with no egregious misses. The biggest positive signal is the CFO/CEO pairing that predates Bunge (both worked together at Gavilon), which provides continuity and shared operating instincts that is rarely available in hired management teams. The biggest uncertainty is that Viterra's full synergy capture ($350M+ operational + $250–300M commercial) is a multi-year project that has just started: the $70M delivered in six months is encouraging, but the endpoint is still unproven and the target has been revised upward from the original proxy number, which is an early reassurance rather than a verified result. No integrity, succession, or governance concerns are present in the data pool.
