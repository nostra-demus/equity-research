All adjusted/non-GAAP figures are labeled as such.

**Data sufficiency note:** Three full years of cash-flow-statement data are available (FY2023–FY2025) from the FY2025 10-K. Balance-sheet data covers two year-ends (Dec-31-2024 and Dec-31-2025) only; FY2022 and earlier balance-sheet and buyback detail are not in the pool. The 2026 DEF 14A is not available. The scorecard is a three-period scorecard; the cap for "no multi-year capital-allocation history" does NOT apply because three years of cash-flow data exist.

Glencore and CPP Investments received board-nomination rights in return. The BP Bunge Bioenergia exit (the geese) was timed to partly fund Viterra's cash consideration (the hen), and the EU Oilseeds Divestment forced the sale of ~$486M of net operating assets at approximately breakeven as a regulatory price. The buyback program — which had been running at $600–$1,100M per year — was effectively paused in Q4 2025 (only $77K repurchased in the final two months of the year). **RF-CAP-004 is triggered.** The M&A discipline component is capped at 50/100 per MODULE_RULES Score Cap Rules.

## 5. Capital-Allocation Read

Over FY2023–FY2025, Bunge deployed capital across every channel simultaneously — $2.3B of buybacks, $1.2B of dividends, $4.2B of growth capex, and a $10.6B acquisition — while generating only $6.1B of cumulative CFO. The gap was filled with $9.8B of new gross debt. The buyback program in FY2023–FY2024 was the best use of capital in the period: the team repurchased ~17M net shares at average prices of roughly $91–$111, reducing diluted share count by a combined ~14M shares before Viterra erased those gains and added 45M more in a single transaction. The Viterra acquisition is simultaneously the largest single capital decision and the most uncertain: at $10.6B total consideration, it cost more than Bunge's own pre-deal market cap, required selling the Brazil bioenergy business and giving up EU operating assets as regulatory conditions, gave Glencore and CPP board-nomination rights, and produced a 44% net share-count increase that will take years and billions of further buybacks to reverse. Year-1 synergies (">$70M") are on track but the full multi-year target is not disclosed in the pool and the combination is less than one year old. The overall verdict is **mixed**: the buyback record in FY2023–FY2024 was genuinely shareholder-oriented and value-creative at the per-share level; the Viterra bet is unproven, structurally dilutive, and carries the highest opportunity cost (BP Bunge Bioenergia exit, EU asset sales, board-seat concessions, paused buyback, and $10B+ new debt). The single biggest capital-allocation signal is the Viterra transaction: it dominates the scorecard and will determine whether the FY2023–FY2025 capital-allocation record is ultimately judged a per-share value creator or a size-driven growth exercise.

**Overall verdict: mixed.**
Best use: FY2023–FY2024 buybacks (net share-count reduction at defensible prices).
Worst use: Viterra acquisition (large, debt- and equity-funded, structurally dilutive, unproven at <1 year post-close).
