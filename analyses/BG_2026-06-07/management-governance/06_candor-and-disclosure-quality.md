## 1. Owning the Misses

From `01_management-and-track-record`, the checkable miss record is limited: Bunge issues late-year (roughly 90–105 days before the actual) refinement guides. Three of the last four full-year adjusted-EPS guides were hit or beaten; only FY2024 came in slightly below (−0.6%, $9.19 vs $9.25 guide). Three category-level misses are assessable: the FY2024 guide miss, the grain segment Q1 2026 miss, and the Viterra per-share value promise (open, not yet assessable).

## 2. Non-GAAP / Adjustment Aggressiveness

Cross-reference: `earnings/06_earnings-quality.md` is available in full. Key data drawn from it below.

| Signal | Detail | Source |
|---|---:|---|
| Recurring "one-off" addbacks | **Viterra integration and acquisition costs have recurred for three consecutive years: $114M (FY23), $244M (FY24), $223M (FY25).** These are excluded from adjusted EPS as "certain charges." They have a legitimate origin (a real transaction cost), but after three years at $100M–$250M per year they are a running cost of an active M&A strategy rather than a one-off. Impairment charges also appear every year in CFO ($104M / $41M / $53M, FY23/24/25). Asset-sale gains run through CFO every year (+$4M / +$205M / +$160M, FY23/24/25). | GAAP-to-adjusted gap (magnitude / trend) | **Very large and widening.** FY2025: adjusted EPS $7.57 vs GAAP $4.91 — a +54% gap. Q1 2026: adjusted EPS $1.83 vs GAAP $0.35 — adjusted is 5.2x GAAP. The gap has grown in magnitude from FY2023 (where GAAP *exceeded* adjusted by $1.21, proving the adjustment is two-sided) to FY2024/25 (net charges dominating). The Q1 2026 quarter is extreme: the MTM timing difference alone was $1.28/share of the $1.48 total adjustment. | `earnings/06_earnings-quality.md §7`; `Q1 2026 earnings release, p.1–2`; `Capital IQ Surprise export` |
| Does adjusted framing flatter reality? | **Partially, but with real economic basis.** The MTM timing adjustment strips non-cash gains/losses on commodity hedges and readily-marketable inventory (RMI) that reverse over time — this is defensible for a commodity processor (the hedge offsets the physical position; the realized value comes at delivery, not at mark date). SBC is correctly kept inside adjusted EPS ($73M FY25), not excluded. Useful lives are unchanged, no reverse factoring. The two genuine concerns: (1) when adjusted EPS is 5.2x GAAP in a single quarter, the user is entirely dependent on management's classification of "timing vs permanent" losses — the 10-K and 10-Q do not give enough information to independently verify the split; (2) the recurring "certain charges" bucket (integration costs) is partly a structural cost of doing business adjusted out every year. The net picture: the adjustment is more defensible than most (genuinely two-sided, no SBC exclusion), but the magnitude forces reliance on a management-defined number. | `earnings/06_earnings-quality.md §4, §8 (traps)`; `Q1 2026 release, p.2` |

**Non-GAAP aggressiveness verdict:** Moderate — not the worst pattern (SBC kept in, direction flips, reconciliation disclosed). However, the sheer scale (adjusted 5.2x GAAP in Q1 2026; recurring "one-offs" three years running) means the adjusted series carries almost all the informational weight, and that is a reliance the user cannot independently validate from public filings alone.

## 3. Disclosure Transparency

Bunge discloses four reportable segments (Soybean Processing and Refining, Softseed Processing and Refining, Other Oilseeds Processing and Refining, Grain Merchandising and Milling) with EBIT at the segment level. Segment EBIT, geographic breakdown, volume data, and corporate reconciliation are all present in the 10-K MD&A. The Q1 2026 call provides granular directional commentary on each segment by region. The primary disclosure gap is **what management does not provide**: sustaining vs growth capex is disclosed only for Q1 2026 (sustaining $95M; growth ~$240M), not annually — making true FCF and maintenance-capex math impossible to replicate from filings alone. Adjusted EBITDA is not reported at all; Bunge reports only adjusted EBIT and adjusted EPS, so the user must construct EBITDA.

One trend toward **less transparency** deserves flagging: the segment structure was redefined in Q3 2025 after the Viterra close, and pre-2025 comparisons do not map cleanly to the new four-segment structure. This is acquisition-driven, not manipulative — but it breaks the historical comparability of the key KPI (segment EBIT) at exactly the moment when that history would matter most for assessing the Viterra integration. Also: the Viterra operations were excluded from the FY2025 ICFR assessment (33% of total assets ex-goodwill) under SEC first-year guidance — a permitted scope gap but a real control-visibility reduction. The Brazil indirect-tax exposure ($760M at Q1 2026, unprovisioned) is disclosed in the contingency notes, but its 21% single-quarter growth rate is only visible by comparing the 10-K to the 10-Q — management does not call it out on the Q1 transcript at all, even as a monitoring item.

## 4. Tone in Bad Times

The worst recent quarter for Bunge was FY2025 as a whole (full-year FCF negative at −$879M; CFO/EBITDA collapsed from 84% to 38%; GAAP EPS fell to $4.91 from $7.99; the adjusted EPS of $7.57 was below prior years' $13.66 and $13.91). The Q1 2026 call is not a "bad times" quarter — Q1 2026 was a strong beat. However, the CEO shareholder letter and the FY2025 MD&A provide the best tone test for the bad-year communication.

The CEO letter is promotional throughout: the year is framed as "a year of transformation" and "profound progress," and every forward statement emphasizes opportunity. The letter does not mention that GAAP EPS fell 39%, that FCF turned negative, or that the stock underperformed significantly. The 10-K MD&A does explain the earnings decline factually (lower Corporate and Other EBIT, higher net interest expense, Viterra dilution), but these disclosures are in the SEC-required format, not volunteered in plain language in the letter.

On the Q1 call Q&A, the tone is more candid: when analysts push on the grain segment shortfall, CFO Neppl says clearly "we got off to a rough start" and "it's hard to see when things are going to turn." When pressed on the implied Q2 EPS being flat despite good processing margins, Neppl walks through the higher tax rate, higher interest, and higher corporate expense without deflecting. Questions are answered directly rather than deflected. No analyst asked a Q&A question that management failed to address or redirected without substance. In total: the prepared remarks are bullish and promotional; the Q&A is substantive and direct.

## 5. Candor Read

Management earns a mixed candor verdict. In live Q&A — the most reliable test — Heckman and Neppl are direct: they name causes of segment weakness (bunker fuel costs, CBE margin pressure, cocoa pricing), acknowledge limits of visibility ("it's hard to see when things are going to turn"), and update guidance honestly including downside segments. This is the strongest positive candor signal. Against that: the shareholder letter is a promotional document that presents 2025 as a year of progress while omitting the 39% GAAP EPS decline, the negative FCF, and the dilution cost of Viterra. The non-GAAP gap (adjusted EPS 5.2x GAAP in Q1 2026; recurring "certain charges" for three consecutive years at $100–250M) requires users to trust a management-defined number that is not independently verifiable from the filings. The biggest single candor signal — one way — is that the MTM adjustment is genuinely two-sided (GAAP exceeded adjusted in FY2023; the sign has flipped multiple times), which is more trustworthy than a one-way add-back engine. The biggest single candor concern is that the Brazil indirect-tax exposure ($760M and rising 21% QoQ) is not mentioned anywhere in the Q1 transcript or the shareholder letter, despite being material relative to one year's net income ($816M FY25). That omission is notable.

**Verdict: mixed.** The Q&A communication is candid; the promotional letter and the recurring "one-off" adjustment pattern are not. No hard integrity disqualifier is present (business-model/01_disqualifier-scan: no triggers), so this does not compound into a low-trust read — but it does not clear the bar for "candid / high-trust" either.
