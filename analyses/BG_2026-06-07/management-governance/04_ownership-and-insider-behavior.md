**Unaligned-owner test (CLAUDE.md §24, Filter 6).** Bunge is a widely-held corporation. No government control. Not a listed subsidiary of a parent with a competing vehicle. Not a sprawling unrelated conglomerate — it is a focused agribusiness-and-processing operator (ABCD peer group). None of the three structural conflict categories from Filter 6 applies. RF-OWN-004 does NOT trigger.

The Glencore/CPP bloc (~31% combined with board rights) is the closest analog to a misaligned-owner risk, but the facts do not reach Filter 6: Glencore is an active commodity trader/mining company, not a government; CPP is a pension fund with passive return objectives; neither is steering Bunge's economics to a parallel vehicle. The shareholder agreements give nomination rights, not veto rights over operational decisions. The risk is influence at the board (four of possibly eleven or more seats), not the structural control of a misaligned parent. The bloc's interests could diverge from minorities on future M&A or capital allocation — but that is a governance-of-the-board risk, not a structural owner-misalignment disqualifier. No Control-structure-fairness cap from Filter 6 applies. This is flagged as a medium-severity monitoring item, not a structural red flag.

**Shareholder-friendliness note:** The registration rights held by Glencore/CPP/BCI represent a meaningful overhang — these are stock received as deal consideration (not purchased at market), and the holders have the legal right to force Bunge to register those shares for public sale. A large coordinated or sequential sale by those holders would dilute price without any corresponding benefit to existing minority holders.

## 5. Ownership / Insider Read

Insiders own approximately 1% or below of shares outstanding — almost entirely through equity compensation grants, not cash purchases. No officer or director has made a visible open-market purchase over the reviewed period, and the only small sale identified (2,831 shares via a Form 144) is immaterial. The insider transaction signal is neutral: no conviction buying, no meaningful selling. The biggest structural feature for minority holders is the ~34% concentrated bloc held by the Viterra sellers — Glencore (~17%), CPP (~14%), and BCI (~3%) — who received those shares as deal consideration and now hold contractual board-nomination rights (four board seats between Glencore and CPP alone). This is not a misaligned-owner situation under Filter 6, but the registration rights these holders possess create a latent selling overhang that could pressure the stock if any of them decides to exit in size, without any offset to minority holders.
