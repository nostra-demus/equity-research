## 3. Triage Verdict

**Partial.**

The pool contains several proven, dated scheduled events — the AGM on June 29, 2026 (carrying the dividend vote and the first capital-return decision by a newly listed entity), the Iveco acquisition targeting Q2 FY27 closure (the single largest financial event on the company's horizon), and a specific NCD maturity wall (₹801 crore zero-coupon bonds due September 1, 2026 as the largest near-term single instrument). The Q1 FY27 results date is a soft window (no board date fixed yet), not a proven date.

Three of the highest-impact forward events are undated or partially evidenced:

1. Iveco deal closure is guided to Q2 FY27 but is conditional on France and Spain regulatory approvals — no specific decision date is disclosed, and an activist hedge fund has acquired 6–7% of Iveco shares, adding execution uncertainty.
2. The TMF Holdings merger is at NCLT application stage with no hearing date.
3. EPR and Labour Code regulatory finalisation carry no disclosed decision dates.

What the calendar will be able to carry: the AGM dividend event (June 29, 2026) with a proven date and the Iveco closure window (Q2 FY27 = July–September 2026) as the two primary dated anchors, supplemented by the NCD maturities and the Q1 results window. The calendar will also carry several real but soft-windowed catalysts (EPR policy, Labour Code, government capex execution). All upstream modules are present in this run and add substantial forward-looking context. The calendar will not be purely thematic, but it will contain a meaningful proportion of events with soft timing rather than proven dates.
