## 2. Cash Conversion Assessment

CFO is tracking Underlying EBITDA extremely well — in fact, it is running well above it in both available periods. The CFO/Underlying EBITDA ratio stands at 130% in the nine-month FY25 period and 145% in FY26. Ratios above 100% are structural in this business because TMCV operates with a large negative working capital position (suppliers effectively fund the business — trade payables of ₹15,704 cr far exceed trade receivables of ₹2,721 cr at March 31, 2026). The FY26 ratio of 145% was additionally boosted by a ₹6,657 cr working capital inflow, of which ₹4,088 cr came from other liabilities (predominantly customer advances / contract liabilities). This is not a quality concern — it reflects cash collected from customers before vehicles are delivered, which is a normal feature of TMCV's commercial vehicle business model. The trajectory is improving, and there is no gap between reported earnings and operating cash flow. If anything, cash generation overstates near-term economic profit slightly because the ₹4,088 cr other-liability build includes deferred revenue that will unwind as deliveries occur.

## 3. Working Capital Trends

*DSO = trade receivables / (revenue / 365). DIO = inventories / (COGS / 365). DPO = trade payables / (COGS / 365). FY25 calculations annualize the nine-month period (273 days) to get a comparable daily rate. Comparisons are directional only given period-length mismatch.*

| Metric | FY25 (9M, directional) | FY26 (full year) | Direction | Risk |
|---|---:|---:|---|---|
| Receivable days — DSO (days) | ~14.4 | ~11.8 | Improving (shorter) | None |
| Inventory days — DIO (days) | ~32.2 | ~35.1 | Marginally worse (+2.9 days, +9%) | Low |
| Payable days — DPO (days) | ~100.1 | ~101.1 | Stable | None |
| Cash conversion cycle (DSO + DIO − DPO) (days) | ~−53.5 | ~−54.2 | Stable / slightly improving | None |

**Computation details:**
- FY26 daily revenue = ₹83,855 / 365 = ₹229.7 cr; DSO = ₹2,721 / ₹229.7 = 11.8 days. [SEBI LODR Balance Sheet + P&L, May 13, 2026]
- FY26 daily COGS = ₹56,732 / 365 = ₹155.4 cr; DIO = ₹5,448 / ₹155.4 = 35.1 days. [SEBI LODR May 13, 2026]
- FY26 DPO = ₹15,704 / ₹155.4 = 101.1 days. Trade payables = micro/small enterprises ₹371 cr + others ₹13,113 cr + acceptances ₹2,220 cr = ₹15,704 cr. [SEBI LODR Balance Sheet, May 13, 2026]
- FY25 daily COGS (9M annualized) = ₹39,196 / 273 = ₹143.6 cr; DIO = ₹4,625 / ₹143.6 = 32.2 days.

**Flag check:**
- DSO rising more than 10% YoY: No — DSO fell from ~14.4 to ~11.8 days (−18%). Revenue recognition concern: not triggered.
- DIO rising more than 15% YoY: No — DIO rose ~9% (2.9 days). The inventory build (₹823 cr) was split between finished goods (+₹397 cr) and raw materials (+₹327 cr) per MD&A p.218. This reflects a modest inventory restocking post H1 supply disruptions, not channel stuffing. Below the flag threshold.
- DPO rising sharply: No — DPO is stable at ~100-101 days. Not a liquidity stress signal.

The negative cash conversion cycle (-54 days) means TMCV collects cash from customers before it pays suppliers. This is a hallmark of a business with pricing power and is structurally healthy.

**Key finding on FVTPL adjustment:** The FVTPL loss of ₹2,418 cr in FY26 is 79.8% of reported PAT (₹3,030 cr). This is a large adjustment. However, the company is fully transparent about it: Note 8 in the SEBI LODR filing explicitly states "certain investments fair valued through profit and loss held by a subsidiary company resulted in loss of ₹2,418 crores." The loss is non-cash, relates to a subsidiary's equity investment portfolio, and is not tax-deductible (driving the higher-than-statutory effective tax rate of 35.01% per Note 9). The adjustment is disclosed and defined in the Underlying EBITDA footnote. The concern is that this item may recur at a different magnitude annually, making GAAP earnings systematically noisy.

**SBC note:** SBC is included in Underlying EBITDA (only ₹11 cr in FY26), so the company is not gaming the "adjusted" metric by stripping out compensation expense.

## 9. Earnings Quality Score

**Score: 72 / 100**

Band: 61–80 — Mostly clean but some working capital or adjustment noise.

**Primary reason for this score:** The core operating earnings are cash-backed and supported by strong CFO-to-EBITDA conversion (145% in FY26), a deeply negative cash conversion cycle, falling DSO, and no evidence of revenue-recognition concerns. The deduction from the top band comes from three factors: (1) the large FVTPL mark-to-market item (₹2,418 cr, ~80% of reported PAT) makes GAAP earnings structurally noisy and unpredictable year to year; (2) this is only the first full year of standalone audited financials for this entity — no multi-year audited history exists to verify that the cash conversion ratio and working capital profile are structural rather than period-specific; and (3) the exceptional items (₹1,428 cr in FY26) were dominated by genuine demerger one-offs, but the recurring appearance of small restructuring/pension items in both available periods is noted.

## 10. The Single Biggest Quality Concern

The single biggest risk to reported earnings overstating economic reality is the FVTPL equity investment mark-to-market item held in a subsidiary. In FY26, this produced a non-cash, non-deductible loss of ₹2,418 cr — equivalent to 80% of reported PAT. In a year when the subsidiary's equity portfolio recovers, this line could swing to a material gain, making reported PAT look far higher than operating performance justifies. The company's Underlying EBITDA definition correctly strips this out, but investors who focus on reported PAT or EPS will see violent swings that have nothing to do with the commercial vehicle business. The effective tax rate will also swing in the opposite direction (because the loss is non-deductible, a gain would reduce the effective rate sharply). In FY26, reported PAT of ₹3,030 cr versus normalised PAT of approximately ₹5,900 cr represents a 94% downward distortion — the largest single gap between economic and reported earnings. This is a disclosure and transparency concern, not evidence of manipulation: the filing is fully transparent about it. However, the synthesizer should use Underlying EBITDA (₹10,314 cr, 12.3% margin) and normalised PAT as the primary metrics, not GAAP PAT, and should flag this item as a persistent source of reported-earnings volatility.
