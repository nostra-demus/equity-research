# Historical Financials — TMCV (Tata Motors Limited, formerly TML Commercial Vehicles Limited)

**Reporting regime:** India / SEBI-LODR. Reporting standard: Ind AS. Currency: INR (₹ crores). Fiscal year ends March 31. TMCV was incorporated June 23, 2024 and received the demerged CV business effective October 1, 2025 (appointed date July 1, 2025). FY26 (April 1, 2025 to March 31, 2026) is the first full 12-month fiscal year for this entity on a consolidated basis. The FY25 comparative in the audited filings covers only nine months (June 23, 2024 to March 31, 2025) and is explicitly flagged as not directly comparable.

**Critical data limitation:** No audited multi-year annual history exists for this entity beyond FY26. The FY25 figure is a nine-month period (not annualised in the filing). Pre-demerger years (FY23, FY24) are available only as CV-segment carve-out data from the old Tata Motors consolidated group — they are not audited stand-alone financials for TMCV. The annual table below covers FY23–FY26 using CV-segment data for FY23–FY24 (source: MD&A commentary and transcript) and audited consolidated data for FY25 (nine months) and FY26 (full year). FY23 and FY24 figures are marked as carve-out/unaudited segment data. YoY growth rates where periods are not directly comparable are noted accordingly.

## 5. Quarterly Seasonality Table

*Insufficient quarterly history for seasonality analysis.* This entity has only four quarters of consolidated financial history (Q1–Q4 FY26). A minimum of three fiscal years of quarterly data is required for a meaningful seasonality table. The prior-period nine-month data (June 23 to March 31, 2025) does not cover a full fiscal year and cannot be used to establish seasonality.

From the available single-year quarterly split, the revenue distribution in FY26 was approximately: Q1 ~18%, Q2 ~23%, Q3 ~26%, Q4 ~31%. This shows a pronounced H2-heavy pattern (Q4 being the strongest quarter), consistent with infrastructure spending seasonality and festive-season freight demand in India. However, one year is not sufficient to confirm this as a structural pattern vs. a cycle-specific effect (the FY26 Q4 was boosted by recovery from H1 supply-chain disruptions and GST 2.0 rollout effects).

## 6. Key Trend Summary

Revenue is growing. On a like-for-like full-year basis, revenue grew from approximately ₹66,000–68,000 cr in FY23 to ₹83,855 cr in FY26 — a three-year CAGR of roughly 7–8% — with the pace re-accelerating in FY26 (+9.8% over the annualised FY25 level per the Chairman's letter). The FY26 revenue of ₹83,855 cr is the highest in the company's history as a CV-focused entity.

Margins are expanding, rapidly and across multiple years. Underlying EBITDA margin widened from approximately 7.8% in FY23 to 12.3% in FY26 — a 550 basis-point structural expansion over three years, confirmed by management in the earnings call. This is not a one-quarter effect. The 11th consecutive quarter of double-digit EBITDA margins was delivered in Q4 FY26. EBIT margin crossed 10% for the first time in FY26, reaching 10.2%.

Seasonality cannot yet be confirmed from one year of data. The FY26 quarterly pattern is heavily H2-weighted (Q4 = ~31% of annual revenue). This is consistent with India's broader infrastructure and freight cycle but must be treated as indicative, not established seasonal fact, until at least two more full years of data are available.

The single biggest inflection in the last three years is the balance sheet transformation: from a net debt position of approximately ₹4,016 cr at the end of the nine-month FY25 period to net cash of ₹13,713 cr (company-defined) at March 31, 2026 — a swing of roughly ₹17,700 cr in one full year, driven by ₹14,981 cr of operating cash flow and disciplined repayment of ₹3,491 cr of long-term borrowings plus ₹963 cr of short-term borrowings. The company moved from net-debt to net-cash in a single year, which substantially changes its financial risk profile going forward.
