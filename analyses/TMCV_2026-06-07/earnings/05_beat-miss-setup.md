# Beat / Miss Setup — TMCV

**Reporting regime:** India / SEBI-LODR. Reporting standard: Ind AS. Currency: INR (₹ crores). Fiscal year ends March 31. All consensus data sourced from S&P Global Market Intelligence Estimates as of April 27, 2026 (pre-Q4 FY26 results release). Consensus data exists; partial-data rule does not apply.

---

## 1. Next Quarter Context

The next reporting quarter is Q1 FY27 (April 1 to June 30, 2026), results expected in late July or early August 2026. This is the seasonally weakest quarter for Indian commercial vehicle (CV) manufacturers: monsoon onset suppresses construction and freight activity in June, fleet owner purchasing decisions are deferred, and the annual base effect from a strong Q4 FY26 (₹26,098 cr revenue, 13.1% EBITDA margin — the best quarter of the year) creates a tough sequential comparison. The Street's FY27 consensus (revenue ₹881,357mm / ~₹88,136 cr; normalized EPS ₹19.17) was set before the Q4 FY26 results landed and embeds +5.1% revenue growth and +20.5% EPS growth over the FY26 actuals. Q1 will almost certainly print below Q4's absolute levels in both revenue and margin, so the question is not sequential improvement — it is whether the company can deliver enough in Q1 to keep the full-year bar intact, while navigating commodity cost inflation that management described as "significantly higher" than the 100 basis-point hit already absorbed in Q4 FY26.

## 6. Seasonality Read

TMCV has only one full fiscal year of consolidated quarterly data (Q1–Q4 FY26), which is insufficient to confirm seasonality as a structural pattern rather than a cycle-specific effect. That single year showed a heavily H2-weighted revenue distribution: Q1 ~18%, Q2 ~23%, Q3 ~26%, Q4 ~31% of annual revenue. This is consistent with India's general infrastructure and freight seasonality — monsoon suppresses June activity, festive demand and construction restarts drive H2 — and with management's own language describing H1 FY27 as expected to be softer ("H1 was muted" echoing FY26 experience per the FQ4 2026 call). For Q1 FY27 specifically, seasonality is a meaningful negative: not only does monsoon onset dampen tipper and HCV demand, but Q1 historically follows the year's strongest quarter (Q4) and therefore always looks weak in absolute terms. For a company that has built its re-rating on margin expansion through operating leverage, a Q1 with lower volumes means fixed cost absorption reverts toward unfavourable territory precisely when commodity costs are already rising. Seasonality therefore compounds rather than offsets the commodity and demand risks identified in the miss scenarios.

## 7. Historical Pattern

TMCV's listed beat-miss history is only two quarters deep (Q3 and Q4 FY26), both beats: Q3 EPS +18.6% above consensus, Q4 EPS +10.7% above consensus. These are meaningful beats, but the context tempers how much weight the synthesiser should put on them. The Q3 beat was extremely large, likely because consensus was newly formed for a just-listed entity with no analyst coverage track record — an initial "too-low bar" effect that tends to moderate after the first few quarters of reported results. The Q4 beat was smaller (+10.7%) and came despite commodity headwinds, suggesting the underlying execution is genuine. On a full-year basis, however, normalized EPS missed consensus by −10.7%, because exceptional items and commodity absorption were worse than modelled — showing that the company can beat quarterly consensus but still disappoint on annual EPS when non-recurring charges accumulate. The synthesiser should weight the quarterly beat pattern modestly positively but apply caution on the full-year EPS number given the Iveco acquisition complication and the history of "quarterly beats that add up to an annual miss."

## 8. Setup Verdict

**Setup is balanced.**

The weight of evidence is genuinely split. On one side, management explicitly described Q1 FY27 commodity pressure as "significantly higher" than Q4's already-material 100 bps impact, monsoon seasonality will suppress volumes, the Iveco bridge loan is a large and under-modelled earnings dilutant, and the base effect from Q4's strong print creates a tough sequential comparison. On the other side, April 2026 MHCV retail was already tracking +20% YoY, the Indonesia 70,000-unit order is in active delivery, non-cyclical revenue is growing independently of the CV cycle, and the Street's FY27 consensus was set before the Q4 beat was confirmed.

The single most important factor in this verdict is **commodity cost absorption in Q1 FY27**. Management was explicit that Q1 commodity impact is "significantly higher" than Q4's 100 bps — the only question is whether VAVE savings and the 2% April price increase together cover enough of it that EBITDA margin holds above 12%. If they do, the setup tilts toward beat. If they don't, and margin prints at 11%–11.5%, the full-year EPS consensus of ₹19.17 (which implies margin expansion) will require downward revision. The single biggest risk that could flip the verdict from balanced to "miss" is a sharp steel price spike in April–June 2026, which would widen the commodity absorption gap beyond what any pricing action can cover in one quarter.

## 9. Second-Quarter Look-Ahead

Q2 FY27 (July–September 2026) looks structurally better than Q1 for two reasons: first, it is the first quarter that should benefit from post-monsoon recovery in infrastructure and freight demand, and second, the Iveco deal is expected to close during Q2, which — while it introduces bridge loan interest expense and acquisition costs — also removes the "deal uncertainty" overhang and may trigger positive analyst coverage of the combined entity. The risk running into Q2 is that the bridge loan (€3.875 billion at Euribor +102.5 bps, per web sources) closes into a high-interest-rate environment, and if TMCV simultaneously raises equity (a rights issue or QIP to refinance ~€1 billion of the bridge), the dilution effect on per-share EPS will be material and the market may reprice earnings expectations downward even if the underlying CV business is recovering. In short, Q2 may deliver better operating numbers than Q1 but carry a messier financial structure that clouds the EPS read for the following 12 months.

---

## 10. Pre-Mortem

If this earnings setup assessment fails and the actual Q1 FY27 print materially surprises in either direction, the most likely reason we missed it is that the **magnitude of commodity cost absorption was under-estimated or over-estimated** — specifically, how much of the "significantly higher" Q1 commodity impact management described is already embedded in the Street's models vs. how much remains a genuine negative surprise. Management gave only qualitative language ("significantly higher"), not a basis-point estimate, leaving wide room for analyst interpretation; the actual Q1 gross margin, which will be reported for the first time on the Q1 results call in late July 2026, is the single most readable number that will confirm or deny whether this setup held.
