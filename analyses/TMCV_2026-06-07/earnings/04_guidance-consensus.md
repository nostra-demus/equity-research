**Key qualitative commentary on FY27 outlook (FQ4 2026 transcript, May 13, 2026):**

- Commodity headwinds described as "certainly serious" — around 100 basis points impact in Q4 FY26 already, with "significantly higher impact being seen in Q1" FY27. ## 7. Bar Assessment

**Bar is low.**

The FY27 Street consensus (EPS INR 19.17, revenue INR 881,357mm as of Apr-27-2026) was set before the Q4 FY26 results landed. Q4 delivered a +10.7% EPS beat and a +3.6% revenue beat. The Q4 beat will mechanically require upward FY27 revisions from at least some analysts once the post-results cycle completes — the consensus available here is pre-Q4 actuals. More importantly, management's qualitative FY27 guidance — "teens" EBITDA (not mid-teens), "single-digit growth if not more" for Q1, and a cautious stance on commodity and MENA headwinds — is deliberately conservative and leaves room to outperform the embedded +20.5% EPS growth assumption in FY27 consensus. The two-quarter beat history (+18.6% and +10.7% EPS surprises) was driven partly by low initial consensus on a newly listed stock with no track record, and the FQ3 bar effect will moderate over time. However, the combination of: (a) consensus set before Q4 results confirmed continued execution, (b) a 2% April price increase partially offsetting commodity costs, (c) Indonesia 70,000-unit order now physically in delivery, and (d) management's refusal to guide to mid-teens margin (implying potential upside to actual margin delivery) all point to a bar that is currently set below what the business is likely to deliver in FY27, assuming commodity and MENA headwinds do not intensify materially beyond the current level.

The primary risk to this assessment: commodity cost absorption is already eating margin in Q1 FY27, the Iveco financing structure is unresolved (bridge loan could be dilutive), and H1 FY27 is expected to be softer than H2 — which means near-term results may disappoint even if the full-year bar proves beatable.
