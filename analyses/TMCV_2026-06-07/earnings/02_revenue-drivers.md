# Revenue Drivers — TMCV (Tata Motors Limited, formerly TML Commercial Vehicles Limited)

**Reporting regime:** India / SEBI-LODR. Reporting standard: Ind AS. Currency: INR (₹ crores). Fiscal year ends March 31. FY26 (April 1, 2025 to March 31, 2026) is the first full twelve-month year for this entity.

---

## 1. Segment Decomposition Status

Segment decomposition applied — 2 segments from business-model module (03_segment-map.md).

However, the company is effectively a single-segment business: the Commercial Vehicle (CV) segment contributes 98.5% of revenue (₹82,611 cr of ₹83,855 cr total in FY26) and over 100% of operating segment profit. The Others segment (insurance broking) contributes 1.2% and is operationally dependent on the parent's vehicle sales ecosystem for its volumes. The analysis proceeds at consolidated level with CV-segment breakdowns by product line where disclosed. Segment-level EBITDA is not separately disclosed in the filings (only Underlying EBITDA at consolidated level); segment results use the CODM operating-profit equivalent metric.

## 2. Revenue Driver Tree

TMCV is a commercial vehicle manufacturer. The applicable formula:

**Revenue = Units wholesaled × Average Selling Price (ASP) + Non-cyclical / downstream revenue (parts, services, digital, Smart City)**

More precisely at the product-line level:
- **Vehicle revenue** = Σ (Volume by product line × ASP by product line)
- **Non-vehicle revenue** = Parts & Services + Smart City Mobility + Fleet Edge subscriptions + Insurance Broking (Others segment)

In FY26, vehicle revenue (HCV ₹37,199 cr + ILMCV ₹11,212 cr + SCV/Pickup ₹7,558 cr + CV Passenger ₹8,807 cr = ₹64,776 cr) accounted for approximately 77% of CV segment revenue; the balance reflects spares, services, and associated revenue. The non-cyclical revenue stream is growing faster than vehicles (management cited noncyclical CAGR running at 2.7x the cyclical business over the multi-year period per FQ4 2026 Earnings Call Transcript, May 13, 2026, p.4).

[Source: FY26 Integrated Annual Report (Ind AS), MD&A, p.213–216; FQ4 2026 Earnings Call Transcript, S&P Capital IQ, May 13, 2026, p.4]

## 7. The Single Biggest Revenue Driver

**Domestic India CV industry volume demand, shaped by transporter profitability and freight activity.**

TMCV earns approximately 77% of its revenue from vehicle sales, and vehicle sales are priced per unit. The company holds roughly 39–40% of the domestic CV market by wholesale volume. That means a 10% shift in industry demand volumes — roughly 1.08 lakh units on a base of 10.79 lakh — would, all else equal, move TMCV's consolidated revenue by approximately ₹7,000–8,000 crore (8–10% of FY26 revenue), assuming blended ASP of roughly ₹14–15 lakh per vehicle (inference from ₹83,855 cr revenue / 435,227 units, blended across vehicle and non-vehicle streams at the consolidated level — actual per-vehicle ASP is higher once non-vehicle revenue is excluded).

The direction of this driver right now is cautiously improving but fragile. The underlying freight fundamentals — e-way bill generation growing, transporter fleet utilization at healthy levels, infrastructure capex budgeted up — all support continued demand. However, diesel price is the direct variable that can break this: management explicitly flagged diesel as having "30–50% impact on total cost of ownership" and stated it will "remain a very, very key monitorable." If diesel prices rise materially in FY27, transporter profitability would compress, fleet purchase decisions would be deferred, and H1 FY27 demand (already seasonally soft due to monsoon) could undershoot the single-digit growth management has tentatively guided for Q1 FY27.

The Indonesia export order (70,000 units over multiple quarters beginning FY27) provides a meaningful volume buffer — at an inferred average export ASP, this could add ₹2,000–3,500 cr of revenue in FY27 (Inference: based on ₹3,000–5,000 per unit export ASP discount to domestic, applied to 70,000 units spread over 4–6 quarters). This partially de-risks revenue from a domestic demand softening.
