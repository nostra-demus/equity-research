# Capital Structure & Leverage — TMCV

**Company:** Tata Motors Limited (formerly TML Commercial Vehicles Limited), NSEI: TMCV
**Reporting regime:** India / SEBI-LODR. Reporting standard: Ind AS. Currency: INR crore.
**Fiscal year:** April–March. FY26 = April 1, 2025 to March 31, 2026.
**Entity note:** TMCV was incorporated June 23, 2024 and became a standalone listed entity via composite scheme of arrangement effective October 1, 2025. FY25 comparatives (9 months, unaudited, re-presented) exist but are not directly comparable to the full FY26 year. This is the only complete audited fiscal year available for TMCV as a standalone consolidated entity.

**TMCV is a net-cash entity as of March 31, 2026 on all three measures.** The minimum net cash position (cash only versus borrowings) is ₹2,082 crore; the auditable liquid-pool measure is ₹8,231 crore net cash. Net cash funds counter-cyclical action — including the ability to withstand a volume trough without refinancing pressure — and removes near-term solvency dependence. This is a positive strategic-flexibility signal, not a signal of an under-deployed balance sheet.

**Forward warning:** The Iveco acquisition (EUR 3.8 bn, ~₹41,691 crore) is pending closure. If completed, the entire liquid pool would be consumed and substantial new debt drawn, converting TMCV from a net-cash entity into a heavily net-debt entity. That scenario is out-of-scope for this agent's balance-sheet snapshot (it is for the stress-test agent) but is flagged here as the dominant forward solvency variable.

Note: the Preliminary Interim Report discloses EBIT-level ratios; reported EBITDA is estimated by adding back depreciation and amortisation of ₹1,945 crore to reported EBIT of approximately ₹6,696 crore. |---|---:|---:|---:|---|
| Gross debt — borrowings (₹ cr) | Not available (pre-demerger) | ~9,156 | 4,817 | Sharply falling |
| Cash and cash equivalents (₹ cr) | Not available | ~1,033 | 6,899 | Rising rapidly |
| Net debt / net cash (basic, ₹ cr) | Not available | ~8,123 net debt | 2,082 net cash | Positive inflection |
| Debt / equity | Not available | 0.87x | 0.38x | Sharply falling |
| Gross debt / Underlying EBITDA | Not available | Not computable (9M) | 0.47x | — |

**FY23 and FY24 figures for TMCV as a standalone consolidated entity are not available.** TMCV did not exist as an independent legal entity before June 2024, and the demerger became effective October 2025. The FY25 comparatives are a 9-month re-presentation (unaudited) of the CV segment carved out of the former Tata Motors Limited. Extending the trend to FY23/FY24 would require a CV-segment carve-out from the former parent — data not available in this pool. The two-point trend (FY25 to FY26) is therefore the only auditable consolidated comparison.

**What drove the change:** Between FY25 (9M) and FY26 (full year), gross borrowings fell by approximately ₹4,339 crore (from ₹9,156 crore to ₹4,817 crore). The driver is operating cash generation: CFO was ₹14,981 crore in FY26 — an exceptionally strong year driven by the negative 31-day working capital cycle, 11.4% underlying EBITDA margins, and ₹690 crore inventory unwind. Net long-term debt repayments were ₹3,413 crore; short-term lines fell by approximately ₹963 crore. Cash simultaneously rose from approximately ₹1,033 crore to ₹6,899 crore because FCF materially exceeded the debt repayments and capex combined. No asset sales are disclosed as driving the deleveraging — it is entirely operating-cash-driven. This is a genuine balance-sheet improvement, not accounting-driven.

## 6A. HoldCo / OpCo & Structural Subordination

| Item | Evidence | Why It Matters |
|---|---|---|
| Where debt sits | ₹4,817 crore borrowings sit at the TMCV consolidated level (operating company). The FY26 Preliminary Interim Report is the consolidated statement of TMCV and its subsidiaries. The Board's Report confirms NCDs ₹2,300 crore were transferred directly to TMCV (the OpCo/ListCo) under the demerger scheme — not to a HoldCo. | Financial creditors of TMCV have a direct claim on the operating entity — no structural subordination risk for existing debt. |
| Iveco acquisition HoldCo vehicles | Two special-purpose entities have been disclosed: TML CV Holdings Pte. Ltd. (Singapore) and TML CV Holdings B.V. (Netherlands). These are the acquisition vehicles for the Iveco tender offer. The EUR 3.8 bn loan facility is being arranged at this HoldCo level. TMCV (the listed Indian OpCo) has provided a corporate guarantee for EUR 1.9 bn and TMPV (former TML) has provided a letter of comfort for EUR 1.9 bn. | If the Iveco deal closes, TMCV will have a direct guarantee obligation of EUR 1.9 bn (~₹20,846 crore) even before any cash is drawn through to the Indian entity. The HoldCo acquisition debt will be serviced using cash flows / dividends from the Iveco operating business — introducing structural subordination within the new combined group. This is a material future structural risk not yet reflected in the FY26 balance sheet. |
| Upstreaming constraints | No dividend blocker covenants or regulatory restrictions on upstreaming cash from TMCV to its parent (Tata Sons / Tata Motors Group) are disclosed. TMCV declared its first dividend (₹4/share, ~₹1,500 crore) in FY26. | Not a current concern for TMCV's own debt service. |
| Restricted / trapped cash | ₹877 crore "bank balances other than cash" may be partially restricted (margin deposits, guarantee cover). The exact restriction level is not disclosed. This amount is excluded from the liquid pool in Section 3. | Liquid pool of ₹13,048 crore may be overstated by up to ₹877 crore if the entire balance is restricted. |
