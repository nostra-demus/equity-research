## 5. Contingency Read

The Iveco corporate guarantee (EUR 1.9 bn / ~₹20,846 cr) is the single largest off-balance-sheet exposure — larger than TMCV's entire equity base of ₹12,734 cr — and it is live: the deal had not closed as of Q2 FY27, with French and Spanish regulatory approvals still pending. If the deal closes, TMCV's HoldCo takes on roughly €3.8 bn of deal financing and TMCV stands behind half that as guarantor; if the HoldCo were to default or the deal structure required consolidation of that debt onto TMCV, it would convert the company from a net-cash business into one carrying net debt of approximately ₹7,000–₹10,000 cr — erasing three years of deliberate deleveraging in a single transaction.

The secondary contingencies — ₹1,196 cr in tax disputes (all classified "possible, not probable"), an unquantified EPFO exposure, and an active but unquantified EPR obligation effective April 2025 — are individually manageable relative to the ₹13,713 cr net-cash position but collectively meaningful, and the EPR item in particular represents a structurally open-ended liability for a manufacturer with a large installed commercial vehicle fleet.

Excluding the Iveco guarantee, TMCV's contingent exposure is modest for a company of its scale; including it, the off-balance-sheet overhang exceeds equity by 82%, and the solvency strength of the current net-cash balance sheet is conditional on either the deal falling through or the post-close debt being held entirely outside the TMCV consolidated perimeter.
