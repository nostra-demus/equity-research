## 3. Rate Exposure

**Fixed vs floating identification:** Notes 22, 23, 23B, 23D, 23E (FY26 Annual Report, Ind AS). All NCDs are privately placed, unsecured, fixed-rate instruments. Perpetual debentures are fixed (7.30%–8.75%). Government concessional loans are fixed at 0.1% p.a. Short-term bank loans (₹428 cr) carry fixed rates 1.11%–6.16%. Only the secured working-capital demand loans (₹250 cr) are floating, linked to the bank rate.

**Weighted-average coupon (WAC) computation:** Using face-value weights on the disclosed NCD schedule and known rates for other tranches. NCD schedule (Note 23D): 6.60% × ₹500 cr + 8.50% × ₹1,051 cr (ZC equivalent coupon on ₹801 cr ZC bonds, using their implicit yield ~8.53% as stated) + 8.50% × ₹250 cr + 7.65% × ₹500 cr + 7.65% × ₹800 cr = weighted NCD coupon ~7.8% on ₹3,101 cr. Perpetuals (₹794 cr total, midpoint rate 8.1%); government loans (₹102 cr, 0.1%); short-term bank loans (₹428 cr, ~4.0% midpoint); WC loans (₹250 cr, ~7.5% bank-rate proxy); other/residual. Blended across ₹4,817 cr → WAC approximately **7.3–7.5%**.

**Current market refi rate benchmark:** India 5-year G-sec ~6.66% as of May 7, 2026 (web-sourced: [Trading Economics, India 5-Year Note Yield](https://tradingeconomics.com/india/5-year-note-yield), indicative, unverified). India 10-year G-sec ~6.9% in late May 2026 (web-sourced: same source). For a 2–3 year NCD, the relevant benchmark is the 3-year G-sec, estimated ~6.40–6.60%. CRISIL/ICRA/CARE AA+ spread over G-sec for Indian corporates at this rating: approximately 50–80 bps based on PSU bond market evidence (AAA spreads ~40–60 bps; AA+ one notch wider). Current market refi rate for TMCV-quality 2–3 year NCD: **~7.0–7.4%** (web-sourced, indicative, unverified). The top end of this range overlaps with TMCV's current WAC, meaning the step-up on new issuance at current rates is minimal to negligible for most tranches. The cheapest legacy tranche (6.60% NCD, ₹500 cr, matured May 2026) would have seen a ~50–80 bps step-up on replacement.

**Floating-rate sensitivity:** Only ₹250 crore (5.2% of gross debt) reprices with the bank rate. A 100 bps move in the RBI repo rate (and hence bank rate) changes annual interest costs by approximately ₹2.5 crore — negligible against ₹874 crore total finance costs (FY26 Preliminary Interim Report).

## 4. Refinancing Exposure

### Refi Funding Plan — within-24-month maturities (Apr 2026–Mar 2028, ~₹5,014 cr per schedule)

| Source of repayment for next-24m maturities | Amount (₹ cr) | Evidence |
|---|---:|---|
| Cash on hand (as of Mar 31, 2026) | 5,217 | FY26 Annual Report (Ind AS), balance sheet — cash and cash equivalents + bank deposits |
| Mutual funds / treasury investments | 7,831 | FY26 Annual Report (Ind AS), balance sheet — current investments; total liquid pool ₹13,048 cr per `01_capital-structure-and-leverage.md` |
| FY26 FCF (run-rate, company-defined) | ~12,438 | FY26 Preliminary Interim Report — FCF ₹12,438 cr (CFO − capex definition); FY27 run-rate unverified |
| Revolver availability | 3,750 | FY26 Annual Report (Ind AS), MD&A p.220 — ₹4,000 cr fund-based limit, ₹3,750 cr unutilized |
| New unsecured NCD issuance | Unknown — not committed or announced | No commitment disclosed; historically accessed NCD market for routine roll-overs |
| Asset-sale proceeds | None announced | No asset sale announced for debt repayment as of FY26 filing |

**Sufficiency assessment.** The liquid pool alone (₹13,048 crore) covers the full 24-month schedule (₹5,014 crore) by 2.6x without any new FCF generation, asset sales, or new debt issuance. Annual FCF of ~₹12,438 crore would, by itself, cover the entire 24-month obligation in less than 6 months of cash generation. The revolver (₹3,750 cr unutilized) provides additional backup that is not needed under the base case.

The near-term wall (12–24 months) is fully covered by cash and FCF without any market access. The company has CRISIL AA+ / ICRA AA+ / CARE AA+ / S&P BBB Stable ratings, so routine NCD roll-overs at market rates are available for the portion that management chooses to refinance rather than retire. FY26 saw deliberate active debt repayment: USD Senior Notes, ₹567 cr bank term loans, ₹953 cr Smart City loans, and ₹700 cr TMFHL perpetuals. This confirms a repay-from-cash posture rather than a roll-and-grow posture.

The 5.2% floating-rate share means that market rate movements barely affect interest cost — there is no meaningful floating-rate repricing risk on the current book.

**Verdict:** Self-funded / low refi risk (current balance sheet).

**Critical forward caveat — Iveco Group acquisition:** TMCV has agreed to acquire Iveco Group's commercial vehicle businesses (Iveco Bus, Iveco Defence, and Iveco Off-Road) for EUR 3.8 billion (~₹41,691 crore at spot). EUR 1.9 billion corporate guarantee has already been provided. CFO G.V. Ramanan confirmed (FQ4 FY26 transcript): "Our initial plan for financing is going to be through a bridge loan and the subsequent refinancing — at this point of time, all options are open. So we've kept both equity and debt still open." Closure is expected Q2 FY27 (pending France and Spain financial regulatory approvals). If completed, Iveco would transform the balance sheet from net-cash (₹8,231 cr) to significantly net-debt (~₹33,000–41,000 cr depending on financing mix). This is a binary forward event that the current maturity wall analysis does not — and cannot — reflect. The refinancing risk for the Iveco acquisition bridge loan is a separate and much larger question; it is addressed in the stress-test module (06).

## 5. Refinancing Read

TMCV's current debt book is an accelerating paydown — ₹3,657 crore comes due within 12 months and ₹5,014 crore within 24 months, representing 95% of total debt. The WAM of ~1.1 years is unusually short, but this is not a distress signal: the liquid pool of ₹13,048 crore and annual FCF of ~₹12,438 crore cover the full schedule multiple times over without any market access needed. Refinancing cost is near-neutral — the current WAC of ~7.3–7.5% sits at or within 30 bps of where a fresh AA+ NCD would price today, so there is no material cost step-up on roll-overs.

The single biggest refinancing risk on the current balance sheet is not refinancing at all — it is the Iveco bridge loan commitment. If TMCV closes the EUR 3.8 billion deal via a bridge, it goes from net-cash of ₹8,231 crore to net-debt of an order of magnitude larger, and the subsequent refinancing (equity or permanent debt) would need to be executed in what is currently an open but not guaranteed capital market. That binary event, not the current NCD roll-overs, is the real watch item.

**12-month market closure survival (current balance sheet):** Yes. Cash and treasury investments (₹13,048 crore) alone exceed the entire gross debt (₹4,817 crore). Even if no new unsecured issuance were available for 12 months, all within-12-month maturities (₹3,657 crore) would be met from existing liquid assets with ₹9,391 crore remaining. The revolver (₹3,750 crore unutilized) provides a further cushion. This assessment applies only to the current balance sheet; a post-Iveco balance sheet would require fresh analysis.
