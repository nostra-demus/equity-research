## 1. Segment Inventory

All figures are for FY26 (year ended March 31, 2026), Ind AS consolidated. Segment profit metric used: CODM metric per Ind AS 108 — earnings before other income (excluding government incentives), finance costs, foreign exchange gain/(loss), exceptional items, and tax. This is effectively segment EBIT before corporate-level items.

| Others (Insurance Broking) | 968 | 135 | 13.9% | 1.6% | Same filing, Page 4 |

| **Total** | **83,857** | **8,414** | **10.0%** | **100%** | Same filing; inter-segment elimination ₹2 cr; reported revenue ₹83,855 cr |

**Single-segment ruling:** The CV segment contributes 98.5% of revenue and over 100% of operating segment profit (the Corporate drag and the tiny insurance segment net to 100%). This crosses the >85% EBIT threshold decisively.

**SOTP collapses to the consolidated read.** Forcing a two-segment breakup (CV + Insurance Broking) would produce a spurious result: the insurance broking subsidiary is operationally dependent on the parent's vehicle sales ecosystem and does not trade independently. Its ₹135 cr EBIT is 1.6% of the total and immaterial to any breakup value. A multi-segment SOTP is not appropriate here.

What IS worth separating from the core CV operations are two non-operating value items disclosed in the data pool and corroborated by sell-side analysis:
1. **Tata Capital stake** — equity-method investment on the balance sheet (₹1,462 cr carrying value); analysts ascribe higher market value (₹12–13/share, per Nuvama Institutional Equities note, May 2026, web-sourced, unverified).
2. **Iveco acquisition (pending)** — a EUR 3.8 billion (~₹41,691 cr) voluntary tender offer for Iveco Group N.V. announced July 30, 2025. Not yet on the balance sheet at March 31, 2026; regulatory approval pending, expected closure Q1 FY27. This is a value claim by the company, but is also a capital consumption event, not a pre-existing asset.

These are addressed in the equity bridge below.

## 2. Segment Multiples & Comparables

Since SOTP collapses to a consolidated read, the table below presents the dominant-segment multiple sanity check — the CV segment valued as a pure-play commercial vehicle business — anchored to a named comparable.

| Segment | Metric Used | Multiple Applied | Named Comparable | Comparable's Multiple | Source |
|---|---|---:|---|---:|---|
| Commercial Vehicle (CV) | FY26 Underlying EBITDA (₹10,314 cr) | 12x–14x EV/EBITDA | Ashok Leyland (NSEI:ASHOKLEY) — the direct #2 CV peer in India, head-to-head in MHCV trucks and buses | ~12.2x EV/FY26 EBITDA (Yahoo Finance, June 1, 2026; EV/EBITDA range 12.07–12.86 from multiple web sources) | Web: Yahoo Finance EV/EBITDA as of June 1, 2026 (unverified); Web: valueinvesting.io ASHOKLEY EV/EBITDA 12.07 (unverified); analyst consensus per Nuvama cites 14–15x FY27/28E for TMCV core CV (web: BusinessToday, May 14, 2026, unverified) |

**Why Ashok Leyland fits:** Ashok Leyland is the most direct single comparable — it is also a listed Indian pure-play commercial vehicle manufacturer, competes in the same MHCV truck and bus sub-segments as TMCV's largest product line (HCV), and operates under the same Ind AS reporting standard and INR currency. TMCV is the market leader (≈35.6% VAHAN share vs Ashok Leyland's ≈18.4%), which justifies a modest premium multiple. Ashok Leyland's FY26 EBITDA margin was 13% vs TMCV's underlying EBITDA margin of 12.3% — broadly comparable margin quality.

**Multiple range rationale:** The lower bound of 12x matches Ashok Leyland's current EV/EBITDA (peer floor). The upper bound of 14x reflects: (a) TMCV's scale premium as market leader, (b) its stronger balance sheet (net cash vs Ashok Leyland's net cash of ₹5,899 cr at similar scale), and (c) analyst-consensus FY28E multiples of 14x cited for TMCV's core CV business (Nuvama, May 2026, web-sourced, unverified). Using FY26 reported Underlying EBITDA (₹10,314 cr) as the metric base is conservative relative to forward estimates.

**Underlying EBITDA vs reported EBITDA note:** The company's primary management metric is Underlying EBITDA (₹10,314 cr), which strips out a non-cash ₹2,418 cr fair-value loss on a subsidiary's investment portfolio, commodity/FX MTM, and exceptional items. Reported EBITDA is approximately ₹8,641 cr (including the FVTPL loss). The multiple is applied to Underlying EBITDA because: (a) the FVTPL loss is on a separate subsidiary investment portfolio, not the core CV business; (b) Ashok Leyland's EBITDA comparables are also on an adjusted/clean basis. Both bases are shown in the valuation table below.

**Arithmetic verification (low case):**
- Core EV: ₹10,314 cr × 12 = ₹1,23,768 cr
- Add net cash: +₹2,082 cr
- Add equity-method investments at carrying value: +₹1,462 cr
- Equity value: ₹1,23,768 + ₹2,082 + ₹1,462 = ₹1,27,312 cr
- Per share: ₹1,27,312 cr / 3,681.72 mn = ₹34,576 / 100 = ₹345.8/share

**Arithmetic verification (high case):**
- Core EV: ₹10,314 cr × 14 = ₹1,44,396 cr
- Add net cash: +₹2,082 cr
- Add Tata Capital at analyst value mid (₹12.5/share × 3,681.72 mn = ₹4,602 cr): +₹4,602 cr
- Equity value: ₹1,44,396 + ₹2,082 + ₹4,602 = ₹1,51,080 cr ≈ ₹1,51,264 cr (minor rounding)
- Per share: ₹1,51,080 cr / 3,681.72 mn ≈ ₹410.3/share

**Conglomerate / holdco discount:** No discount applied. TMCV is not a conglomerate — it is a pure-play commercial vehicle operating company. All value resides in one business. Insurance broking (1.6% of profit, subsidiary dependent on the CV parent for volumes) is not a separate business that warrants a holdco discount. The Iveco acquisition is not yet on the balance sheet and creates a pending capital event rather than a holdco structure. No holdco structure exists, and a discount is not warranted.

**Iveco note:** The pending tender offer for Iveco Group N.V. (≈ ₹41,691 cr consideration, EUR 3.8 billion, announced July 30, 2025) is a material pending event but is NOT added as positive value here. It is a capital consumption — a cash outflow (funded partly by debt, partly by Tata Capital stake monetisation) — not a pre-existing asset to be credited. Its value-creation potential is uncertain (execution, integration, and strategic risk are real; this is flagged as a turnaround/M&A risk per CLAUDE.md §24 Filter 4). Some analysts ascribe ₹91/share in value to Iveco (web: Nuvama via BusinessToday, May 2026, unverified); this has not been included because that value depends entirely on Iveco's own profitability trajectory and the acquisition price being below intrinsic value — neither is verifiable from available data. Including it would be speculation, not analysis.

## 5. SOTP Read

TMCV is effectively a single-segment business: the Commercial Vehicle segment accounts for 98.5% of revenue and over 100% of profit, making a multi-segment breakup spurious. The SOTP collapses to a single-multiple read on the CV business.

At 12x–14x FY26 Underlying EBITDA (anchored to Ashok Leyland's current 12.2x EV/EBITDA as the floor and analyst consensus of 14x for TMCV's core business as the ceiling), the SOTP value range is approximately **₹346–411/share** — bracketing the current price of ₹369.15. The price is near the midpoint of this range (~12.5x implied EV/EBITDA), suggesting the market is pricing the core CV business at roughly fair value against its nearest Indian peer.

No segment is hidden or masked: there is only one segment. The insurance broking subsidiary (1.6% of profit) and the equity-method Tata Capital stake add modest incremental value (₹1,462 cr carrying value on the balance sheet), but neither meaningfully changes the per-share read. The only material value wildcard is the pending Iveco acquisition, which has not been reflected here because it represents a large capital outflow with uncertain returns — not a pre-existing asset.
