Whether the stock's own six-month history constitutes a meaningful reversion target is a calibration judgment, not a data certainty.

## 3. Re-Rating / De-Rating Read

On all three primary multiples, the current price is at or near the low end of the observable range since listing. EV/EBITDA has fallen from approximately 15.3–15.5× (Q Mar-2026 intra-quarter average) to 12.68× today — a compression of roughly 17% relative to the six-month average. P/Normalised EPS has moved from an average of ~30.8× during Q Mar-2026 to 25.6× today, a compression of about 17%.

The most likely driver is not a deterioration in underlying earnings — FY26 delivered the highest-ever revenue and margins, with Q4 FY26 beating consensus on both revenue and EPS. The de-rating appears driven by three specific concerns that emerged after the FY26 results: (a) management's explicit refusal to guide to "mid-teens" EBITDA margins for FY27 (correcting to "teens only"), implying a margin plateau; (b) "significantly higher" commodity cost impact flagged for Q1 FY27, with only partial pass-through via a 2% April price increase; and (c) unresolved Iveco acquisition financing (≈ ₹ 41,691 cr consideration / €3.8 billion), which could add material leverage to a balance sheet that only recently turned net-cash positive. [Source: FQ4 2026 Earnings Call Transcript, May 13, 2026, CFO G.V. Ramanan and CEO Girish Wagh Q&A; earnings/04_guidance-consensus.md.]

The stock is not in a period of multiple expansion — it is in a period of multiple contraction driven by forward-year uncertainty layered on top of a strong trailing year. This is a distinct pattern from a simple "cyclical" de-rating; the core business is doing well, but the market is discounting event risk (Iveco) and margin uncertainty.

## 4. Implied Value from Reversion

**Important caveat:** With only six months of standalone listed history, mean-reversion analysis carries much lower reliability than for an entity with 3–5 years of trading history. The three reference points used to construct the mean/median are: one is the FY25 re-presented cross-section at today's price (not an actual trading period), and the other two are intra-quarter averages from recent months. Treating this mean as the "warranted" multiple assumes the stock should trade where it traded in early 2026 — which itself reflected a newly-listed entity's pricing in an uncertain environment, not a long-run equilibrium.

The bridge from implied EV to implied equity value uses the CIQ net cash convention: Cash & ST Investments ₹ 13,050 cr minus Total Debt ₹ 5,615 cr = net cash ₹ 7,435 cr (broad) = ₹ 74,350 mn. Shares used: 3,681.72 mn (balance sheet date, March 31, 2026, SEBI LODR filing).

**Implied value range:** The three multiples at mean/median reversion produce a range of approximately **₹ 423–₹ 465** per share — roughly 15–26% above the current price of ₹ 369.15. The midpoint of this range is approximately ₹ 444.

**Reversion assumption:** These implied values assume the warranted multiple is unchanged from the six-month average. Whether that is justified depends on whether the drivers of the recent de-rating (Iveco event risk, commodity cost absorption, margin plateau guidance) resolve or persist. If Iveco financing is confirmed as dilutive (equity raise or high-cost bridge converting to permanent debt), the multiple the market assigns to the remaining business will not revert to its six-month average — it will reset to a new, lower level reflecting a more leveraged entity. If commodity headwinds normalise and the "teens" EBITDA guidance proves conservative (as the business-model evidence of 11 consecutive double-digit quarters suggests is possible), a reversion to the six-month mean is more plausible. Neither outcome is certain.

## 5. Own-History Read

The stock is at the low end of every multiple it has traded at since listing, driven not by deteriorating trailing performance but by forward uncertainty. Reverting to the six-month mean on EV/EBITDA implies a price of roughly ₹ 423–433 per share, or about 15–17% above today's level.

The single biggest caveat is that six months of data is not a reliable reversion anchor. More importantly, the Iveco acquisition (₹ 41,691 cr consideration, expected to close Q2 FY27) was not in the balance sheet at March 31, 2026. If this deal closes with substantial debt, the company moves from net-cash positive to meaningfully leveraged, and the EV-based multiples that looked reasonable against a clean balance sheet will need to be recalibrated against a very different capital structure. The implied-value range above is only valid for a TMCV that remains net-cash positive post-Iveco — a condition that depends entirely on the financing mix, which management had not disclosed as of the Q4 FY26 results date.

The governance module flagged the TMF Holdings merger as a borderline RF-OWN-004 alignment conflict (Amber, not formally triggered). Tata Sons is not a government entity or an extractive parent in the classic sense, but the board-approved decision to absorb NBFC liabilities and a ₹ 2,418 cr FVTPL investment drag into the listed CV entity is a concrete example of intra-group restructuring that transfers costs to minority holders. This does not make reversion to the six-month mean impossible, but it does mean the market may persistently apply a discount to the "clean" multiple, and that discount may widen if the TMF merger proceeds on unfavourable terms.
