## 3. Premium / Discount to Peer Median

**Primary comparison: TMCV vs Ashok Leyland (only pure-play comparable)**

| Multiple | TMCV | Ashok Leyland (sole pure-play peer) | TMCV vs AL |
|---|---:|---:|---:|
| EV/EBITDA (FY26 Underlying / Reported) | 12.5× | 23.5× | **−47% discount** |
| EV/EBIT (FY26 Underlying / Op. PBT) | 15.1× | 26.1× | **−42% discount** |
| EV/Sales (FY26) | 1.53× | 2.39× | **−36% discount** |
| P/E (reported, distorted — see [A]) | 44.9× | 22.9× | +96% premium |
| P/EBITDA (mktcap / EBITDA, debt-neutral) | 13.2× | 14.9× | **−11% discount** |

**Critical reconciliation — the EV/EBITDA gap is overwhelmingly structural, not operational:**

The 47% EV/EBITDA discount (12.5× vs 23.5×) looks dramatic but is almost entirely explained by a difference in capital structure, not by the market placing a lower value on TMCV's earnings power. TMCV has net cash of ₹7,435 cr (broad) — its EV is below its market cap. Ashok Leyland's consolidated EV includes the net debt of its captive finance subsidiary Hinduja Leyland Finance (₹46,856 cr consolidated net debt), which inflates its EV relative to market cap.

On a debt-neutral basis — comparing market cap (equity value) to EBITDA — TMCV trades at **13.2× P/EBITDA** versus Ashok Leyland's **14.9× P/EBITDA**. That gap is only **11%**. This is the correct lens for measuring relative market valuation. The 47% EV/EBITDA gap is largely a capital structure artefact, not a market view on relative business quality.

## 4. Is the Gap Warranted?

**The core finding: the apparent 47% EV/EBITDA discount to Ashok Leyland is largely a capital structure artefact. The true equity-level discount is approximately 11%. Both parts of this are important.**

TMCV is the larger, higher-margin, FCF-generative, net-cash business. On the metrics that matter for an operating industrial company — EBITDA margin, FCF yield, balance sheet strength, and scale — TMCV outperforms Ashok Leyland across the board. TMCV's Underlying EBITDA margin of 12.3% is 210 basis points higher than Ashok Leyland's 10.2%. TMCV generates ₹12,438 cr of FCF (company-defined, 9.2% yield) versus Ashok Leyland's negative FCF in FY26. TMCV carries net cash; Ashok Leyland's consolidated entity carries ₹46,856 cr of net debt (8.2× EBITDA), much of it in the finance arm. On these factors, TMCV warrants a premium to Ashok Leyland on EV/EBITDA, and the current 12.5× multiple is below where it should trade even without quality adjustments.

The 11% discount on a P/EBITDA basis, however, requires explanation. Two specific risk factors justify the market pricing TMCV at a modest discount to Ashok Leyland on a debt-neutral basis:

1. **Iveco pending acquisition (₹41,691 cr, or €3.8 billion):** This transaction, announced July 30, 2025 and expected to close in Q2 FY27 [SEBI LODR Audited Results, May 13, 2026, Note 7], would convert TMCV from a net cash company (₹7,435 cr broad) to a company with approximately ₹34,000 cr of net debt — raising its leverage sharply and fundamentally changing the EV bridge. The market is correctly discounting this pending event. [business-model/11_capital-allocation-governance.md upstream evidence; Note 7, SEBI LODR filing]

2. **First year as a standalone entity:** TMCV has only one full year of consolidated audited history (FY26). Ashok Leyland has decades of standalone data. The market applies a credibility discount to a new entity's track record and management team's ability to operate independently. This is a rational, though time-limited, penalty.

These two factors justify a moderate discount. They do not justify a 47% EV/EBITDA discount, which is largely arithmetical. The correct framing is that Ashok Leyland's EV/EBITDA is inflated by its finance arm debt rather than TMCV's EV/EBITDA being depressed by fundamental undervaluation.

**Verdict: discount is partially warranted (Iveco and track record), but the apparent depth overstates the true gap. On a debt-neutral basis (P/EBITDA), TMCV trades at a modest 11% discount to AL — the gap is narrow and defensible given near-term catalysts.**

## 5. Implied Value from Peer Multiples

**Method:** Apply a quality-adjusted EV/EBITDA multiple to TMCV's FY26 Underlying EBITDA of ₹10,314 cr. Bridge to equity by adding broad net cash ₹7,435 cr (total debt incl. leases ₹5,615 cr minus cash and short-term investments ₹13,050 cr, per 01_price-and-capital-structure.md). Divide by 368.17 crore shares (SEBI LODR Balance Sheet, March 31, 2026, per 01_price-and-capital-structure.md).

**Quality adjustment rationale:**
- TMCV merits a modest premium to Ashok Leyland on EV/EBITDA for: better operating margins (+210 bps), net cash vs AL's leveraged consolidated position, and higher FCF yield.
- However, a discount is applied for the pending Iveco acquisition (balance-sheet and execution risk), the single-year track record as a standalone entity, and CLAUDE.md §24 Filter 5 (fast-changing technology environment; industry rate-of-change scored 35/100 per business-model/07_business-quality.md — caps conviction).
- Net quality adjustment: TMCV is modestly better than AL operationally but faces non-operational (Iveco) and structural (technology) risks. A range of 18×–23.5× is appropriate, with 23.5× (at-parity to AL) as the bull case and 18× (material discount for Iveco completion) as the bear.

This is a material downside to the implied value range and illustrates why the Iveco risk dominates the relative valuation.

## 6. Relative Read

TMCV appears to trade at a 47% discount to Ashok Leyland on EV/EBITDA, but this is almost entirely a capital-structure illusion: Ashok Leyland's consolidated EV is inflated by ₹46,856 cr of net debt in its captive finance subsidiary. On a debt-neutral basis — market cap versus EBITDA — TMCV's discount to Ashok Leyland is only 11%, with TMCV at 13.2× P/EBITDA vs 14.9× for AL. That modest 11% discount is defensible on the evidence: it reflects the pending Iveco acquisition (which will consume net cash and add leverage) and the fact that FY26 is the company's first full standalone year. The implied value range from peer multiples (18×–23.5× EV/EBITDA applied to ₹10,314 cr EBITDA) is **₹524–₹679 per share**, or 42%–84% above the current price of ₹369.15 — assuming Iveco does not close. If Iveco closes, that range compresses to approximately **₹411–₹565**, or 11%–53% above current price. The Iveco transaction is the single variable that most determines whether relative upside materialises or is absorbed by balance-sheet dilution.
