# Price & Capital Structure — TMCV (Tata Motors Limited, formerly TML Commercial Vehicles Limited)

**Reporting regime:** India / SEBI-LODR. Reporting standard: Ind AS. Currency: INR (₹ crores, except per-share items in ₹). Fiscal year ends March 31. No FX conversion is required — all figures are in INR throughout.

**Entity note:** TMCV (NSEI: TMCV) is the demerged commercial-vehicles entity of the old Tata Motors Group. It received the CV business effective October 1, 2025 (appointed date July 1, 2025) under a Composite Scheme of Arrangement approved by the National Company Law Tribunal (NCLT). The company was incorporated June 23, 2024; FY26 (April 1, 2025 to March 31, 2026) is its first full twelve-month fiscal year on a consolidated basis.

## 4. Enterprise Value Bridge

The EV bridge uses the Capital IQ convention (consistent with its reported EV), which includes lease liabilities within total debt and includes short-term investments within cash. Both conventions are presented; the Capital IQ convention is primary because it reconciles precisely to the Capital IQ-stated EV.

**Primary bridge (Capital IQ convention, consistent with reported EV of ₹ 1,28,498 cr):**

| Component | Amount (₹ cr) | Source |
|---|---:|---|
| Market capitalization | 1,35,933 | S&P Capital IQ Key Stats, Current Capitalization, downloaded 2026-06-07; confirmed by shares × price arithmetic above |

| − Equity-method investments | Not deducted (see note) | See adjustment note below |
| **= Enterprise value (EV)** | **1,28,498** | S&P Capital IQ Key Stats: TEV = 1,284,982.626342 INR mn = ₹ 1,28,498 cr. Bridge arithmetic: 1,35,933 + 5,615 − 13,050 = ₹ 1,28,498 cr — exact match |

**Adjustments NOT made and why:**

1. **Equity-method investments (₹ 1,462 cr):** The balance sheet shows "Investment in equity accounted investees" of ₹ 1,462 cr (mainly TMF Holdings subsidiary stakes and some JV interests). Capital IQ does not deduct these from EV in its standard bridge, and the company's primary segment (Tata Commercial Vehicles) is treated as the operating entity; the investees are ancillary. This item is flagged but not deducted from EV in the primary anchor bridge. Downstream agents running a sum-of-the-parts analysis may choose to deduct this if they treat these investments separately.

2. **Non-current financial investments (₹ 6,547 cr):** These are financial investments held at fair value, primarily by a subsidiary (the same subsidiary that recorded a ₹ 2,418 cr FVTPL loss in FY26 per filing Note 8). Capital IQ includes these within short-term investments in the cash netting. Since Capital IQ has already incorporated them in the ₹ 13,050 cr cash & ST investments figure — and this reconciles exactly to the stated EV — no further adjustment is made. If only operational cash is used (₹ 6,899 cr cash + ₹ 877 cr bank balances = ₹ 7,776 cr), EV rises to ₹ 1,33,772 cr.

3. **Operating lease liabilities (included):** Lease liabilities (₹ 798 cr total) are already included in the Capital IQ "Total Debt" figure. This is the standard adjusted-EV convention. Downstream agents should be aware that the EV includes right-of-use asset liabilities per this convention.

4. **Pension / post-employment obligations:** The company has gratuity and post-retirement obligations. The filing notes a ₹ 389 cr exceptional item for the statutory impact of new Labour Codes on gratuity and long-term compensated absences. A precise under-funded pension amount is not separately disclosed in the available data pool. This adjustment is not made; it is flagged as a minor potential addition to EV if separately valued.

5. **Iveco acquisition (pending):** The company announced on July 30, 2025 a voluntary tender offer for Iveco Group N.V. at an approximate consideration of ₹ 41,691 cr (€3.8 billion). As at March 31, 2026 this transaction has not closed (regulatory approvals pending; expected to close in Q2 FY27). It is not reflected in the balance sheet or the EV bridge. This is a material pending event that downstream agents must note — if the acquisition closes, EV will increase materially by the acquisition consideration plus any debt assumed.
