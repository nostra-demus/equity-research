## 4. Read

TMCV is geographically concentrated, not customer-concentrated. On the customer side, revenue is spread across a fragmented base of fleet operators, logistics players, infrastructure contractors, and small-vehicle owners, with no single buyer accounting for a material share — this is a structural feature of the mass-market commercial vehicle model. On the geography side, India accounts for 87.9% of consolidated revenue, which is a genuine and material concentration in a single national market. This concentration is not contractually secured: domestic India sales flow through dealers to end-buyers under no overarching long-term agreements; the government bus segment is the only area with multi-year order visibility. The single biggest dependency the synthesizer should know about is India's commercial vehicle demand cycle: TMCV's revenues move directly with India's freight activity, infrastructure spending, diesel prices, and financing conditions — all of which are cyclical and partially policy-driven, leaving the business significantly exposed to any domestic demand downturn with limited international revenue to offset it (international = only 12.1% of revenue in FY26, though growing fast).
