# Moat — TMCV

## 1. Named Competitors

*(Inherited from 08_competitive-map.md)*

- **Ashok Leyland Limited (NSEI:ASHOKLEY)** — direct head-to-head rival in MHCV trucks and buses across all of India; #2 overall with 30.2% domestic MHCV truck share and 34.1% bus share in FY26.
- **Mahindra & Mahindra Limited — CV division (NSEI:M&M)** — dominant rival in SCV/pickup sub-3.5 tonne segment; ~54% share in vehicles under 3.5 tonnes; ~26.7% overall India CV share in FY26.
- **VE Commercial Vehicles Limited (VECV / Eicher, unlisted JV; parent NSEI:EICHERMOT)** — focused rival in light and medium-duty trucks and medium buses; 103,404 units FY26, revenue ₹27,077 crore.

## 4. Where The Company Sits

**TMCV sits at the top of named peers on gross margin** (36.8% vs Ashok Leyland's approximately 28.6%) and on auto ROCE (72.3%); Ashok Leyland's EBITDA margin at 13.0% is modestly above TMCV's 12.3%, reflecting a mix difference and Ashok Leyland's stronger southern market pricing in Q4. Mahindra CV and VECV margin data are not disclosed, so a full four-way comparison is not possible — insufficient data to compare against those two named peers.

## 5. Moat Verdict

**Narrow moat** — TMCV holds a real but limited advantage on two dimensions: (1) distribution depth — 314 dealers across all Indian geographies, with the widest eastern India presence among CV peers, underpins a spares/services recurring layer that competitors cannot replicate quickly; and (2) scale-driven cost and working capital efficiency — at ₹83,855 crore revenue and 435,227 units, TMCV is roughly 1.9× the size of Ashok Leyland, enabling negative working capital (-31 days cash conversion cycle), supplier negotiating leverage, and lower unit overhead. These two advantages translate into real margin and ROIC superiority: TMCV's gross margin is approximately 820 basis points (about 8 percentage points) above Ashok Leyland's.

The durability test this moat must pass over the next five years: can TMCV maintain its distribution and scale advantages as the product set shifts from diesel to BEV/hydrogen/software-defined platforms? The answer is not yet clear. Ashok Leyland's Switch Mobility is already profitable in electric buses; TMCV's i-MOEV electric truck platform is earlier stage. In a fast-changing technology environment — which the business quality module scored 35/100 on rate-of-change — the incumbent distribution and scale moat decays faster because new technology platforms can equalise or leapfrog the network advantage. Per CLAUDE.md §24 Filter 5 and MODULE_RULES.md: the industry rate-of-change is Weak (score 35), which means moat durability is discounted and this thesis is closer to a sector/technology-cycle bet than a durable compounder. A narrow moat in a fast-changing industry is harder to underwrite than the same moat in a stable one. The moat is real today; whether it persists through the powertrain transition is unproven.
