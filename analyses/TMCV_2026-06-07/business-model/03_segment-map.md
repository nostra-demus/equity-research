## 2. Dominant Segment

The company is effectively a single-segment business: the Commercial Vehicle segment contributes 98.5% of revenue and over 100% of operating segment profit in FY26. The "Others" segment (insurance broking) contributes 1.2% of revenue and 1.6% of profit, and is operationally dependent on the parent's vehicle sales ecosystem for its volumes. No sub-segments are manufactured — this is a pure-play commercial vehicle group, and everything that matters economically happens in the CV segment.

The CV segment itself is further broken down by management in the MD&A into four product lines — Heavy Commercial Vehicles (HCV, 29.5% of volumes and ₹37,199 cr in vehicle revenue), Intermediate and Light Medium Commercial Vehicles (ILMCV, 19.8% of volumes, ₹11,212 cr), SCV and Pickup (36.6% of volumes, ₹7,558 cr), and CV Passenger Vehicles (14.1% of volumes, ₹8,807 cr) — but these are not reported as separate Ind AS 108 segments. The CODM reviews them as one segment.

## 3. Segment Disclosure Quality

**Consistency year-over-year:** The company was incorporated on June 23, 2024 and received the demerged CV business from Tata Motors Passenger Vehicles Limited (formerly Tata Motors Ltd) effective October 1, 2025 (appointed date July 1, 2025). This means FY26 is the first full 12-month year for this entity, and the prior comparative period (nine months, June 23, 2024 to March 31, 2025) is explicitly flagged as not comparable. Segment definitions — two segments: Automotive (CV) and Others (Insurance Broking) — are consistent across the FY26 annual report and Q4 FY26 quarterly results filing. The segment note states segments are reviewed by the CODM, consistent with Ind AS 108 requirements.

**"Corporate/Unallocable" bucket:** The filing separates Corporate/Unallocable within the Automotive heading as a named sub-line in the segment table. Its revenue in FY26 is ₹278 cr (0.3% of total) and its segment loss is (₹448 cr). This is a disclosed, named bucket rather than an opaque residual. Its size is under 10% of revenue but its loss contribution is meaningful relative to total segment profit. No disclosure gaps here — it is quantified each period.

**Profit metrics at segment level:** Segment results are disclosed at the operating-profit-equivalent level (before other income, finance costs, forex, exceptional items, and tax) for both segments and the unallocable bucket. This is adequate for understanding relative profitability. Segment assets and liabilities are also disclosed. What is not disclosed: depreciation or capex allocated by segment, which limits a direct segment-level EBITDA or free-cash-flow calculation. Underlying EBITDA (₹10,314 cr, 12.3% margin) and Underlying EBIT (₹8,538 cr, 10.2% margin) are disclosed at the consolidated level only, not by segment.

**Changes in the last three years:** The entity itself did not exist in its current form more than two years ago. The demerger from the old Tata Motors group (which reported JLR, PV and CV as three segments in its consolidated statements) created this pure-play CV company. The two-segment structure (Automotive CV + Others) has been applied since the entity's inception. Downstream agents (`unit-economics`, `competitive-map`) should note: no multi-year segment comparables exist on a like-for-like basis; FY26 is the only clean full-year period available.
