# Unit Economics — TMCV

## 1. Natural Unit

The natural economic unit for the Commercial Vehicle segment is one vehicle wholesale (a unit sold to a dealer or direct buyer).

The business also has a secondary unit type — the downstream spare parts, services, and digital-fleet subscription attached to each vehicle over its working life. Management tracks this as a "non-cyclical" revenue stream and refers to it internally when discussing lifecycle value, but it is not reported separately at a per-unit level.

## 3. Value Creation Read

Each new unit sold by TMCV's CV segment currently creates value, with the evidence being directional rather than built from full per-unit disclosure.

The key anchors are: (a) EBITDA of ₹10,314 cr at 12.3% margin — roughly ₹2.37 lakh per vehicle — growing consistently over three years from a 7.8% base; (b) an auto ROCE of 72%, meaning the business earns back its capital in less than two years at current rates; (c) free cash flow of ₹12,438 cr in FY26, which is 14.8% of revenue and exceeds net profit of ₹3,030 cr by a wide margin (FCF-to-PAT conversion well above 1x); and (d) a negative working capital cycle of minus 31 days, meaning each incremental unit generates cash before the company pays suppliers.

The math cannot be completed in strict "contribution margin × lifetime > acquisition cost" form because per-unit variable cost and per-unit gross margin are not separately disclosed. The single most valuable missing disclosure is a product-line-level gross margin (or at minimum a split between vehicle margin and downstream margin), which would allow a true per-unit contribution test. What is already clear is that the aggregate economics are strongly positive and improving, and that the business earns well above any reasonable cost of capital (Inference: a CV manufacturer at ₹83,855 cr revenue with a 72% ROCE and net cash of ₹13,713 cr is covering its cost of capital — likely 12%–15% for an Indian listed manufacturer — by a large margin).

Verdict: **Creates value**, with high confidence from aggregate evidence but incomplete per-unit disclosure.

## 4. Sensitivity

The input that would most change the value-creation read if it moved 20% is the **price per unit (ASP — average selling price)**. Revenue per unit at roughly ₹18.98 lakh is already a blended figure across a wide range: HCV trucks implying around ₹29 lakh per unit (₹37,199 cr ÷ 128,292 units), ILMCV around ₹13 lakh (₹11,212 cr ÷ 86,224 units), SCV/Pickup around ₹4.7 lakh (₹7,558 cr ÷ 159,398 units), and CV passenger around ₹14.4 lakh (₹8,807 cr ÷ 61,313 units). A 20% decline in ASP — without a matching reduction in material cost — would wipe out approximately ₹16,771 cr of revenue against a consolidated EBITDA of ₹10,314 cr, destroying profitability entirely at current cost structures. ASP is the most historically volatile input: management acknowledged that the aggregate blended ASP has been declining in FY26 due to mix shift (fewer high-priced electric buses in the volume base), even as truck ASPs rose, and that commodity pressure in steel, aluminium, and copper in Q4 FY26 and into FY27 is already squeezing margins by roughly 100 basis points. Material cost (roughly 62.9% of revenue) is the second most sensitive lever — a 20% rise in steel/aluminium/copper would add approximately ₹10,546 cr to the cost base against ₹10,314 cr EBITDA, making the business breakeven at the EBITDA line. Management's strategy of partial price pass-through (2% in April FY27) combined with cost engineering is the primary offset mechanism for both risks.

## 5. Data Notes and Limitations

- FY25 comparatives cover only nine months (June 23, 2024 to March 31, 2025) due to the demerger; all "prior year" directional reads use either management's three-year commentary (FY23–FY26) or the unaudited nine-month period.
- Per-unit gross margin and per-unit contribution margin are not disclosed. The analysis uses segment-level and consolidated P&L data as the closest available proxies.
- Revenue per unit calculations use wholesale volumes (435,227 units), which may differ from retail volumes. No retail volume is separately disclosed.
- Product-line revenue and volume data from MD&A (Pages 213–216 of FY26 Integrated Annual Report) are management commentary, not audited segment notes.
- The auto ROCE of 72% is a management-defined metric ("auto ROCE") stated in the Chairman's message and KPI highlights; its precise definition (capital base and EBIT numerator) is not separately reconciled in the documents reviewed.
