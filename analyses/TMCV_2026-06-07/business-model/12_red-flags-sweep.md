| **TMF Holdings subsidiary — chronic losses, FVTPL write-down, and parent loan exposure** | TMF Holdings Limited (TMCV's NBFC/financial-services subsidiary, 81.77% owned) generated a consolidated P&L drag of approximately ₹2,478 crores in FY26, making it the single largest earnings headwind inside the consolidated group. The underpayment has compounded for over five years. No quantum is stated in the available data, but the statutory auditor considered it material enough to call out in the CARO annexure. | **Audit trail gaps in price master and variable cost systems (CARO disclosure)** | The CARO 2020 annexure to the standalone auditor's report discloses that the audit trail (edit log) was NOT enabled at the database level — as distinct from the application level — for three systems: (a) the price master system, (b) the variable marketing expenses system, and (c) the product development time recording system. ## 3. Most Severe New Flag

The single most material new flag is the **TMF Holdings subsidiary drag and parent loan exposure**. At ₹2,478 crores of consolidated earnings destruction in FY26 — primarily from a ₹2,418 crore FVTPL investment loss inside the subsidiary — TMF Holdings was the largest single drag on TMCV's consolidated profitability in its first full standalone year. Without this drag, consolidated PAT would have been roughly double the reported ₹3,030 crores. The risk is not historical: TMF Holdings holds an NBFC balance sheet of financial assets that remain on a fair-value basis, meaning future mark-to-market moves can create further P&L volatility that is entirely outside TMCV's core CV manufacturing operations. The parent has already written off ₹661 crores of its ₹2,666 crore loan to the subsidiary, and the auditor treated inter-company loan recoverability as a Key Audit Matter. Most critically, Note 11 discloses a pending scheme to amalgamate TMF Holdings into TMCV — if that scheme closes, the NBFC's liabilities, regulatory capital requirements, and mark-to-market portfolio will migrate permanently onto TMCV's standalone balance sheet, converting what is today a consolidated subsidiary risk into a direct standalone balance-sheet risk. The synthesizer should model TMF Holdings as a contingent liability of potentially ₹2,000–3,000 crores, not a stable equity-method investment, and treat the amalgamation scheme as a material event that could change TMCV's risk profile substantially.

## 4. Cross-Cutting Patterns

Three flags — warranty provision divergence from actuals (Key Audit Matter), audit trail gaps in pricing and variable cost systems (CARO), and the EPR obligation with no provision (flagged in capital-allocation-governance) — form a cluster of estimation and internal-control weaknesses that all date to the same cause: TMCV is operating its first full standalone year with IT systems, provisioning models, and statutory compliance frameworks inherited from the demerger, not all of which were fully adapted to its standalone entity status by March 31, 2026. Individually, each looks manageable; together, they suggest that reported FY26 earnings contain a higher-than-normal degree of provisioning uncertainty, and that FY27 results may carry catch-up charges as these gaps close. The synthesizer should treat FY26 as a year of potential earnings over-statement relative to a normalised provisioning run-rate, not a clean baseline.
