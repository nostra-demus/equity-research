# Competitive Map — TMCV

## 1. Dominant Segment

Commercial Vehicles (trucks, buses, SCVs, pickups) — 98.5% of revenue and over 100% of operating segment profit in FY26. [SEBI LODR Reg 33/52 Consolidated Audited Results, Year ended March 31, 2026, filed May 13, 2026, Page 4]

---

## 2. Named Competitors

### Competitor A — Ashok Leyland Limited

- **Ticker / listing:** NSEI:ASHOKLEY (NSE/BSE listed; part of Hinduja Group)
- **Where they compete:** Domestic India CV market — direct head-to-head in Medium and Heavy Commercial Vehicles (MHCV trucks and buses) and Light Commercial Vehicles (LCV). Ashok Leyland is the most direct and significant competitor to TMCV in the HCV/MHCV segment, which is TMCV's highest-revenue product line.
- **Scale:** FY26 revenue ₹44,007 crore; 220,437 total units (including defence vehicles); domestic MHCV trucks 105,905 units; domestic MHCV bus volume 20,840 units. [Ashok Leyland FQ4 FY26 earnings call transcript, May 28, 2026, prepared remarks]
- **Source named in:** TMCV segment-map upstream (explicitly flagged as "competitive pressure from Ashok Leyland"); Ashok Leyland FQ4 FY26 earnings call transcript in the data pool (May 28, 2026) — the second transcript digest — confirms this is the direct peer.
- **One-line read:** Ashok Leyland is the #2 MHCV player in India with 30.2% domestic MHCV truck share and 34.1% bus share in FY26, making it the clearest head-to-head rival to TMCV across trucks and buses, though at roughly half TMCV's revenue scale.

---

### Competitor B — Mahindra & Mahindra Limited (Commercial Vehicles division)

- **Ticker / listing:** NSEI:M&M (part of the broader M&M conglomerate; CV is not a separately listed pure-play)
- **Where they compete:** Domestic India CV market — primarily SCV and LCV (sub-3.5 tonne segment), which is TMCV's SCV/Pickup line (36.6% of TMCV's unit volumes, ₹7,558 crore revenue). Mahindra commands roughly 54% share in vehicles under 3.5 tonnes in India and is the leading challenger to TMCV in the last-mile and small-transporter segment.
- **Scale:** FY26 CV retail units approximately 298,237 in India; ~26.7% overall CV market share. Mahindra CV revenue is not separately reported as a stand-alone entity (it sits inside M&M's automotive division). [Web: rushlane.com/cv-retail-sales-march-and-fy-2026, data as of FY26 retail VAHAN; unverified, labelled web source]
- **Source named in:** FQ4 FY26 TMCV earnings call transcript (May 13, 2026, Q&A section) references SCV segment pressure: "the onslaught of 3-wheelers" and competitor SCV dynamics discussed in context of Ace Pro and Intra EV launches; Autocar Professional (web, unverified) specifically names Mahindra as the entity Tata is defending SCV share against.
- **One-line read:** Mahindra is the strongest rival in the sub-5 tonne SCV/pickup sub-segment — TMCV's highest-volume but lowest-revenue-per-unit product line — and held or grew share in FY26 even as TMCV launched the Ace Pro to defend its position.

---

### Competitor C — VE Commercial Vehicles Limited (VECV / Eicher)

- **Ticker / listing:** VECV is an unlisted joint venture between Volvo Group and Eicher Motors Limited (NSEI:EICHERMOT); Eicher Motors is the listed parent.
- **Where they compete:** Domestic India CV market, principally Light and Medium Duty trucks (LMD, 5–19 tonnes) and buses, with some Heavy Duty (HD) trucks. This overlaps with TMCV's ILMCV and bus sub-segments.
- **Scale:** FY26 total CV sales 103,404 units (+~15% YoY), its highest-ever annual volume; consolidated revenue ₹27,077 crore; PAT ₹1,471 crore. [Web: eichertrucksandbuses.com press release, VECV FY26 sales milestone, unverified web source; autoguideindia.com Eicher Motors FY26 results, unverified web source]
- **Source named in:** Capital IQ Credit Health Panel screenshot (data pool, June 7, 2026) shows VECV/Eicher in the peer comparison group for TMCV; web sources name VECV as a top-4 India CV competitor [web: rushlane.com FY26 CV retail sales, unverified].
- **One-line read:** VECV is the #4 CV player by volume with a premium positioning in LMD trucks and medium buses, and its FY26 milestone of 100,000+ units signals growing scale ambitions, though at roughly one-third of TMCV's volume it remains a focused segment rival rather than a full-range challenger.

## 3. Competitive Position

TMCV is gaining share in HCVs and holding or modestly losing ground in SCVs. In Q4 FY26, TMCV management stated: "Tata Motors volumes outpaced the industry at 25% Y-o-Y growth" versus the total industry volume (TIV) growing at 19% Y-o-Y; for the full year FY26, TIV grew 12.5% while TMCV grew 11.6% — a marginal full-year underperformance vs industry, largely attributed to a muted H1 and early monsoon effects, which corrected in H2. In HCVs specifically, management claimed the "highest offtake market share in a decade" in Q4 FY26. [FQ4 FY26 earnings call transcript, May 13, 2026, Girish Wagh prepared remarks, p.6] The SCV/pickup segment has faced competitive pressure, with TMCV launching the Ace Pro and Intra EV to defend position against Mahindra's sub-3.5 tonne dominance; the transcript references market share gains in SCV pickup in Q4 as "measurable gains." Overall VAHAN retail share for FY26 was approximately 35.6% across all CVs vs Ashok Leyland at ~18.4% and Mahindra at ~26.7%. [Web: rushlane.com FY26 CV retail sales data, unverified; VAHAN-derived figures consistent with management's own references to market share trends in the transcript]

Verdict: **Holding / modestly gaining** in HCVs; **holding with effort** in SCVs where Mahindra is structurally stronger.

---

## 4. Competitive Shape

The Indian commercial vehicle market is a **tight oligopoly**: four players — Tata Motors, Mahindra, Ashok Leyland, and VECV/Eicher — together held over 88% of FY26 retail volumes (approximately 3,62,428 + 2,98,237 + 1,89,567 + 88,184 = ~938,416 units out of 1,060,906 total, or ~88.5%). [Web: rushlane.com FY26 CV retail data, unverified; web: mymotoco.com FY26 CV trends, unverified] The market splits by weight class: MHCV trucks (>7.5 tonnes) are primarily a two-horse race between TMCV and Ashok Leyland; SCVs and LCVs (<5 tonnes) are dominated by Mahindra with TMCV defending; medium-duty trucks and buses see three-way competition between TMCV, Ashok Leyland, and VECV. No HHI is publicly published for this market, but the top-4 concentration above 88% is consistent with a consolidated oligopoly structure. This structure is broadly stable — no new entrant has taken meaningful share in more than a decade — which supports pricing discipline, though not fully rational pricing given periodic competitive intensity in discount cycles.
