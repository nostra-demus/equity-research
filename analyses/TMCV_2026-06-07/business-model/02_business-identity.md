# Business Identity — TMCV

## 1. What The Company Actually Does

Tata Motors Limited (formerly TML Commercial Vehicles Limited, ticker NSEI:TMCV) is India's largest manufacturer of commercial vehicles — trucks, buses, vans, and small commercial pickups. It was demerged from the old Tata Motors passenger-car group and listed independently on NSE/BSE on November 12, 2025, making it a pure-play CV company for the first time. The company designs, assembles, and sells vehicles across four product lines: Heavy Commercial Vehicles (trucks above roughly 12 tonnes), Intermediate and Light Medium Commercial Vehicles (5–12 tonnes), Small Commercial Vehicles and pickups (sub-5 tonne, last-mile delivery and rural), and CV Passenger Vehicles (buses, vans, and passenger carriers). Customers are fleet operators, transport companies, state transport undertakings, logistics firms, infrastructure contractors, small entrepreneurs, and export markets across 52 countries. The core problem each customer pays to solve is moving goods or people at the lowest cost per kilometre over the vehicle's working life — so total cost of ownership (fuel, maintenance, uptime, financing) is the purchase criterion, not sticker price. A downstream "non-cyclical" business selling spare parts, service contracts, telematics subscriptions (Fleet Edge, over one million connected vehicles), and automotive fluids runs alongside the vehicle business and provides a more stable earnings stream through cycles.

## 2. How The Company Makes Money

**Core vehicular revenue (trucks, buses, SCVs):**

`Revenue = units wholesaled × average selling price per unit`

FY26 consolidated: 435,227 units × blended average realisation = ₹83,390 crore vehicle revenue [FY26 Integrated Annual Report (Ind AS), MD&A, p.214]. Margin is earned after paying material costs (67.7% of revenue in FY26), employee costs (6.9%), and other manufacturing overheads; EBITDA margin (earnings before interest, tax, depreciation, and amortisation — the cash profit generated before financing and accounting charges) reached 12.3% in FY26 [FY26 Integrated Annual Report, Key Performance Highlights, p.28].

Revenue by product category (FY26, standalone, TML-manufactured vehicles):

- Heavy Commercial Vehicles: ₹37,199 crore
- Intermediate & Light Medium CVs: ₹11,212 crore
- CV Passenger Vehicles: ₹8,807 crore
- SCV and Pickup: ₹7,558 crore

[FY26 Integrated Annual Report (Ind AS), MD&A, p.216]

**Downstream / non-cyclical revenue:**

`Revenue = installed base of vehicles × attach rate for parts, service, and telematics`

This includes genuine spare parts, workshop services, Fleet Edge telematics subscriptions, automotive fluids, and fleet care solutions. Management calls out "noncyclical CAGR running at 2.7x a cyclical CAGR" over three years [FQ4 FY26 earnings call transcript, May 13, 2026, prepared remarks, p.4]. Revenue from "Other operations" (primarily insurance broking services under a subsidiary) contributed ₹968 crore at 13.9% operating margin [FY26 Integrated Annual Report (Ind AS), MD&A, p.215].

**International / export revenue:**

`Revenue = export units × ASP in destination currency, converted at spot INR rate`

Export volumes were 35,320 units in FY26 (+34% vs 25,273 units implied by FY25 totals), spanning 52 countries; the South Asia (SAP) corridor led growth [FY26 Integrated Annual Report (Ind AS), MD&A, p.213]. South Korea operations (Tata Daewoo — trucks) contributed roughly ₹3,400 crore of revenue [FY26 Integrated Annual Report, region-wise revenue chart, p.216].

**What drives volume:** freight activity (measured by e-way bill generation and road BTKM growth), GDP and infrastructure spending, fleet replacement cycles (fleet owners typically replace after 4–6 years), and financing conditions. **What drives price:** product mix shift (heavier, higher-payload trucks carry higher ASPs), pricing discipline, and commodity pass-through. **What drives margin:** operating leverage at high volumes, material cost control (steel, aluminium, copper are the key inputs), and growing share of higher-margin downstream and non-cyclical businesses.

## 3. Business Type Classification

Asset-heavy, volume-cyclical Indian commercial vehicle manufacturer and market leader, with a growing non-cyclical aftermarket and telematics overlay.

## 4. What Drives Variance

Revenue and margins swing most directly with domestic CV industry volumes, which follow India's freight cycle — itself driven by GDP growth, infrastructure capex, and e-commerce logistics demand. When freight rates are firm and fleet profitability is healthy, operators buy new vehicles; when diesel prices spike or freight rates soften, purchase decisions are deferred — this is the primary volume risk. Price variance comes from commodity input costs (steel, aluminium, copper): material costs are 67.7% of revenue, so a 5% move in steel prices directly affects EBITDA margin by roughly 100–150 basis points (1–1.5 percentage points) if not passed on. Mix also matters — a shift toward heavier trucks (HCVs carry higher ASPs) expands realisation per unit, while a shift toward lower-value buses or SCVs compresses it. Foreign exchange affects export revenues and South Korea operations, though at present these are a small part of consolidated revenue (roughly 12–15% of total).
