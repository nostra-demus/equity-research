## 5. Capital-Allocation Read

The core CV business generates capital with genuine efficiency: a negative working capital cycle (−31 days), auto ROCE of 72.3%, FCF of ₹12,438 cr (14.8% of revenue), and ₹13,713 cr net cash at year-end. In FY26 itself, the dominant use of that cash was right: paying down ₹4,376 cr of debt and distributing ₹1,473 cr to shareholders as dividends, while keeping capex at a measured ₹2,248 cr (1.16x depreciation — growth but not over-investment). That is a picture of operating discipline converting to per-share value.

The worst use of capital in the observable period is the pending Iveco acquisition at ~₹41,691 cr (€3.8 billion), which is 3.3x book equity and carries zero disclosed financial targets — no ROIC floor, no synergy number, no post-deal leverage ceiling. At the time of writing it has not closed; but if it does at the stated terms, it will consume the entire net cash pile built over three years of discipline and require substantial new debt, reversing the balance-sheet improvement that management rightly highlighted as a FY26 achievement. The best use of capital was debt repayment in FY26, which moved the company from net debt of ₹4,016 cr (company-defined, end FY25) to net cash of ₹13,713 cr in a single year. The Iveco transaction, if completed, would undo that in a single step.

The single biggest capital-allocation signal is the Iveco deal: a management team that has demonstrated genuine operating discipline is betting that on a single all-cash, all-narrative acquisition at 3.3x its own book equity, funded by new debt on a balance sheet that took three years to deleverage. Per the base-rate rule (CLAUDE.md §9): most large cross-border industrial acquisitions do not generate positive returns for the acquirer.

**Verdict: mixed.** The underlying capital-allocation discipline is strong (per-share FCF creation, debt repayment, reasonable capex), but the Iveco deal — announced but not closed — is a large, undisciplined capital-allocation decision by any evidence-based standard.
