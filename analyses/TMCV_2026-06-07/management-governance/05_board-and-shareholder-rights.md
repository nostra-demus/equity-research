**RPT analysis:**

Total RPT outflows of ₹6,841 crore equal 8.2% of revenue (₹83,855 crore) — this is above the 5% threshold that triggers a "High" materiality flag under MODULE_RULES but below the 10% "Critical" threshold and well below the 25% hard disqualifier. As a percentage of PAT (₹3,030 crore) the figure is 226%, and as a percentage of consolidated net worth (₹12,734 crore) it is 54% — both are elevated in absolute magnitude relative to earnings, reflecting the thin margin structure of a commercial vehicle manufacturer rather than economic leakage per se.

The dominant RPT is the purchase of engines and components from Tata Cummins Private Limited (a 50:50 joint operation with Cummins, a global diesel engine manufacturer) for ₹2,999 crore. This is a manufacturing necessity — TMCV does not produce its own diesel engines and Tata Cummins supplies specifically to TMCV's truck and bus specifications. The arm's-length nature of this transaction is defensible because Cummins (a global, independent entity) is the 50% partner in Tata Cummins, providing market pricing discipline. It is not a promoter-to-subsidiary value transfer.

The remaining product purchases (₹2,007 crore from Tata Sons subsidiaries, ₹834 crore from Automobile Corporation of Goa) and services received (TCS IT services ₹244 crore, Tata Sons Brand/IP ₹212 crore, manufacturing support ₹234 crore from TMDAIL, logistics ₹143 crore from TMGSL) reflect the integrated nature of the Tata Group ecosystem. These are ordinary operating expenses for a manufacturer embedded in a large industrial group. All were disclosed as arm's length and approved by the Audit Committee.

No royalty or brand fee payable specifically to Tata Sons is broken out as a separate line item in Note 42. The ₹212 crore "services received" from Tata Sons may embed group services or brand licensing fees — this is not fully transparent from available disclosures and warrants monitoring.

The ₹2,925 crore in intercorporate deposits / loans to subsidiaries is the highest-concern RPT item. This capital is deployed to group entities (subsidiaries) rather than retained in the listed entity or distributed to shareholders. The current-account nature of ₹2,774 crore (callable but extended within the group) means TMCV's functional cash is partially employed within the broader group structure. This triggers RF-RPT-003 at High materiality.

The pending TMF Holdings Limited amalgamation (a subsidiary with ₹(2,478) crore negative PAT contribution to consolidated accounts in FY26, per Note 45) represents a forward minority-shareholder risk if completed. Folding an NBFC with substantial liabilities into the listed commercial-vehicle entity would dilute per-share book value for minority holders and potentially burden TMCV with TMF's credit/asset-quality risks. This has not yet been put to a minority shareholder vote as of analysis date (2nd AGM is June 29, 2026).

RPTs are disclosed as recurring, arm's length, and Audit Committee approved. They are not growing faster than revenue based on available one-year data — trend assessment is limited by the entity's short listing history.

Cross-reference: `01_disqualifier-scan.md` confirms RPT outflows at 8.2% of revenue — below the 25% hard disqualifier. No disqualifier triggered on RPTs.

**Summary on takeover defenses:** The Indian legal framework provides less built-in anti-takeover architecture than US structures. There is no poison pill, no staggered board, and no dual-class voting — all of which is positive for minority shareholders. Voting standards default to a combination of ordinary resolutions (simple majority) and special resolutions (75%), consistent with Companies Act 2013. The promoter at 42.56% cannot alone force a special resolution or block one — material governance decisions require minority shareholder support, which is a structural protection.

## 5. Minority-Shareholder Protection Read

The board is formally well-constituted: 62.5% independent directors, a clean chair/CEO split, an all-ID Audit Committee chaired by an experienced financial professional (Varsha Purandare), and committees that comply with SEBI LODR requirements across the board. The Indian structural environment also works in minority holders' favor — no dual class shares, no staggered board, no poison pill, and a promoter that at 42.56% cannot force special resolutions without minority support.

The practical concern is the TMF Holdings amalgamation. If completed at or near the announced terms, this transaction transfers the financial risks and losses of an NBFC — contributing ₹(2,478) crore negative PAT to consolidated accounts in FY26 alone — into the listed commercial vehicle entity. This is a classic group-structure decision that benefits the controlling promoter (simplifying group consolidation, removing a loss center) at the direct expense of minority holders' per-share book value and earnings quality. That decision is pending the 2nd AGM vote (June 29, 2026), and proxy-advisor and institutional investor positions are not yet visible in the data pool. In addition, N. Chandrasekaran's 44% board attendance rate and 7 other directorships (at the SEBI cap) raise a practical independence concern — not a legal one, but a real-world one about bandwidth.

The single biggest risk to minority holders is the TMF Holdings amalgamation: it would dilute book value, reduce earnings quality, and potentially burden TMCV with NBFC-scale credit risks — representing a textbook instance of §24 Filter 6 (a controlling owner acting in the group's interest rather than per-share value for TMCV minorities).

**Verdict: adequate** — the formal board structure and absence of anti-shareholder voting features are genuinely positive; the TMF Holdings amalgamation risk, RPT intensity from group procurement (8.2% of revenue), and intercorporate deposits to subsidiaries (₹2,925 crore) prevent a "strong protection" verdict. The §24 Filter 6 concern is present but not conclusive — Tata Sons is not a government entity or value-extracting predator, but the amalgamation test in June 2026 will determine whether this entity respects minority shareholders or uses them as a recipient for group restructuring costs.
