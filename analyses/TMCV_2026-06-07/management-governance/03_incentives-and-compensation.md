## 2. Performance Metrics

### 2A. Annual Bonus / Commission (Variable Pay)

The CG Report (p.233) states the NRC review of CEO pay uses a **Balanced Scorecard** covering the performance of the Company and the individual director on:

> *"certain defined qualitative and quantitative parameters such as volumes, EBITDA, market share, cashflows, cost reduction initiatives, safety, strategic initiatives and special projects as decided by the Board vis-à-vis targets set in the beginning of the year."*

The specific weights assigned to each metric are **not disclosed** in the Annual Report. This is a partial-data limitation: the CG Report describes the scorecard categories but does not publish the individual metric weights or the actual targets set for FY26.

| Annual bonus / commission | EBITDA | Not disclosed | **Size-based** — absolute EBITDA, not margin or return on capital | FY26 Annual Report, CG Report, p.233 |

| Annual bonus / commission | Cost reduction initiatives | Not disclosed | **Returns-adjacent** — cost discipline helps margins but is not explicitly a ROIC / ROCE metric | FY26 Annual Report, CG Report, p.233 |
| Annual bonus / commission | Safety, strategic initiatives, special projects | Not disclosed | **Non-financial / qualitative** — not per-share or returns-based | FY26 Annual Report, CG Report, p.233 |
| Long-term incentive (TML SLTI Scheme — PSUs) | "Certain performance criteria" (exact metrics not disclosed) | Not disclosed | **Undetermined** — performance criteria referenced but not specified in the Annual Report | FY26 Annual Report, Board's Report, p.121; CG Report, p.234–235 |

**Key observations on the metric mix:**

1. The disclosed annual bonus scorecard is dominated by **size-based metrics** (volumes, absolute EBITDA) and market share. There is no explicit mention of ROIC (return on invested capital), ROCE (return on capital employed), EPS, or FCF per share in the disclosed parameters.

2. "Cashflows" appears in the scorecard but is not described as FCF per share or free cash flow yield — it reads as an absolute cash generation metric, which is size-adjacent.

3. The LTIP (PSU scheme under TML SLTI) vests on "fulfillment of certain performance criteria" — but those criteria are not published in the Annual Report. The PSU exercise price is a nominal ₹2 per share (the face value), meaning the economic incentive is almost entirely the share price appreciation between grant and exercise, with no premium hurdle. This makes the PSU economically close to a time-vested equity grant, with the undisclosed performance criteria determining whether vesting occurs at all.

4. The Balanced Scorecard language does reference "returns and cash generation" in the MD & CEO's letter (p.11) as the lens through which growth is evaluated — but this appears in a strategic narrative, not in the disclosed compensation metrics.

5. No EPS, ROIC, ROCE, TSR, or dividend-per-share metric is listed in the disclosed bonus parameters. This is the central gap.

**Partial-data note:** Metric weights are not disclosed. This is standard practice for Indian listed companies under the Companies Act / SEBI LODR framework, where the specific sub-weights within the Balanced Scorecard are treated as internally confidential. The PSU performance criteria are similarly undisclosed. This limits the ability to score incentive alignment precisely; the assessment rests on which metrics are named.

## 4. Incentive Alignment Read

The pay structure at TMCV is conventional for a large-cap Indian industrial conglomerate subsidiary. The variable component is substantial (commission/bonus is the largest single element of CEO pay at 48% of total pro-rata pay), which creates meaningful pay-for-performance sensitivity. However, the disclosed scorecard metrics are dominated by **absolute size and volume metrics** — volumes, EBITDA, market share, and cashflows — rather than per-share or returns-based measures such as ROIC, ROCE, EPS, FCF per share, or TSR. No returns-on-capital metric is named in the disclosed parameters.

The LTIP (PSU scheme) has the structural potential to reward share price appreciation, but its performance criteria are undisclosed and the PSU exercise price is nominal (₹2 face value), making it effectively a near-free equity grant conditional on unnamed criteria. Until TMCV discloses the specific PSU performance conditions — ROIC targets, TSR hurdles, or similar — it cannot be classified as a returns-based LTIP.

The single biggest incentive signal is the bonus scorecard's leading position for "volumes" and "EBITDA" — both absolute metrics — which creates a structural pull toward growing the business in size terms. In the context of the Iveco acquisition (₹41,691 crore all-cash bid, announced in FY26), this incentive structure does not discourage empire-building: a CEO paid on volumes and EBITDA has no compensation-level reason to resist a large acquisition that grows those numbers, even if it destroys per-share value.

**Verdict: Mixed.** The pay structure has meaningful variable pay, but the disclosed metrics are size-based rather than per-share/returns-based, and the PSU performance conditions are undisclosed. The incentive architecture is not misaligned in a flagrant way (there is no pure revenue-growth bonus with no cash flow check), but it is not oriented toward per-share value creation the way a ROIC / TSR / EPS-weighted scorecard would be.
