# Management-Governance Module — TMCV (Synthesis)

## Abstract

TMCV's management team — led by Girish Wagh, 33 years at Tata Motors and 8 years running the CV business — delivered on every operating commitment it made as a standalone issuer in FY26: net cash achieved (₹7,451 crore), EBITDA margin expanded 100 bps to 12.3%, and two consecutive quarters beat consensus. Capital allocation in FY26 itself was disciplined: the dominant use of cash was paying down debt (₹4,376 crore) and distributing ₹1,473 crore to shareholders; auto ROCE of 72.3% reflects genuine organic capital productivity. The biggest alignment gap is the pay scorecard, which rewards volumes and absolute EBITDA (size metrics) with no disclosed ROIC, EPS, or TSR hurdle, and the PSU vesting conditions are undisclosed — a structure that does not discourage empire-building. The single biggest governance risk is the pending Iveco acquisition at ₹41,691 crore (€3.8 billion, 3.3x book equity), committed with zero disclosed financial targets, reversing three years of deliberate deleveraging; simultaneously, the board-approved TMF Holdings merger would fold NBFC liabilities — which dragged ₹2,478 crore from FY26 PAT — into the listed CV entity without a quantified financial impact being shared with minority shareholders. On current evidence, this is a competent operating team in a well-governed framework but with a large, narrative-only capital allocation decision and a parent-initiated restructuring that tests whether minority interests are protected.

Ramanan have run the CV business for years from inside the Tata Group structure, and in TMCV's first full year as a listed entity they delivered on every measurable operating commitment: net cash positive, EBITDA margin up 100 basis points to 12.3%, strong free cash flow of ₹12,438 crore, first dividend at ₹4/share, and two consecutive consensus beats at the quarterly level.
