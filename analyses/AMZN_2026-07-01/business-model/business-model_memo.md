## What This Module Found

Amazon runs three businesses at once — cloud infrastructure (AWS), e-commerce retail and marketplace, and digital advertising — and the economics are dominated by AWS. AWS made $45.6 billion in operating income on $128.7 billion of revenue in FY2025 (a 35.4% operating margin), which is 57% of total operating income on only 18% of total revenue [FY2025 10-K; `05_segment-map.md`]. The single most important driver going forward is the AI capital-spending cycle: AWS carries a $244 billion remaining-performance-obligation backlog at end-2025 (contracted revenue already sold but not yet delivered), and the AWS AI revenue run rate is above $15 billion and growing triple digits [FY2025 10-K; `03_business-identity.md`].

The single most important risk is the cash cost of that build. Capital spending was $128 billion in FY2025 and guided to roughly $200 billion for FY2026, which has already compressed free cash flow (operating cash flow minus capex, the cash the business actually throws off) from $38 billion in FY2024 to $11 billion in FY2025 [`12_red-flags-sweep.md`; FY2025 10-K]. On top of that, GAAP net income is inflated by about $5.5 billion of non-cash fair-value gains on Amazon's Anthropic convertible notes — paper marks on an illiquid Level 3 investment (no market price, valued by model) that could reverse [`12_red-flags-sweep.md`]. If AI inference prices fall faster than volumes grow, hundreds of billions in committed capital could earn below their cost. Through-cycle operating margins are estimated at 8–10%, below the current 13.1% [`07_business-quality.md`].

## What Would Change This Read

The synthesis names four things that would move the answer toward lower quality:

1. **Q4 2026 free cash flow still below $20 billion annualized despite the capex ramp** — confirms AI monetization is lagging the build.
2. **AWS revenue growth decelerates below 20% for two consecutive quarters without a matching operating-margin expansion** — suggests volume miss, not temporary pricing pressure.
3. **An adverse regulatory ruling on marketplace structure** (for example, EU DMA enforcement ordering structural separation) — impairs the Prime–advertising flywheel.
4. **Anthropic fair-value marks reverse by more than $20 billion** — signals a technology-strategy failure on Amazon's primary AI supply partner.

Positive triggers (implicit in the bull case): backlog conversion into AWS revenue at ≥28% YoY, Trainium continuing to displace NVIDIA at scale, and free cash flow recovering as depreciation catches up with capex.

## Bottom Line

- **Verdict:** High-quality business — worth deeper work. Narrow moat, real but not wide (~320 bps ROIC-over-WACC gap).
- **Could be better than it looks:** AWS holds a $244B contracted backlog and an AI revenue run rate above $15B growing triple digits; Trainium custom silicon gives an estimated "tens of billions" annual capex advantage vs relying on NVIDIA; retail runs structurally negative working capital (suppliers fund the operation — accounts payable $94.4B vs inventory $34.2B).
- **Could be worse than it looks:** GAAP earnings are inflated by ~$5.5B of non-cash Anthropic mark-ups; FCF has collapsed from $38B to $11B in one year on a $128B capex bill, with $200B guided next year; four coordinated AWS patent actions were filed in H2 2025.
- **What is missing:** AWS-specific free cash flow and net dollar retention rate (how much revenue a cohort of customers keeps and grows year-over-year) — the synthesis flags this as the single most important gap for the valuation module.
- **One thing to watch next:** whether FY2026 free cash flow recovers above ~$20 billion annualized as the capex ramp continues — the cleanest early signal on whether the AI build is being monetized fast enough.

## Plain-English Glossary

- **Operating margin:** operating profit divided by revenue — how much profit each dollar of sales makes before interest and tax.
- **Free cash flow (FCF):** cash from operations minus capital spending — the cash the business actually generates after keeping the assets running.
- **Capex:** capital expenditure — money spent on long-lived assets like data centers and warehouses.
- **ROIC:** return on invested capital — profit earned on each dollar of capital put in.
- **WACC:** weighted average cost of capital — the blended cost of the equity and debt used to fund the business.
- **Remaining performance obligations (backlog):** revenue already contracted with customers but not yet delivered.
- **Level 3 fair value:** an accounting label for an asset with no market price, valued by a model — the value is estimated, not observed.
- **Net dollar retention rate:** how much revenue a set of existing customers keeps and grows over the following year, net of churn and downgrades.
- **Basis points (bps):** hundredths of a percentage point — 100 bps = 1%.
- **RSU dilution:** new shares issued to employees as pay, which increases the share count and shrinks each existing shareholder's slice.
