## 2. The Nearest Dated Catalyst

The nearest **proven**-dated catalyst is the **Viterra goodwill measurement-period adjustment closure**, which must occur by **July 2, 2026** — the one-year anniversary of the deal close. This is a statutory deadline, not a management election, making it the only hard-dated event inside four weeks of today. The bullish path: the final purchase-price allocation is filed with no material goodwill increase or impairment — the ~$2,659M of provisional goodwill becomes confirmed, and the Grain Merchandising unit's ~9% headroom above carrying value survives, removing an accounting overhang from the Viterra story. The bearish path: the final PPA adjusts goodwill upward or identifies a first impairment in the Grain Merchandising unit (the unit with the thinnest cushion) — this would immediately hit GAAP earnings, raise the GAAP net-debt/EBITDA ratio, and reinforce the skeptical view that the Viterra deal was done at a price requiring optimistic spread assumptions that are not materializing. The market may already be discounting this risk given the Q2 earnings window is the more immediate market focus, but any goodwill charge before the Q2 print would compound the bearish setup.

## 3. The Single Most Important Catalyst

**The Q2 2026 earnings release and full-year guidance update (~late July 2026) is the highest-impact event on the calendar.** It is the first opportunity to verify or refute the FY2026 thesis in three ways simultaneously: whether the soybean/softseed crush spread held through Q2, whether the management-guided 40%/60% H1/H2 split is on track, and — most critically — whether the full-year guide is raised above $9.50. The Street ($9.43 adj. EPS consensus) has already arbitraged the April guidance raise away, so an in-line quarter is not sufficient; the bullish path requires an explicit FY2026 guide raise to above $9.50 AND constructive H2 commentary on the crush/biofuel spread. The bearish path is an in-line Q2 print accompanied by unchanged or narrowed FY guidance and cautious H2 spread commentary — which would read as a soft miss despite a nominal EPS beat, given that back-half analyst breadth is already net-negative (FQ3/FQ4 more down-revisions than up). The timing is vague (exact date not disclosed), which means a buyer today cannot time this event with precision; the ~4–7 week window is real but not pinned.

## 4. Negative / Bearish Catalysts

The following events skew **down**. Buyers ignore these at their peril.

**1. Q2 guidance miss / no raise (~late July 2026, Vague).** The most immediate downside risk. With consensus above the midpoint and back-half breadth negative, a flat or narrow guide is a disappointment. Biofuel mandate dependence means a single adverse policy signal between now and the print could shift the H2 setup materially.

**2. Viterra goodwill impairment (by July 2, 2026, Proven deadline).** Grain Merchandising unit has only ~9% headroom. A $100–200M impairment charge — even one the market "expected" — crystallizes the accounting uncertainty, directly reduces GAAP book value ($15,904M equity), and reopens the 5.8x GAAP leverage debate at the worst possible time (ahead of the Q2 print).

**3. Viterra seller lock-up expiry (Undated, Vague).** ~64M shares (~33% of outstanding) held by Glencore, CPPIB, and BCI. When the lock-up expires, any coordinated or sequential selling creates a sustained supply overhang in a stock with a $23.9B market cap. Glencore in particular is a commodity company that may not wish to be a long-duration Bunge equity holder. The expiry date is **Not Proven from available data** — this is a structurally live risk of uncertain timing.

**4. 3.25% Senior Notes ~$700M 2026 maturity (2026 calendar, Vague exact date).** While manageable on committed revolver capacity, the company must execute this refinancing at market rates (~5.0% vs 3.25% legacy coupon). Any market dislocation or spread-widening event between now and maturity raises the cost of the financing and highlights the dependence on rollover rather than FCF.

**5. 2028 wall: $2,759M + $3.5B revolver expiry (Oct 3, 2028, Proven date for revolver).** Not inside 12 months but the largest single structural risk on the medium-term balance sheet. The company's strategy of FCF recovery plus IG capital market access must work before this date. If FCF stays negative into 2027, the 2028 refinancing is executed under pressure.

**6. US-China trade escalation or biofuel mandate under-delivery (Undated, Vague).** Together these are the two external variables that could cut FY2026 adjusted EPS by $1.50–$2.00/share (~16–22% of the $9.25 guidance midpoint) per the earnings sensitivity analysis. Neither is company-controlled or dated.

**7. Brazilian indirect tax (~$668M unprovisioned, no resolution date).** ICMS/PIS-COFINS assessments issued; growing $104M/quarter; no settlement date in pool. This is not an imminent trigger but a reserve build or adverse court ruling would be a one-time GAAP charge with no forewarning.

## 5. Calendar Read

The calendar contains **two proven-dated events** (Viterra PPA deadline by July 2, 2026; 2028 revolver expiry Oct 3, 2028) and **one bounded-but-undated hard obligation** (3.25% notes ~$700M due 2026). The rest — Q2 earnings, buyback tranche, EPA 2027 RVO, synergy run-rate, lock-up expiry, US-China trade, rating review — are **vague**, making this a predominantly vague calendar. Near-term events (next 8 weeks) skew **balanced to bearish**: the July 2 PPA deadline is a downside-asymmetric accounting event, and the Q2 guidance result is the one catalyst that could re-rate the stock, but consensus is already above the guidance midpoint, setting a high bar. The single most important timing caveat is this: **the thesis rests on the biofuel-mandate and crush-spread cycle continuing, neither of which has a scheduled date — and both of which moved the EPS guidance by $1.50/share in a single policy decision**. A buyer who needs the thesis to work within 12 months must own the Q2 earnings event and accept that the rest of the positive calendar is soft-dated or undated.
