Reporting currency USD, FY ends Dec-31. Primary sources: FY2025 Form 10-K (filed Feb 19, 2026) and Q1 FY2026 10-Q. The **2026 DEF 14A is NOT in the data pool** — the 10-K defers all of Part III (Items 10–14: director independence, board structure, director RPT, audit fees) to the "definitive proxy statement for the 2026 Annual Meeting" [`FY25 10-K, Items 10–14, l.3803–3846`]. Where the proxy is required, this report uses the pool 10-K for board structure, the ~34% bloc, supermajority thresholds, share class, and related-party data, and supplements with **web-sourced** AGM voting results and independence framing (clearly labelled, unverified) from the May 20, 2026 AGM 8-K (Item 5.07, filed ~May 22, 2026). Per the partial-data rule, the board read is capped where it rests on absent proxy disclosure.*

**Partial-data status:** Board read **partly assessable**. **Materiality (MODULE_RULES RPT thresholds, % of revenue/assets):**
- Purchases at ~8.6% of revenue (9% of COGS) fall in the **High band (5–10%)** on size alone, but are **far below the >25%-of-revenue/expenses hard disqualifier** owned by `business-model/01_disqualifier-scan` (confirmed **not triggered** there).
- Sales at ~2.0% of revenue are in the **Medium band (1–5%)**.
- Receivables ≤4% / payables ≤3% are **Low-to-Medium**.

**Recurring / arm's-length / approval:** RPTs are **recurring** (each disclosed as "9% or less / 2% or less ... for each of the years ended Dec 31, 2025, 2024, and 2023" — i.e., stable, not growing faster than revenue), and management asserts arm's-length pricing. Crucially, the counterparties are Bunge's **unconsolidated investees (joint ventures Bunge co-owns), not promoter or insider entities** — there is no controlling family to leak value to. Audit-committee/minority approval of specific RPTs is in the absent Item 13/proxy and cannot be confirmed from the pool.

**The core question — is economic value leaking to a promoter/group?** On the evidence: **no promoter exists, and the RPT is JV-related commodity flow at stated arm's-length, stable as a share of revenue.** This is a normal-course leakage channel for an ABCD trader, not promoter self-dealing. It does **not** trigger RF-RPT-002 (promoter-linked) or RF-RPT-001 (above High/Critical threshold on the disqualifier test). It is logged as a monitoring item (Amber on size, not on conduct), pending Item 13 director-level RPT and confirmation that the related ~34% bloc (Glencore/CPP — themselves large commodity counterparties) does not transact with Bunge on non-arm's-length terms, which is the one place a future leakage risk could surface.

## 5. Minority-Shareholder Protection Read

On structure, minority holders are **adequately** protected: one share/one vote with no dual-class, no poison pill, no staggered board, every director elected annually, Swiss preemptive rights against dilution, an independent non-executive chair split from the CEO, and a clean 2026 AGM where say-on-pay drew <2% opposition and all 12 directors cleared comfortably. The single biggest entrenchment risk is **not management self-dealing but ownership concentration**: the ~34% Glencore/CPP/BCI bloc holds hard rights to name up to four directors and sits near the **2/3 supermajority** needed to block mergers and preemptive-rights withdrawals — a structure the 10-K itself says could deter a value-creating takeover and that lets a single coordinated group shape the board against a dispersed minority. Verdict: **adequate** protection, gated to no better than that because formal director-independence determinations, director-level related-party detail, and audit-fee disclosure all sit in the **absent 2026 proxy**, and because the bloc's influence — not any disclosed abuse — is the live structural exposure to watch (especially any future related-party dealing between Bunge and its large-counterparty shareholders Glencore/CPP).
