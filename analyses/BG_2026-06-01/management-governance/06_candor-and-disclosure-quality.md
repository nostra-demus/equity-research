## 1. Owning the Misses

The track record from `01` contains very few clean misses to test. On the metric management commits to — adjusted EPS — the late-year guide was hit or beaten in three of the last four full years; the only miss was **FY2024 adjusted EPS −0.6%** (immaterial), and quarterly adjusted EPS has beaten five quarters running [`01_management-and-track-record §4`; `earnings/04_guidance-consensus §6`]. The cleaner candor tests are therefore (a) the **down year on GAAP/EBIT** (FY2025 GAAP EPS fell $3.08 to $4.91; Total EBIT −$259M), (b) the **one segment that went backwards** in the only available transcript (Grain Merchandising), and (c) the **negative free cash flow** (FY2025 FCF −$879M) — all places where management could spin.

That is a presentation choice, not a disclosure failure — the audited numbers are all present — but it is the single softest spot in the candor picture.

## 2. Non-GAAP / Adjustment Aggressiveness

Cross-checked against `earnings/06_earnings-quality` (available). Bunge's reconciliation has exactly **two** adjustment categories — mark-to-market (MTM) timing and "certain (gains) & charges" — disclosed in the release/slides only, not in the 10-K/10-Q.

| Signal | Detail | Source |
|---|---|---|
| **Recurring "one-off" addbacks** | **Viterra acquisition & integration costs adjusted out for three consecutive years: $114M (FY23) → $244M (FY24) → $223M (FY25)**, plus +$0.20 EPS in Q1 FY26. A three-year running cost of an active M&A/portfolio-reshaping strategy is being treated as a "certain charge." Asset-sale gains and impairment charges also recur in CFO every year. **RF-DISC-002 triggered** (recurring "one-off" adjustments). | `earnings/06_earnings-quality §5, §8`; `FY25 10-K, lines 2720, 2730`; `Q1 FY26 release, p.2` |
| **GAAP-to-adjusted gap (magnitude / trend)** | Very large and widening on a single-period basis: FY2025 adjusted EPS $7.57 was **+54%** above GAAP $4.91; **Q1 FY26 adjusted EPS $1.83 was 5.2x GAAP $0.35** (the adjustment, +$1.48, was 423% of the GAAP number). The dominant driver is MTM timing ($336M of the $377M Q1'26 EBIT adjustment). When the adjustment dwarfs the reported figure ~4–5x, the adjusted number effectively *is* the earnings number, and its integrity rests on a management-computed MTM split that is **not independently verifiable from the filings**. | `earnings/06_earnings-quality §4, §7, §10`; `Q1 FY26 release, p.1–2` |
| **Does the adjusted framing flatter reality?** | **Two-sided, not a one-way flatter — a genuine mitigant.** The MTM sign flips period to period (Q1'25 +$0.08 vs Q1'26 +$1.28 EPS), and in **FY2023 GAAP EPS ($14.87) EXCEEDED adjusted ($13.66)** — i.e., the adjustment has *reduced* reported earnings in a good year. Stock-based comp is **correctly left inside** adjusted EPS ($73M FY25, $23M Q1'26) — a positive vs many large-caps. No reverse factoring; no useful-life change; contract liabilities ("advances on sales") rising. The flatter risk is concentrated in the recurring integration addback, not in the MTM line. | `earnings/06_earnings-quality §4, §7, §8` |

**Read on non-GAAP:** mixed-to-disciplined. The structure is honest where it matters most (two-sided MTM, SBC kept in, soft levers not abused), which is better than the typical large-cap. The two real haircuts are (1) the **recurring "Viterra integration" addback** dressed as a one-off for a third straight year (RF-DISC-002), and (2) the sheer size of the GAAP-to-adjusted gap, which forces reliance on a number only management can compute. Net: **non-GAAP is moderately aggressive in form (recurring addback, huge gap) but not deceptive in substance.**

## 3. Disclosure Transparency

Disclosure is **above average for the sector and got broader, not narrower, over the period reviewed.** Segment economics are disclosed in four reportable segments with a Net-income-to-Total-EBIT reconciliation, and management *enhanced* volume reporting and *added* a sustaining-vs-growth capex split (Q1 FY26) [`FY25 10-K, lines 2153–2171, 2362`; `Q1 FY26 transcript, lines 174–179`]. The contingencies that matter for this name are disclosed with specifics rather than buried: the **Brazil indirect-tax claims** (ICMS $155M, PIS/COFINS $490M at YE2025, with "years examined" back to 1990/2002 and the recognition policy stated — "will recognize them if and when loss is considered probable"), the **divestiture indemnity tail** ($125M recorded vs ~$1.6B maximum potential), and the **off-balance-sheet performance guarantees** ($2,151M) are all in the notes [`FY25 10-K, Note 20, lines 7819–7840`; `Q1 FY26 10-Q, Note 15`; `business-model/12_red-flags-sweep §2`]. **No disclosure got LESS transparent over time** — the partial-data gaps (compensation, ownership, board independence) are proxy-deferred, not withheld. Two real limits remain: (a) the **Q3 2025 segment redefinition** (Viterra-driven) recast prior periods to conform — GAAP-correct and properly disclosed (line 2171), but it breaks pre-2025 segment comparability; and (b) the headline segment/geography presentation does **not surface the escalation rate** of the Brazil exposures (PIS/COFINS +21% in one quarter; farmer-financing book +75% YoY) that the contingency notes reveal on a careful read [`business-model/12_red-flags-sweep §2, §4`]. The substance is disclosed; the emphasis is not.

## 4. Tone in Bad Times

**Cannot be properly assessed — Insufficient Data.** The only transcript in the pool is the Q1 FY2026 call, which followed a **+110% adjusted-EPS beat and a ~20% guidance raise** [`earnings/04_guidance-consensus §6`]. The prepared remarks and CEO letter are **promotional** ("Bunge today is stronger, more agile, and better positioned than at any point in our history," "durable earnings power and the ability to create value in any environment," "premier agribusiness… built for the 21st century") — banned-phrase-style language used without a number attached in the same breath [`Q1 FY26 transcript, lines 224–245`; `FY25 10-K, CEO letter, lines 5–24`]. Against that, the **Q&A was specific and direct even on negatives**: management guided Grain and Tropicals down with concrete causes, flagged "heavily inverted" forward curves and "low" H2 visibility, conceded the Q2 tax-rate and interest-cost headwinds, and acknowledged leverage is "a little elevated with Moody's right now versus where we want to be by the end of the year" [`Q1 FY26 transcript, Q&A, lines 320, 343–391, 658–660`]. So in a good quarter the prepared script is promotional while the Q&A is candid and unevasive — but **how this team communicates in a genuinely bad quarter is untested from the pool**, and that is the single most important candor signal a bad-times transcript would provide.

## 5. Candor Read

**Conclusion: MIXED (leaning candid, but unproven in bad times).** The evidence available points to a team that tells the truth on the concrete items it can be pinned to: the FY2025 MD&A names its own Viterra **dilution** as a driver of the EPS decline, the weak Grain segment was owned in plain language ("rough start… below where we would expect"), guidance was cut on the two segments going backwards, and the high-risk Brazil/indemnity/guarantee exposures are disclosed with specifics in the notes. The strongest evidence the other way is structural, not behavioral: a **management-defined adjusted EPS that runs 5x GAAP** and a **"Viterra integration" cost adjusted out as a one-off for a third straight year** (RF-DISC-002), plus a call that leads with an adjusted "discretionary cash flow" construct that steps around a −$879M consolidated FCF. **The single biggest candor signal is unavailable: there is no bad-times transcript in the pool** — the one call covers a beat-and-raise, so tone-under-stress is untested and the Disclosure-candor score is capped at 65. Net: no deception is visible, the misses that exist were owned, and the non-GAAP is two-sided rather than one-way; but the recurring "one-off" and the absence of a down-quarter to test keep this short of "candid / high-trust."
