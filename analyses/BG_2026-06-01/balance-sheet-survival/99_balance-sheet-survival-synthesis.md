# Balance-Sheet-Survival Module — BG (Synthesis)

## Abstract

Bunge is highly levered on the conservative reading — ~5.8x net debt/EBITDA at YE2025, ~6.1x TTM on GAAP-built EBITDA, up from ~1.3x a year earlier after the July-2025 Viterra deal, against management's 1.6x inventory-adjusted figure. The maturity profile looks short (37% within 12 months) but ~$3.9bn is rolling working-capital debt; the real cluster is 2028 ($2.76bn plus the $3.5bn revolver expiry), refinanceable in most markets but not self-funded (FCF negative). Liquidity runs ~16 months conservatively; the tightest covenant, a 1.60x current ratio, has an undisclosed threshold (headroom Not assessable). The structure survives a 30–60% EBITDA fall without breaching anything — no EBITDA or coverage covenant exists — breaking only on a deep inventory write-down. Verdict: Adequate.

## 1. Solvency Verdict

- **Verdict:** **Adequate** — leverage is elevated (GAAP-built net ~5.8–6.1x) but serviceable; the near-term wall is refinanceable on committed bank capacity in most markets; covenants are balance-sheet only and not earnings-linked, so an earnings shock does not directly trip them — but refinancing dependence, negative FCF, and undisclosed covenant thresholds keep this short of "Solid."
- **Net leverage (net debt / EBITDA):** **5.78x** (YE2025) / **~6.13x** TTM (Q1 2026), on GAAP-built EBITDA (Total EBIT + D&A = $2,236M FY2025; Bunge reports no GAAP EBITDA line). Gross debt/EBITDA = **6.28x**. Management's reported figure is **1.6x** (Q1 2026) — it nets ~70% of ~$11.4bn readily-marketable inventory (RMI) against debt AND uses adjusted EBITDA; it is real but is itself the assumption the stress test must break. [01 §5, §7]
- **Liquidity runway:** **~16 months** on the conservative all-maturities-are-cash-uses basis; **~50 months** on the non-rollable-uses basis. ~$10.5bn usable committed liquidity (cash $839M + $9,665M unused committed revolver), of which only ~8% is in-hand cash. [03 §3, §5]
- **Maturity wall (% within 24 months):** **46.4%** of total debt — but the within-12-month figure (36.8%) is dominated by ~$3,883M of rolling, self-liquidating working-capital debt; the hard-dated term-maturity load over 24 months is ~$2.2bn ($1.4bn 2026, ~$575M of which was repaid April 2026, + $1.4bn 2027). The genuine concentration is **2028 ($2,759M, incl. $1.55bn Viterra floating term loans, alongside the $3.5bn revolver expiry)**. [02 §1–2]
- **Tightest covenant + headroom:** **Minimum current ratio, actual 1.60x** (down from 2.15x pre-Viterra) — the only covenant disclosed-and-falling. Headroom is **Not assessable**: Bunge discloses the covenant's existence and its compliance but NOT the numeric threshold. Against a labeled market-typical 1.0–1.1x floor, indicative headroom is +31–38%. [04 §1–3]
- **Stress break point (EBITDA decline that breaks it):** **No EBITDA decline in the 0–60% range breaks the structure** — there is no EBITDA or interest-coverage covenant to trip, and committed liquidity covers the 12-month cash need by ~$8bn even at −60%. The first analytically meaningful deterioration is rating/coverage optics (EBITDA/interest falls to ~2.1x at −40%, ~1.4x at −60%; net leverage to 10–15x). The true break is a **balance-sheet event — a ~89% RMI write-down (or ~38% drawdown of the current book) plus a short-term-debt build pushing the current ratio toward ~1.0x** — which is the same event as RMI failing to monetize at carrying value. [06 §2–4]
- **Solvency strength /100:** **55** (no cap binding; elevated GAAP leverage and negative FCF offset by no earnings-linked covenant, deep committed liquidity, and IG access)
- **Liquidity runway /100:** **66**
- **Refinancing risk /100 (higher = WORSE, inverted):** **45** (committed revolver backstops a 12-month market closure, but ~56% floating, negative FCF, and the 2028 cluster keep it mid-band)
- **Covenant headroom /100:** **Not assessable** (numeric thresholds undisclosed)
- **Downside resilience /100:** **64** (survives −30/−40/−60% without external action; tempered by 25–38% cash conversion of the EBITDA the coverage rests on)
- **Data quality /100:** **78** (full debt note, year-by-year maturity table, 3-yr + Q1 cash flows; gaps: no rating-agency report, no numeric covenant thresholds)
- **Overall usefulness /100:** **74** (capped at 75 — covenant thresholds undisclosed)
- **Biggest solvency risk (one line):** A deep RMI/inventory write-down that simultaneously detonates the 1.6x adjusted-leverage story, cuts the current ratio toward its (undisclosed) floor, and stops the ~$3.9bn working-capital book from rolling — the one event that links leverage, covenant, and liquidity into a single failure.

## 3. Reconciliation

- **Leverage — 1.6x vs ~5.8x (the defining disagreement, but between management and the conservative lens, not between specialists).** Specialists `01` and `06` agree: the conservative GAAP-built net leverage (~5.8x YE2025 / ~6.1x TTM) is the survival anchor; management's 1.6x is real but stacks two adjustments (a ~70% RMI debt offset on ~$11.4bn of inventory + adjusted EBITDA) and is itself an assumption the stress test must break. **Reconciled (conservative) view: anchor on ~5.8–6.1x; carry 1.6x only as the management view, contingent on RMI monetizing at carrying value.** Not averaged.
- **Maturity figure timing.** `03` ran before `02` was available and pulled the 2026 maturity ($5,261M = ST debt $3,883M + 2026 LTD $1,378M) directly from the FY25 10-K Contractual Obligations table; `02` later built the full schedule from the same table and ties to the same $5,261M. **No conflict — same primary source, confirmed.**
- **Treatment of the ~$3,883M short-term block.** `03` (conservatively) treats the full $5,261M within-12m figure as a cash use; `02` decomposes it and shows ~$3,883M is rolling working-capital debt that self-liquidates. **Reconciled: both readings are carried — the conservative all-cash-use basis drives the ~16-month runway, the decomposed basis drives the "refinanceable" maturity read; the difference is explicitly a rollover-access question, not a numbers dispute.**
- No other material disagreements between specialists.

## 5. Survival Summary

Bunge is levered ~5.8x net (GAAP-built, YE2025) rising to ~6.1x TTM, and the trend is sharply worse year-on-year — Viterra took net debt from $2.9bn to $12.9bn in 2025 and FCF turned negative (−$879M FY2025, −$1,161M TTM), so debt was not paid down; on management's RMI-adjusted measure the same period reads 1.9x falling to 1.6x, the opposite direction. The near-term wall is refinancing-dependent, not self-funded: cash ($839M) plus negative FCF cannot retire the ~$2.2bn of hard 2026–2027 term maturities, so the company leans on $9,065M of committed undrawn revolver capacity and demonstrated investment-grade bond/CP access (four issuances in ~18 months). The liquidity runway is ~16 months on the conservative basis (~50 on the non-rollable basis), and the tightest covenant — a 1.60x current ratio, down from 2.15x — has no disclosed threshold, so its true headroom is Not assessable. The stress break point is unusual: because no covenant keys off EBITDA or coverage, no EBITDA decline in the 0–60% range breaks the structure, and a normal recession (−30/−40%) is survivable without an equity raise, asset sale, or waiver; the structure breaks only on a balance-sheet tail — a deep RMI write-down plus a short-term-debt build forcing the current ratio toward ~1.0x.

## 8. Simple Summary

- Debt is big and went up fast: ~$14.6bn gross / ~$13.7bn net at Q1 2026; net leverage ~5.8–6.1x on the conservative GAAP basis (vs 1.3x a year ago), or 1.6x on management's inventory-adjusted measure — the gap is the whole debate.
- The maturity wall looks scary (37% within a year) but most of it is working-capital debt that rolls; the real squeeze year is **2028** ($2.76bn of term loans plus the $3.5bn revolver expiring together).
- Liquidity runway is ~16 months on the cautious view (~50 on the realistic view) — but it is almost all undrawn committed revolver, not cash, and FCF is currently negative, so the company leans on rolling its debt, not on generating cash.
- The tightest covenant is a 1.60x current ratio, but Bunge does not disclose the actual limit, so how close it is to breaking **cannot be measured** — call it Not assessable.
- Biggest off-balance-sheet item to watch is ~$668M of unprovisioned Brazilian tax claims (growing); pension is a non-issue.
- It survives a 30–60% earnings drop without breaching anything or needing a rescue, because no covenant is tied to earnings — it only breaks on a deep inventory write-down that forces the current ratio down.
- Ratings are investment grade (A-/Baa1/BBB+, affirmed post-Viterra) but **no agency report and no numeric covenant thresholds are in the data** — the two key gaps.
- Useful for the master synthesizer: yes (74/100, capped at 75 by the undisclosed covenant thresholds) — it gives a clear survival floor and a clean bear-case break point.
