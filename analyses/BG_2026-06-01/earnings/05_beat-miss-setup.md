The two are tracked separately, as upstream `01` and `06` establish that GAAP single-period EPS is uninformative for BG (Q1 2026 GAAP $0.35 vs adjusted $1.83, a 5.2x gap, almost entirely mark-to-market timing). All beat/miss analysis below is against the **adjusted** series, because that is the metric management guides and the Street tracks.*

*All four required upstream outputs (01 historical-financials, 02 revenue-drivers, 03 margin-drivers, 04 guidance-consensus) were read, plus sibling 06 earnings-quality. Consensus data is present (Capital IQ exports, data as of 2026-05-09), so the partial-data cap to "Unclear" does NOT apply.*

## 1. Next Quarter Context

The next print is **Q2 2026** (quarter ended Jun-30-2026). Q2 2025 was still pre-Viterra, so the YoY revenue comparison remains acquisition-inflated and not like-for-like (Viterra first laps in Q3 2026) [01 §2; 02 §3]. There is **no clean revenue seasonality** for BG — the only pre-acquisition year (FY2024) showed near-flat quarterly revenue (24.3%–25.5% each) and the FY2025 Q3/Q4 skew is a Viterra artifact, not a season [01 §5]. The consensus bar for Q2 2026 is **adjusted EPS $1.93** (up from $1.61 three months ago, +20%), revenue **$22,757M**, and EBITDA **$896M**; the full-year bar is adjusted EPS **$9.43** mean against management's **$9.00–$9.50** guide (midpoint $9.25), with the Street now sitting just above the midpoint and inside the range [04 §3, §4]. The bar was assessed **"fair"** by `04`.

## 5. In-Line Print But Bad Guidance Risk

This is the central risk: the Q2 quarter can print fine while the forward setup disappoints, because the Street has already arbitraged the guidance gap away and the back half is where the doubt sits.

- **In-line current quarter but guide-down (or no raise):** H2 visibility "low"; FQ3/FQ4 2026 adjusted-EPS breadth net **negative**; forward curves "heavily inverted" [04 §5, §7; 03 §8]. Since consensus ($9.43) already sits above the $9.25 midpoint, "no raise" reads as a soft miss. The beat thesis requires a *guide raise*, not just a quarter beat.
- **Beat current quarter but weak H2 margin guide:** the upgrade is margin/biofuel-led not volume-led; Grain and Tropical Oils guided **lower**; biofuel oil-leg draw-down is back-half-weighted [04 §4, §5; 03 §5]. A soft H2 spread signal marks down the high-margin engine (~80% of EBIT net of corporate drag).
- **Beat EPS due to one-offs/tax, miss quality:** Q1 leaned on a tax benefit (~18% rate) that won't repeat; GAAP dominated by MtM; earnings quality **52/100** [06 §8, §9]. Check **adjusted segment EBIT**, not headline EPS.
- **Beat revenue but working capital deteriorates:** CFO/EBITDA 38% (FY25)/25% (TTM); FCF negative; net debt $13.7bn, ND/EBITDA ~5.8x [01; 06]. A further cash-conversion miss at ~5.8x leverage is a quality flag.

## 6. Seasonality Read

Seasonality does **not** cleanly help or hurt Q2 revenue: BG has no usable revenue seasonality (FY2024 near-flat quarterly shares; FY2025 Q3/Q4 skew is the Viterra consolidation, not a cycle) [01 §5]. The one real seasonal pattern is at the **cash** level: Q1 is consistently an operating-cash-outflow quarter (CFO −$285M Q1'25, −$541M Q1'26), so Q2 cash flow should be read against that working-capital cadence. Net: seasonality is neutral-to-irrelevant for the Q2 earnings line, a known headwind only for cash.

## 7. Historical Pattern

BG has a **systematic adjusted-EPS beat pattern**: beat in all 5 recent quarters (+38.2%, +21.3%, +15.2%, +9.3%, +110.3%); annual surprise modest-positive (FY23 +6.9%, FY24 −1.7%, FY25 +3.1%) [04 §6]. The mirror: **revenue missed in 3 of the last 5 quarters** — the signature of a commodity processor. Weight the adjusted-EPS beat as a genuine repeated tendency but discount the magnitude (the +110% Q1'26 surprise was off a depressed $0.87 estimate now reset to $1.93). A revenue "miss" is near-baseline noise, not a thesis event.

## 8. Setup Verdict

**Setup favors beat — narrowly, and only on the quarter, not on the guide.**

The most important factor is BG's repeated adjusted-EPS beat cadence (5 of 5) colliding with one-sided positive near-term revision breadth (FQ2: 6 up / 0 down) and a confirmed external tailwind (record EPA RVO; Midwest crush margins at a 2.5-yr high) — all pointing at the soy/softseed crush spread holding through Q2. The biggest risk that could flip it is **the guide, not the quarter**: the Street ($9.43) has moved above the $9.25 midpoint and back-half breadth is net negative, so a fine Q2 paired with an unchanged guide and cautious H2 commentary would read as a soft miss. Balanced-to-negative on the forward guide.

## 9. Second-Quarter Look-Ahead

**Q3 2026** looks structurally harder: it is where **Viterra first laps** (consolidated from Q3 2025), removing the ~doubling YoY revenue tailwind and exposing organic trends for the first time; and it is where management's back-half thesis (biofuel-driven soy-oil draw-down framed "Q3 and especially Q4") must start delivering, yet FQ3 breadth is net negative and H2 visibility "low." A tougher comp and the burden of proving the H2 ramp, with thinner Street support.

## 10. Pre-Mortem

If this setup fails, the most likely reason is that we **over-weighted the five-quarter beat streak and the favorable RVO headline, and under-weighted that the Street had already arbitraged the guidance gap away** — so a genuine Q2 EPS beat met a flat/negative reaction because the FY guide was not raised and H2 spread commentary turned cautious. The second most likely failure is **mis-reading the metric**: treating a headline adjusted-EPS beat as the result when it was driven by below-the-line tax/interest timing or MtM, while adjusted *segment* EBIT and cash conversion disappointed — the recurring trap in a 52/100 earnings-quality name.
