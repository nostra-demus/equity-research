## 2. Aggregate Quality Score

**Aggregate business quality: 28 / 100 (Weak).**

This is not a strict average of the rows (a simple mean would be ~30). The aggregate is dominated by the three factors that define what this business *is*: commodity dependence (12), competitive intensity (18) and cyclicality (18). Those three travel together — Bunge earns an exchange-priced spread that it cannot set, against the other global majors, and the result swings violently with crops, FX and biofuel policy. Pricing power (18) and margin stability (22) confirm the same read from a different angle. Disclosure quality (66) is the one genuinely above-average factor, but disclosure describes the business; it does not improve it, so it is weighted as secondary and is not allowed to drag the aggregate into the mixed band. The result lands in the upper part of the Weak band: a well-run, well-disclosed business whose underlying economics are structurally low-quality.

## 4. Read

Bunge is a cyclical, commodity-spread agribusiness — not a durable compounder. Its profit is the crush and merchandising margin between exchange-priced inputs and exchange-priced outputs, earned against the other global majors (ADM, Cargill, Louis Dreyfus, Wilmar, COFCO), and it swings hard with crops, FX, mark-to-market and biofuel policy: diluted EPS ran $1.48 → $2.61 → $0.84 → $0.49 across the four quarters of FY2025, and net income nearly halved over two years [FY25 10-K, Note 27, line 8585; line 4706]. Disclosure is clean and granular, which is the one quality strength, but it does not change the economics. The single factor a buyer should watch over the next 24 months is the crush/refining spread and the biofuel policy that underpins oil demand — management itself flagged an inverted crush curve and policy dependence (Brazil, Indonesia, EU, US) on the Q1 2026 call [q1_call.txt, lines 456, 506] — because that spread, not any customer or geography, is what the entire earnings stream rides on.
