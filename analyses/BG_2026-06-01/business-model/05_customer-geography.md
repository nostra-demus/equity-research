# Customer And Geography Map — BG

Company: Bunge Global SA (NYSE: BG). Primary source: FY2025 Annual Report / Form 10-K (year ended December 31, 2025) in the data pool. Figures are FY2025 unless stated. FY2025 total Net sales = $70,329M [FY25 10-K, Note 26, p.F-79].

Note on the Viterra acquisition: Bunge closed the Viterra acquisition during 2025; Viterra contributed $15.3B of Net sales in FY2025 [FY25 10-K, Note 4 / Business Combinations]. This materially changed the revenue base and the geographic booking mix versus FY2024–FY2023, so trend reads below are flagged where the acquisition drives the move.

Contract structure context: Bunge's revenue is split between commodity contracts under ASC 815 ($53,385M, ~76% of FY25 Net sales) and contracts with customers under ASC 606 ($16,944M, ~24%) [FY25 10-K, Note 26 disaggregation, p.F-80]. ASC 606 customer contracts generally settle within "30-60 days" [FY25 10-K, revenue policy, ~p.F-? / Note 1]. This points to short-cycle, transactional sales rather than multi-year locked contracts; no long-term customer contract is disclosed as material.

## 2. Geography Map

Authoritative source is Note 26 geographic disclosure. Critical caveat: revenue geography is "determined based on the location of the subsidiary making the sale" [FY25 10-K, Note 26, p.F-79] — i.e., trading-desk booking location, NOT end-customer location. For a global commodity merchant, Switzerland is a trading/marketing hub, so its 22% share overstates Swiss end-demand and understates the dispersion of ultimate customers. End-customer geography is not disclosed.

Physical-footprint cross-check (where assets, and therefore likely real activity, sit): Long-lived assets (PP&E, net) FY2025 — United States $3,888M (33%), Canada $1,869M (16%), Argentina $1,414M (12%), Brazil $1,328M (11%), Rest of world $3,179M (27%); total $11,678M [FY25 10-K, Note 26, p.F-79]. Headcount is also Americas-heavy: South America 43%, EMEA 24%, North America 20%, Asia-Pacific 13% [FY25 10-K, Human Capital, p.~10] (headcount, not revenue — shown only as a footprint cross-check). The asset/headcount view shows the real operating base is the Americas, while the revenue-by-selling-subsidiary view is distorted toward trading hubs (Switzerland, Netherlands).

## 4. Read

This is not a concentrated business on either the customer or the geography axis. On the customer side, Bunge sells commodities and refined products to broad, fragmented buyer categories (feed/livestock, biofuel, food processors, millers); the 10-K names no single customer and discloses no single-customer percentage, and related-party sales are ≤2% of revenue — so customer concentration is not proven and is best treated as low. On the geography side, the largest reported region is 22% (United States and Switzerland tied), with no country above 50%; the Swiss and Dutch revenue shares are a trading-desk booking artifact ("location of the subsidiary making the sale"), not end-demand, while the real operating footprint (~73% of long-lived assets) sits in the Americas. The single biggest dependency the synthesizer should know about is not a customer at all: it is the Americas-centric physical and supply base — Brazilian/Argentine/US/Canadian origination and crush assets plus Brazil farmer financing — which means crop, weather, FX, and agricultural-policy shocks in the Americas, not loss of any one customer, are the concentration that matters.
