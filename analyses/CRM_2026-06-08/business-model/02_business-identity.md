# Business Identity — CRM

*Salesforce, Inc. (NYSE: CRM). US/SEC filer, US GAAP, reporting in USD, fiscal year ends January 31. "FY2026" ended Jan-31-2026; "Q1 FY2027" ended Apr-30-2026 [Triage 00, Filing Regime]. All figures USD.*

## 1. What The Company Actually Does

Salesforce sells cloud-based business software that companies rent by subscription rather than buy and install [FY2026 10-K, Item 1 "Overview" p.4; Item 7 MD&A "Sources of Revenues"]. The core product is customer relationship management (CRM) software — tools that hold a company's records of its customers and prospects and run the work around them: sales pipelines, customer-service cases, marketing campaigns, online commerce, plus add-ons for joining up data (Data 360, Informatica, MuleSoft), team messaging (Slack), and analytics [FY2026 10-K, Item 1 "Our Service Offerings" p.4–7]. The buyers are businesses of essentially every size and industry worldwide — "the number of paying subscriptions at each of our customers ranges from one to hundreds of thousands" [FY2026 10-K, Item 1 "Customers" p.~9]. Customers pay to put all their customer information in one place that many teams can use, so they can sell, serve, and market without each department keeping its own disconnected records. Salesforce reaches these customers mostly through its own sales force, and also through partners, and lets outside developers build extra apps on top of its platform [FY2026 10-K, Item 1 "Overview" p.4]. Most of the latest selling is wrapped around "Agentforce" — software that lets a customer build automated AI helpers ("agents") that act inside the same Salesforce apps [FY2026 10-K, Item 1 "Agentforce" p.5–6]. A smaller part of the company is paid consulting and training that helps customers set the software up [FY2026 10-K, Item 1 "Customer Service and Support" p.~9]. The work is sold globally, with the United States the largest market and Europe and Asia Pacific next [FY2026 Annual financials, Segments tab — Geographic].

## 2. How The Company Makes Money

Salesforce earns money in two ways. The recurring software subscription is ~95% of revenue; the rest is one-off services [FY2026 10-K, Item 7 "Sources of Revenues" — "Subscription and support revenues accounted for approximately 95 percent of our total revenues for fiscal 2026"].

- **Subscription & support (~$39.4bn of $41.5bn total in FY2026):**
  `Subscription revenue ≈ number of paying subscriptions × price per subscription × contract term, recognized evenly (ratably) over the term`
  Customers sign contracts (commonly multi-year), are billed in advance — usually once a year — and Salesforce books the revenue in equal slices across the life of the contract rather than all at once [FY2026 10-K, Item 7 "Sources of Revenues" and "Seasonal Nature of Unearned Revenue" p.~38; FY2026 10-K, Note "Revenue Recognition"]. Money billed but not yet earned sits as unearned (deferred) revenue, and all future contracted revenue not yet booked is the "remaining performance obligation" — a backlog of revenue under contract [FY2026 10-K, Item 7 "Remaining Performance Obligation"]. A small slice (~6% of subscription revenue) is term software licences booked at a point in time, not spread out [FY2026 10-K, Item 7 — "Revenues from term software licenses... represented approximately six percent of total subscription and support revenues for fiscal 2026"].
- **Professional services & other (~$2.1bn, ~5% of total; down 4% in FY2026):**
  `Services revenue ≈ hours of consulting/setup work × rate, or fixed project fees`
  Paid help to install, integrate, and train staff on the software; contracts are time-and-materials or fixed-price [FY2026 10-K, Item 7 revenue table; FY2026 10-K, Note "Professional Services and Other Revenues"]. This line is a low-margin enabler of the subscription, not a profit engine.

What drives each lever: **volume** is the main growth driver — new customers plus existing customers buying more seats and more products ("land and expand"); management states plainly that "Pricing was not a significant driver of the increase in revenues for the period," with growth "primarily caused by volume-driven increases from new business" [FY2026 10-K, Item 7 "Subscription and support revenues" commentary]. **Price/mix** moves with which products and tiers customers buy and how much new AI usage (Agentforce, Data 360) they add — Agentforce passed >$1 billion of annual recurring revenue, with most Agentforce and Data 360 bookings coming from existing customers expanding [Q1 FY2027 earnings call, May-27-2026, prepared remarks]. **Retention** sets the floor: trailing-12-month attrition (the annualised value of contracts lost or cut) was ~8% as of Jan-31-2026, so roughly 92% of contract value renews [FY2026 10-K, Item 7 "Subscription and support revenues" — attrition ~8%]. **Margin** is driven by gross margin on subscriptions (~78% group gross margin: $32,255M gross profit on $41,525M revenue in FY2026) minus heavy sales-and-marketing and R&D spend [FY2026 Annual financials, Income Statement tab].

## 3. Business Type Classification

Recurring-subscription enterprise software vendor — a broad CRM-and-data platform sold to businesses, billed in advance and recognised over multi-year contracts, growing mainly by selling more to existing customers.

## 4. What Drives Variance

When revenue moves, the most likely cause is **volume** — new customers and existing customers adding seats, products, and AI usage — not list-price changes, which management says were not a meaningful FY2026 driver [FY2026 10-K, Item 7 "Subscription and support revenues" commentary]. Because revenue is recognised evenly over contract terms, reported revenue is smoothed and lags demand; sharp swings show up faster in bookings, unearned revenue, and remaining performance obligation than in the revenue line, and the fourth quarter (ending January 31) is the heaviest for new business and renewals [FY2026 10-K, Item 7 "Seasonal Nature of Unearned Revenue"]. The professional-services line is the more volatile piece and fell in FY2026 on "less demand for larger, multi-year transformation engagements" [FY2026 10-K, Item 7 "professional services and other revenues" commentary]. Foreign exchange matters because Europe and Asia Pacific together are a meaningful minority of revenue, and unbilled foreign-currency backlog is revalued each period at period-end rates [FY2026 10-K, Item 7 "Remaining Performance Obligation"; FY2026 Annual financials, Segments tab — Geographic].
