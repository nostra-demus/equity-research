# Ownership & Insider Behavior — CRM

Salesforce, Inc. (NYSE: CRM). US filer (SEC). Reporting currency USD. Fiscal year ends January 31. Sector: enterprise software (IT). This report maps who owns the stock, whether insiders bought or were granted it, whether they are buying or selling, whether shares are pledged, and who controls the votes. It does not value the company or rate the stock.

**Upstream status.** The in-module upstream `01_management-and-track-record.md` is not present in the output folder — proceeding on this module's own read of the data pool. Cross-module `business-model/01_disqualifier-scan.md` and `business-model/11_capital-allocation-governance.md` are also not present; only `business-model/00_data-triage.md` exists. The triage notes the Informatica acquisition (Nov 2025, ~$6.0bn borrowed) — relevant to the serial-acquirer question, which is `02`'s scope, not this agent's. No pledging or related-party disqualifier was routed to this agent.

**Data basis.** Ownership percentages and the controlling-owner read come from the audited proxy: `FY26 DEF 14A (filed Apr-16-2026), Security Ownership of Certain Beneficial Owners and Management` (director/NEO data as of Mar-25-2026; 5% holders per their latest 13G/13G-A). Insider-transaction behavior comes from the Capital IQ Public Ownership "Insider Trading" workbook tab (Form 4 / Form 144 records, last 12 months, data as of the May-29-2026 export). The institutional holder trend comes from the Capital IQ Public Ownership "History" workbook tab (quarter-end positions Jun-30-2025 to Mar-31-2026). Ownership (a static %) and insider transactions (the behavior signal) are reported separately, as required.

**Net insider activity: net selling by dollar value, but the read is benign.** In gross dollars, sales (led by Benioff's near-daily 10b5-1 program and Harris's December block) far exceed purchases. Three points keep this from being a red flag:

1. **The big seller's stake did not shrink.** Benioff's reported holding went 21,969,457 (Jul-2025) to 22,018,571 to 22,041,486 (Apr-2026) — essentially flat, because grants and a 93,109-share acquisition offset the open-market and to-issuer sales. He is monetizing a slice of a very large position on a schedule, not exiting. His 2.8% stake is intact.
2. **The discretionary sells are pre-set 10b5-1 plans, not opportunistic dumps.** Benioff's fixed 2,250-share/day cadence at a fixed $161.50 strike is the hallmark of an automatic plan; Harris's and Tallapragada's plans are explicitly disclosed under Item 408 of the FY26 10-K. Pre-arranged, scheduled selling carries far less signal than discretionary selling.
3. **The only open-market *buying* is a positive tell.** Two independent directors (Kirk twice, Alber once) and the activist ValueAct put fresh cash in — Kirk and Alber bought at ~$258 in December and again near ~$194 in March, i.e. adding as the price fell. ValueAct added 96,000 shares at $260.58. These are the genuine conviction signals in the table.

Open-market buys are distinguished from option exercises and 10b5-1 sales above: the only **discretionary open-market purchases** are ValueAct, Kirk, and Alber. Everything in the Benioff/Harris/Tallapragada columns is either a pre-set plan sale, a sell-to-cover on vesting, a buyback participation, or a grant.

**Unaligned-owner test (CLAUDE.md Sec 24, Filter 6).** Salesforce has no controlling owner. Votes are widely dispersed: the three largest holders are passive index managers (Vanguard, BlackRock, State Street, roughly 25% combined), and the founder-CEO controls only 2.8% of votes on a strict one-share-one-vote basis. None of the three structures that create an inherent conflict with minority holders applies — there is no government control, no parent that maximizes value in a separate listed vehicle, and no unrelated-diversified conglomerate sprawl. **RF-OWN-004 does NOT trigger.** There is no shareholder-friendliness cap from Filter 6 and no value-trap note to hand to valuation on ownership grounds. The control structure is, if anything, a positive for minorities: one-share-one-vote, no super-voting founder shares, pledging and hedging banned, and an activist (ValueAct) on the board whose interests align with per-share value. The governance counterweight to note for other agents is that low insider control (2.8%) means founder alignment rests on the dollar size of the stake, not voting control — and the company is an active acquirer (Informatica, Nov 2025), which is a capital-allocation question for `02`, not a control-structure defect here.

## 5. Ownership / Insider Read

Skin in the game is real in dollars but thin in control: the founder-CEO owns 2.8% (~$4.1bn) and all insiders together 3.5%, on a clean one-share-one-vote structure with no controlling owner, no dual-class, and pledging/hedging affirmatively banned — so minority holders face no founder-entrenchment or pledge risk, and the Sec 24 unaligned-owner filter does not trip. The net insider signal is selling by dollar value, but it is benign: Benioff's sales run through a fixed-cadence Rule 10b5-1 plan while his percentage stake stays flat (grants offset sales), Harris's and Tallapragada's sales are disclosed 10b5-1 plans, and the only discretionary open-market *buying* — two independent directors (Kirk, Alber) and activist ValueAct adding into a falling price — is a modest positive. The biggest thing to hand downstream is not a control or pledging risk (there is none) but the structural fact that founder alignment here rests on the dollar size of a 2.8% stake rather than on voting control, in a company that is actively acquiring (Informatica) — making capital allocation, not ownership, the place where alignment will actually be tested (`02`'s scope).
