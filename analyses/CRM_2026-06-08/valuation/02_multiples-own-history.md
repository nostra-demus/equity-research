# Multiples — Own History — CRM

**Company:** Salesforce, Inc. (NYSE:CRM) · **Reporting standard:** US GAAP · **Currency:** USD (millions unless per-share) · **Fiscal year ends Jan 31** · **As-of:** 2026-06-08
**Business type:** Operating (subscription / enterprise software). Per the Business-Type Method Map (MODULE_RULES.md), the lead multiples are **EV-based** (TEV/Revenue, TEV/EBITDA, TEV/EBIT) plus **P/E** and **FCF yield**. **P/Book and P/Tangible-Book are dropped as value metrics** — `01` shows tangible book is negative (−$38.71/share) and the comps carry "P/TangBV — NM"; book-based methods are not informative for a software roll-up. P/BV is shown in the band table for completeness only, with the distortion flagged.

**Anchor numbers (from `01_price-and-capital-structure.md`, used verbatim):** price **$185.66** (2026-06-08, last close, Capital IQ comps "Day Close Price Latest"); shares **819 M** (Apr-30-2026); market cap **~$152,056 M**; net debt **$30,711 M** (total debt $42,548 M − cash & ST investments $11,837 M); **EV ~$182,767 M**. Alternate pool price $176.17 (Capital IQ Estimates snapshot, 5.4% lower) is noted where it matters — see the band-source caveat in §2.

**One-line headline:** On every earnings-, revenue-, and cash-flow-based multiple, Salesforce trades at the **bottom of its own 5-year range** — at or below the 10th percentile and **35–55% below its own 5-year mean** — but the multiple **de-rated for reasons the business itself changed** (revenue growth fell from ~25% to ~9–10%, and the balance sheet flipped from net cash to $30.7 billion of net debt), so a full reversion to the old mean is not the base case.

## 3. Re-Rating / De-Rating Read

Salesforce has **de-rated hard against its own history** — this is a genuine compression, not a denominator illusion, because the forward and revenue/EBITDA multiples (which are immune to the FY23 earnings trough) show it too: **EV/NTM Revenue 3.88× is a fresh 5-year low** (below the prior 3.91× floor), **NTM EV/EBITDA 10.6× sits ~45% below its own 5-year mean** of 19.3× and ~36% below even the post-trough 3-year mean, and **NTM GAAP P/E 13.3× is the lowest in the series**, ~59% under the 5-year mean of 32.5×. The de-rating is steady and one-directional across the last six quarters, not a single-print dislocation `[Capital IQ Financials_quarterly, Multiples tab]`. The most likely cause is **fundamental, not sentiment-only**: (1) revenue growth fell from the ~25% FY22 era to ~9–10% now and the Street is being asked to underwrite a second-half-FY27 reacceleration that bookings/cRPO have not yet confirmed (cRPO printed "in line" with guidance two quarters running, no upside) `[earnings/04, §6; Q1 FY27 transcript]`; and (2) the balance sheet flipped from ~$2.0 billion net cash to **$30.7 billion net debt** in ~12 months after the ~$8 billion Informatica deal and ~$25 billion of new notes `[01_price-and-capital-structure, §5]` — a higher-debt, slower-growth software compounder rationally carries a lower multiple than the net-cash, 20%+-growth version the old mean was built on.

**Implied value as a RANGE (own-history reversion, primary 3-year anchor, reliable metrics — EV/Sales, EV/EBITDA, EV/NTM-EBITDA, P/NTM-EPS):** **~$302 to ~$356 per share**, roughly **+63% to +92%** above the current $185.66. **Reversion assumption — stated explicitly (CLAUDE.md §16; MODULE_RULES Core Principle 3).** This table assumes the **warranted multiple has not structurally changed** and that the metric (revenue / EBITDA / EPS) is held at its current level. The business-model and earnings evidence argues the warranted multiple **has** moved down somewhat: growth has roughly halved versus the period that built the old mean, and the company now carries $30.7 billion of net debt instead of net cash. So the lower end of the range (the 3-year-window, revenue/EBITDA-based ~$302–$327) is the more defensible reversion read; the upper end (NTM-P/E and full-history multiples) should be discounted unless the second-half-FY27 revenue reacceleration management is guiding actually shows up in bookings/cRPO. The cross-check: a structurally slower-growth software name might warrant a multiple closer to mature-software peers — Adobe trades at NTM EV/EBITDA ~8.1× and NTM P/E ~10.4×, SAP at ~14.0× / ~21.5× `[Capital IQ comps, Trading-Multiples, As-Of 2026-06-08]` — which brackets CRM's current ~10.6× / ~13.3× and suggests today's level is not an obvious dislocation versus mature peers, even though it is a deep discount to CRM's own faster-growing past. (Peer adjudication is `03`'s job; shown here only to test whether the own-mean reversion is warranted.)

## 5. Own-History Read

Salesforce trades at the **bottom decile of its own 5-year multiple range on every earnings-, revenue-, and cash-flow-based metric** — EV/NTM Revenue (3.88×) and NTM GAAP P/E (13.3×) are fresh 5-year lows, and NTM EV/EBITDA (10.6×) sits ~45% below its own 5-year mean and ~36% below even the post-trough 3-year mean `[Capital IQ Financials_quarterly, Multiples tab]`. A reversion toward its own recent (3-year) mean implies **~$302–$356 per share (+63% to +92%)** on the reliable metrics; reverting to the full-5-year mean implies more (~$450+), but that anchor predates both the growth slowdown and the leveraging. **The single biggest caveat: the multiple did not de-rate on noise — the company itself changed.** Revenue growth fell from ~25% to ~9–10% and the balance sheet swung from ~$2 billion net cash to ~$30.7 billion net debt in a year, so part of the compression is a lower *warranted* multiple, not a mispricing — underwriting a full snap-back to the old mean is **not** the base case, and the defensible reversion is the lower, revenue/EBITDA-anchored end of the range, conditional on the guided second-half-FY27 revenue reacceleration actually appearing in bookings/cRPO (which it has not yet). No structurally misaligned controlling owner is present (CRM is a widely-held operating company, no government or parent control), so the §24 Filter-6 structural-value-trap overlay does not apply here — but the warranted-multiple-has-moved caveat is the real constraint on reading this purely as "trades cheap to its own history."
