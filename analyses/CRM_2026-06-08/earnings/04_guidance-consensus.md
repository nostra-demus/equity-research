# Guidance & Consensus — CRM

Company: Salesforce, Inc. (NYSE:CRM). Reporting standard: US GAAP. Currency: USD (all figures USD millions unless per-share). Fiscal year ends January 31 — "FY27" runs Feb-01-2026 to Jan-31-2027. Estimates are presented on the Capital IQ basis (non-GAAP EPS = "EPS Normalized"; GAAP EPS shown separately). The market trades CRM primarily on **non-GAAP EPS, revenue, and current remaining performance obligation (cRPO)** — cRPO is contracted subscription revenue due in the next 12 months, the best near-term demand signal for a subscription-software (SaaS) business.

Plain-English note on the metrics used here:
- **cRPO** — money already under contract that converts to revenue within ~12 months; a leading indicator of next-year revenue.
- **Non-GAAP operating margin** — operating profit as a percent of revenue, excluding stock compensation, amortization of acquired intangibles, and restructuring. It is management's headline profitability metric.
- **FCF (free cash flow)** — operating cash flow minus capital spending; the cash the business actually generates.

Pattern, stated bluntly: CRM has **beaten non-GAAP EPS every quarter** in this window, and the beat magnitude has **widened sharply** (from ~1% to ~24%). But the **revenue beats are thin** (mostly +0.1% to +1.0%, with one tiny miss). The large EPS beats are a margin-plus-buyback story, not a demand surprise — revenue lands right on guidance. On the May call an analyst pushed exactly this point: cRPO has come in "spot in line with guidance" for two straight quarters with no upside, and bookings momentum looks like it is lagging the headline. So the EPS beat habit is partly mechanical (share-count reduction) and may not signal genuine demand upside.

## 7. Bar Assessment

**Bar is fair.**

Consensus is anchored almost exactly to management's guidance — FY27 revenue, non-GAAP operating income, and non-GAAP EPS all sit within ~0.5% of the guided midpoints, and Q2 is in-line to the dollar [Sections 3]. That means there is **no consensus cushion below the guide to absorb a stumble**: the Street is carrying management's own numbers, and after the Q1 beat the headline FY27 non-GAAP EPS bar was just raised ~7% (40 analysts up, 0 down), so the easy, mechanical part of the beat — the ~10% share-count reduction from the $50bn buyback/ASR — is now baked into estimates rather than available as future upside [Section 4–5]. The binding swing factor from here is the top line, not EPS: revenue beats have run thin (~0.7% in Q1 FY27) and cRPO has printed "in line" for two straight quarters with no upside, while management is asking the Street to underwrite a second-half FY27 organic-revenue reacceleration that the bookings data has not yet confirmed [Section 6; Q1 FY27 transcript]. With the demand bar essentially equal to guidance and the per-share upside lever already spent, the setup is neither a soft lay-up nor a clearly over-set bar — it is fair, and it tilts to "harder than it looks on EPS" because the next EPS beat must come from operations and revenue rather than from buyback math.
