# Historical Financials — BG (Bunge Global SA)

Reporting currency: USD millions, except per-share figures. Fiscal year ends December 31. Bunge does not formally disclose company-defined "adjusted EBITDA/EBIT/EPS" in the 10-K; however, on the Q1 2026 earnings call management discussed adjusted EPS and adjusted segment EBIT — those are reported in Section 4 with the company's own labeling. Capital IQ "Normalized" figures (which strip out items they classify as exceptional) are labeled as such where used. Viterra Limited was acquired on July 2, 2025; results from H2 2025 onward include Viterra and prior-year comparatives have NOT been recast for the consolidated income statement — this is the single largest distortion in all year-over-year and quarter-over-quarter comparisons below.

Material seasonality flag: FY2023 and FY2024 show essentially FLAT seasonality — every quarter is in the 23.9%–25.7% range, well within 24%–26%. FY2025 is distorted by the Viterra acquisition closing July 2, 2025: H2 2025 includes Viterra revenue, H1 does not. This is an acquisition timing effect, not a structural seasonal pattern. On a pre-Viterra basis, Bunge's business has **no material seasonality** — agricultural processing volumes are smoothed across multi-region Northern/Southern hemisphere operations. Going forward (FY2026+) with a full year of Viterra, the company has not yet disclosed expected steady-state seasonality.

No quarter consistently breaches the >30% or <20% thresholds on a like-for-like basis.

## 6. Key Trend Summary

- **Revenue trend: Volatile.** Top-line swung from $59.2B (FY21) up 13.7% to $67.2B (FY22), then down two consecutive years to $59.5B (FY23, -11.4%) and $53.1B (FY24, -10.8%), then up 32.4% to $70.3B (FY25) almost entirely due to Viterra. Underlying organic revenue trajectory in commodity processing tracks crop prices, processing volumes, and biofuel/policy demand, not unit growth.
- **Margin trend: Decelerating across all margin metrics through FY25.** Gross margin compressed from 8.14% (FY23) → 6.39% (FY24) → 4.85% (FY25), a cumulative 329 bps. EBITDA margin compressed from 6.36% → 4.26% → 3.18%, a cumulative 318 bps. EBIT margin compressed from 5.60% → 3.37% → 2.18%, a cumulative 342 bps. The compression reflects (i) crush margin normalization from FY21–22 peak conditions, (ii) FY25 SG&A inflation including $223m Viterra integration costs and $118m pension settlement loss [FY25 10-K MD&A, p.43], (iii) Viterra-segment revenue mix that is lower-margin grain merchandising. Q1 2026 GM at 3.50% is 163 bps below Q1 2025 — consistent with ongoing compression.
- **Seasonality: None pre-Viterra; unclear post-Viterra.** FY23 and FY24 quarters all sat in 23.9%–25.7% revenue share. FY25 quarterly weighting is acquisition-distorted, not seasonal.
- **Inflection point: FY25 — twofold.** First, the July 2, 2025 closing of the $9.5B Viterra acquisition (≈$4.2B cash + 65.6m new shares worth $5.3B at deal close [FY25 10-K, p.5]) added ~$25B of annualized revenue at H2 run-rate and re-segmented the business. Second, net debt jumped from $2.9B (Dec 2024) to $12.9B (Dec 2025), with Net Debt / EBITDA inflecting from 1.3x to 5.78x — though management's "adjusted leverage ratio" was reported at 1.6x at Q1 2026 vs 1.9x at FY25 end [5], reflecting different definitions including readily marketable inventory netting.
- **TTM trajectory: Mixed.** TTM revenue +56.9% (Viterra effect), TTM EBIT (CIQ norm) +31.1%, but TTM diluted GAAP EPS -45.3% (shareholder dilution from 65.6m new shares + GAAP charges). TTM FCF is negative $1,161m given working capital build for the expanded business plus elevated capex.
