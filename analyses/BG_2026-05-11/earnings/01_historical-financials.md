**Observations:**
- FY24 (pre-Viterra): revenue is **evenly spread quarter-to-quarter** (range 24.3%–25.5%) — Bunge as a global crusher does not exhibit material seasonal revenue concentration, because Northern and Southern Hemisphere crops offset each other. **No quarter takes >30% or <20% in FY24.**
- FY25: the Q3/Q4 jump to 31.5%/33.8% is **acquisition-driven (Viterra added July 2, 2025)**, not seasonal.
- Margin: FY24 Q4 was the strongest gross-margin quarter (7.98%); FY25 Q1–Q4 all decelerated (5.13% → 4.26%).
- FY23 quarterly breakdown not available in the data pool (the 2025 10-K only provides Note 27 quarterly data for FY24 and FY25).

## 6. Key Trend Summary

1. **Revenue direction: Volatile, with a step-change up in 2H 2025.** Revenue trajectory was 2021 $59.2B → 2022 $67.2B (commodity price spike) → 2023 $59.5B → 2024 $53.1B → 2025 $70.3B. The FY25 jump is acquisition-driven (Viterra closed July 2, 2025); pre-Viterra organic revenue had been **decelerating for two straight years** (-11.4% FY23 and -10.8% FY24) on lower commodity prices. Q1 2026 organic revenue at $21.9B (annualizes ~$87B) — comparison to Q1 2025 ($11.6B, pre-Viterra) is not informative for organic trend.

2. **Margin direction: Compressing materially.** Gross margin compressed from 8.14% (FY23) → 6.39% (FY24) → 4.85% (FY25); Q1 2026 dropped further to 3.50%. EBIT margin (company-defined) went 5.60% → 3.37% → 2.18% over the same period. The compression is **broad-based**: gross-margin compressed YoY in every quarter of FY25 vs. FY24, both pre- and post-Viterra. Soybean Processing segment EBIT margin (the dominant ~52% of segment EBIT) was 3.4% FY25 vs. 6.1% FY23 — typical cyclical commodity-crush margin contraction. [FY2025 10-K, MD&A pp.38–43]

3. **No material seasonality in revenue under the pre-Viterra footprint.** FY24 quarterly revenue ranged 24.3%–25.5% — essentially even. Q4 has historically been the highest gross-margin quarter (7.98% in FY24). Post-Viterra (FY26+), the company should still show low seasonality given hemispheric crop offsets, but this needs at least one full year (2026) to validate.

4. **Two major inflection points in the last 5 years.** (a) **Margin peak/trough:** EBIT peaked at $3,333m in FY23 then compressed to $1,533m FY25 — a $1.8B / 54% decline as crush margins normalized post-pandemic and biofuel-policy uncertainty pressured volumes. (b) **Capital structure inflection:** Net debt jumped from $2.9B FY24 to $12.9B FY25 (4.4x increase) to fund the $4.2B cash portion of Viterra plus assumed debt. Net Debt / Adj. EBITDA went from 1.18x → 4.72x. Capex also tripled from $399m FY21 to $1,723m FY25, and is guided to remain $1.5–$1.7B in 2026. [Q1 2026 earnings release, p.9; FY2025 10-K, p.36]

5. **Adjusted vs. GAAP EPS gap is widening.** Adjusted EPS exceeded GAAP EPS by 8% in FY21 (12.93 vs 13.16 approx.) → 54% in FY25 ($7.57 vs $4.91) → 423% in Q1 2026 ($1.83 vs $0.35). Mark-to-market timing differences and Viterra integration costs are driving the gap. Quality of GAAP earnings is therefore deteriorating relative to the adjusted picture — flagging for `earnings-quality` downstream.
