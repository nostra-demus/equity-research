# Earnings Module — BG (Synthesis)

## Abstract

Bunge's earnings setup is mixed and unusually externally driven. Recent guidance raised FY26 adjusted EPS to $9.00–$9.50 from $7.50–$8.00 on a +110% Q1 beat, yet GAAP EPS direction is decelerating sharply — Q1 2026 GAAP EPS of $0.35 was 19% of adjusted EPS of $1.83, with mark-to-market timing dominating the gap. The single biggest driver is the global oilseed crush spread, which alone has an observed ±$800m EBITDA swing range — wider than the entire 2026 guidance. The biggest risk is the heavily inverted forward crush curve into 2H 2026 paired with 4.72x net debt to adjusted EBITDA post-Viterra. Mixed earnings setup.

## 3. Reconciliation

The principal disagreement to reconcile is between **revenue-drivers / margin-drivers** (current direction tailwind, Q1 2026 strong) and **earnings-quality** (cash conversion deteriorating sharply, GAAP earnings increasingly disconnected from cash). Both are correct: Adjusted Segment EBIT genuinely accelerated +62.8% YoY in Q1 2026 on crush economics, but reported GAAP earnings deteriorated because of MTM timing reversals and Viterra integration costs. Reconciled view: **operating economics are improving (cyclical crush tailwind) while reported-earnings quality is deteriorating (recurring MTM, recurring integration "one-offs," CFO/EBITDA below 50% red-flag threshold).** The synthesis weights both, hence "Mixed."

A second tension exists between **guidance-consensus** (Street has chased the raise — bar is hard) and **historical beat pattern** (BG beat 6 of last 7 prints). The reconciliation in `05_beat-miss-setup` is explicit: the beat pattern is real but the slack to over-deliver has been compressed by the Q1 raise that pulled consensus from $8.23 to $9.43 in one month, and H2 breadth is already net-negative. Setup is therefore Balanced rather than Favors-beat, which the synthesis adopts.

A third tension is between consensus-level direction (rising sharply on EPS / EBITDA / EBIT) and revenue revisions (60% net DOWN for FY26). This is a coherent pattern of "operating-leverage beat" being modelled by analysts, not a contradiction — the synthesis treats it as informational, not conflicting.

## 5. Earnings Setup Summary

### Revenue Setup

Revenue accelerated +32.4% in FY25 to $70.3B and +87.8% YoY in Q1 2026 — but this is **almost entirely the Viterra acquisition** (closed July 2, 2025), not organic growth. Pre-Viterra, organic revenue declined two consecutive years (-11.4% FY23, -10.8% FY24) on lower commodity prices. The forward revenue driver is global biofuel-policy-driven demand for soybean and softseed oil (~67.6% of consolidated revenue), supported by EPA RVO endorsement, expanding Indonesia/Brazil/EU mandates, and Iran-conflict-led crude pull-through. Risk: forward curves are heavily inverted into 2H 2026, implying H2 spread compression that could erase the Q1 tailwind. Consensus revenue revisions are 60% net DOWN for FY26 despite the EPS raise.

### Margin Setup

Margins are compressing. Gross margin moved 8.14% (FY23) → 6.39% (FY24) → 4.85% (FY25) → 3.50% (Q1 26). Total EBIT margin went 5.60% → 3.37% → 2.18% over the same window. The compression is broad-based — not just Viterra mix — with QoQ gross-margin compression every quarter of FY25 vs. FY24 pre- and post-Viterra. The single biggest margin variable is the soybean crush spread; Soybean P&R EBIT swung from $2,222m FY23 to $872m FY24 — a $1,350m / 60% drop — purely on spread compression. Current direction is tailwind on the RVO-plus-crude-plus-meal stack, but D&A is guided up 39% to $975m, interest expense to $620–$660m, and there is no contractual cost pass-through on either raw materials or freight.

### Quality Check

Earnings are **not currently cash-backed**. CFO/EBITDA collapsed to 37.7% in FY25 from 87% in FY23 — below the 50% red-flag threshold. Adjusted EPS exceeded GAAP EPS by 54% in FY25 and 423% in Q1 2026 ($1.83 vs $0.35), driven by recurring MTM timing differences and three consecutive years of Viterra "one-off" integration costs ($114m → $244m → $223m). FY25 FCF was negative $879m. The biggest quality concern is that quarterly GAAP EPS is being dominated by MTM swings of $300–$1,000m, while the company guides only on Adjusted EPS.

### Consensus Bar

The bar is hard. Consensus FY26 Adj. EPS at $9.43 sits in the top half of the $9.00–$9.50 guide range, with 100% upward revision breadth in the last month — implying the easy upgrades are already in the numbers. H2 breadth is mixed-to-negative (33% net DOWN on FQ3/FQ4 EPS), and management itself flagged "significant uncertainty in the second half." Revision direction is rising sharply on FY26 EPS and EBITDA but the bar is now anchored at the top end of the guide, removing slack to beat without another macro tailwind.
