# Earnings Module — BG (Synthesis)

## Abstract

Bunge's reported earnings look like an acceleration but the underlying business is decelerating beneath an acquisition-driven veneer that anniversaries in Q3 2026. The dominant driver is Viterra consolidation, not organic crush-spread expansion, with reported gross margin compressing 329 bps over three years even as top-line surges 32% to $70.3B. Consensus EPS of $9.43 sits within $0.07 of the top of management's raised $9.00–$9.50 guide, leaving no easy beat at the headline level. The biggest risk is that forward crush curves are already heavily inverted into H2 2026 while net debt has stepped from $2.9B to $13.7B with no contractual pass-through on inputs, energy, or freight. The verdict is mixed earnings setup.

## 3. Reconciliation

The specialists generally aligned but several disagreements need explicit treatment.

**Q1 2026 revenue YoY math.** Agent 01 (historical-financials) reports Q1'26 revenue +65.1% YoY; agent 02 (revenue-drivers) reports +87.7% from raw filing math ($21,861m / $11,643m − 1). The raw filing math is the correct number — **use +87.7%**. Agent 01 should be corrected on this figure; the directional read (acquisition-driven, not organic) is unchanged.

**Viterra synergies progress.** Agent 02 (revenue-drivers) cites management's claim that synergies are "running ahead of plan"; the business-model cross-module synthesis indicates only $70m realized by year-end 2025 against a multi-hundred-million target. Both can be true if "ahead of plan" refers to the timing curve, not the cumulative number — but the more conservative reading (filings beat decks per MODULE_RULES) gives credit to the $70m realized figure rather than the adjective. The synthesizer should treat synergies as **in early-stage realization**, not the multi-hundred-million tailwind management framing implies.

**CFO collapse causation.** Agent 06 (earnings-quality) at one point characterizes the FY25 CFO/EBITDA collapse to 38% as "mostly mechanical, not accounting-quality-driven" (Viterra inventory step-up), but in Section 6 of the same output flags that FY24 (pre-Viterra) already showed CFO deteriorating faster than revenue. Both are true. The reconciled view: FY25 step is Viterra-mechanical, but the underlying trend was already deteriorating in FY24 before Viterra closed. The quality concern is therefore **structural plus mechanical**, not purely mechanical.

**"Quality is fine vs. quality is concerning."** Agent 06 reports inventory is "mostly Readily Marketable Inventory" (86%, quasi-cash) — defending the print — while simultaneously noting unrealized derivative losses doubled to ~$3B in Q1 2026 per business-model 12. Both can be true: RMI is quasi-cash on the asset side, but the derivative book on the other side has not validated the GAAP earnings. Reconciled view: the quality picture is **mixed, not clean**.

**Verdict consistency with red-flag agent.** Agent 08 (red-flags) recommends downgrading the framing from "earnings accelerating" to "earnings inflecting — positive (acquisition-driven) with high reversion risk" or "mixed earnings setup." This synthesis adopts **Mixed earnings setup**, which is consistent with the red-flag agent's reading. No conflict with the agent's "Material concerns" severity verdict.

## 5. Earnings Setup Summary

### Revenue Setup

The reported revenue trajectory looks accelerating but is mechanically driven by the July 2, 2025 Viterra close, not organic demand growth — management cites "Net sales contributions from the Acquisition of Viterra" as the first cause in every segment narrative in both the FY25 10-K MD&A and the Q1 2026 10-Q. The single factor that would have to change to flip direction is U.S. biofuel policy (RVO endorsement) combined with sustained crude oil pricing, which together set the demand pull-through for soy oil into renewable diesel; an RVO walk-back or RIN restriction reinstatement would compress the soy P&R segment by hundreds of millions. The gap between reported revenue and organic revenue is large enough that no clean organic YoY read is available until Q3 2026, when Viterra anniversaries; until then, "+87.7% Q1 2026 growth" overstates the underlying business by an unknown but plausibly 60-percentage-point margin.

### Margin Setup

Current consolidated margins are at a cyclical pressure point that is structurally pulled lower by Viterra-mix dilution (grain merchandising is 96% raw-material cost versus soybean processing's 91%) even as the spread environment improves. The single segment that takes the largest bite if it moves 10-20% adversely is Soybean Processing & Refining, where FY23→FY24 took segment EBIT from $2,222m to $872m on rising volume — a $1.35B swing larger than total FY25 Total EBIT of $1,533m. Bunge has zero contractual pass-through on raw materials, energy, or freight — confirmed by Q1 2026 Grain Merchandising EBIT swinging from +$46m to -$76m on the bunker-fuel spike from the Iran conflict with no offsetting price increase passed to ocean freight customers. The only margin-protection mechanism is hedge-execution discipline, which converts price-direction risk into execution-quality risk but does not eliminate cyclical exposure.

### Quality Check

The single largest gap between reported and economic earnings is the $1.28 per share mark-to-market timing difference in Q1 2026, which makes GAAP EPS only 19% of the adjusted figure — a 423% adjustment that is structurally recurring and not narrowing. The Viterra integration cost adjustment has now been used in four consecutive reporting periods (FY23 $114m, FY24 $244m, FY25 $223m, Q1 2026 ~$58m), totaling $639m+ across the bridge — calling these "one-offs" through four periods is unsupportable, and any normalized earnings model should treat at least a meaningful portion as recurring operating cost. For a forward model, starting from adjusted EPS and explicitly adding back the recurring Viterra integration cost gives the cleanest economic read; starting from GAAP introduces quarterly MTM noise that averages but does not zero out in any single period.

### Consensus Bar

For Bunge to beat the current $9.43 consensus by a material margin, two things must compound: the H2 2026 crush spread must hold above the heavily-inverted forward curve, and either bunker fuel must normalize (rebuilding Grain Merch EBIT) or softseed crop conditions must improve enough to deliver a like-for-like segment recovery. The bar appears set too high at the FY level (consensus chased to the top of guide with no slack) and too low at the H2-quarter level (FQ3 and FQ4 EPS each trimmed -2 net last month) — meaning the market is pricing strong H1 / soft H2 with no room for FY upside without another guide raise. Roughly the entire current consensus is anchored on the Q1 2026 +110% surprise, the EPA RVO endorsement, and elevated crude pricing from the Iran conflict — all three of which could reverse, and the forward curve says at least one is expected to.

## 5b. Leverage & Capital Structure

**TRIGGER A fires** (Net Debt / EBITDA > 3.0x): FY25 reported Net Debt / EBITDA = 5.78x. **TRIGGER B also fires** (Net Debt / EBITDA increased > 1.0x YoY AND total debt increased > 50% YoY AND net debt > 2x in absolute terms): the ratio inflected from 1.30x to 5.78x; net debt stepped from $2,927m to $12,916m at FY25 year-end and to $13,714m at Q1 2026 end.

1. **Current leverage level.** Net debt at Q1 2026 year-end was $13,714m ($3,245m short-term + $1,361m current LT + $9,947m long-term debt − $839m cash) [Q1 2026 10-Q p.5]; FY25 reported Net Debt / EBITDA was 5.78x using GAAP EBITDA of $2,236m [FY25 10-K p.F-12, p.F-13].
2. **Year-over-year change.** Ratio: 1.30x at FYE24 → 5.78x at FYE25 (+4.48x). Absolute net debt: $2,927m at FYE24 → $12,916m at FYE25 → $13,714m at Q1 2026 end (4.4x absolute increase in 15 months).
3. **Single largest driver.** The Viterra acquisition closed July 2, 2025 for ~$4.2B cash consideration plus 65.6m shares issued (worth ~$5.3B at close) plus debt assumed and refinanced [FY25 10-K p.5]; this is mechanically nearly the entire leverage step-up, supplemented by FY25 negative FCF of -$879m and working-capital build of $502m for the expanded business.
4. **GAAP vs Adjusted denominator.** Management reports an "adjusted leverage ratio" of 1.6x at Q1 2026 end (vs 1.9x at FYE25), which uses an undisclosed adjusted-EBITDA denominator that includes Readily Marketable Inventory netting [Q1 2026 transcript, Slide 8]. Using the same Q1 2026 net debt of $13,714m against GAAP TTM EBITDA of $3,054m (CIQ normalized) gives 4.49x; using FY25 GAAP EBITDA gives 5.78x. The reported 1.6x and the underlying 4.5x–5.8x represent the same balance sheet under different definitions; management's bridge to the 1.6x is not published in the available data.
5. **Debt maturity profile.** Not disclosed in the data pool with specificity sufficient to state the % maturing within 24 months or the weighted-average interest rate against current market rates — only the FY26 net interest guidance of $620–660m (implying a blended rate of ~4.5–4.8% on average debt) is available [Q1 2026 transcript].
6. **Capital-allocation constraint signal.** DPS estimate revisions are -3 / 0 (all three revisions down) over the last month [04_guidance-consensus §5], the first concrete signal that capital is being diverted to integration and leverage management rather than dividends. FCF turned negative -$879m in FY25 and TTM FCF is -$1,161m, indicating capital allocation is currently constrained by the deal and working-capital build. No buyback pause or rating-agency action is disclosed in the available data, but the DPS-trim breadth is a clear analyst signal that the dividend trajectory has weakened.

**Cross-reference applied:** The "Biggest earnings risk" line in Section 1 references both leverage AND the H2 crush-spread risk, consistent with the trigger requirement (though the verdict is Mixed, not Decelerating or Inflecting-Negative, so the strict mandate does not bind — the cross-reference is included anyway given the magnitude of the leverage step-up).
