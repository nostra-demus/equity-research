The consensus bar is **$1.93 mean / $1.91 median Adj. The fuller bar is the FY 2026 Adj. EPS consensus of **$9.43**, sitting $0.18 above the $9.25 guide midpoint and only $0.07 below the top end of the new $9.00–$9.50 range [04: §3].

## 6. Seasonality Read

Seasonality is **near-neutral for Q2**. Pre-Viterra, FY24 quarterly revenue shares were essentially flat (Q1 25.3%, Q2 24.9%, Q3 24.3%, Q4 25.5%), and gross margins varied modestly with Q4 historically the strongest (7.98% in FY24) and Q1 the weakest (5.13% in FY25) [01: §5]. Q2 is structurally an average-to-slightly-below quarter on both revenue and margin lines. The dominant variable for Q2 2026 is **South American crush season cadence** (which is timing-driven from the calendar-Q1 Brazilian/Argentine harvest, with farmer-selling carrying into Q2). Post-Viterra, hemispheric offsets should keep Q2 revenue mix ~22–26% of full year. **Seasonality neither helps nor hurts the Q2 setup meaningfully** — what matters is whether the Q1 South-American strength carried through April–June, and whether the bunker-fuel / cocoa-butter / wheat S&D headwinds from Q1 deepened.

## 7. Historical Pattern

BG has beaten EPS Normalized consensus in **6 of the last 7 reported periods** including all four quarters of FY25 and Q1 2026 (Q1'25 +38%, Q2'25 +21%, Q3'25 +15%, Q4'25 +9%, Q1'26 +110%) [04: §6]. Management's quarterly internal guidance has consistently been set low — Q1 2026's actual $1.83 vs the management-communicated "low $0.80 range" two months prior — implying systematic conservatism in management's own framing. **However**, the synthesizer should NOT weight this pattern heavily for Q2 because: (a) the Q1 2026 +110% beat was a true macro/forward-curve surprise that has now been absorbed into the raised guide; (b) consensus has chased from $8.23 (1 month ago) to $9.43, removing slack; (c) breadth on FQ3/FQ4 is already turning net-negative (33% down) suggesting analysts are not extrapolating Q1 strength forward. **The pattern says: management still tends to under-promise, but the room to over-deliver has been compressed by the Q1 raise.**

## 8. Setup Verdict

**Setup is Balanced.**

The single most important factor is the **soybean crush spread** — Q1 2026 strength on RVO clarity, Iran-conflict crude spike, and "surprising-upside" meal demand drove a +110% Q1 EPS beat that has now been fully priced into the raised guide and consensus at $9.43 (top-half of $9.00–$9.50 range) [03: §8; 04: §3]. The biggest risk that could flip the verdict to **Favors miss** is the **heavily inverted forward crush curve**, which signals H2 spread compression that, if it begins appearing in Q2's prepared-remarks language even without a numeric guide cut, would re-rate consensus quickly given how much it has chased. The biggest risk that could flip to **Favors beat** is a continuation of the macro tailwinds (sustained crude prices, expanding biofuel mandates, China–US trade flow shifts on soy) past current forward-curve assumptions — but this requires an external surprise, not operational delivery.

## 9. Second-Quarter Look-Ahead

The setup for the **Q3 2026 print** (reported ~late October 2026) looks **harder than Q2**. Management's revised 40/60 H1/H2 cadence and 45/55 Q3/Q4 split implies Q3 Adj. EPS of roughly $2.50 — an above-average quarter — while analyst revisions for FQ3 are already 33% net DOWN with management explicitly flagging "significant uncertainty in the second half" [04: §2, §5, §7]. The Q3 print will also be the **first clean year-over-year comparison including a full quarter of Viterra in both periods** (Q3 2025 included Viterra from July 2; Q3 2026 is fully comparable), so organic-versus-acquisition decomposition will be transparent for the first time — and the inverted forward curve implies that comparison will be unflattering on crush economics. Visibility into Q3 will not improve materially until forward curves settle and tropical-oils / cocoa-butter dynamics clarify.

## 10. Pre-Mortem

If this setup-call is wrong, the most likely reason is that **we under-weighted management's track record of low-balling quarterly internal guidance** (six beats in seven prints, including the +110% Q1 2026 beat against a self-issued "low $0.80" handle), and BG cleared a Q2 print that looked anchored at $1.93 consensus with another +10–20% beat driven by Q1's macro tailwinds simply persisting into April–June. The mirror-image miss case is that we under-weighted the **specific signal of net-down revisions in revenue (60% net down) and H2 EPS (33% net down) even amid a positive 1-month FY breadth** — indicating analysts already see the soft spots clearly and the bar for Q2 alone may be set ambitiously enough that an in-line print reads as a disappointment.
