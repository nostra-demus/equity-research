# Beat / Miss Setup — BG

## 1. Next Quarter Context

The next print is **Q2 2026** (reporting late July 2026), the first full quarter with Viterra in both periods (Q2 2025 included only the first day of Viterra; the close was July 2, 2025). On Bunge's own seasonality, Q2 is structurally not a peak quarter — pre-Viterra Q2 averaged ~22.8% of annual revenue [01_historical-financials sec.5]. The consensus bar for Q2 2026 EPS Norm is **$1.93** (up 20% in 3 months from $1.61), and FY 2026 EPS Norm consensus of $9.43 sits at the **top end** of management's raised $9.00–$9.50 guide range, only $0.07 from the top [04_guidance-consensus sec.3, sec.4]. Management explicitly framed the implied Q2 guide as "somewhat conservative" but flagged higher Q2 interest, tax, and tropical-oils headwinds [04_guidance-consensus sec.7].

## 6. Seasonality Read

Seasonality is a **neutral-to-mild-positive factor** for the Q2 2026 setup. On a pre-Viterra basis (FY23 and FY24), Bunge had no material seasonality — every quarter sat in 23.9%–25.7% of annual revenue, with Q2 specifically at 25.3% (FY23) and 24.9% (FY24) [01_historical-financials sec.5]. Crucially, Q2 has historically been a **near-average** quarter, not a weak one. FY25 quarterly weighting is distorted by Viterra's July 2 close and is not informative for Q2 2026. The one mild positive for Q2 2026: management's revised 40/60 H1/H2 cadence (vs prior 30/70) explicitly pulls earnings forward into Q1+Q2 — meaning the company's own forward-look puts more weight on Q2 than on H2 quarters. So the seasonality factor neither helps nor hurts on a like-for-like basis, but management's cadence commentary tilts the read slightly toward "Q2 has more earnings to deliver than the historical pattern would suggest."

## 7. Historical Pattern

Bunge has a **strong and consistent beat pattern**: 6 of 7 quarters of adj EPS beats, with the average surprise ~28%, and Q1 2026 was a +110% beat ($1.83 vs $0.87 estimate) [04_guidance-consensus sec.6]. The single miss was Q4 2024. Importantly, this pattern persists even after the FY guide was raised — the closest historical parallel cited in the data pool is the 2007-style "guide raised, then beat" pattern referenced in the consensus module [04 sec.7]. EBITDA also beat in 6 of 7 quarters. Revenue beat record is mixed (3 of 7), so the beat pattern is concentrated in margin/EPS, not top-line. The synthesizer should weight this pattern meaningfully — it is statistically significant and reflects a structural under-guidance / over-deliver tendency rooted in Bunge's hedging-disciplined approach to giving narrow ranges. The caveat: the Q1 2026 +110% beat magnitude is itself an outlier driven by the MTM-reversal/spread surge combination, and the consensus has now caught up (FY estimates at top of guide); the easy beat path is gone.

## 8. Setup Verdict

**Setup is balanced.** The single most important factor is that the Street has already revised FY 2026 EPS Norm consensus up to $9.43 — within $0.07 of the top of management's raised $9.00–$9.50 guide — with breadth of 6/0 in the last month, so the easy-beat path has been priced out at the FY level even while Q2 specifically retains some beatability ($1.93 Q2 estimate after H2 estimates were trimmed by -2 net per quarter). The biggest single risk that could flip the verdict to "Favors miss" is the bunker-fuel / Iran-conflict pressure persisting into Q2, which directly hit Grain Merchandising in Q1 with no contractual pass-through and which management has already used to take down FY26 grain merch guidance.

## 9. Second-Quarter Look-Ahead

The quarter AFTER next (Q3 2026) has a **more favorable setup** than Q2 on the bar-side: H2 estimates were just actively cut (-2 net revisions for Q3 and -2 for Q4 in the last month) as analysts reallocated earnings into H1 to match the revised 40/60 cadence [04 sec.5], and Q3 also annuals the Viterra close (July 2, 2025) — meaning Q3 2026 is the first quarter with like-for-like Viterra comparatives, which removes the YoY distortion that has clouded analyst models. However, Q3 also has more exposure to the variables management flagged as H2 risks (China-US trade, Argentine farmer selling, El Niño weather, RIN restriction delays, tropical-oil cocoa headwinds), so the lower bar is partly compensated by genuinely higher uncertainty rather than being a soft target.

## 10. Pre-Mortem

If this earnings setup fails, the most likely reason we missed it is that we under-weighted the **mathematics of "consensus at top of guide"** — that is, even with Bunge's strong 6-of-7 beat pattern and management's tendency to under-guide, a 100%-positive revision wave combined with consensus already at $9.43 vs a $9.50 guide cap means the **stock-reaction beat threshold** is structurally higher than the **estimate beat threshold**. A second possible miss vector is bunker fuel: a sustained Middle East energy spike could compound across Q2 in Grain Merchandising in a way that overwhelms the soybean crush-spread tailwind, particularly because Bunge has zero contractual pass-through on freight inputs.
