## 7. Bar Assessment

**Bar is fair, with one-sided risk skewed toward beat on H1 / miss risk on H2.**

Rationale:

1. **At the headline level, consensus has caught up to the raised guide.** FY 2026 EPS Norm consensus of $9.43 sits at the **top end** of management's $9.00-$9.50 range (+$0.18 above midpoint). All 6 contributing FY 2026 EPS estimates were revised up in the last month, with breadth at +6/-0 — a fully one-sided positive revision wave. EBITDA and EBIT FY 2026 breadth is also unanimously up (5/0 and 4/0). The Street fully repriced after the Q1 print; there is now no "soft bar" at the consolidated FY level.

2. **The quarterly mix tells a more nuanced story.** Q2 2026 EPS consensus has moved from $1.61 (3 months ago) to $1.93 (current), and Q&A on the call described this implied Q2 guide as "somewhat conservative" — management explicitly flagged higher Q2 interest and tax expense, weaker tropicals (cocoa butter, palm). The Q3 2026 and Q4 2026 EPS estimates were cut (-2 net last month each) as analysts reallocated earnings into H1 to match the revised 40/60 cadence. So H2 estimates are now lower than they were 3 months ago even as the FY number rose — meaning the H2 bar has been actively lowered.

3. **Track record favors continued beats.** EPS Norm beats in 6 of 7 quarters, with last-7-quarter average surprise of roughly +28% (Q1 2026 alone +110%, Q1 2025 +38%, Q2 2025 +21%, Q3 2025 +15%). Bunge has a structural tendency to under-guide and over-deliver. Adjusted EBITDA beat consensus in 6 of 7 recent quarters as well.

4. **Two specific risks to the "fair" verdict.** First, consensus EPS Norm of $9.43 leaves only **$0.07 of slack to the top of guidance ($9.50)** — if Bunge prints in the upper half of its own range, the Street will be in-line, not impressed. Second, management explicitly cited H2 uncertainty ("significant uncertainty remains, particularly in the second half") tied to Middle East conflict, biofuel policy, tropical oils (cocoa, palm) margins, and Argentina/Brazil crop sizing — meaning the H2 beat path is exposed to several variables outside Bunge's control.

Net: the consensus bar is **fair-to-slightly-stretched at the FY level** (Street near top of guide; 100% positive revision breadth removed the easy beat path), but **H2 quarterly estimates have just been trimmed** giving Bunge a more beatable Q3/Q4 setup. Management's track record of beating their own raised guide (the 2007 example in Guidance.xlsx showed a similar pattern — guide raised, then beat) is a positive baseline factor.
