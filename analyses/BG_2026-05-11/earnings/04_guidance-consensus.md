## 7. Bar Assessment

**Bar is fair — leaning slightly high after the Q1 raise.**

Three things drive that read. First, the Street has fully chased the raised guide: consensus at $9.43 sits $0.18 above the $9.25 midpoint and only $0.07 below the top end of $9.50, with 100% upward revision breadth on FY 2026 EPS Normalized in the last month — implying the easy upgrades are already in the numbers. Second, breadth turns mixed-to-negative in H2 (FQ3/FQ4 EPS revisions show 4 down vs 2 up per quarter), and management explicitly flagged second-half uncertainty (tropical oils, cocoa butter equivalents, tariff exposure, grain merch headwinds, higher interest costs from working-capital build) — so the H2 implied earnings of roughly $5.50–$5.70 (60% of $9.00–$9.50 less Q1's $1.83 and an implied Q2 ~$1.93) sit above where breadth signals are pointing. Third, Bunge's 6-of-7 beat record on EPS Normalized suggests the bar is rarely set unbeatably high in absolute terms, but with consensus now anchored to the upper half of the new guide and Q1's outsized beat already absorbed, incremental upside on the full year requires another macro/biofuel tailwind in H2 — not just operational delivery.
