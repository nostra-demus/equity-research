# Revenue Drivers — BG (Bunge Global SA)

## 1. Segment Decomposition Status

Segment decomposition applied — 4 reportable segments from business-model module (Soybean P&R, Softseed P&R, Tropical Oils & Specialty Ingredients — formerly Other Oilseeds P&R, and Grain Merchandising & Milling) plus Corporate & Other. FY2025 segment-level net sales, volumes, and price/mix commentary are disclosed in Note 26 and MD&A pp.38–43 of the FY2025 10-K, and at the quarterly level in the Q1 2026 10-Q pp.39–43.

**Critical caveat in all YoY analysis below:** Viterra was acquired July 2, 2025. Q3 2025 onward (annual FY25 H2; Q1 2026 vs Q1 2025) is not organic — Viterra footprint, Viterra volumes, and Viterra revenue are mechanically additive in every segment except Tropical Oils & Specialty Ingredients (which received no Viterra assets) [FY2025 10-K, MD&A, pp.38–43; Q1 2026 10-Q, pp.39–43; q1_call.txt, prepared remarks].

## 2. Revenue Driver Tree

| Business Type | Revenue Formula |
|---|---|
| Commodity processor / merchandiser | Volume × realized price, where realized price ≈ raw-material price + crush/refining/merchandising margin |

**Company-specific revenue formula:**

`BG Revenue = Σ_segments [Processed volume × processed-product price + Merchandised volume × commodity flat price + Refined-oil volume × refined oil price + Freight / other services]`

In practice, ~95% of BG revenue is `Volume × Price` where price is a near pass-through of commodity flat prices (soy, canola, sunflower, palm, corn, wheat). Bunge captures the **crush margin** and **merchandising margin** (the spreads), not the absolute commodity price level. The flat price moves revenue dollars; margin moves earnings. The two diverge frequently — this is the key feature of the business [FY2025 10-K, p.4; q1_call.txt, prepared remarks].

## 7. The Single Biggest Revenue Driver

**Global biofuel policy — specifically the U.S. RVO and the parallel mandates in Brazil, Indonesia, and Europe — is the single biggest revenue driver of BG over the next 3–12 months.** Direction: **improving.** The Soybean P&R segment ($36.3bn; 51.6% of FY25 revenue) and the Tropical Oils & Specialty Ingredients segment ($4.6bn; 6.6%) — together 58% of total revenue — both depend directly on demand pull from biofuels for soy oil, canola oil, and palm/tropical oils. Management on the Q1 2026 call explicitly identified biofuels as "a bright spot in agriculture right now" and cited the EPA's RVO decision, expected soy oil inventory drawdown in Q3/Q4 2026 from RVO clarity, evolving Brazil/Indonesia/Europe mandates, and higher crude/diesel prices (which make discretionary biofuel blending economic) as the convergent drivers of crush margins and refined-oil pricing [q1_call.txt, prepared remarks; Q&A with Manav Gupta; Q&A with Pooran Sharma]. A 10–20% change in expected biofuel volumes (e.g., a hawkish or dovish RVO revision, an EU mandate shift, an Indonesian B40-to-B50 transition) would flow through to soy oil and tropical oil prices, processed volumes, and refining premiums in a way that no other single driver matches in scale.

The closest competing candidate is the **Viterra integration / synergy realization**, but that is largely already in revenue (Viterra is now consolidated) and is a margin story (cost synergies, network optimization) more than a revenue story going forward. M&A as a revenue driver was overwhelming for FY25 and Q1'26 reported growth but anniversaries in Q3 2026 — after which YoY growth will be on a like-for-like base and the biofuel policy / crush margin variables dominate.
