# Revenue Drivers — BG

## 1. Segment Decomposition Status

**Segment decomposition applied — 4 segments from business-model module.** Per `analyses/BG_2026-05-11/business-model/03_segment-map.md`, Bunge reports four segments effective Q3 2025: Soybean Processing & Refining (51.6% of FY25 revenue), Softseed Processing & Refining (16.0%), Tropical Oils & Specialty Ingredients (formerly Other Oilseeds; 6.6%), and Grain Merchandising & Milling (25.8%). Soybean P&R is dominant; the company is not single-segment. Segment-level Net sales and volumes are disclosed in [FY2025 10-K, Note 26, p.F-77] and [Q1 2026 10-Q, MD&A pp.39–41], so a segment-level driver decomposition is feasible.

## 2. Revenue Driver Tree

Bunge is a **commodity producer/processor** type. Revenue is principally `Volume (metric tons) × Realized commodity price`, with separate volume streams for processed (crushed) oilseeds, merchandised oilseeds/grains, and refined oil. For Tropical Oils & Specialty Ingredients and Milling, revenue is `Sales volume × ASP` of specialty oils and finished products.

Company-specific revenue formula (consolidated, simplified):
```
BG Revenue = Σ_segment [(processed volume × processed-product ASP) + (merchandised volume × commodity flat price) + (refined oil volume × refined ASP)]
+ M&A contribution (Viterra Jul-2025; IFF Soy Protein Mar-2026; ViOil Q4-2025)
± FX translation impact
```

Volumes are tied directly to crush capacity utilization and origination footprint; ASPs follow soybean/softseed/palm/wheat/corn flat-price curves, which are driven externally by biofuel policy, energy prices, weather (S&D balance), and trade flows. [FY2025 10-K, MD&A pp.33–35; Item 1, pp.3–6]

The 33% YoY rise in Soybean processed volume and 130% rise in merchandised volume in Q1'26 are almost entirely capacity additions, not organic share gains.

## 7. The Single Biggest Revenue Driver

**The single biggest forward revenue driver is global biofuel-policy-driven demand for vegetable oil — specifically the price and volume of soybean and softseed oil flowing into renewable diesel and biodiesel.** Across the Q1 2026 call, CEO Heckman and management cited biofuels as the dominant pricing and demand variable, and the segment financials confirm it: Q1 2026 sales increases "across all regions" were explicitly attributed to "biofuel mandates in North America" and Iran-conflict energy spillover [Q1 2026 10-Q, MD&A pp.39–40]. A 10–20% move in soy oil and soft seed oil prices, driven by RVO levels, Indonesia/Brazil mandate enforcement, or a reversion in crude oil prices, would directly move ~$8–10B of Bunge's revenue (the vegetable-oil and refined-oil components of the two oilseed segments, which together are 67.6% of consolidated revenue). Direction now is **improving** — EPA RVO decision in Q1 2026, expanding mandates in Indonesia/Brazil/EU, and high crude prices all support veg-oil price levels and processing volumes. The principal downside risk is the heavily inverted forward curve, which suggests H2 2026 weakness if the energy/biofuel tailwind fades. The Viterra acquisition is a one-time step-change to revenue base (not a forward driver beyond synergy realization), so it is not the answer here — but it is the reason the 10–20% move is now sized against a ~$85B run-rate revenue base instead of ~$53B.
