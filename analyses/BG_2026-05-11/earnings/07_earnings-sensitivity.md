# Earnings Sensitivity — BG

## 1. Variable Selection

Six variables were selected from the upstream driver tables in `02_revenue-drivers.md` and `03_margin-drivers.md`, prioritized by magnitude rating (High first) and validated against the business-model `10_external-dependency.md` cross-module output. The selection ladders from the soybean crush spread (rated High in both upstream agents and named the #1 driver by both), through global biofuel policy/crude oil linkages, Viterra integration/synergy capture, ocean freight/bunker fuel, European-Black Sea softseed crop conditions, and FX (Brazilian real / Argentine peso / euro). Interest rate sensitivity is folded into the table per the external-dependency module's $78m/100bps disclosure, even though it sits below EBIT. SG&A from Viterra labor was deliberately excluded as a discrete variable since it is captured inside the Viterra integration/synergy line. Mark-to-market timing was excluded because it averages to zero across a full fiscal year and is not an economic driver. All six variables map directly to driver-table rows in upstream outputs; none are invented.

## 4. The Single Highest-Sensitivity Variable

**The soybean crush spread is the single variable that moves Bunge's earnings the most.** Its historical observed swing — Soy P&R segment EBIT moving from $2,222m (FY23) to $872m (FY24) and recovering to $1,225m (FY25) on essentially rising volumes [FY25 10-K MD&A p.38] — exceeds the bull/bear range of every other variable combined. The crush spread is **almost entirely externally driven** (oil-leg price, meal-leg price, and bean-leg price are all CBOT-traded), with management's only lever being hedge execution discipline; the spread itself cannot be company-controlled. Current direction is **tailwind near-term**: Q1 2026 adjusted EPS of $1.83 beat the prior year's $1.81 baseline despite Viterra dilution, and management raised FY26 adjusted EPS guidance from $7.50–$8.00 to $9.00–$9.50 explicitly citing crush-spread strength [q1_call.txt prepared remarks]. The adverse case requires a combination of (i) RVO walked-back or RIN restriction reinstated tightening soy oil demand, (ii) good 2026/27 Northern-hemisphere soybean harvests pressuring the oil and meal legs, and (iii) US–China trade re-disruption rerouting flows in a way that compresses Argentine and Brazilian crush margins. Any one of those alone could move segment EBIT by hundreds of millions; in combination, a return to the FY24 trough would compress consolidated EBITDA by ~$350m and pull EPS down by ~$1.65 versus the FY26 baseline.

## 5. Interaction Effects

Several of these variables co-move and amplify each other. **Crude oil and biofuel-policy demand are functionally linked**: management explicitly framed the biofuel tailwind as a function of both RVO clarity AND elevated crude making "discretionary blending work" [q1_call.txt prepared remarks]. A crude collapse would simultaneously weaken biofuel pull-through and the soy oil leg of the crush spread — compounding the bear case for Variables 1 and 4. **Bunker fuel (Variable 2) is also a function of crude**: a sustained crude decline would simultaneously help Grain Merchandising via lower bunker costs AND hurt Soybean P&R via weaker soy oil pricing — partial offset, not compounding. **FX and commodity prices**: Argentine and Brazilian commodity prices are dollar-denominated, so a sharp BRL or ARS devaluation typically translates into better local-currency farmer realizations and stronger farmer selling, which feeds Bunge's processing volumes — a partial offset between Variable 5 (FX headwind) and Variable 1 (crush-spread support). **Viterra synergy capture (Variable 3) and crush spread (Variable 1)** are mechanically independent but psychologically correlated in market perception: a weak crush environment plus integration cost overruns would compound the bear case for the equity story.

## 6. Non-Linear Or Asymmetric Risks

Three material asymmetries exist.

First, **bunker fuel and ocean freight is asymmetric to the downside**: Bunge has no contractual pass-through to grain merchandising customers on bunker fuel [business-model 06_value-chain.md; 03_margin-drivers sec.3]. A bunker spike instantly compresses Grain Merchandising EBIT (see the +$46m → -$76m swing in a single quarter in Q1 2026), but a bunker decline does not immediately expand it because freight rates are negotiated forward. The bear-case impact is larger and faster than the symmetric bull case.

Second, **the soybean crush spread can compress non-linearly via operating deleverage in high fixed-cost plants**. Q1 2026 segment data shows Industrial Expenses (fixed + variable) of ~$886m run-rate across segments [Q1 2026 10-Q Note 19 p.32], which are utilization-leveraged. A 10% volume decline at flat spread would not just lose a tenth of variable contribution — it would also leave fixed costs on a smaller revenue base, compounding margin compression. This is most acute in Softseed segment plants in Europe where the small sun crop has already forced underutilization.

Third, **interest rate and leverage interact via covenant risk at $13.7bn net debt**. At end-Q1 2026, Bunge's reported GAAP Net Debt / EBITDA was 5.78x at FY25 year-end inflecting from 1.30x [01_historical-financials sec.1], though management's "adjusted leverage ratio" was 1.6x reflecting RMI netting. Specific covenant thresholds are not in the data pool; however, a combination of (i) +100 bps rate hike, (ii) crush-spread collapse to FY24 trough, and (iii) sustained bunker-fuel headwind would simultaneously raise interest expense ~$78m, compress EBITDA by ~$500m+, and could trigger leverage-ratio breaches. *Inference, not from filings* — specific covenant thresholds are not disclosed in the data pool, so this is a flagged risk rather than a quantified one.

FX downside risk is roughly symmetric given active hedging; biofuel-policy is roughly symmetric; Viterra synergy capture is symmetric.

## 7. Earnings Volatility Score

**Score: 72 / 100** (higher = WORSE — more volatile / sensitive to small input changes).

Reason: Total EBIT moved from $3,333m (FY23) to $1,533m (FY25) — more than halving in two years on commodity-cycle factors — and the #1 variable (soy crush spread) is entirely external. This sits clearly in the 61–80 band ("High volatility — multiple variables with large impact"). It is not pushed into 81–100 because management does have real hedging discipline, Bunge has N/S hemisphere geographic balance, Item 7A FX exposure is hedged to "not material," and the disclosed commodity VaR of $131m at high position is a fraction of full-year segment EBIT swings.
