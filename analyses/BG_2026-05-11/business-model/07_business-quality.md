## 4. Read

**This is a globally scaled, capital-intensive commodity merchant — closer to a midstream oilseed processor with a trading-desk overlay than to a "food company." It is the textbook cyclical: pricing is set by CBOT and Matif futures, not by Bunge; margin is the spread; spread compresses and expands on harvests, biofuel mandates, freight, and FX; and EBIT can halve in a year (FY23→FY25 total EBIT down 54%) on factors no executive controls.** The single quality factor a buyer should keep their eye on over the next 24 months is **margin stability — specifically, the soy crush spread**, because Soybean P&R is 52% of revenue and 53% of segment EBIT and its margin has already proven to swing 4–5 EBIT points peak-to-trough; a Viterra synergy thesis or a capital-allocation thesis lives or dies on whether crush spreads hold while integration costs roll off, not on anything intrinsic to the platform.
