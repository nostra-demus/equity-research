## 3. Value Creation Read

**Verdict: Unclear / Insufficient data — the per-ton economics are positive in dollar terms (FY2025 contribution margin ~$48/mt and EBIT ~$20/mt), but Bunge does not disclose enough to know whether those dollars cover the full economic cost of building and maintaining the asset base that produces them.** Working from segment financials, each FY2025 ton of soybean throughput generated about $20 of segment EBIT against an asset intensity of $266/ton (a ~7.5% pre-tax return on segment assets, before any allocation of corporate cost or financing). Management's own trailing-twelve-month adjusted ROIC is 8% versus a stated cost of equity of 7.2% [Q1 2026 transcript, prepared remarks] — i.e. the whole company is barely covering its hurdle, and the dominant segment is unlikely to be materially better given that Soybean P&R carries the largest asset base. The unit's economics also swung from $46/mt of EBIT in FY2023 to $17/mt in FY2024 to $20/mt in FY2025 on roughly stable tonnage, which means a single year's per-unit math is not a reliable signal of the underlying economics.

**The single most valuable disclosure that's missing is a per-ton crush margin (or processing margin) net of variable cost, segmented by region.** Without it, an outsider cannot separate volume effects from price-spread effects, cannot compare to industry peers (ADM, Cargill private, Wilmar publish crush proxies), and cannot answer whether one extra processed ton at the marginal South American or North American facility is value-accretive at today's spreads.

## 4. Sensitivity

The single input that would most change the value-creation read is **the crush spread per ton** — the difference between revenue and raw material cost. In FY2025 that gross spread was ~$56/mt; in FY2023 it was ~$89/mt; in FY2024 it was ~$56/mt. A 20% move in this gross spread (≈$11/mt) applied to the FY2025 throughput of 61.5m mt is roughly $680m of segment EBIT — more than half of the FY2025 segment EBIT of $1,225m and more than 40% of total Bunge EBIT of $1,533m. By contrast, a 20% move in capex/ton (~$2.6/mt) is only ~$160m, and a 20% move in fixed industrial expense is ~$185m. **Crush spread is also the most volatile of these inputs historically** — it has moved roughly ±37% across FY23→FY24→FY25 on stable tonnage, far more than capex or operating-cost lines, and management itself attributes the year-over-year EBIT swings primarily to "crush margins" and "soybean processing margins" rather than to volume or cost actions [MD&A pp.38–39]. Secondary sensitivity is **biofuel-policy-driven refined oil demand**, which Q1 2026 commentary identified as the swing factor for 2026 outlook (EPS guide raised from $7.50–$8.00 to $9.00–$9.50 largely on RVO clarity) [Q1 2026 transcript, prepared remarks].
