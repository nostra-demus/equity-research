# Business Model Reality Check — BG (Synthesis)

## Abstract

Bunge Global SA is a 200-year-old globally diversified agricultural commodity processor and merchandiser that crushes oilseeds, refines vegetable oils, mills grain, and trades crops at scale across 50+ countries. It earns money tonne-by-tonne through Soybean Processing & Refining (52% of FY25 revenue and segment EBIT) plus three smaller segments, with profit set by undisclosed crush spreads rather than by Bunge's own pricing. The strongest positive is scaled physical infrastructure — 35 oilseed plants and 298 storage facilities now enlarged by Viterra. The strongest negative is acute commodity dependence: total EBIT fell 54% from $3,333M to $1,533M over FY23–FY25 on cycle factors management does not control. Verdict: average business — worth deeper work only if valuation is cheap; no disqualifier triggered.

Total EBIT fell 54% over FY23–FY25 ($3,333M → $1,533M) on commodity-cycle factors no executive controls, and the FY25 gross margin (4.85%) is below every named public peer (ADM 6.30%, Wilmar ~7.7%). The TTM adjusted ROIC of 8% only marginally clears the company's own stated 7.2% cost of equity.

## 5. Simple Summary

- Bunge buys oilseeds and grains from farmers, crushes them into protein meal and vegetable oil, refines a portion, mills wheat and sugar in South America, and resells everything globally to feed makers, biofuel producers, and food manufacturers — a tonnes-through-assets crush-and-trade business.
- It makes money on the spread between input cost and output price (the crush spread) plus trading P&L on inventory and derivatives — not on its own pricing decisions.
- Whether each new unit creates value is unclear from disclosed data — FY25 segment EBIT was ~$20 per ton on ~$266 of segment assets per ton (a ~7.5% pre-tax return), but per-ton crush margins are not disclosed and the ratio swings wildly year to year.
- Soybean Processing & Refining is the segment that matters — 52% of revenue, 53% of segment EBIT, and the dominant earnings lever; Soybean + Softseed combined are 75% of segment EBIT.
- No moat proven over the named ABCD/Big-Six peers (ADM, Cargill, Louis Dreyfus, Wilmar, COFCO) — BG's FY25 gross margin is the lowest of the public peer set; the strongest individual moat candidate (Location/South American footprint, 30/100) is shared in some form by every peer.
- It depends on commodity prices, biofuel mandates (US RVO, EU/Brazil mandates), weather, FX (BRL/CAD/EUR/CNY), ocean-freight rates, US-China trade flows, and geopolitics — total EBIT fell 54% over FY23–FY25 on these forces despite stable management.
- Capital is allocated by a stable, professional team (CEO/CFO since 2019, Deloitte audit since 2002) with no governance red flags, but the team just made a near bet-the-company $10.6B Viterra acquisition that more than doubled debt and share count — outcome still unproven.
- Worth deeper work only if valuation is cheap relative to mid-cycle EBIT — there is no moat, no pricing power, no recurring revenue, and no disqualifier; this is a cyclical commodity merchant trading at the cycle's discretion.
