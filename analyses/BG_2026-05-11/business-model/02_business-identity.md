# Business Identity — BG

## 1. What The Company Actually Does

Bunge Global SA is an agricultural commodity processor and merchandiser. It buys oilseeds (soybeans, canola/rapeseed, sunflower seed) and grains (corn, wheat, barley, cotton, pulses, sugar) from farmers, mostly in the Americas and Europe, then stores them, ships them, crushes oilseeds into protein meal and vegetable oil, refines or further processes a portion, and resells the outputs to animal-feed makers, food manufacturers, biofuel producers, and other oilseed/grain traders worldwide [FY25 10-K, Item 1 Business, p.3]. It also mills wheat, makes biodiesel and fertilizer in some geographies, sells packaged plant-based oils, and runs a wheat-flour/bakery-mix business in South America [FY25 10-K, Item 1, p.3]. The customer pays Bunge to bridge the gap between where crops are grown and where calories, proteins, and fuels are consumed — i.e., origination, logistics, processing capacity, and risk management. On July 2, 2025 Bunge closed its acquisition of Viterra, materially enlarging the grain-handling/origination footprint and the soybean and softseed processing scale [FY25 10-K, Item 1, p.7; MD&A, p.39]. Bunge also extends financing to farmers (notably in Brazil) to lock in supply [FY25 10-K, Item 1, "Financial Services and Activities," p.6].

## 2. How The Company Makes Money

Bunge is essentially a flow business: tonnes through assets at a margin per tonne, plus trading P&L on inventory and derivatives. The company itself reports volumes for each segment in metric tons [FY25 10-K, MD&A "Volumes," p.38].

Segment-level revenue formulas (all expressed in tonnes × commodity-linked price):

- **Soybean Processing and Refining**: `Revenue ≈ (soybeans processed + soybeans merchandised + refined oil) × commodity price`. FY25 net sales $36,313m on 41.0m t crushed + 20.5m t merchandised + 3.6m t refined oil [FY25 10-K, p.42].
- **Softseed Processing and Refining**: `Revenue ≈ softseed crushed/merchandised tonnes × commodity price`. FY25 net sales $11,252m [FY25 10-K, p.44].
- **Tropical Oils and Specialty Ingredients** (renamed from "Other Oilseeds" in Q1 2026): `Revenue ≈ specialty-product tonnes × price`. FY25 net sales $4,633m on 2.5m t [FY25 10-K, p.45; Q1 2026 release, p.2].
- **Grain Merchandising and Milling**: `Revenue ≈ grain tonnes sold × commodity price + ocean-freight & financial-services fees`. FY25 net sales $18,128m on 67.2m t [FY25 10-K, p.46].

Volume is driven by harvest size in origination geographies (Argentina, Brazil, U.S., Canada, Ukraine, Australia), farmer selling decisions, and crush-plant utilization [FY25 10-K, MD&A "Overview," p.38]. Price is set by global commodity markets (CBOT soybeans, soy oil, wheat, corn) plus regional basis. Margin is the crush spread (oilseed cost vs. meal + oil revenue), the grain-trading spread, biofuel-mandate-driven oil premiums, and trading/hedging P&L; Bunge explicitly states profitability depends on "availability and market prices of agricultural commodities" plus "capacity utilization rates" [FY25 10-K, MD&A, p.38].

## 3. Business Type Classification

Globally diversified agricultural-commodity processor and merchandiser — a tonnes-through-assets crush-and-trade business with policy-driven biofuel exposure.

## 4. What Drives Variance

Variance is dominated by (i) crush-spread movements — soybean processing EBIT swung from $2,222m in FY23 to $872m in FY24 to $1,225m in FY25 with broadly similar volumes, indicating margin per tonne, not volume, drives results [FY25 10-K, p.42]; (ii) commodity prices and FX (Argentina and Brazil exposure produces meaningful FX gains/losses, e.g., a $79m softseed FX gain in FY25) [FY25 10-K, p.44]; (iii) weather and crop disease in origination regions (e.g., the FY25 European sunflower drought lifted softseed prices) [FY25 10-K, p.44]; and (iv) biofuel and trade policy — tariffs and renewable-diesel/biodiesel mandates set demand for vegetable oils [FY25 10-K, Item 1, p.10]. The Viterra closing on 2 July 2025 also distorts year-on-year comparisons across all three affected segments [FY25 10-K, MD&A, p.39].
