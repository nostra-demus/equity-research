## 2. Dominant Segment

**Soybean Processing and Refining is the dominant segment on every metric: 51.6% of FY2025 reportable revenue ($36,313m of $70,326m), 52.6% of FY2025 segment EBIT ($1,225m of $2,329m), and the largest capex draw at $790m** [FY2025 10-K, Note 26, p.F-77; p.37]. Profit and revenue dominance agree, so the call is unambiguous. However, the Soybean segment is itself a commodity crush business — its EBIT swung from $2,222m (FY23) to $872m (FY24) to $1,225m (FY25) on roughly flat tonnage [MD&A p.38, p.42], so "dominant" here means dominant share of an inherently cyclical earnings stream, not a stable annuity. Soybean + Softseed combined are 68% of revenue and 75% of segment EBIT; Bunge is effectively a diversified oilseed crusher with a large (but lower-margin) grain-trading add-on.

## 3. Segment Disclosure Quality

Disclosure quality is mid — adequate but with material recent disruption. Bunge changed its reportable-segment structure effective Q3 2025, splitting the legacy "Agribusiness" segment into three (Soybean P&R, Softseed P&R, Other Oilseeds P&R) and combining the legacy "Milling" with merchandising into "Grain Merchandising and Milling," after the Viterra acquisition closed July 2, 2025 [Note 26, p.F-76]. The Sugar & Bioenergy segment was eliminated and reclassified to Corporate & Other effective January 1, 2025 (BP Bunge Bioenergia JV stake was sold October 2024) [Note 26, p.F-76]. Prior-year comparatives in the 2025 10-K have been recast to the new structure, so 2023–2025 are comparable in the filing. The "Corporate & Other" bucket is meaningful: it carries a -$796m EBIT drag in FY25 (vs. -$367m FY24), or 52% of total EBIT, inflated by $223m Viterra integration costs, a $118m pension settlement loss, and a $30m investment impairment [MD&A p.43] — investors should not treat this as a pure overhead line. Segment-level EBIT, total assets, capex, depreciation, volumes, and a clean revenue/cost breakdown are all disclosed; income from affiliates and noncontrolling interests are also broken out per segment. The Q1 2026 release further renamed "Other Oilseeds P&R" to "Tropical Oils and Specialty Ingredients" with no compositional change [Q1 2026 release, p.2]. Net: definitions are NOT consistent over the last three years (two material changes: sugar de-segmented Jan-25; full re-segmentation Q3-25), but the filing provides recast comparatives so YoY analysis on the new structure is feasible.
