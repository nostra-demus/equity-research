## 4. Where The Company Sits

**BG sits at the bottom of the named peer set on margin and roughly mid-pack on ROIC.** Gross margin: BG 4.85% < ADM 6.30% < Wilmar ~7.7%; operating margin: BG 1.84% < LDC 4.24% < ADM 4.0% < Wilmar 6.06% EBITDA. ROIC: BG adjusted 8% (9% ex-CIP) > ADM adjusted 6.3% > Wilmar 2.65% — but BG's ROIC barely covers its stated 7.2% cost of equity [Q1 FY26 transcript, p.5], and the FY25 GAAP ROIC is only 6.7%, below the cost of equity. Cargill, LDC, and COFCO do not disclose enough for a clean ROIC comparison. The headline picture: BG's FY25 reported margins are the *worst* of the public ABCD peers, even after a year in which BG's soybean segment EBIT *rose* 40% while ADM's Crushing collapsed 81% [FY25 10-K Note 26 p.F-77; ADM Q4 FY25 release].

## 5. Moat Verdict

**No moat proven.** The strongest individual moat candidates — Location (30/100, South American mix) and Distribution (28/100, global logistics network) — are necessary scale features shared by every named ABCD/Big-Five peer, not advantages BG holds over them. BG itself states in the FY25 10-K that competition is "principally based on price" and that "neither our business as a whole nor any segment is dependent on any specific trademark, patent, or license" [Item 1, pp.3–5; "R&D, Innovation, Patents, and Licenses"]. The empirical test confirms this: BG's FY25 gross margin (4.85%) and operating margin (1.84%) are *below* ADM, LDC, and Wilmar; its trailing-twelve-month adjusted ROIC of 8% only marginally clears its 7.2% cost of equity [Q1 FY26 transcript, p.4–5]. Over the next 5 years, the durability test BG would need to pass is: maintain a gap of ≥200 bps in operating margin or ≥300 bps in ROIC vs the median of ADM + Wilmar + LDC across at least three of the next five fiscal years. The FY23–FY25 data does not show such a gap — the rank shuffles year to year on commodity-cycle factors none of these companies controls.
