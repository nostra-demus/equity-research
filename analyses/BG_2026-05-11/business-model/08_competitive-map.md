# Competitive Map — BG

## 1. Dominant Segment

Soybean Processing and Refining — 51.6% of FY2025 reportable-segment revenue ($36,313m of $70,326m) and 52.6% of segment EBIT ($1,225m of $2,329m), per upstream segment-map [FY2025 10-K, Note 26, p.F-77].

## 2. Named Competitors

The 2025 10-K names five companies as Bunge's "Key Competitors" in the Soybean Processing and Refining segment: ADM, Cargill, Louis Dreyfus, Wilmar, and COFCO International [FY2025 10-K, Item 1, p.8]. The 2–3 most credible (largest scale, most direct overlap in dominant segment) are profiled below; brief notes on the other two follow.

### Competitor A — Archer Daniels Midland Company ("ADM")

- **Ticker / listing:** NYSE:ADM (public)
- **Where they compete:** Global oilseed crushing and refining; strong overlap with BG in North America (US 21% crush capacity share) and South America; also in grain merchandising. ADM is named in all four of BG's reportable segments [FY2025 10-K, pp.8–10].
- **Scale:** FY2025 revenue ~$80.3B (year ended Dec 31, 2025), down from ~$85.5B FY2024 [ADM Q4 2025 release via S&P / Food Business News, Feb 2026]. Roughly 2.2x BG's pre-Viterra revenue base; comparable post-Viterra.
- **Source named in:** FY2025 10-K, Soybean P&R competitor list, p.8 [FY2025 10-K, Item 1, p.8]; named again in Softseed (p.9), Other Oilseeds (p.9), and Grain (p.10).
- **One-line read:** ADM is the closest functional analog to BG — a public, US-listed integrated oilseed crusher/grain trader; ADM's FY2025 Crushing segment operating profit collapsed 81% to $159M on biofuel-policy uncertainty [ADM Q4 2025 release; S&P Global, Nov 2025], whereas BG soybean segment EBIT rose from $872M (FY24) to $1,225M (FY25), a directional divergence in BG's favor.

### Competitor B — Cargill Incorporated

- **Ticker / listing:** Private (US, family-controlled)
- **Where they compete:** Global oilseed crushing, grain origination/merchandising, beef/protein, food ingredients. Direct soybean-crush overlap with BG in North America (US ~21% crush capacity share) and South America [Mordor / industry concentration data, 2024].
- **Scale:** $154B revenue for fiscal year ended May 31, 2025, down 3.75% from $160B prior year [Bloomberg / Star Tribune, Aug 2025; Cargill 2025 Annual Report]. Roughly 2.2x BG, but Cargill is far broader — meat, feed, food, and financial services account for a large portion.
- **Source named in:** FY2025 10-K, Soybean P&R competitor list, p.8 [FY2025 10-K, Item 1, p.8]; also Softseed (p.9), Other Oilseeds (p.9), Grain (p.10).
- **One-line read:** Largest of BG's named competitors by revenue and the only one with comparable global crush-and-trade footprint, but private status means no quarterly margin comparison; Cargill cut 5% of workforce (~8,000 jobs) end-2024 and consolidated five business units to three in FY2025 [Bloomberg, Aug 2025], suggesting margin pressure across the peer set.

### Competitor C — Wilmar International Limited

- **Ticker / listing:** SGX:F34 (Singapore-listed)
- **Where they compete:** Asia-Pacific oilseed crushing, palm oil, edible oils — overlaps BG in soybean crushing (notably China and India) and is named in Softseed and Other Oilseeds segments as well [FY2025 10-K, pp.8–9].
- **Scale:** FY2025 revenue US$70.4B (+4.5% YoY); core net profit US$1.28B (+10%) [Wilmar FY2025 results briefing, Feb 26, 2026]. Roughly 2x BG by revenue.
- **Source named in:** FY2025 10-K, Soybean P&R competitor list, p.8 [FY2025 10-K, Item 1, p.8]; named also in Softseed (p.9) and Other Oilseeds (p.9).
- **One-line read:** Asia-anchored oilseed processor where BG has under-indexed exposure (Asia-Pacific = 15% of BG soybean crush capacity); Wilmar's crushing margins held up in FY2025 as ADM's collapsed [Food Ingredients First, Feb 2026], suggesting regional market structure matters more than a single global crush spread.

**Other named competitors (smaller / more specialized):**
- **Louis Dreyfus Company B.V.** — private (Abu Dhabi Investment Authority owns 45%); FY2025 revenue €49.6B with Grains & Oilseeds at ~€38.7B (~78% of group); FY25 net profit US$1.1B [LDC FY25 results press release, Mar 2026]. Direct ABCD peer; named in BG's Soybean and Grain segments.
- **COFCO International** — private (subsidiary of state-owned COFCO Group, China); 2024 revenue US$38.5B on 108Mmt commodities handled [COFCO International, June 2025]. Named in BG's Soybean and Grain segments.

## 3. Competitive Position

**Position vs peers in soybean crush: appears to be Holding / modestly Gaining, but on directional read only — BG does not disclose its own crush market share.** BG's Soybean Processing & Refining EBIT moved from $872M (FY24) to $1,225M (FY25) on roughly flat tonnage [FY2025 10-K, Note 26, p.F-77; MD&A, p.42], while ADM's Crushing operating profit collapsed 81% from ~$857M to $159M in FY2025 [ADM Q4 2025 release, Feb 2026] and Wilmar's core profit rose 10% to $1.28B [Wilmar FY2025 release, Feb 2026]. BG outperformed ADM and roughly tracked Wilmar in the same period, despite a shared biofuel-policy headwind. Industry-survey data attributes BG ~26% of US soybean crush capacity vs. ADM and Cargill ~21% each [Mordor Intelligence; Farm Action concentration data, 2024], making BG the largest single US crusher by capacity — though this is a third-party estimate, not a BG disclosure. Inference, not from filings: BG's relative outperformance vs. ADM in FY2025 is more likely a function of geographic mix (BG's 47% South America capacity vs. ADM's heavier US weighting) than share gain in any specific market.

## 4. Competitive Shape

The market is a **consolidated oligopoly at both the regional and global level**. In US soybean crushing, the top 4 firms (Bunge 26%, ADM 21%, Cargill 21%, Ag Processing 12%) control 80% of capacity [Mordor Intelligence; Farm Action concentration data, citing USDA / industry sources, 2024] — a CR4 of 80% is squarely in oligopoly territory. Globally, the "ABCD" (ADM, Bunge, Cargill, LDC) plus COFCO and Wilmar form a Big Five/Six that dominate cross-border grain and oilseed flows [Green European Journal, 2025; WWF South American soy actors report, 2026]. BG's own 10-K acknowledges the structure: "We participate in an intensely competitive industry with numerous global and regional competitors. Over the past few years, certain of our competitors have added oilseed processing and refining capacity in response to growing demand" [FY2025 10-K, Risk Factors, p.13]. The Viterra acquisition (closed July 2025) further consolidated the top tier by removing one independent ABCD-adjacent merchant.

## 5. Caveat

Two limitations to flag: (i) **BG does not disclose its own market share in any segment**, so the "Holding/Gaining" read in Section 3 rests on third-party capacity estimates and inference from peer EBIT directional data, not from BG filings.
