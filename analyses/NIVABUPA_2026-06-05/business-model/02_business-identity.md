# Business Identity — NIVABUPA

## 1. What The Company Actually Does

Niva Bupa Health Insurance Company Limited is an India-only health insurer that sells indemnity-based and defined-benefit health insurance policies to individuals and corporates, entirely within India. It collects annual or multi-year premiums from retail policyholders and corporate groups in exchange for bearing the cost of hospitalisation and, increasingly, outpatient medical expenses. The retail segment (individuals and families buying directly through agents, banks, digital channels, or brokers) is the dominant business, generating roughly 67% of gross written premium; the group segment (corporate employers buying group cover for employees) contributes approximately 31%, with travel and personal accident making up the remainder. [Q1 FY26 transcript, prepared remarks, Jul 31, 2025] Customers pay a recurring annual premium to transfer the financial risk of medical events — primarily hospital admission costs — to the insurer; the insurer then recoups losses partly through claim settlement, partly through investment returns on the float, and manages total cost through a preferred provider hospital network. The company operates no international business and has no life, motor, or property lines; it is a pure-play health insurer, which is unusual among Indian general insurers. [Q4 FY26 transcript, prepared remarks, May 8, 2026]

## 2. How The Company Makes Money

**Primary revenue formula (insurance operations):**

`Gross Written Premium (GWP) = number of in-force policies × average premium per policy`

`Underwriting result = Earned Premium − Claims Incurred − Expense of Management (EOM)`

`Net profit = Underwriting result + Investment income on float`

**Investment income formula:**

`Investment income = AUM × annualised yield`

Where AUM = accumulated reserves from unearned premiums and claims reserves

---

Niva Bupa had FY26 GWP of INR 9,433 crores, up 27.4% year-on-year. Retail health GWP was approximately INR 6,582 crores for FY26, growing 35% on a full-year basis. [Q4 FY26 transcript, prepared remarks, May 8, 2026]

Investment AUM stood at INR 9,670 crores as of 31 March 2026, earning an annualised yield of 7.2% for FY26. [Q4 FY26 transcript, prepared remarks, May 8, 2026]

**What drives volume:** New policy sales through a multichannel distribution architecture — agency (individual agents, with 58,000 net additions in FY26), bancassurance (bank branches and NBFCs), digital (own app and third-party aggregators), and brokers. The company's "Bharat" strategy targets Tier 3 cities and below through virtual agency and bank partnerships. [Q4 FY26 transcript, Q&A, May 8, 2026]

**What drives price:** Premium per policy is set at renewal and for new business using actuarial pricing; the company executed high-single-digit price revisions on mature products in FY26 (e.g., 7% increase on the ReAssure 2.0 product in Q1 FY26). [Q1 FY26 transcript, prepared remarks, Jul 31, 2025] A structural price tailwind occurred in H2 FY26 when the Indian government reduced GST on health insurance from 18% to 0%, which lowered the effective price to policyholders by 18% and stimulated volume growth of approximately 29% in Q3 FY26, with ticket size (sum insured per policy) rising 15% as customers moved to higher-coverage products. [Q3 FY26 transcript, prepared remarks, Jan 29, 2026]

**What drives margin:** The combined ratio (loss ratio + expense of management ratio) is the primary margin lever. FY26 combined ratio under IFRS improved by 160 basis points to 101.4%. The retail loss ratio stabilised at approximately 66.8% for FY26. The expense of management ratio improved from 39.2% in FY25 to 33.7% in FY26, driven by operating leverage as GWP growth outpaced expense growth. The regulatory cap on total expense of management (EOM) constrains acquisition and operating costs; Niva Bupa was compliant at approximately 36% allowable EOM including additional allowances as of FY26. [Q4 FY26 transcript, prepared remarks, May 8, 2026] The company targets a steady-state combined ratio of approximately 99% by FY29 under IFRS, which it believes would translate to mid-to-high teens ROE. [Q4 FY26 transcript, Q&A, May 8, 2026] ROE for FY26 was 10.7%. [Q4 FY26 transcript, prepared remarks, May 8, 2026]

## 3. Business Type Classification

Pure-play retail-oriented health insurer operating in a regulated Indian market, with premium growth driven by rising insurance penetration and policy tailwinds, and profitability gated by claims cost management and regulatory expense limits.

## 4. What Drives Variance

Revenue variance is primarily a function of new policy volume (distribution reach and conversion), retention of the existing book, and premium revisions at renewal; in the near term, the GST removal has compressed effective prices but stimulated volume, with the net effect being positive GWP growth. Margin variance is dominated by the claims or loss ratio, which is affected by post-monsoon infection seasonality (Q1 and Q2 tend to be loss-making quarters), medical inflation embedded in hospital tariffs, and claims mix between retail (higher loss ratio, approximately 67%) and group (lower loss ratio, approximately 60%). [Q4 FY26 transcript, Q&A, May 8, 2026] The expense ratio is directionally improving through operating leverage but is bounded by IRDAI's EOM regulatory ceiling. Investment income is secondary but stable, with the float growing as GWP scales. There is no meaningful foreign exchange exposure; all business is denominated in Indian rupees.
