## 2. Dominant Segment

**Retail Health Insurance** is the dominant segment. It accounts for approximately 70% of FY26 GWP (INR ~6,582 crores out of INR 9,433 crores total) and is the explicit strategic priority of management, with retail market share tracked as a primary KPI (10.1% as at FY26 vs 9.4% in FY25). Segment-level profit is not disclosed, but management consistently directs investors to retail loss ratio (~66.8% IFRS FY26) as the key margin indicator, and the renewal-heavy, agency/bancassurance-distributed retail book is where operating leverage accumulates — expense of management ratio improved from ~39% to 33.7% over two years, driven primarily by scale in the retail book. The company is effectively a retail health insurer that also writes group business opportunistically; it does not aspire to be a group-led insurer.

The profit share statement above relies on revenue share as a proxy because segment-level EBIT or underwriting profit is not disclosed. The fallback to revenue is noted explicitly as a limitation in Section 3.

## 3. Segment Disclosure Quality

Niva Bupa does not publish formal IRDAI-style segment financial statements with separate profit and loss at the segment level in the transcripts available in the data pool; the company reports its primary financials under IFRS 17 at the entity level. Loss ratios by product segment (retail vs group) are disclosed verbally on earnings calls but are not published as a formal schedule. Segment EBIT, segment underwriting profit, or contribution margins are not quantified in any of the quarterly transcripts reviewed. The IFRS 17 accounts (going live April 2026) are expected to include detailed schedules, which management stated "will be available on our website" after Q4 FY26 close [Q4 FY26 earnings call, May 8, 2026, Q&A, Vishwanath Mahendra].

On consistency: the three-part product definition — retail, group, and travel/PA — has been stable across all quarters reviewed (Q2 FY25 through Q4 FY26). There is no evidence of segment reclassification in the period.

There is no meaningful "Other" bucket; the three product lines account for 100% of GWP, with travel/PA at ~2% being disclosed but immaterial. Within group, management distinguishes corporate (large employer-employee) from SME/Banca informally on calls but does not provide a revenue split between these sub-segments. The internal group split ("1/3 corporate, 2/3 Banca-based") was disclosed verbally in Q4 FY26 Q&A [Q4 FY26 earnings call, May 8, 2026, Q&A, Sanketh Godha/Vishwanath Mahendra exchange] but has not appeared in a filed document.

Investment income is material to profitability (7.2% yield on INR 9,670 crores AUM = ~INR 697 crores annualized gross) and is disclosed at entity level in IFRS financials but is not allocated across product segments. Downstream agents (`unit-economics`, `competitive-map`) should note that the combined ratio exceeds 100% on an underwriting basis and the business is profitable only when investment income is included — this is structurally common in Indian health insurance and is not a red flag per se, but it means segment-level underwriting margin analysis is incomplete without the investment attribution.
