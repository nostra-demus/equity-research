## 4. Read

Niva Bupa is a customer-dispersed but geographically maximally concentrated business. On the customer side, there is no meaningful concentration: the revenue base is fragmented across millions of individual retail policyholders (~67% of GWP) and a large number of employer group clients, with no single named customer disclosed as material. The business is closer to a utility with many small counterparties than a B2B company with key-account dependency. On the geography side, concentration is total: 100% of revenue is India, and no international diversification exists or is planned. The business is therefore not concentrated on customers but is maximally concentrated geographically.

The concentration on geography is structurally naked — it is not contractually secured against regulatory change, and IRDAI controls all key economic levers including product design, pricing approval, commission structures, and expense-of-management limits. Recent evidence confirms this is a live risk: the GST removal on health insurance premiums (effective October 2025) altered the economics of the entire distribution chain, and ongoing discussions around commission caps (EOM regulation) carry material revenue and margin implications. The single biggest dependency the synthesizer should know about is: the entire revenue base is subject to IRDAI regulatory discretion with no contractual, geographic, or customer hedge against an adverse ruling on commissions or pricing.
