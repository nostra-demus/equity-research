# Competitive Map — NIVABUPA

## 1. Dominant Segment

Retail Health Insurance — approximately 70% of FY26 GWP (INR ~6,582 crores out of INR 9,433 crores total); the company's explicitly stated strategic priority, tracked via retail market share as the primary external KPI.

---

## 2. Named Competitors

### Competitor A — Star Health and Allied Insurance Company Limited

- **Ticker / listing:** NSEI: STARHEALTH
- **Where they compete:** Retail health indemnity insurance, pan-India; agency-dominant distribution with semi-urban and rural reach; de minimis group exposure (retail-to-group mix 95:5 as of 9M FY26)
- **Scale:** FY26 GWP INR 20,369 crores (N basis, full year), up 16% year-on-year. Retail health market share 31.3% for 9M FY26 — the largest single share in the retail health sub-segment.
- **Source named in:** Star Health Q3 FY26 earnings call, Jan 29, 2026 (prepared remarks, CEO Anand Shankar Roy); Star Health Q4 FY26 earnings call, Apr 29, 2026 (prepared remarks, CEO). Niva Bupa management referenced Star Health's former Joint MD joining the General Insurance Council as CEO for Health, signalling direct industry awareness of Star Health as the category incumbent. [Niva Bupa Q3 FY26 earnings call, Jan 29, 2026, prepared remarks, CEO Krishnan Ramachandran]
- **One-line read:** Star Health is the incumbent leader in retail health by a wide margin (~31% share vs Niva Bupa's ~10%), operating primarily through a captive agency force of over 8 lakh agents; it is undergoing a multi-year loss ratio correction and combined ratio improvement (98.8% FY26 vs 101.1% FY25), which if sustained, would intensify competition for renewal retention and agency recruitment.

---

### Competitor B — ICICI Lombard General Insurance Company Limited

- **Ticker / listing:** NSEI: ICICIGI
- **Where they compete:** Retail Health Indemnity insurance (growing rapidly from a smaller base) and Group Health insurance across India; embedded within a diversified general insurer also writing Motor, Commercial Lines, and Marine.
- **Scale:** Total FY26 GDPI growth 7% overall; Retail Health specifically grew 51.1% in FY26, well above the industry's 19.9% retail health growth rate. Retail Health market share reached 4.1% in FY26 (up from 3.3% in FY25). Group Health market share 8.7% FY26. Total company FY26 revenue approximately INR 25,895 crores.
- **Source named in:** ICICI Lombard Q4 FY26 earnings call, Apr 15, 2026 (prepared remarks, CEO Sanjeev Mantri); data pool includes full transcript as a competitor file alongside Star Health and New India Assurance — indicating analyst community treatment of ICICI Lombard as a benchmark for Niva Bupa.
- **One-line read:** ICICI Lombard competes in retail health from a much smaller share base (~4%) but is growing fastest among comparable listed players (+51% retail health GWP FY26), backed by its multi-line bancassurance distribution and IL TakeCare app (21 million downloads); its diversified insurer status means health is a growth option rather than the core book, but the pace of share gain warrants monitoring.

---

### Competitor C — The New India Assurance Company Limited

- **Ticker / listing:** NSEI: NIACL
- **Where they compete:** Health insurance (retail and group), fire, marine, motor, and other general insurance lines, pan-India and international; the dominant state-owned general insurer.
- **Scale:** FY26 gross domestic premium INR 42,822 crores (domestic segment), total GWP INR 47,174 crores. Overall domestic market share 12.74% FY26 (up from 12.56%). Health is one of several large lines; a specific retail health sub-share is not disclosed in available transcripts. Combined ratio 122.57% FY26 — structurally above peers, reflecting wage revision impact and Motor third-party claims pressure.
- **Source named in:** New India Assurance Q4 FY26 earnings call, May 14, 2026 (prepared remarks, CMD Girija Subramanian); included as a competitor file in the NIVABUPA data pool alongside Star Health and ICICI Lombard transcripts, consistent with its status as India's largest general insurer and a systemic participant in the retail health segment.
- **One-line read:** New India Assurance competes in health insurance primarily by scale and distribution legacy (government-sector tie-ups, PSU employer group covers), not by product innovation; its combined ratio of 122.57% FY26 and government ownership constraints make it an unlikely share gainer in the retail indemnity segment where Niva Bupa and Star Health are competing, but its absolute size and distribution breadth keep it relevant as a market participant.

## 3. Competitive Position

Niva Bupa is gaining share in the dominant retail health segment. Retail market share rose from 9.4% in FY25 to 10.1% for full-year FY26, and reached 10.4% in Q4 FY26 specifically. [Q4 FY26 earnings call, May 8, 2026, prepared remarks, CEO] This occurred against an industry retail health backdrop of approximately 20% growth for the full year [Star Health Q4 FY26 earnings call, Apr 29, 2026, prepared remarks, CEO], while Niva Bupa's own retail GWP grew 35%. The spread — approximately 15 percentage points above industry — is the proxy for share capture, as IRDAI segment-level share data is disclosed by management on a quarterly basis rather than from a filed document. The gain is partly structural (GST removal benefited the entire industry in H2 FY26 with industry H2 retail health growth of 30%), but Niva Bupa's H2 retail growth exceeded 40%, suggesting above-market execution rather than a pure policy tailwind. The primary share donor appears to be Star Health, whose 9M FY26 retail market share was stated at 31.3% — its retail GWP grew 16% against the category's 33.6% growth in Q3 FY26 alone, implying Star Health underperformed the industry during that period.

## 4. Competitive Shape

The Indian retail health insurance market is oligopolistic with one dominant incumbent and several fast-growing challengers. By retail health market share, Star Health (~31%) commands a commanding lead, followed by Niva Bupa (~10%), ICICI Lombard (~4%), and a long tail of state-owned general insurers and other private players. The top three standalone health insurers — Star Health, Niva Bupa, and Care Health (unlisted, not separately profiled here as no transcript is present in the data pool) — are widely understood to account for a large majority of the standalone retail health segment, though precise HHI data is not available in the data pool. The General Insurance Council's GDPI figures cited by ICICI Lombard [Q4 FY26 earnings call, Apr 15, 2026] show the full general insurance industry at INR 3.36 lakh crores for FY26, within which health (retail and group combined) grew at 15.4% and specifically retail health at 19.9%. The retail health sub-segment structure is therefore: one large incumbent (Star Health), two mid-scale challengers with share momentum (Niva Bupa and ICICI Lombard), a disengaged state-sector participant (New India Assurance and other PSU insurers), and an unlisted challenger (Care Health Insurance). This is closer to a 3–4 player oligopoly in the commercially contested retail indemnity segment, with no evidence of meaningful fragmentation among smaller players winning share.

## 5. Caveat

The data pool does not include a Niva Bupa prospectus, DRHP, or annual report that lists named competitors in a formal Risk Factors or Business Overview section. Competitors are identified from: (a) the analyst-curated data pool, which includes transcripts for Star Health, ICICI Lombard, New India Assurance, SBI Life, HDFC Life, and LIC — the latter three being life insurers with no direct overlap in retail health indemnity and therefore excluded from the named list; (b) Niva Bupa management's verbal references across seven earnings calls reviewed; and (c) IRDAI share data disclosed by multiple management teams. Care Health Insurance (formerly Religare Health Insurance), which is consistently cited in industry analyst notes as the third standalone health insurer and a direct competitor, is not included in this data pool and is therefore not profiled with a structured block — its omission is a data gap, not a conclusion about its competitive standing. Resolving this gap would require a Care Health transcript, IRDAI annual report segment tables, or a Capital IQ peer export including Care Health's premium figures.
