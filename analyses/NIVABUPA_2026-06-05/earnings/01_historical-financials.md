# Historical Financials — NIVABUPA

**Niva Bupa Health Insurance Company Limited**
**Fiscal year ending:** 31 March
**Reporting currency:** Indian Rupee (INR)
**Accounting note:** Numbers in the annual table are drawn from the Capital IQ financial database (sourced from statutory filings), which uses the Insurance template. All monetary figures in INR millions unless otherwise stated. CIQ restates earlier years on a "latest filings" basis. The company reports under both India GAAP (statutory / IRDAI) and IFRS 17 (management-preferred since FY24). Where CIQ figures and management IFRS disclosures diverge, both are noted. The FY22–FY23 gross margin line is not comparable to FY24–FY26 due to a mid-year IRDAI-mandated accounting reclassification (the "1/N" rule effective October 2024) that shifted multi-year premium recognition; see note [13] below.

**Metric definitions for this insurance company (adaptations to standard template):**
- **GWP (Gross Written Premium):** total premium written before reinsurance cession. Analogous to gross revenue in a non-insurance business.
- **NEP (Net Earned Premium):** GWP net of ceded reinsurance, earned over the policy period. Shown as "Premiums and Annuity Rev." in CIQ.
- **Total Revenue:** NEP + net investment income + realised gains/other.
- **Combined Ratio (IFRS):** Claims incurred + Expense of Management (EOM) as % of NEP. Below 100% = underwriting profit; above 100% = underwriting loss covered by investment income.
- **Loss Ratio:** Claims incurred / NEP.
- **EOM Ratio (Expense of Management):** Total operating expense / GWP (IRDAI definition).
- **EBITDA / EBIT:** As reported by CIQ from statutory filings. Not management's preferred measure; combined ratio is more analytically relevant for this insurer.
- **FCF:** CFO − total capex (capex = capital expenditures + purchase of intangibles, both shown as cash outflows).
- **Solvency Ratio:** Regulatory capital metric; minimum required = 1.5x. More relevant than Net Debt/EBITDA for a health insurer.

## 6. Key Trend Summary

**Revenue:** Total revenue has grown at a stable 33–34% per year in FY25 and FY26, down from 47–48% in FY23–FY24. GWP growth (the operational top-line) is decelerating from ~45% to ~27% over four years, reflecting base effects on a now-larger premium base (INR 9,433 crores GWP in FY26 vs INR 2,810 crores in FY22). At 27%, absolute INR additions remain substantial (INR ~2,027 crores added in FY26 vs INR ~1,799 crores in FY25). The company gained retail health market share from 9.1% in FY24 to 10.1% in FY26 despite deceleration in sector-wide growth rates.

**Margins:** All margin lines are expanding. EBITDA margin improved from (8.3%) in FY22 to 6.1% in FY26. PAT margin moved from (9.1%) to 4.3% over the same period. The key insurance metrics confirm the direction: combined ratio (IFRS) improved 160 basis points to 101.4% in FY26; EOM ratio improved from 41.2% (FY23) to 33.7% (FY26), a 750-basis-point improvement over three years. The insurer remains technically underwriting-loss-making at entity level (combined ratio above 100%), but the gap is closing rapidly. Investment income (INR 6,190 mn in FY26, 7.2% yield on INR 9,670 crores AUM) more than covers the underwriting deficit and generates the reported PAT.

**Seasonality:** Q4 (January–March) is structurally dominant, accounting for approximately 32–33% of annual India GAAP revenue based on FY26 data. The renewal wave concentrates in March. Q1 (April–June) is the weakest quarter. This pattern introduces material seasonality in quarterly EPS and IFRS PAT, with Q4 generating approximately 43% of full-year IFRS PAT in FY26.

**Inflection points:** Three material inflection points in the five-year record. First, FY23–FY24: EBITDA turned positive and PAT turned positive for the first time; the company moved from a high-growth loss-making state to a growing profitable state. Second, October 2024: IRDAI's 1/N accounting regulation changed multi-year premium recognition under India GAAP, creating FY25 India GAAP noise and suppressing reported India GAAP EPS in Q1–Q3 FY26. Third, October 2025: GST on health insurance reduced from 18% to 0% (effective November 2025), representing an approximately 15% price cut to consumers, which boosted H2 FY26 volume (management cites H2 retail growth of 40% vs industry H2 of 30%).
