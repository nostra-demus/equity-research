## 3. Implied Scenario Interpretation

Taken together, the multiple-implied scenario (Block 3) and short-interest/positioning data (Block 5) tell a coherent story: the market has partially but not fully priced the Qatar disruption's benefit into the primary affected industries.

For Cheniere Energy (DIR-002 proxy), the current EV/EBITDA multiple of 10.2x sits at the midpoint of the sector's historical normal range and is consistent with management's already-raised EBITDA guidance of $7.5 billion — nothing more. The 23 analysts covering the stock are unanimous (Strong Buy), and the consensus price target of $302 implies 28% additional upside, meaning sell-side models have updated to absorb the guidance raise but are not yet reflecting a further JKM/TTF-driven upside scenario. The estimate dispersion block (Block 1) shows a wide $5.52 high-to-low EPS spread ($15.25 to $20.77), which captures precisely this uncertainty: bears have $15 EPS, bulls have $21 — the stock is pricing something close to the mean but the bulls are not yet winning the tape.

For Flex LNG (DIR-001 proxy), the picture is more striking: the stock trades at 11.5x EV/EBITDA, above the normal-cycle midpoint for the sector, and 19% above the consensus fair-value target — yet options (Block 4) show only a 4.9% expected move and IV at the 56th percentile, with a notably elevated put/call ratio of 2.67. Together these signals say: the price has run ahead of fundamental models (sell-side is Sell), options traders are not pricing a further large move, and hedgers or short-positioned traders are buying puts to defend against a giveback. The 14.5% short interest (Block 5) adds to this: the stock has attracted the highest short position among LNG peers, consistent with the view that the spot-rate windfall has already been priced in (or over-priced, given that most FLNG fleet days are fixed on long-term charters at ~$73k–$78k/day, far below the $300k/day spot spike).

**In plain words: the market is pricing "Qatar disruption known and partially absorbed" — not "LNG supply crisis, multiples should re-rate dramatically higher." Prices have moved up from pre-disruption levels but the implied scenario is closer to "disruption is priced in at current guidance" than to "full monetisation of the spot-rate spike."**
