# M0.6.4 Mispricing Reason — SIG-20260612-dd716589

## 1. Primary Category

- **primary_category:** complexity

- **primary_category_rationale:** Both missing mechanisms require the analyst to close a multi-step, cross-domain inference chain that neither the commodity specialist nor the equity analyst completes on their own. Mechanism 1 (charter renewal rate cascade) demands: (a) tracking which ~75 LNG vessels have charters expiring within 24 months, (b) recognising that vessel owners negotiate renewals against the disruption-elevated spot market rather than a historical floor, (c) translating the resulting per-day rate step-up into forward EBITDA for specific issuers, and (d) comparing that to the pre-disruption contracted rates that sell-side models currently use as steady-state — four steps across shipping contract analytics, commodity market dynamics, and corporate finance. Mechanism 2 (TTF backwardation → storage injection breakdown) requires: (a) reading the TTF forward curve structure (commodity analytics domain), (b) computing the injection break-even spread (storage economics domain), (c) identifying the EU policy response (ARERA emergency mandate, regulatory domain), (d) projecting the resulting EU storage fill shortfall (energy supply modelling), and (e) mapping all of that back to duration and floor for non-Qatar LNG exporter EBITDA (equity valuation). The sell-side gap evidence from M0.6.3 confirms this is the operative barrier: commodity specialists (Timera Energy, European Gas Hub, S&P Global Commodity Insights) have done steps (a)–(d) in isolation, and equity analysts have updated issuer EBITDA models for the immediate guidance raise, but no published equity research note was found that closes the full five-step chain from TTF curve structure to equity valuation. This is a complexity mispricing, not a timing one: the mechanism is not merely lagged in analyst models — it is structurally absent. Timing would apply if analysts understood the mechanism and their models update on a known calendar; here, four separate searches on 2026-06-12 returned zero equity analyst reports containing the mechanism at all. It is also not primarily behavioral: the market has moved in the right direction (both FLNG and Cheniere have re-rated since the Qatar disruption), so the issue is not anchoring preventing any re-rating — it is an incomplete re-rating because the second-order chain from commodity structure to equity cash flow has not been assembled.
