## 3. Ranking Rationale

FLNG ranks first because it is the only name where the specific variant mechanism — charter renewal rate reset from ~$73k/day to $100k+/day on 2–3 expiring contracts — adds a quantified $20M–$42M EBITDA step-up that no current sell-side model prices in. The entire 13-vessel fleet is in the thesis-relevant business; there is no diluting segment. GLNG ranks second because it too is 100% LNG infrastructure revenue with zero unrelated businesses after the 2025 carrier exit, though the tolling-fee mechanism reaches earnings more slowly and with more contractual buffering than FLNG's spot-sensitive vessels. Woodside ranks third at 58/100 because 51% of its LNG is priced on gas-hub indices — meaningful direct exposure — but the 49% long-term-contract book and its deliberately insulated shipping strategy dilute the mechanism. Cheniere ranks fourth despite being the most liquid name and the most covered by sell-side analysts: the 95% long-term contract book means the spot-price thesis reaches only ~5% of production. Excelerate and Enagás rank fifth and sixth as secondary-tier (IND-002 and DIR-003/IND-002) expressions where the mechanism is real but slower to reach earnings and partially capped by regulation or re-contracting timing. Enel ranks last as the only short candidate, with a score of 18 reflecting unquantified, heavily-diluted exposure — it is included as the closest available short leg rather than a clean expression of the HARM-002 mechanism.
