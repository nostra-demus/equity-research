## 3. Implied Scenario Interpretation

The market is pricing TCS as a structurally slower-growth business that no longer deserves the premium multiple it commanded during the 2020–2022 upcycle. The forward P/E of 13.85x (Block 3) is roughly half the 5-year historical average of 27x, and the EV/EBITDA of ~11x is 37% below the 10-year median of 17.9x. These are not cyclical-discount levels — they imply the market has concluded that a structural de-rating is here to stay, driven by AI pricing pressure, BFSI client caution, and slower revenue growth. Within that already-depressed framework, the options market (Block 4) is pricing an 8.4% move around the July earnings print, which tells us the Q1 FY27 report is not fully absorbed — investors are uncertain whether TCS will use the results call to signal FY27 recovery or to confirm continued softness. The $70M DXC exceptional charge sits inside this picture as a known quantity: at 13.85x forward, a ~Rs 4–5 EPS drag translates to roughly Rs 55–70 of theoretical price impact, which is small relative to the Rs 1,400+ multiple compression already in the stock versus its 5-year average price level. The FII positioning data (Block 5) reinforces this: foreign investors have been quietly selling for at least one quarter. The stock's +1.57% move on the day of the DXC disclosure (thesis record, WC-004) is consistent with this picture — the charge was already partly anticipated given the prior $150M provision, and the market cared more about legal closure removing an overhang than about the incremental Rs 5 EPS hit to Q1.
