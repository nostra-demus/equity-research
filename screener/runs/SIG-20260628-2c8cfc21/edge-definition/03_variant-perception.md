## 1. The Variant

The consensus treats the Centaline City Leading Index (CCL) recovery to ~159–160 as proof that the property-collateral stress mechanism is over. It is not. The CCL measures whole-market median residential prices; the 2322.HK filing specifically describes borrowers in its trading and money lending book who cannot refinance despite the headline index rising. The stress is concentrated in weaker, lower-quality borrower segments and non-prime collateral — exactly the pool that small licensed HK money lenders hold, and exactly the pool the CCL does not measure. The market is pricing zero additional ECL burden at peer money lenders in FY2026; we think the number is materially above zero.

## 3. Mechanisms Missing from Consensus

1. **mechanism_1: CCL headline recovery masks sub-prime collateral deterioration still ongoing in non-bank money lending books.** (Attacks consensus assumption #1: "The residential property collateral cycle has turned: nine consecutive months of CCL price gains through February 2026 mean the 'declining property collateral' mechanism stated in the 2322.HK filing describes a past condition, not a current or worsening one.")

   The CCL is an index of all HK residential transactions, weighted by volume across all quality tiers. It does not measure the collateral quality of property-backed receivables held by small licensed money lenders, whose borrowers are typically sub-prime or non-mainstream. A headline CCL of 159–160 is consistent with prime-district and new-development prices recovering while weaker collateral — older stock, non-prime locations, borrowers who cannot service refinanced debt — remains stressed. The 2322.HK filing directly states that clients cannot obtain re-financing even as the profit warning is filed on 26 June 2026, after nine months of CCL gains. The consensus's reliance on the CCL as proof that the collateral mechanism is over is a category error: it is tracking the wrong part of the market. No sell-side research found frames this sub-prime / non-bank collateral divergence as a live ECL risk.

2. **mechanism_2: AEON Credit is the wrong benchmark for the micro-cap sub-segment.** (Attacks consensus assumption #3: "AEON Credit (the main listed sector proxy) is the right read-through: improving credit performance and 17% profit growth in FY2026 show the sector is not experiencing the same ECL pressures disclosed by 2322.HK.")

   AEON Credit (0900.HK) operates primarily in the personal-credit-card / instalment-finance space, with a broader and better-diversified borrower base than a small licensed money lender whose book is concentrated in trading-account and property-backed loans to a narrow client list. AEON's lower impairment charges in FY2026 describe its own product mix and borrower pool — not the property-backed money lending sub-segment. Using AEON's credit metrics to dismiss a profit warning from a lender with a structurally different book is a product-mix error. The consensus has no alternative sector benchmark: coverage of the small licensed-money-lender sub-segment is, in practice, zero (M0.6.2 Block 1).
