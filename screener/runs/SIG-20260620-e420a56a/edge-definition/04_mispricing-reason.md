# M0.6.4 Mispricing Reason — SIG-20260620-e420a56a

## 1. Primary Category

- **primary_category:** complexity

- **primary_category_rationale:** The mispricing requires three sequential steps that cross two separate analyst silos. Step one: read the Jio Platforms DRHP (filed 19 Jun 2026) to extract the use-of-proceeds detail confirming that Rs 27,500 crore of the Rs 37,700 crore fresh issue is earmarked specifically for RJIL debt repayment, leaving Jio at near-zero net debt (~Rs 79 crore residual). Step two: translate that balance-sheet change into a claim about Jio's post-IPO pricing capacity — specifically that removing Rs 76,255 crore of annual EBITDA from any debt-service obligation gives Jio unconstrained room to run a targeted price cycle. Step three: back-solve Airtel's EV at a mean-reversion multiple (9x vs current 11.3x) to quantify what a single pricing cycle does to the Airtel equity story. No single sell-side team does all three steps: Jio DRHP coverage sits in ECM/IPO desks, Airtel equity coverage sits in a separate telecom team, and the cross-domain connection from "Jio balance sheet improvement" to "Airtel EV compression scenario" is precisely what six web searches on 22 Jun 2026 confirmed has not appeared in any published research note. This is complexity, not timing: the DRHP data is public and available now — the barrier is the multi-step, cross-team work required, not a model-update lag. It is also not behavioral: the Airtel analyst community is not ignoring the Jio risk out of anchoring alone — they have simply not been asked to model the specific conditional scenario of Jio's post-IPO balance sheet feeding into Airtel pricing disruption, because that scenario lives in the gap between two coverage teams.
