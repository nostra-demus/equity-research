## 3. Ranking Rationale

EVR ranks first because it is the only candidate whose exposure mechanism is already done: advisory fees on a separation of this size are earned at or before distribution (June 29, 2026), and 84% of Evercore's total revenue comes from advisory work — the fee channel is direct and the business has no trading-book or balance-sheet risk from the transaction itself. GE Aerospace ranks second because it is the largest pure-play aerospace listing and its 4–6% organic growth guidance clearly outpaces the 3% HONA print that anchors the thesis — it sits in the tier that the thesis says HONA doesn't belong in, making it the most natural long counterpart. TDG ranks third for the same pure-play reason but sits behind GE because its heavy leverage introduces a company-specific risk layer that dilutes exposure purity. ITW is the cleaner short leg of both pairs: it is more fully diversified than 3M (which already ran a major spin-off) and its organic growth at 1–3% gives it the weakest claim to a pure-play multiple premium. HEI rounds out the long aerospace side with the highest organic growth rate in the group but lower market cap limits how broadly the re-rating signal flows through it first. GS and MMM trail because their business-mix dilution (GS) and partial de-conglomeration (MMM) make them weaker expressions of their respective mechanisms.
